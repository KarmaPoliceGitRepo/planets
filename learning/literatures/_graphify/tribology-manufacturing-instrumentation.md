# Graphify input — Tribology, Manufacturing & Instrumentation (literature corpus)

## Overview
This corpus covers the science and practice of abrasive manufacturing (grinding, honing), engineering drawing and materials, temperature and dimensional instrumentation, piezoelectric transducer standards, and ultrasonic measurement techniques applied to tribological interfaces. It exists to support in-situ sensing research (viscosity, friction, wear, bore distortion) conducted at the University of Sheffield Tribology Group, contextualised by the manufacturing processes and component tolerances that create the surfaces being measured.

## Entities & Relationships

- **Flash temperature** —governs→ **scuffing onset** : Blok (1963) shows sum of bulk + flash temperature at scuffing is approximately constant (~130–300 °C) for a given steel/oil pair, independent of speed or load.
- **Thermal contact coefficient (b = √(k·ρ·c))** —controls→ **heat partition ratio** : heat splits between two rubbing bodies in proportion b₁√v₁ : b₂√v₂; for equal b, flash temperature ∝ f·p·(√v₁+√v₂)/b.
- **Grindable (foil/workpiece) thermocouple** —suffers→ **transparency error** : FEM analysis (Lefebvre 2006) shows MTR peak error can reach ~30%; cooling-phase signal is immune and preferred for partition ratio extraction.
- **Honing (crosshatch process)** —produces→ **plateau-honed bore surface** : simultaneous rotation + reciprocation of bonded-abrasive stones; Rvk ≥ 0.6 µm target for oil retention, Rpk set by finish pass only.
- **Plateau honing** —characterised by→ **ISO 13565-2/3 Rk parameter family** : Rk (core), Rpk (peak height), Rvk (valley depth), Mr1/Mr2; ISO 13565-3 probability model (Rpq, Rvq, Rmq) recommended over Rk method for valley description.
- **Triboconditioning®** —reduces→ **engine friction FMEP** : mechanochemical WC-ledge + synthetic fluid process cuts friction mean effective pressure 5–15% on 1.6 L GDI engine while inducing compressive subsurface stresses to ~10 µm depth.
- **STAMINA (standing wave method)** —measures→ **film thickness / viscosity / deflection** : continuous-wave frequency sweep; resonant amplitude S and frequency shift Δf_s encode interface properties; ~20× more sensitive to temperature than to 1 µm thickness change — temperature compensation mandatory.
- **Shear wave reflection viscometry** —exploits→ **acoustic impedance of liquid** : z_L = √(ρ·η·ω) for Newtonian fluids; Maxwell viscoelastic extension adds relaxation time τ for multigrade engine oils; demonstrated in-situ in a motored journal bearing (Schirru 2016).
- **Contact Acoustic Nonlinearity (CAN)** —encodes→ **friction coefficient** : shear ultrasound at a Coulomb-friction contact generates odd harmonics (3ω, 5ω); A3/A1 ratio peaks at intermediate contact pressure and increases with μ; extraction validated against tribometer (Li 2018).
- **Pulse-echo ultrasound** —measures→ **cylinder bore distortion** : TOF gives thickness T = v·t/2; viable for static mapping but temperature-induced velocity changes (~20× signal of 1 µm change per 10 °C) require compensation before dynamic in-situ use.
- **PZT (hard vs. soft)** —selected by→ **application power level** : hard PZT (high Qm, low d33) for resonant/high-power transmitters; soft PZT (high d33, high coupling) for sensors; recommended operating temperature ≤ 50% of Curie temperature.
- **EN 60751 tolerance classes (AA/A/B)** —specify→ **Pt100/Pt1000 accuracy** : Class B ±(0.30+0.005|t|) °C; Class A ±(0.15+0.002|t|) °C; Class AA ±(0.10+0.0017|t|) °C; once operated outside class range, tolerance is void.
- **3-wire RTD connection** —compensates→ **lead resistance error** : standard for Pt100 up to ~30 m cable; 4-wire fully eliminates error (lab/calibration); Pt1000 tolerates 2-wire due to higher base resistance.
- **ISO 492 bearing tolerance classes** —define→ **bore runout limits** : Normal → Class 6 → 5 → 4 → 2 (tightest); d = 18–30 mm Normal class: Δdmp = 0/−10 µm, Kia = 13 µm; ISO 492:2014 migrates symbology to GPS framework, deletes grade E.
- **ISO/GPS datum system** —constrains→ **tolerance zones** : primary datum removes most DOF (e.g., 3 rotations for a plane), secondary then tertiary remove remaining; association criterion (minimum zone by default) maps real surface to ideal geometry (ISO 5459:2011).
- **ASTM E8/E8M** —specifies→ **tensile test geometry** : E8 uses 4D gauge length (inch-pound), E8M uses 5D (metric); not numerically equivalent; standard specimen 12.5 mm dia × 50 mm GL; 0.2% offset yield method.
- **Laser flash method (LFA)** —measures→ **thermal diffusivity** : Cape–Lehman model with improved Bessel-function polynomials and real pulse-shape fit (Blumm 2002) achieves <1.5% accuracy vs. FEM reference; applicable to solids and liquids at high temperature.
- **Non-Gaussian surface height PDF** —characterised by→ **ultrasonic pitch-catch + pulse-echo** : Saniman (2020) shows pitch-catch (region A: Rq·cosθ/λ ≤ 0.037) estimates Rq within ±3%; pulse-echo (region B: ≥ 0.081) estimates Rsk within ±20% using Johnson distribution.
- **Matching layer** —improves→ **STAMINA viscosity sensitivity** : magnesium layer between steel and oil reduces S_min from ~0.97 to 0.62–0.70 for η ≈ 1–2 Pa·s; layer thickness and material constants must be known accurately.
- **Abrasive machining (grinding)** —generates→ **elevated grinding temperature** : specific energy ~10× conventional cutting due to small chip thickness; temperature governs thermal damage threshold; moving heat source theory (Jaeger/Rosenthal) is the analytical framework.
- **cBN abrasive** —preferred for→ **hardened steels and exotic alloys** ; **diamond abrasive** —preferred for→ **cast iron, carbide, ceramics, glass** (both in honing and grinding contexts).
- **Piezoelectric coupling factor (k)** —measured by→ **resonance/anti-resonance frequency pair** : keff² = (fp²−fs²)/fp²; individual mode factors (k31, k33, kt, kp, k15) corrected via EN 50324-2 graphical curves; must wait ≥ 24 h post-poling.
- **Amplitude-frequency (A-F) effect** —limits→ **hard PZT high-power operation** : resonance frequency decreases with drive level; Type 300 (Qm ≥ 800) shows significantly lower nonlinearity coefficients α, β than Type 100 (Qm ≥ 300), making Type 300 preferred for continuous high-power use.

## Glossary

- **flash temperature** — transient peak surface temperature rise at a sliding/grinding contact, superimposed on bulk temperature; governed by heat source velocity, contact width, and thermal contact coefficient b = √(k·ρ·c).
- **thermal diffusivity (α, a)** — ratio of thermal conductivity to volumetric heat capacity; governs rate of temperature diffusion; measured by laser flash method.
- **honing** (also: bore honing, cylinder honing) — controlled low-speed abrasive sizing and surface-finishing process using bonded-abrasive stones that simultaneously rotate and reciprocate inside a bore.
- **plateau honing** (also: multistep honing, rough + plateau honing) — two-step bore-finishing process producing flat load-bearing plateaus and deep oil-retaining valleys; characterised by Rpk, Rvk, Rk, and the probability parameters Rpq, Rvq, Rmq.
- **Triboconditioning®** (also: mechanochemical surface finishing, in-manufacture running-in) — WC-ledge + synthetic-fluid process that creates a plateau surface, low-friction tribofilm, and compressive subsurface stresses without abrasive removal.
- **STAMINA** (STAnding wave Measurements of INterfaces and lAyers; also: Multiple Standing Wave Method) — continuous-wave ultrasonic technique driving a frequency sweep to measure interface/layer properties from resonant standing wave amplitude S and frequency shift Δf_s.
- **Contact Acoustic Nonlinearity (CAN)** — generation of odd-order harmonics (3ω, 5ω) when high-power shear ultrasound interacts with a stick-slip frictional contact; harmonic ratio A3/A1 encodes friction coefficient μ.
- **RTD / Pt100 / Pt1000** — resistance temperature detector exploiting PTC of platinum; Pt100 = 100 Ω at 0 °C, Pt1000 = 1000 Ω; standardised by EN 60751 / IEC 60751; tolerance classes AA, A, B.
- **Callendar–Van Dusen equation** — polynomial relating Pt100/Pt1000 resistance to temperature using constants A = 3.9083×10⁻³, B = −5.775×10⁻⁷, C = −4.183×10⁻¹² (°C units); two forms for below and above 0 °C.
- **acoustic impedance (z)** — product of material density and wave speed (z = ρ·c); determines reflection and transmission at interfaces; for liquids z_L = √(ρ·η·ω) for shear waves (Newtonian model).
- **PZT** (lead zirconate titanate; also: hard PZT Type 100/300, soft PZT Type 200) — dominant piezoelectric ceramic; hard grades (high Qm) for power ultrasound, soft grades (high d33) for sensors; Curie temperature sets maximum operating temperature.
- **electromechanical coupling factor (k)** — √(converted energy / input energy) in a piezoelectric resonator; mode variants k31, k33, kt, kp, k15; measured from fs/fp pair per EN 50324-2.
- **bearing ratio curve** (Abbott-Firestone curve) — cumulative material ratio vs. depth; basis for Rk family (ISO 13565-2) and plateau-hone parameterisation; 40% window method defines the core segment.
- **GPS / datum system** (ISO 5459:2011) — ISO/GPS framework for specifying datums from real surfaces; primary datum removes most DOF; association criterion (minimum zone default) derives situation feature from real surface.
- **scuffing** (also: seizure, adhesive wear) — sudden surface damage on lubricated rubbing components caused by oil-film breakdown at high flash temperature; Blok's postulate: bulk + flash = constant critical temperature for a given material/oil pair.
- **specific grinding energy** — energy per unit volume removed in grinding (~10× cutting); consequence of ~100× smaller chip thickness at ~10× higher speed; drives unusually high grinding temperatures.
- **Maxwell viscoelastic model** — spring + dashpot in series used to extend Newtonian ultrasonic viscosity equation; adds relaxation time τ; converges to Newtonian result when τ → 0; necessary for multigrade oils at high shear rates.
- **Johnson distribution** — flexible probability family (SU unbounded type) generalising Gaussian to describe non-Gaussian surface height PDFs (negative skewness typical of honed/ground surfaces); used in Kirchhoff ultrasonic scattering model.
- **microhoning** (also: superfinishing, short-stroke honing) — honing variant with fine grit (320–1200), short strokes (1–4 mm), high frequency (300–2500 cpm); achieves Ra < 0.1 µm; converts rough crests to wide plateaus.

## Key findings

- Blok's scuffing temperature postulate: for a given steel/oil pair the critical temperature (bulk + flash) is constant, validating flash temperature as a lubrication design criterion; scuffing temperatures for non-additive mineral oils span ~130–300 °C depending on viscosity grade.
- Foil thermocouple MTR peak error reaches ~30% at high workpiece velocity (≥ 300 mm/s) or with thick mica insulation (10 µm); measuring the energy partition ratio from the cooling phase (after wheel separation) is inherently immune to these systematic errors.
- In both STAMINA and pulse-echo methods, a 10 °C temperature change produces a signal shift ~20× larger than a 1 µm mechanical thickness change in cast iron — temperature compensation is equally and critically important for both methods.
- STAMINA offers three advantages over pulse-echo: (1) higher sensitivity via multiple reflections, (2) faster time resolution (each cycle is a data point), (3) lower hardware cost (function generator + oscilloscope, no pulsed hardware).
- Shear wave reflection viscometry in a motored journal bearing (Schirru 2016): sputtered PZT on shaft surface; Maxwell model required for multigrade oils; measured viscosity consistent with off-line viscometer; matching layers improve sensitivity but require accurate layer characterisation.
- CAN friction measurement (Li 2018): only odd harmonics generated by Coulomb stick-slip; A3/A1 peaks at intermediate contact pressure and is zero at high pressure (fully stuck); excitation frequency must avoid element self-harmonics (5 MHz element preferred over 1 MHz).
- Plateau honing probability model (ISO 13565-3): Rpq, Rvq, Rmq from bimodal normal-probability decomposition is the recommended approach; Rk method (ISO 13565-2) is acknowledged to poorly model the valley region; skidless profilometer required (skidded instruments introduce trailing-skid artefacts).
- Triboconditioning® reduces FMEP 5–15% on a 1.6 L GDI engine and grows a low-friction tribofilm + compressive stresses to ~10 µm depth — without abrasive material removal, preserving oil-retention valley geometry.
- Piezoceramic operating temperature should not exceed 50% of Curie temperature; for engine bearing sensing, PIC255 (max 175 °C) and PIC300 (max 200 °C) recommended; for very high-temperature sensing Del Piezo BiT DL-10 (Curie 650 °C); Type 300 (Qm ≥ 800) preferred for continuous high-power operation over Type 100/200.
- EN 60751 RTD tolerance: once a sensor is operated outside its class temperature range, the tolerance class is permanently void regardless of dwell time or return to range; Pt1000 is preferred for 2-wire connections because higher base resistance reduces fractional lead-error.
- ISO 492 bearing tolerances: for d = 18–30 mm, Normal class Δdmp = 0/−10 µm, Kia = 13 µm; Class 6 tightens to Δdmp = 0/−8 µm, Kia = 8 µm; ISO 492:2014 deleted grade E and migrated to GPS symbology.
- Laser flash method accuracy: improved Cape–Lehman model (Blumm 2002) with real pulse-shape fit and higher-order radial Biot terms achieves thermal diffusivity within 1.5% of FEM reference at 2000 °C; mica insulation in grindable thermocouples should be ≤ 5 µm for contact times < 1 ms.
- Ultrasonic roughness measurement (Saniman 2020): pitch-catch estimates Rq within ±3%; pulse-echo estimates Rsk within ±20%; honing/grinding/milling surfaces have Rsk typically −1 to +1 and Rku 2–10 — within method's design range.
- Abrasive selection rule for honing: alumina for carbon/alloy steels; SiC for soft/tough steels, cast iron, non-ferrous; cBN for hardened steels and exotic alloys; diamond for carbide, ceramics, glass; mirror finishes viable on thermal-spray bore coatings (LDS/TWAS) because oil is retained in coating pores.
- Compression testing (ASTM E9): bearing block faces must be flat to 0.0002 in./in.; barreling from end friction and elastic buckling (Euler: Scr = Cπ²E/(L/r)²) are the two dominant failure modes for compression specimens; three specimen form categories (short L/D ≈ 0.8–2, medium L/D ≈ 3, long L/D ≈ 8–10).

## Gotchas

- Temperature compensation is the dominant challenge in all ultrasonic thickness/viscosity measurements: a 10 °C change in cast iron swamps a 1 µm mechanical change by ~20×; any in-situ measurement rig must include a thermocouple calibration scheme for wave speed.
- RTD tolerance class is permanently voided if the sensor is operated (even briefly) outside its rated temperature range — select class and range conservatively, and note that Class AA thin-film sensors are only rated 0–150 °C.
- Skidded (shop-floor) profilometers suppress waviness and introduce spurious depressions when the skid rides over peaks — they are unsuitable for plateau-hone specification; always use a skidless instrument and robust (not Gaussian) profile filter.
- Foil thermocouple MTR readings are unreliable at high workpiece velocities or with thick mica insulation; use the cooling-phase slope for energy partition ratio rather than the peak MTR.
- PZT elements self-generate odd harmonics, especially 1 MHz elements; for CAN measurements the excitation and detection frequencies must be chosen so the element's own harmonic response does not overlap the CAN harmonics — a 5 MHz element is significantly cleaner.
- STAMINA matching-layer configuration dramatically improves viscosity sensitivity but model accuracy degrades sharply if layer thickness or material constants are not precisely known; mismatch caused ~5× viscosity error in student experiments.
- Hard PZT (Type 100/200) under high-power continuous drive is subject to the A-F effect (resonance frequency decrease with drive) and risks thermal runaway / depolarisation; Type 300 (Qm ≥ 800) has significantly lower nonlinearity and is preferred for continuous high-power use, while Type 100/200 are acceptable for pulsed (low duty-cycle) applications.
- Bore honing process capability targets have tightened from Cpk 1.33 → 1.67 → 2.0+; specifying only Ra or only Rz leaves bore surfaces under-constrained; a complete specification requires parameters from at least two of the three families (traditional, Rk, probability).
- ASTM E8 and E8M use different gauge-length conventions (4D vs. 5D) and are not numerically equivalent; results reported under one standard cannot be directly compared with the other.
- Scuffing temperature postulate applies only to a specific material + oil combination; dynamic load effects and friction coefficient uncertainty at design stage make direct application of Blok's Tmax formula prone to systematic error in practice.
