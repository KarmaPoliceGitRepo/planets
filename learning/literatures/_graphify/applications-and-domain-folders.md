# Graphify input — Applications & Domain Folders (literature corpus)

## Overview
This digest covers ten application-focused or domain-specific literature folders: **effect-of-temperature**, **effect-of-stress**, **microstructure**, **airborne-ultrasonics**, **transducers-and-piezoelectricity**, **bore-and-liner**, **range-measurement**, **contact-study-using-us-waves**, **lubrication-film**, and **wear-measurement**. Together they document how core ultrasonic physics is deployed in practical measurement and engineering contexts: from non-invasive temperature profiling through EHD film thickness and bearing-load monitoring to engine bore distortion, wear gauging, and non-contact air-coupled inspection. Heavy cross-pollination with the Sheffield/Tribosonics cluster (Dwyer-Joyce, Drinkwater, Brunskill) is present throughout.

## Entities

- **Ultrasonic thermometry (V(T) inversion)** —basis for→ **2D temperature field reconstruction** : wave speed in steel decreases ~0.48 m/s/°C; pulse-echo TOF differences inverted layer-by-layer (bisection / Ihara method) or tomographically; 1 ns timing error ≈ 1°C at 40 cm scale (wadley-1986-ultrasonic-internal-temperature, wei-2020-2d-temperature-reconstruction).
- **Bisection / layer-peeling algorithm** —extends to→ **2D conjugate-gradient inversion** : 1D Ihara method reconstructs radial profile from single-face pulse-echo; Wei (2020) generalises to 2D with Fermat ray-bending and adjoint heat-equation; minimum ~26 measurement points needed for < 5% surface heat-flux error (wei-2020-2d-temperature-reconstruction).
- **Curie temperature anomalies** —create→ **velocity / attenuation discontinuities in steel** : cementite (Fe₃C) Curie point ~200–215°C causes attenuation peak in carbon steels; ferrite–austenite transformation ~910°C causes velocity discontinuity requiring separate V(T) calibrations either side; EMAT limited to below ferromagnetic Curie temperature (darbari-1968-attenuation-carbon-stainless-steel, dubois-2001-laser-ultrasonics-phase-transformations).
- **Acoustoelastic effect** —governed by→ **Murnaghan third-order elastic (TOE) constants (l, m, n)** : fractional velocity change proportional to strain via acoustoelastic coefficient L; for bearing steel L_zz = −2.24; Hughes-Kelly (1953) and Man-Lu (1987) models provide Eulerian/Lagrangian formulations (chen-mills-2015-bearing-tof, smith-1963-acoustoelastic-review).
- **Three-component ToF model (bearing load)** —separates→ **geometric deflection + acoustoelastic + contact phase-shift contributions** : all three are comparable in magnitude; Hilbert-transform envelope cross-correlation removes the phase-shift contribution; applied to NU2244 wind-turbine bearing (367 mm OD) at 380–980 kN, ToF change 22.8–43.9 ns (chen-mills-2015-bearing-tof).
- **Acoustic nonlinearity parameter β** —sensitive to→ **dislocation density / micro-damage / plastic strain** : second-harmonic amplitude A₂ ∝ β·k²·A₁²·x; resonance modulation method achieves Re ~ 0.05 with Q ~ 10⁵; Zarembo-Krasilnikov (1971) provides comprehensive TOE constant tables for ~30 materials (zarembo-krasilnikov-1971-nonlinear).
- **Grain scattering attenuation** —three regimes→ **Rayleigh (α ∝ f⁴D³), stochastic (α ∝ f²D), geometrical** : Stanke-Kino unified theory spans all regimes; anisotropy factor W controls magnitude; EMAT / laser ultrasonics enable non-contact grain-size measurement (muller-gurevich-fluid-flow-2005, yang-orthorhombic-cluster-2013).
- **Wave-induced fluid flow (WIFF)** —governed by→ **Biot slow-wave diffusion length vs. heterogeneity scale** : maximum Q⁻¹ when slow-wave diffusion wavelength = heterogeneity correlation length a; universal asymptotics Q⁻¹ ∝ ω at low f, Q⁻¹ ∝ ω⁻¹/² at high f; Müller-Gurevich (2005) provides closed-form Q⁻¹(ω) for exponential, Gaussian, and von Kármán correlation functions (muller-gurevich-fluid-flow-2005).
- **Air-coupled transducer** —limited by→ **impedance mismatch (56–90 dB insertion loss at air-solid interface)** : dominant over atmospheric absorption (< 10 dB at 1 MHz, 50 mm path); commercial 400 kHz C-scan achieves > 40 dB SNR with thin porous matching layer; pressurised gas coupling extends range to pulse-echo detection in steel pipelines at 6.9 MPa (nde-applications-air-coupled-transducers, ultrasonic-sensors-in-air-review).
- **PZT complex material constants** —three loss types→ **dielectric (tan δ_ε), elastic (tan δ_c), piezoelectric (tan δ_d)** : Holland (1967) convention writes constants as a − ib; Debye / Havriliak-Negami / Zener dispersion models apply per type; passivity requires |d_i| ≤ √(s_i^E · ε_i^T)/2 (sherrit-complex-material-constants-1998).
- **Polymer/metal multilayer matching layer** —achieves→ **arbitrary intermediate acoustic impedances below λ/15 thickness** : effective impedance Z_MK = √(M·K) where M = metal areal mass, K = polymer spring constant; 11 µm PVDF + 22 µm Cu realises 9.25 MRayl (intermediate between PZT5H 36 MRayl and water 1.5 MRayl); stacked copper-tape absorbers: 10 layers → 4 MRayl, 220 dB/cm attenuation at 3 MHz (toda-thompson-polymer-metal-matching-2012).
- **Bore distortion (Fourier orders)** —ring conformability→ **degrades sharply with order for same amplitude** : Hill & Newman formula Ui = 3Fr·d·(d−a)³ / [4Eha³(i²−1)²]; 2nd-order 5 µm → ring conforms; 3rd-order 5 µm → 3 gaps ~10 µm; 4th-order 5 µm → 4 gaps ~20 µm; siamesed-bore engines dominated by 2nd-order thermal ovality up to 37 µm RMS at 6000 rpm (chittenden-priest-piston-assembly-bore-distortion-1993, bird-gartside-federal-mogul-fired-bore-distortion-2002).
- **Impact-Echo (IE) / S1-ZGV method** —subject to→ **near-field systematic underestimation of thickness (5–15%)** : first-arrival velocity near source is a mix of P-wave and Rayleigh wave, both lower than theoretical VP in near field; FEM (COMSOL) confirms; receivers must be outside near-field zone (baggens-2015-impact-echo-near-field).
- **Interfacial stiffness K** —linear in→ **contact pressure P** : K = K₀ + b·P confirmed across steel-steel contacts 0–1000 MPa; first loading deviates from subsequent (plastic asperity shakedown); measured with broadband focused transducer 4–17 MHz validating spring model (interaction-ultrasound-partially-contacting-interface-drinkwater-1996, kendall-tabor-1971-ultrasonic-contact-area).
- **Spring model vs. continuum model for lubricant films** —valid domains→ **h < λ/10 (spring), h ~ λ/2 (resonance), h > 40 µm (ToF)** : spring model stiffness K = ρc²/h; for EHD lubricant, speed of sound rises from ~1450 m/s at ambient to ~3550 m/s at 0.8 GPa; Jacobson-Vinet equation corrects acoustic impedance under pressure; method spans 2 nm to >40 µm total range (dwyer-joyce-lubricant-film-ultrasound-2003).
- **Phase-comparison wear measurement** —theoretical resolution→ **0.015 µm, practical ~0.75 µm** : CW frequency adjusted to maintain fixed echo phase; sensitivity amplified by factor N for Nth echo; main errors are temperature-induced velocity shift (~1 MPa equivalent per °C) and vibration; reference-notch and FFT resonant-dip methods (Brunskill 2015) overcome this without explicit thermometry (birring-kwun-ultrasonic-wear, brunskill-realtime-wear-reflectometry).
- **Diffraction errors in ultrasonic flow meters (USMs)** —flow calibration compensates→ **volumetric flow but NOT velocity-of-sound (VOS) errors** : VOS accuracy requirement ±0.3 m/s (≈ ±0.075%) for custody transfer; FEM-based transducer models required; simplified plane-piston corrections insufficient for high-accuracy meters (lunde-2003-transient-diffraction-flow-meters).
- **Air-coupled absolute β measurement** —enabled by→ **model-based receiver calibration using MGB propagation model** : Torello (2017) derives force-to-voltage calibration from Al 2024 reference; transmission coefficient at metal-air interface T ≈ 10⁻⁴ (80 dB loss) makes secondary harmonics generated in air negligible; validated on Al 2024 and fused silica (torello-air-coupled-nonlinearity-2017).
- **Rayleigh wave reflection at soft contacts** —enables→ **remote in-situ characterisation of rubber/seal contacts** : reflection coefficient decreases with increasing load on rubber O-rings; Gasni PhD thesis demonstrates this as alternative to bulk longitudinal-wave measurement for low-modulus materials (gasni-phd-thesis-ultrasonic-oil-film).

## Glossary

- **V(T) calibration** — measured relationship between ultrasonic wave speed and temperature for a specific material; used as the transfer function in ultrasonic thermometry; steel shear wave: V(T) = −0.4521·T + 3259.9 m/s (25–230°C) (wei-2020-2d-temperature-reconstruction).
- **bisection / layer-peeling algorithm** — iterative 1D inversion reconstructing internal temperature layer-by-layer from consecutive pulse-echo TOF differences and a calibrated V(T) curve; first described for steel billets by Ihara-Takahashi and independently by Wadley (1986).
- **a priori heat-conduction constraint** — use of the mathematical form of the heat-conduction solution (Bessel series for cylinders, adjoint CGM for arbitrary geometries) to reduce the number of ultrasonic paths needed from thousands to ~3–26 (wadley-1986-ultrasonic-internal-temperature).
- **acoustoelastic constant (L_zz)** — material-specific coefficient relating fractional velocity change to strain in the z-direction; L_zz = −2.24 for bearing steel; related to Murnaghan TOE constants l, m, n (chen-mills-2015-bearing-tof).
- **shear wave birefringence** — splitting of a shear wave into fast and slow polarisations when propagating along a stress axis; magnitude proportional to the stress; basis for non-invasive stress mapping without knowing TOE constants (effect-of-stress _concepts.tsv).
- **acoustic Reynolds number (Re = ρωu₀λ/η)** — dimensionless measure of acoustic nonlinearity; determines ratio of maximum second-harmonic amplitude to fundamental; practical modulation method achieves Re ~ 0.05 with Q ~ 10⁵ resonator (zarembo-krasilnikov-1971-nonlinear).
- **stabilisation distance (x_s = ln2/2α)** — distance at which second acoustic harmonic reaches its maximum amplitude before attenuation dominates; ~44 cm in Mg-Al alloy rod at 5 MHz (zarembo-krasilnikov-1971-nonlinear).
- **Stanke-Kino unified theory** — self-consistent perturbation theory for elastic wave propagation in polycrystals, valid across Rayleigh, stochastic, and geometric scattering regimes; uses Dyson equation for mean wave propagation (microstructure _concepts.tsv).
- **Biot slow wave / WIFF** — highly attenuated diffusive compressional wave in fluid-saturated porous media; governs wave-induced fluid flow between mesoscopic heterogeneities; maximum Q⁻¹ when slow-wave diffusion length equals heterogeneity correlation length (muller-gurevich-fluid-flow-2005).
- **impedance matching layer (sub-λ/15)** — thin polymer/metal bilayer structure achieving effective acoustic impedance Z_MK = √(M·K); allows arbitrary intermediate impedances without λ/4 constraint (toda-thompson-polymer-metal-matching-2012).
- **bore distortion Fourier orders** — decomposition of radial bore deviation into harmonics (order 0 = diameter, 1 = eccentricity, 2 = ovality, 3 = 3-lobe, 4 = 4-lobe); higher orders disproportionately harmful for ring sealing at equal amplitudes; measured by Incometer or in-engine eddy-current sensors (chittenden-priest-piston-assembly-bore-distortion-1993).
- **ring conformability (Hill-Newman criterion)** — minimum free-shape ring deviation amplitude per Fourier order required to conform to bore distortion; formula Ui = 3Fr·d·(d−a)³/[4Eha³(i²−1)²]; 2nd-order rings conform freely, higher orders do not (chittenden-priest-piston-assembly-bore-distortion-1993).
- **S1-ZGV mode** — first symmetric Lamb mode at its zero-group-velocity frequency; its frequency peak is used in the Impact-Echo method for concrete plate thickness estimation; accurately detected even in the near-field where P-wave velocity is unreliable (baggens-2015-impact-echo-near-field).
- **asperity shakedown** — progressive elimination of plastic contact deformation under repeated loading until nominally elastic response; evidenced by hysteresis loop changes in K-vs-load ultrasonic cycling experiments (interaction-ultrasound-partially-contacting-interface-drinkwater-1996).
- **resonant-dip method (wear measurement)** — frequency-domain technique in which subsurface holes near the wear surface create interference dips in the FFT spectrum; dip position tracks remaining material thickness; temperature errors negligible because path length from hole to surface is only ~1 mm (brunskill-realtime-wear-reflectometry).
- **phase-shift method (film thickness)** — uses the phase change of the reflected ultrasonic pulse (rather than amplitude alone) to determine film thickness; necessary for acoustically dissimilar contacts (e.g., Perspex-steel) where amplitude method fails (gasni-phd-thesis-ultrasonic-oil-film).
- **Jacobson-Vinet equation of state** — equation relating lubricant bulk modulus (hence speed of sound) to applied pressure; corrects lubricant acoustic impedance for EHD conditions; speed of sound rises 2–4× from ambient to 0.8 GPa (dwyer-joyce-lubricant-film-ultrasound-2003).
- **sing-around flow meter** — ultrasonic flow measurement using reciprocal time difference (upstream minus downstream transit time) proportional to flow velocity; Siemens household gas meter achieves < 0.5% error over 1:1000 dynamic range (ultrasonic-sensors-in-air-review).

## Key findings

- Steel shear-wave velocity decreases linearly ~0.45 m/s/°C (25–230°C); AISI 304 longitudinal velocity ~5700 to ~5300 m/s (25–750°C, slope ~−0.48 m/s/°C); ferrite-austenite transition at ~910°C and Curie point of cementite ~200–215°C produce calibration discontinuities requiring separate treatment (wadley-1986-ultrasonic-internal-temperature, darbari-1968-attenuation-carbon-stainless-steel).
- 2D temperature field reconstruction requires < 1 ns timing resolution for < 5% heat-flux error, and a minimum of ~26 uniformly distributed measurement points; non-uniform placement in high-gradient regions outperforms uniform at the same count (wei-2020-2d-temperature-reconstruction).
- For wind-turbine bearing load monitoring (NU2244, 367 mm OD), the three ToF contributions — geometric deflection (Δt_δ), acoustoelastic speed change (Δt_c), and contact phase shift (Δt_φ) — are all comparable; ignoring the contact phase shift introduces systematic load error; envelope cross-correlation using the Hilbert transform removes Δt_φ; validated 380–980 kN with ToF change 22.8–43.9 ns (chen-mills-2015-bearing-tof).
- Acoustoelastic constant for bearing steel L_zz = −2.24 (±5%); agrees with literature range −2.1 to −2.45; ~5% uncertainty in L_zz propagates to ~5% uncertainty in estimated load (chen-mills-2015-bearing-tof).
- Second longitudinal harmonic in Mg-Al alloy at 5 MHz stabilises at ~44 cm path; maximum (u₂/u₀) at stabilisation ~ Re = ρωu₀λ/η; non-synchronous transverse-driven longitudinal harmonic is two orders of magnitude smaller; Raman scattering of sound by sound (T+T→L) combination signal ~10⁻³–10⁻⁴ of single-wave signal (zarembo-krasilnikov-1971-nonlinear).
- Grain scattering: Rayleigh regime α ∝ f⁴D³ for D << λ; stochastic regime α ∝ f²D for D ~ λ; Stanke-Kino theory spans both and predicts a Rayleigh-stochastic hump for longitudinal waves where dominant scattering into shear modes creates excess attenuation near the transition (microstructure _concepts.tsv, yang-orthorhombic-cluster-2013).
- Wave-induced fluid flow (WIFF) in porous rocks: universal asymptotic scaling Q⁻¹ ∝ ω¹ (low f), Q⁻¹ ∝ ω⁻¹/² (high f) holds in 3D random media, not just 1D periodic layers; exact shape depends on the heterogeneity correlation function (exponential vs. Gaussian vs. von Kármán) (muller-gurevich-fluid-flow-2005).
- Air-to-solid transmission loss dominates air-coupled NDE (56–90 dB) versus atmospheric absorption (< 10 dB at 1 MHz over 50 mm); commercially demonstrated > 40 dB SNR for composite C-scan at 400 kHz using impedance-matched porous layer; pressurised methane at 6.9 MPa enables pulse-echo wall-thickness monitoring of steel pipelines (nde-applications-air-coupled-transducers).
- For polymer/metal sub-wavelength matching layers the effective impedance follows Z_MK = √(M·K); a non-traditional low-Z polymer adjacent to PZT (high-Z metal outer) is equally valid; stacked copper-tape backing: 10-layer stack → 4 MRayl, 220 dB/cm attenuation at 3 MHz (toda-thompson-polymer-metal-matching-2012).
- Bore distortion in a fired siamesed-bore 2.0L engine: 2nd-order thermal ovality 37 µm RMS at 6000 rpm full load dominates all other orders; combustion pressure contributes only ~7 µm; bolt-induced 4th order ~5 µm RMS is nearly constant with speed; rings conform most easily to 2nd order — siamesed bores are less detrimental to sealing than the absolute distortion amplitude implies (bird-gartside-federal-mogul-fired-bore-distortion-2002).
- Impact-Echo near-field: systematic thickness underestimation of 5–15% occurs when receivers are placed in the near-field zone because first-arrival picks reflect a mix of P-wave and Rayleigh contributions; the S1-ZGV frequency remains accurate even in the near field (baggens-2015-impact-echo-near-field).
- Interfacial stiffness K varies linearly with contact pressure (K = K₀ + b·P) up to ~690 MPa; dry steel-steel contacts show K approximately 10× lower than oil-lubricated contacts at the same pressure; first loading cycle shows plastic deformation (asperity shakedown) irreversibly; numerical (Webster-Sayles) contact model is closest to measurement but still underpredicts (interaction-ultrasound-partially-contacting-interface-drinkwater-1996, kendall-tabor-1971-ultrasonic-contact-area).
- Lubricant speed of sound rises from ~1450 m/s at ambient to ~3550 m/s at 0.8 GPa (2–4×); not correcting for this gives large film-thickness errors in EHD contacts; spring-model lower limit ~2 nm at 60 MHz; ToF method requires > 40 µm (dwyer-joyce-lubricant-film-ultrasound-2003).
- Ultrasonic wear measurement: phase-comparison technique has theoretical resolution 0.015 µm but practical accuracy in-engine only ~0.75 µm due to temperature and vibration; Brunskill (2015) reference-notch and resonant-dip methods reduce temperature error by placing the reference reflector within ~1 mm of the wear face — effectively eliminating the thermal path (birring-kwun-ultrasonic-wear, brunskill-realtime-wear-reflectometry).
- USM diffraction errors: flow calibration corrects volumetric flow errors but NOT VOS errors; VOS accuracy requirement is ±0.3 m/s (≈ ±0.075%); FEM-based transducer models required for high-accuracy (< 0.1%) custody-transfer meters (lunde-2003-transient-diffraction-flow-meters).
- Air-coupled absolute β measurement: metal-to-air transmission coefficient T ≈ 10⁻⁴ (~80 dB insertion loss); second harmonic generated in air by transmitted fundamental is negligible; model-based calibration using MGB (N = 15 Gaussian beams) and a reference specimen avoids self-reciprocity limitation; validated on Al 2024 and fused silica (torello-air-coupled-nonlinearity-2017).

## Gotchas

- **Separate V(T) calibrations required for ferritic and austenitic steel**: the ferrite–austenite phase transformation at ~910°C and the cementite Curie point at ~200–215°C produce velocity discontinuities; a single linear calibration spanning both regimes gives large systematic errors (wadley-1986-ultrasonic-internal-temperature).
- **Ray bending direction is opposite in solids vs. gases**: ultrasonic rays bend toward high-velocity (low-temperature) regions in air, but toward low-velocity (high-temperature) regions in solids because V decreases with T in most solids; failing to account for this reversal corrupts 2D reconstruction and creates detection blind zones at critical angles (~73.6° for steel shear waves at the tested conditions) (wei-2020-2d-temperature-reconstruction).
- **Contact phase shift mimics a physical ToF shift in bearing load measurement**: zero-crossing timing of a reflection from an oil-film-loaded contact acquires a phase advance (0 to π) depending on film thickness and frequency; this appears as an apparent ToF shift that must be removed (Hilbert-transform envelope method) before acoustoelastic analysis (chen-mills-2015-bearing-tof).
- **Plastic asperity shakedown makes the first loading cycle non-representative**: K-vs-P curves on first loading are significantly lower than on subsequent unloading/reloading cycles; experiments quoted without specifying load cycle number are ambiguous (interaction-ultrasound-partially-contacting-interface-drinkwater-1996).
- **Spring model breaks down when film thickness exceeds ~λ/10**: near layer resonance the spring model overestimates K; the continuum multi-layer model must be used for thicker films; switching between methods at the wrong threshold causes a step artifact in the film-thickness trace (dwyer-joyce-lubricant-film-ultrasound-2003).
- **Higher Fourier orders of bore distortion are disproportionately harmful for the same amplitude**: a 5 µm 4th-order distortion leaves ~20 µm gas leakage gaps while 5 µm 2nd-order distortion is fully accommodated by ring elasticity; reporting only total bore distortion without Fourier decomposition is misleading (chittenden-priest-piston-assembly-bore-distortion-1993).
- **In-engine ultrasonic wear measurement errors are dominated by temperature, not timing**: temperature change of 1°C produces ~1 MPa-equivalent TOF shift; a 30-minute tribometer test can shift TOF by 12 ns from temperature alone, comparable to the wear signal; the resonant-dip method is nearly immune because the reference path is only ~1 mm (brunskill-realtime-wear-reflectometry).
- **USM flow calibration does not fix VOS errors**: diffraction-induced systematic errors that affect volumetric flow can be cancelled by calibration against a reference meter, but the same errors corrupt the velocity-of-sound (VOS) channel used for mass/energy measurement; these require FEM-modelled transducer-specific corrections (lunde-2003-transient-diffraction-flow-meters).
- **Air-coupled NDE: impedance mismatch, not atmospheric absorption, is the dominant loss**: a common misconception leads practitioners to over-specify operating frequency to minimise absorption while underestimating the 56–90 dB transmission loss at the air-solid boundary; matching layer design (optimal impedance √(Z_air·Z_solid)) is the critical variable (nde-applications-air-coupled-transducers).
- **Transverse second harmonics in isotropic solids require a symmetry-breaking mechanism**: a pure transverse wave in an ideal isotropic solid generates no synchronous transverse second harmonic by symmetry; experimentally observed transverse harmonics trace to dislocations, residual internal stresses, or grain anisotropy — not direct lattice nonlinearity; values reported without this caveat overstate true material β (zarembo-krasilnikov-1971-nonlinear).
