## Overview
A short JASA Express Letter describing the design, fabrication, and characterization of a 10 mm diameter, natively spherically focused capacitive-film (electrostatic) air-coupled ultrasonic transducer. Unlike prior approaches that used external acoustic mirrors, Fresnel zone plates, or cylindrical focusing elements, this device achieves diffraction-limited focusing through a spherically curved flexible copper/polyimide backplate and a conformally shaped aluminized Mylar film, eliminating all auxiliary focusing hardware.

## Unique contribution
The key innovation is making both the backplate and the polymer film conform to a spherical surface so that focusing is intrinsic to the transducer geometry. A warm steel ball bearing is used to stretch the 6 µm Mylar film into a spherical shape without wrinkling. The result is a device that matches the ideal spherically focused piston radiator model with no free parameters: measured focal point at 24.9 mm vs. theoretical 25.4 mm geometric focal length, and measured FWHM of 1.38 mm vs. theoretical 1.37 mm.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer: 10 mm diameter, 25.4 mm geometric focal length, ±15° angular sensitivity, fabricated from a flexible Cu/PI backplate patterned with 40 µm depressions at 80 µm center-to-center spacing, deformed onto a spherical fixture.
- Characterized with a quasi-point receiver (200 µm aperture baffled capacitive transducer) scanned in the focal zone; receiver biased to 200 V DC.
- Broadband excitation (200 µs random-phase signal): center frequency 805 kHz, 6 dB bandwidth 760 kHz (446–1207 kHz), far wider than piezoceramics.
- Measured focal point: 24.9 mm (within 0.01 mm of theory); focal zone: 17.1–34.1 mm.
- For narrowband 800 kHz tone burst, measured FWHM = 1.38 mm, theory = 1.37 mm — effectively no deviation from ideal spherically focused piston behavior.
- Practical takeaway: native focusing removes need for mirrors or zone plates, and the flexible substrate approach solves the historically difficult problem of curving a rigid backplate.
