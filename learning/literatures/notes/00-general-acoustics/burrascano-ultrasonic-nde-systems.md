## Overview
A multi-author engineering book addressing practical challenges of implementing ultrasonic NDE at industrial scale. Contributors come from ultrasonics, signal processing for telecommunications, earth exploration, and geophysics, deliberately cross-pollinating methods from these sibling fields to improve industrial UT performance. Topics include coded excitations, pulse compression, advanced signal processing, and system-level design.

## Unique contribution
Applies telecommunications signal-processing techniques (coded excitation, Golay codes, pulse compression, adaptive filtering) to ultrasonic NDE, improving SNR and resolution beyond what conventional tone-burst or spike excitation can achieve. Bridges the gap between academic UT research and industrial implementation constraints (transducer bandwidth, electronic noise, reverberant clutter).

## Key concepts
- Coded excitation: chirp, Golay codes, pseudo-random sequences
- Pulse compression, matched filtering
- Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) improvement techniques
- Time-gain compensation (TGC)
- Digital signal processing for UT
- Phased array imaging and beamforming
- TOFD (time-of-flight diffraction)
- Acoustic emission
- Model-based inversion for defect characterisation
- Adaptive and nonlinear filtering

## Methods / results / takeaways
Chirp excitation with matched-filter reception achieves a compression gain equal to the time-bandwidth product TB, allowing peak power reduction while maintaining SNR. Golay complementary pairs have perfect autocorrelation sidelobes (sum to zero) enabling sidelobe-free range profiles. Phased array synthetic aperture focusing (SAFT) improves lateral resolution from the transducer near-field limit to approximately λ/2 independent of depth. TOFD uses mode-converted diffracted waves from crack tips to size defects to better than 1 mm accuracy in welds, independent of reflector orientation. Industrial implementation must account for transducer bandwidth, cable losses, and surface coupling variability.
