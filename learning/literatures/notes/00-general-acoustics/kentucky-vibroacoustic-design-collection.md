## Overview
Course materials for a graduate-level vibro-acoustics design course at the University of Kentucky (circa 2012-2021). Contains large slide-based chapter PDFs covering fundamental vibration/acoustics concepts (Ch.1), mathematical methods (Ch.3: Fourier, Laplace, modal analysis), the wave equation (Ch.4), reflection/transmission and standing waves (Ch.5), energy methods (Ch.7: statistical energy analysis, mechanical energy flow), vibration isolation (Ch.9: isolator design for machinery), and sound in ducts (Ch.10: plane wave modes, lined duct attenuation). Also includes ANSYS FEA project materials for vibroacoustics simulation. Note: Chapter_02_6_Slides.pdf likely covers chapters 2–6 in condensed slide format. Chapters 6 and 8 are absent.
