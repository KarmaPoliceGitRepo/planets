## Overview
A 12-chapter set of computational hydrodynamics/astrophysical fluid dynamics lecture notes (dated April 2021). Covers the mathematical and numerical foundations for solving hyperbolic conservation laws (Euler equations, Navier-Stokes), with particular emphasis on numerical methods: advection algorithms, flux-conservative schemes, Riemann solvers (Godunov-type methods), higher-order schemes, and multidimensional fluid solvers. Later chapters address fluid instabilities, point explosions (blast wave theory), turbulence modeling (Navier-Stokes + Reynolds averaging + subgrid models), and Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH). Acoustic wave propagation is implicit as the hyperbolic character of the Euler equations is the unifying theme (Riemann invariants, characteristics, shock capturing).

## Note on relevance
This folder is misplaced under "General Acoustics" — it is primarily computational fluid dynamics / astrophysical hydrodynamics content. Acoustic relevance is limited to: hyperbolic character of wave equations (Ch.2), Riemann solvers applicable to compressible flow (acoustic regime), point explosion / blast wave (Ch.10), and turbulence (Ch.11). The Euler equations of Ch.1 are the starting point for acoustic wave derivation.
