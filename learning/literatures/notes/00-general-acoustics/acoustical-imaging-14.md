## Overview
Proceedings of the 14th International Symposium on Acoustical Imaging, bringing together researchers from seismic exploration, medical ultrasound, underwater acoustics, and nondestructive testing under the common framework of acoustic wave-based imaging. Emphasises high-resolution imaging techniques and the unity of physical principles across diverse frequency ranges.

## Unique contribution
Illustrates the cross-disciplinary nature of acoustical imaging: the same wave-theoretic framework underlies seismic depth profiling (kHz), underwater robotics (tens of kHz), medical echography (MHz), NDT microscopy (tens of MHz), and acoustic microscopy (GHz). Key insight in the preface: different application domains differ mainly in frequency scale, so methods developed in one field (e.g., seismic migration algorithms) transfer to others (e.g., synthetic aperture ultrasound imaging).

## Key concepts
- Seismic exploration and migration (layered media)
- Medical echography; tissue characterisation
- Underwater robotics and sea-bottom investigation
- Acoustic microscopy and NDE of integrated circuits
- High-resolution ultrasound imaging
- Transducer technology for imaging
- Signal processing for acoustical imaging
- Holographic and synthetic aperture techniques
- Quantitative characterisation (beyond defect detection)

## Methods / results / takeaways
The symposium spans contributions on seismic modelling (reflectivity migration, inversion), medical B-scan systems, focused transducer arrays, signal processing algorithms (matched filters, deconvolution), and acoustic microscopy. The editorial preface notes that resolution scales with frequency, and attenuation limits penetration depth, so each application naturally occupies an optimal frequency band. Contributions by Lizzi, Coleman, and Driller include methods for spectral analysis of backscattered ultrasound for tissue characterisation, relating spectral slope and midband level to microstructural parameters such as scatterer size and concentration.
