## Overview
A collaborative Wikibooks textbook covering a broad range of acoustics topics at an introductory to intermediate level. Chapters span fundamental wave theory, room acoustics, psychoacoustics, sound speed, filter design, active noise control, musical acoustics, human vocal folds, and more. Intended as a freely available teaching resource.

## Unique contribution
A multi-topic survey useful as a reference compendium. The breadth (from wave equations to psychoacoustics and vocal fold modelling) makes it a convenient first-stop reference, though depth in each topic is lighter than specialist monographs.

- Musical acoustics applications

## Methods / results / takeaways
The Helmholtz equation ∇²p + k²p = 0 is derived from the wave equation assuming time-harmonic fields; it governs room modes, open-space radiation, and duct propagation. Sabine's formula for reverberation time: T₆₀ = 0.161 V / (A·ᾱ) where V is room volume and ᾱ is mean absorption coefficient. Psychoacoustic equal-loudness curves (Fletcher-Munson) show that sensitivity is strongly frequency-dependent, peaking near 3–4 kHz. Active noise control cancels unwanted sound by generating an anti-phase signal; feedforward systems require a coherence reference and adaptive filtering (LMS algorithm). The source–filter model of voice production separates glottal source (periodic impulse train) from vocal tract filter (formant resonances), enabling speech synthesis and analysis.
