## Overview
The landmark Academic Press series "Physical Acoustics — Principles and Methods" edited by W.P. Mason (and later R.N. Thurston and A.D. Pierce). Published continuously from 1964–1999, it covers the full breadth of physical acoustics: propagation in crystals, piezoelectricity, lattice dynamics, surface waves, nonlinear acoustics, resonance scattering, underwater acoustics, medical ultrasonics, and ultrasonic NDE. Individual volumes focus on specific sub-topics. The early volumes (I-A, II-A, II-B) feature Mason's own classic chapters on piezoelectricity, transducer design, and electromechanical coupling.
