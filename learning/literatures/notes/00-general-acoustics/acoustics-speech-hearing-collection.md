## Overview
MIT OpenCourseWare lecture slides for 6.551J "Acoustics of Speech and Hearing" (Fall 2004). The course begins with physical acoustics (wave equation, plane waves, spherical waves, scattering/diffraction), then develops lumped-element circuit analogs for acoustic systems (compliance, mass, resistance), electromechanical transducers, and the middle ear as a mechanical transformer. The second half covers speech production (vowel formants via vocal tract tube models, fricative noise sources, consonant articulation) and speech perception (auditory physiology, psychoacoustics). A zip archive of all lectures is included.
