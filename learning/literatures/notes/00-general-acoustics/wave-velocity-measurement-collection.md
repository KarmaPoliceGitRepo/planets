## Overview
A curated set of classic papers on precise ultrasonic velocity measurement in solids. The collection traces the development of the pulse-echo-overlap (PEO) method from McSkimin's foundational 1960–61 work through Papadakis's diffraction-corrected variant (1968) and later refinements (Chung 1969; Negita 1989). These methods are used to measure elastic constants of materials at acoustic frequencies (typically 1–100 MHz) to high precision (< 0.01%).

## Key methods covered
- McSkimin's pulse superposition method: RF carrier burst matched to round-trip transit time
- Pulse-echo-overlap (PEO): oscilloscope overlap of successive echoes to measure transit time
- Papadakis diffraction phase corrections: accounts for beam spreading-induced phase shift
- Modified PEO (Chung–Silversmith): phase-sensitive detection for improved accuracy
- Negita superposed pulse overlap: more recent variant with improved signal processing
