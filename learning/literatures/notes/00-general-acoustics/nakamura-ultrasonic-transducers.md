## Overview
A multi-author handbook covering the full range of ultrasonic transducer technology, from fundamental piezoelectric materials through modelling, characterisation, and applications. Sixteen chapters written by specialists, divided into three parts: (I) Materials and Design, (II) Modelling and Characterisation, (III) Applications. Covers both traditional PZT ceramics and advanced materials including single crystals, thin-film PZT, and EMAT transducers.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive modern (2012) survey spanning classical transducer theory (Mason model, KLM model) to recent materials (PIMNT single crystals with high Curie temperatures and large d coefficients), sol-gel composite transducers for high-temperature NDE, SAW devices, airborne ultrasound, power ultrasonics, and biomedical nonlinear acoustics (tissue harmonic imaging). Unique coverage of boundary-element methods for near-field analysis and Laser Doppler Vibrometry for transducer characterisation.

## Key concepts
- Piezoelectric effect; d₃₃, d₃₁ coefficients; coupling factors
- PZT ceramic composition and properties; Uchino review
- Thin-film PZT (hydrothermal, RF sputtering); bending (d₃₁) and thickness (d₃₃) modes
- Pb(In₁/₂Nb₁/₂)O₃–PMN–PT ternary single crystals (PIMNT): high Curie temperature, large d₃₃
- Electromagnetic acoustic transducers (EMATs): Lorentz-force and magnetostriction types
- 1D transducer models: wave equation approach, equivalent electrical circuit (Mason/KLM), linear systems model
- Boundary-element method (BEM) for acoustic near-field
- Equivalent circuit characterisation; electrical impedance measurement
- Laser Doppler Vibrometry (LDV) for vibration mapping
- Optical field visualisation: schlieren, Fresnel, photoelastic methods
- Surface acoustic wave (SAW) devices: IDTs, SAW filters, SAW resonators
- Airborne ultrasound: design principles, radiated field patterns, applications
- High-temperature transducers: sol-gel PZT composite films, structural health monitoring
- Neural network flaw detection: frequency-diverse systems, order statistics
- Power ultrasonics: high-intensity processing of fluids and multiphase media
- Nonlinear acoustics: B/A parameter, tissue harmonic imaging, calibration

## Methods / results / takeaways
The 1D Mason model maps a piezoelectric resonator to a three-port network (two acoustic ports, one electrical); KLM model improves accuracy for broadband modelling. PIMNT single crystals achieve d₃₃ > 500 pC/N with Curie temperatures > 200°C, outperforming PMN-PT for high-temperature applications. Sol-gel PZT films on metal substrates allow in-situ NDE at 500–1000°C. EMATs require no coupling fluid, enabling inspection of hot surfaces and moving parts. SAW filters and resonators use lithographic IDTs to exploit surface-confined wave propagation. Tissue harmonic imaging exploits the nonlinear B/A parameter of tissue (~6–7) to generate harmonics of the fundamental that have better beam profiles and less clutter.
