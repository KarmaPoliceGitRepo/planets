# Viscosity and Shear Elasticity Measurements of Liquids by Means of Shear Vibrating Crystals

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Describes the use of torsionally and thickness-shear vibrating piezoelectric crystals (ADP and quartz) as rheometers. When a crystal resonates in contact with a liquid, the viscous loading shifts the resonant frequency and resistance; from these shifts, both viscosity and shear elasticity (storage modulus) of the liquid can be extracted. Results are reported for polyisobutylene, arochlor, and silicone putty.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration that high-frequency crystal oscillators can simultaneously measure viscosity AND shear modulus of viscoelastic liquids. Shows that polymer liquids (polyisobutylene) exhibit an anomalously low shear stiffness (~10⁶–10⁷ dyne/cm²) compared with simple viscous liquids or crystals, attributed to combined chain rotation-translation relaxation. Proposes extending the technique to MHz frequencies using AT/BT thickness-shear crystals.

## Key concepts
- Torsional crystal (ADP, quartz) as viscometer
- Shear viscous wave; attenuation in liquids
- Mechanical loading (resistance and reactance) on crystal resonator
- Shear elasticity / viscoelastic compliance
- Relaxation frequency f_c = μ/(2πη)
- Equivalent transmission-line circuit for viscous medium
- Polyisobutylene, arochlor, silicone putty rheology
- Frequency-dependent viscosity and shear modulus
- Eyring activation-energy model for polymer flow

## Methods / results / takeaways
Crystal constants K₁, K₂ relate electrical resistance/frequency shifts to mechanical loading. For a purely viscous fluid, the resistance and reactance loadings are equal and each proportional to sqrt(ηρ). Adding shear elasticity (compliance C_s) modifies the loading: at frequencies above the relaxation frequency f_c = 1/(2πηC_s), the medium behaves more elastically. Fitting data for polyisobutylene "B" at multiple temperatures gives: viscosity 30–9700 poise (0–50°C), shear stiffness 5.6–42 × 10⁶ dyne/cm², relaxation frequencies 5.6–42 MHz. Arochlor shows two relaxation times with stiffness ~6 × 10⁹ dyne/cm² (near crystalline). The ADP torsional crystal agrees with falling-ball viscometry to ≈5%.
