## Methods / results / takeaways
The pulse-echo method transmits a short burst from a piezoelectric transducer and records the time of flight and amplitude of echoes to locate and size reflectors. Angle-beam techniques use the shear wave refracted by mode conversion at a wedge to access flaws not accessible to normal-incidence beams (e.g., weld toe cracks). The near-field length N = D²/4λ separates the interference-dominated near field from the well-behaved far field; probe frequency and element diameter are chosen to position the near-field boundary correctly. TOFD uses two angled probes (transmitter and receiver) to detect flaw tips by their diffracted signals, giving height and depth measurement independent of orientation.
