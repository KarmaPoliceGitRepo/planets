## Overview
A comprehensive reference on scanning acoustic microscopy (SAM), covering both the physical principles of high-frequency ultrasound and the wide range of industrial and biological applications. Maev covers V(z) contrast, surface and subsurface imaging, and quantitative material characterisation at microstructural scales.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive treatment of the V(z) curve technique for quantitative measurement of surface acoustic wave velocity and elastic constants from acoustic microscope data. Bridges theory of focused ultrasonic beams with practical applications in microelectronics inspection (flip-chip, BGA, delamination), biological tissue characterisation, and materials science.

## Methods / results / takeaways
The V(z) curve is the acoustic signal amplitude as a function of defocus z. Its periodic oscillation arises from interference between the specularly reflected ray and the leaky Rayleigh wave ray; the period Δz gives the Rayleigh wave velocity as V_R = V_w / sqrt(1 − (V_w/(2Δz·f))²). Typical resolution at 1 GHz is ~1 μm, allowing imaging of grain structure in metals and subsurface features in ICs. Quantitative elastic constants (Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio) of thin films and coatings can be extracted by fitting V(z) data to model curves. Applications include detection of delamination in flip-chip packages (<0.1 mm defects) and measurement of cell mechanical properties in biology.
