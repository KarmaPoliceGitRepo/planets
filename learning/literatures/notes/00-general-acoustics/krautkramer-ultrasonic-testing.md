## Overview
The definitive reference on ultrasonic non-destructive testing (NDT) of materials, covering the full chain from physical wave propagation through instrumentation to industrial applications and standards. Part A treats wave physics (free waves, boundary behaviour, sound fields, attenuation, piezoelectric generation); Part B covers pulse-echo instruments and techniques; Part C covers defect sizing; Part D covers applications in specific industries (nuclear, welds, forgings, composites, concrete).

## Unique contribution
Encyclopaedic coverage of practical UT: DGS diagrams for sizing reflectors, phased-array and imaging methods as they existed in 1990, and extensive bibliographic coverage (~1900 references). The collaborative authorship includes Krautkrämer company engineers and academic experts, giving it both theoretical rigour and practical grounding.

## Methods / results / takeaways
Establishes that the on-axis sound pressure in the near field oscillates due to Fresnel interference, and becomes a monotonically decaying spherical pattern in the far field beyond distance N = D²/4λ (natural focus). Mode conversion angles at interfaces are governed by Snell's law; critical angles produce longitudinal or shear wave total reflection. Attenuation in polycrystalline metals is dominated by grain-boundary scattering proportional to f⁴ (Rayleigh regime) or f² (stochastic regime). Practical calibration uses the IIW block and DGS charts linking echo height to equivalent reflector size. Phased arrays allow steering and focusing but were not yet in wide industrial use at publication.
