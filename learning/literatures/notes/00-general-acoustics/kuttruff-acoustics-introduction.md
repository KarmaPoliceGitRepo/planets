## Overview
A broad undergraduate-level introduction to acoustics covering the physics of sound in fluids and solids, wave equations, energy transport, radiation, reflection/refraction, absorption, room acoustics, and musical/physiological applications. Originally published in German by S. Hirzel Verlag; the English edition is aimed at students and engineers needing a solid grounding in classical acoustics theory.

## Unique contribution
Unusually broad scope: bridges fundamental wave physics and applied engineering acoustics (room acoustics, musical instruments, hearing) in one volume. Includes non-linear effects, electromechanical analogies, and Fourier/transfer-function methods alongside traditional wave theory.

## Methods / results / takeaways
The book derives the wave equation from first principles (conservation of mass and momentum in a fluid), then systematically covers its solutions. Key results include: the sound velocity in an ideal gas as c = sqrt(γp₀/ρ₀); the inverse-square pressure decay from a point source; the Rayleigh integral for piston radiation; the Sabine and Eyring reverberation formulae. Non-linear effects (steepening, shock formation) are introduced qualitatively. The Fourier analysis and transfer-function framework is presented for signal-processing applications of acoustics.
