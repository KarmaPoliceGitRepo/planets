## Overview
An engineer-focused textbook on biomedical ultrasound, building from first principles of wave physics to clinical and therapeutic applications. The chapter sequence takes the reader from fundamental wave concepts through acoustic propagation in fluids and solids, attenuation, reflection/transmission, lenses and mirrors, transducer physics, B-mode imaging, Doppler imaging, and finally safety and HIFU. Appendix A (acoustic properties of tissues) is a useful quick reference.

## Key concepts
- Wave fundamentals: displacement, pressure, intensity, impedance
- 1D wave equation; standing waves; resonance
- Acoustic propagation in fluids: bulk modulus, speed of sound
- Acoustic propagation in solids: longitudinal, shear, surface waves
- Attenuation mechanisms: absorption (viscous, thermal, relaxation), scattering; α ∝ f^n
- Dispersion: Kramers-Kronig relations between attenuation and phase velocity
- Reflection/transmission at interfaces; angle-dependent coefficients; critical angles
- Acoustic lenses: focusing, f-number, depth of field
- Piezoelectric transducers; beam patterns; near/far field; focusing
- Pulse-echo imaging: A-scan, B-scan, resolution
- Coded excitations, harmonic imaging, contrast agents
- Doppler imaging: CW, pulsed, colour flow
- Bioeffects of ultrasound; thermal and mechanical indices; HIFU
