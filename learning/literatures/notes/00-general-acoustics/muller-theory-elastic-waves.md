## Overview
German lecture notes on elastic wave theory, translated into English posthumously. Covers body waves and surface waves in layered elastic media with emphasis on seismological applications. The text balances theory with physical insight, using simplified models (SH waves, ideal waveguides) before tackling full 3-D P-SV problems.

## Unique contribution
Unusual pedagogical approach: uses SH (horizontally polarised shear) waves and liquid media as simpler surrogates before tackling full elastic problems, keeping algebra tractable while retaining physical content. Provides comprehensive normal-mode theory for layered waveguides. Widely used as a teaching reference at German universities.

## Key concepts
- P-waves (compressional) and S-waves (shear)
- SH and SV wave polarisation
- Helmholtz decomposition of displacement field
- Elastic moduli (Lamé constants λ, μ)
- Reflection and transmission in layered solids
- Rayleigh waves, Love waves
- Normal-mode theory for waveguides
- Ray seismics in inhomogeneous media
- Seismogram synthesis
- Dispersion of guided waves

## Methods / results / takeaways
The displacement field is decomposed via Helmholtz into scalar potential φ (P-wave) and vector potential ψ (S-wave), each satisfying a scalar wave equation with velocities α = sqrt((λ+2μ)/ρ) and β = sqrt(μ/ρ). Rayleigh waves propagate at ≈0.92β and have retrograde elliptical particle motion. Love waves require a soft layer over a harder half-space; their dispersion equation is transcendental and yields a fundamental and higher modes. The normal-mode approach expands the seismogram as a sum of modes, each with a frequency-dependent phase velocity; the group velocity governs the arrival of energy for impulsive sources.
