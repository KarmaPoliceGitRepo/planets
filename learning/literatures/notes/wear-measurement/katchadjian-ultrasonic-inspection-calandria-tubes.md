## Overview
This conference paper (AIP Conf. Proc. 1650, 2015) describes the development of an automated ultrasonic technique to measure wall thickness reduction (wear) in the thin-walled Zircaloy-2 calandria tubes (CT, nominal 1.34 mm thick) of the Embalse CANDU nuclear power plant in Argentina. Accelerated creep in aging pressure tubes (PT) causes PT–CT contact and potential wear of the CT against Liquid Injection Shutdown System nozzles. Access is restricted to the interior of the PT and radiation levels are high, requiring a fully automated, non-conventional ultrasonic approach.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates practical measurement of very thin metallic tubes (down to ~0.3 mm remaining thickness) through three fluid-coupled interfaces (water, PT wall, annular gap, CT wall) using an oblique-incidence transmitter-receiver configuration at 25° that mode-converts to shear waves in the CT and avoids multiple-echo overlap problems encountered with normal-incidence techniques. Measurement accuracy within ±0.02 mm even at 0.31 mm thickness.

## Key concepts
- CANDU reactor calandria tube (CT) and pressure tube (PT) geometry
- Ultrasonic thickness measurement through multiple interfaces
- Normal incidence vs. oblique incidence (transmitter-receiver) configuration
- Mode conversion (longitudinal to shear wave) at critical angle
- PT–CT gap variation and amplitude optimization
- Immersion technique with water coupling
- Zircaloy-2 / Zr-2.5%Nb ultrasonic velocity
- B-scan imaging of wall thickness reductions
- Minimum acceptable remaining thickness criterion
- Automated inspection device for remote/high-radiation environments

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Normal-incidence and 9.5° incidence failed due to overlapping echoes from successive interfaces; 25° incidence selected because it generates only shear waves in CT (above the first critical angle of 18°), avoiding PT multiple-echo contamination.
- Ultrasonic velocity: Zircaloy longitudinal 4720 m/s, shear 2362 m/s; water 1480 m/s.
- Optimal PT–CT gap for measurement is 5.5 mm (the crossing point where water–CT interface echo and CT backwall echo have equal amplitude); gap variation between 2–6 mm must be compensated by adjusting transducer–PT distance.
- Validated on CT specimens with machined thickness reductions (0.316–0.823 mm real; 0.31–0.81 mm measured). All deviations ≤ 0.02 mm, including well below the 0.6 mm minimum allowed thickness.
- Future work includes a PT-insertable measuring tool and active gap-compensation mechanism using a normal-incidence transducer to sense gap in real time.
- Design constraint: transducers operate in water-filled chamber; device must navigate inside PT under reactor radiation.
