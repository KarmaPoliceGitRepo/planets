## Overview
This paper from Argentina's CNEA and Nucleoeléctrica describes the development of an automated ultrasonic technique to measure residual wall thickness of calandria tubes (CT) in the CANDU-design Embalse nuclear reactor. The challenge is that the CT (nominal thickness 1.34 mm, material Zircaloy-2) can only be accessed indirectly through the interior of concentric pressure tubes (PT, Zr-2.5Nb), requiring ultrasound to traverse three distinct material interfaces. The work covers transducer configuration selection, device design, characterization, and mock-up validation.

## Unique contribution
This paper addresses an unusual multi-interface measurement problem: the transducer must transmit through water → PT (Zr-2.5Nb) → water gap → CT (Zr-2), all within a high-radiation environment accessible only through the PT interior. A 25° inclination angle transmitter-receiver (T-R) configuration that generates only transverse waves at 42° in the CT was found to cleanly separate the CT backwall echo from PT multiples, enabling wall-thickness measurement down to 0.31 mm — well below the 0.6 mm minimum-acceptable threshold.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Problem context:** End-of-life creep deformation in PT can cause contact with LISS nozzles, wearing CT. Wear below 0.6 mm remaining thickness triggers safety concern; actual wear could reach 0.3 mm.
- **Normal-incidence focalized transducer (22 MHz):** rejected because multiple echoes from PT interfaces overlapped with CT echoes.
- **T-R at 9.5° inclination:** longitudinal waves at 32° in CT also produced overlapping echoes.
- **T-R at 25° inclination:** generates only transverse waves (42° in CT, above first critical angle ~18°), cleanly isolating CT echoes. Transducer separation of 22 mm; PT-water path 8.15 mm.
- **Optimal PT-CT gap = 5.5 mm:** at this gap, water-CT interface echo amplitude equals CT backwall echo amplitude, providing balanced measurement.
- **Device:** two-part fixture — fixed arc conforming to PT inner diameter, adjustable mobile part for transducer-to-PT distance setting.
- **Validation results on mock-up CT:** measurement errors ≤ 0.02 mm across all test points including 0.31 mm residual thickness.
- **Sensitivity to PT-CT gap:** gap variation shifts amplitudes significantly; automatic gap-compensation mechanism designed but not yet implemented.
- **Future work:** full in-reactor tool housing the ultrasonic head, plus active gap-compensation using a separate normal-incidence transducer.
