## Overview
This is the 1987 revision of the foundational IEEE standard defining notation, tensor conventions, material constants, measurement methods, and equivalent circuit representations for piezoelectric materials and devices. It covers both crystalline piezoelectrics (notably quartz) and piezoceramics, providing the analytical framework used in computer-aided design of piezoelectric resonators, transducers, and filters.

## Unique contribution
Consolidates earlier IRE/IEEE standards (176, 177, 178, 179) into a single authoritative reference. Notably standardized the right-handed coordinate system for both left-handed and right-handed quartz (abandoning the legacy left-handed convention), unified sign conventions for piezoelectric constants across material types, and replaced energy-ratio coupling factor definitions with analytically derived resonance-based ones.

## Key concepts
- Piezoelectric tensor notation (d, e, g, h constants)
- Electromechanical coupling factor (k)
- Right-handed coordinate system for crystal orientation
- Quartz enantiomorphism (left-handed vs right-handed)
- Resonance and anti-resonance frequencies
- Equivalent circuit (Butterworth–Van Dyke model)
- Elastic compliance (s) and stiffness (c) constants
- Permittivity under constant stress (free) and constant strain (clamped)
- Frequency constant N
- Mechanical quality factor Qm

## Methods / results / takeaways
The standard provides tensor equations relating mechanical stress/strain and electric field/displacement in matrix form for all crystal symmetry classes. For measurements, it specifies the use of impedance analysis near resonance to extract material constants from characteristic frequencies (series resonance fs, parallel resonance fp) and free capacitance. The coupling factor k is defined from stored energy ratios appropriate to the vibration mode. Sign conventions for quartz d constants were changed relative to older standards, creating a persistent discrepancy with device-industry practice that the standard itself acknowledges. The document is prerequisite knowledge for interpreting EN 50324 series standards.
