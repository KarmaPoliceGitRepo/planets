## Overview
The fifth edition of ISO 492 specifies dimensional and geometrical characteristics, limit deviations from nominal sizes, and tolerance values that define the interface of radial rolling bearings. It supersedes ISO 492:2002 and aligns with the ISO/GPS (Geometrical Product Specifications) system, replacing older symbol sets with GPS-compliant terminology and specification modifiers.

## Unique contribution
Migrated bearing tolerance specifications from traditional dimensional-only symbology to the full GPS framework of ISO 14405-1, introducing specification modifiers (ACS, GN, ALSGN, etc.) and GPS symbols alongside the legacy symbols via Annex A. Covers diameters up to 3000 mm and adds tapered bore specifications at 1:12 and 1:30. Deleted tolerance grade E (present in the 2002 edition).

## Key concepts
- Tolerance classes: Normal, 6, 5, 4, 2 (for general bearings); 6X, 5, 4, 2 (for tapered rollers)
- Bore diameter (d), outside diameter (D), ring widths (B, C)
- Radial runout Kea, Kia; axial runout Sea, Sia
- Mean bore diameter deviation Δdmp; variation Vdmp
- GPS specification operator; two-point size; mid-range size
- Tapered bore (1:12 and 1:30 tapers)
- Assembled bearing width T; effective widths T1, T2
- Perpendicularity Sd, SD

## Methods / results / takeaways
Tolerances tighten from Normal class through Class 6, 5, 4 to Class 2. For a Normal-class bearing with bore 18–30 mm, Δdmp = 0/−10 µm and radial runout Kia = 13 µm; at Class 5, Δdmp = 0/−8 µm with Kia = 8 µm. Tolerance tables cover bore diameters from below 0.6 mm up to 2000 mm. Practical note: chamfer limits are in ISO 582 (not this standard). Annex C provides detailed illustrations reconciling GPS definitions with traditional ISO 1132-1 terms; Annex D explains specification modifiers for linear sizes.
