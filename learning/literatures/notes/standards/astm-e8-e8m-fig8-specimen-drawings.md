## Overview
A short supplementary extract containing Figure 8 from ASTM E8/E8M-13a: dimensional tables for standard and small-size round tension test specimens in both the 4D gauge-length (E8, inch-pound) and 5D gauge-length (E8M, metric) variants. Not a standalone standard; a companion document to the full ASTM-E-8-yr-13.pdf file.

## Unique contribution
Provides a convenient at-a-glance dimensional reference for the five standard and small-size round specimen sizes in both unit systems, with notes on end design, grip length, and gauge length marking conventions. Useful as a machining reference without reading the full standard.

## Key concepts
- Round tension specimens: 5 sizes (Standard down to smallest)
- E8: gauge length = 4 × diameter; E8M: gauge length = 5 × diameter
- Gauge length G; diameter D; radius of fillet R; reduced section length A
- Grip end form flexibility (wedge, pin, threaded)
- Taper allowance: ends not more than 1% larger in diameter than center

## Methods / results / takeaways
Standard E8 specimen: G = 50.0±0.1 mm, D = 12.5±0.2 mm, R ≥ 10 mm, A ≥ 56 mm. Standard E8M specimen: G = 62.5±0.1 mm, D = 12.5±0.2 mm, R ≥ 10 mm, A ≥ 75 mm. Smallest specimens (Specimen 5): E8 G = 10.0±0.1 mm, D = 2.5±0.1 mm; E8M G = 12.5±0.1 mm, D = 2.5±0.1 mm. Use of specimens smaller than 6 mm diameter is restricted and requires agreement of all parties. The five inch-pound diameters (≈0.505, 0.357, 0.252, 0.160, 0.113 in.) give areas near 0.2, 0.1, 0.05, 0.02, and 0.01 in.², enabling mental stress calculation; metric equivalents do not share this convenience.
