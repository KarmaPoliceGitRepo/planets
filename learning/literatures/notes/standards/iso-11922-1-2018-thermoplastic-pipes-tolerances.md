## Overview
Second edition of ISO 11922-1, specifying tolerance grades for outside diameter, out-of-roundness, and wall thickness of metric thermoplastics pipes used for fluid conveyance. Applies to smooth circular-cross-section thermoplastic pipes manufactured by any method, in any material. The philosophy is to consolidate tolerances previously scattered across material-specific standards into a single framework, from which product standards select the appropriate grade.

## Unique contribution
Unifies previously fragmented tolerance standards for individual thermoplastic materials (PVC, PE, PP, etc.) into a single two-part series covering all extruded thermoplastics pipes. Extends coverage to diameters up to 3000 mm (previously limited in the 1997 edition) and deletes tolerance grade E, reducing the number of grades to simplify selection.

## Key concepts
- Mean outside diameter dem (circumference/π); minimum and maximum values
- Out-of-roundness (max minus min diameter in one cross-section)
- Wall thickness at any point e; mean wall thickness em
- Tolerance grades (A, B, C, D for different parameters)
- Nominal outside diameter dn (= minimum mean outside diameter)
- ISO 161-1 nominal pressure series; ISO 4065 wall thickness table

## Methods / results / takeaways
Tolerances on mean outside diameter are expressed as upper deviations above the nominal. Tighter grades have smaller upper deviations. Out-of-roundness tolerances are stricter for larger diameters. Wall thickness tolerances are given at any point (tighter) and for the mean (slightly relaxed). Measurements follow ISO 3126: diameter is determined from the measured circumference divided by π (rounded up to 0.1 mm). Responsibility for selecting the appropriate grade lies with the bodies preparing the product/system standards for each application and material combination.
