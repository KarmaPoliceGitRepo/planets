## Overview
Part 2 of EN 50324, specifying the small-signal (low-power) measurement methods for all piezoelectric and elastic material constants of piezoceramics. It defines a systematic step-by-step procedure to extract a complete set of elastic, dielectric, and piezoelectric coefficients from resonance frequency measurements on standard test specimens.

## Unique contribution
Provides the complete mathematical framework (equations 1–74) linking measured quantities — resonance/anti-resonance frequency pairs, free capacitance, density, dimensions — to the full elasto-piezo-dielectric matrix. Includes explicit formulae for coupling factors in each vibration mode, temperature coefficients, and ageing rates. Supersedes the need to use IEC 60483 for most practical measurements.

## Key concepts
- Dynamic method of measurement (impedance analysis at resonance)
- Quasistatic method (d33 measurement below resonance)
- Effective coupling factor keff (from frequency pair fs, fp)
- Mechanical quality factor Qm (3 dB bandwidth method)
- Piezoelectric moduli d31, d33, d15, g31, g33, g15
- Elastic compliances sij^E and sij^D
- Free and clamped permittivity ε33^T and ε33^S
- Temperature coefficient TC
- Ageing rate cy (logarithmic time dependence)
- Vector impedance diagram; motional resistance R; motional capacitance C1

## Methods / results / takeaways
Specimens must be measured at 20–25°C, at least 24 h after poling, below 65% RH. A minimum of 10 specimens is required for statistical reliability. The coupling factor keff is extracted from the formula keff² = (fp² − fs²)/fp². Individual mode coupling factors (k31, k33, kt, kp, k15) are then obtained via graphical correction curves (Figures 4–7). Ageing rates should be determined from measurements at 3, 10, 30, and 100 days after poling. Temperature coefficient calculations use the error propagation rule: TCd ≈ ½ TCε to first approximation.
