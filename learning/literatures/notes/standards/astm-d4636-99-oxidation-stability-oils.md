## Overview
ASTM D4636 defines a standardised accelerated oxidation and corrosion test for highly refined petroleum and synthetic oils — primarily hydraulic fluids and aircraft turbine engine lubricants. The test measures oil degradation (viscosity change, acid number increase, sludge formation) and metal corrosion (mass change, surface attack) caused by passing heated air through the oil in the presence of multiple metal specimens.

## Unique contribution
Consolidates two earlier US Federal Test Methods (5307.2 and 5308.7) into a single standard with three procedure variants. Its combination of periodic oil sampling throughout the test and multiple metal species (titanium, magnesium, two steels, bronze, silver, aluminum) in a single run distinguishes it from simpler single-metal oxidation tests. The three procedures (Standard, Alt 1, Alt 2) offer flexibility in oil volume, sampling scheme, and specimen shape (washers vs. squares).

## Key concepts
- Oxidation stability; acid number (TAN); viscosity change
- Corrosion of metals: mass change mg/cm²
- Test temperatures 100–360°C; air flow 5–10 L/h
- Sludge volume (centrifugation per D91)
- Metal specimens: Ti (R56080), Mg AZ31B, M50 steel, 1010 steel, bronze C65900, Ag 99.95, Al 2024
- Standard procedure (200 mL oil, 10 L/h, periodic sampling at 16, 24, 40, 48, 64, 72, 88, 96 h)
- Alternative Procedure 1 (165 mL, no periodic sampling)
- Alternative Procedure 2 (100 mL, 5 L/h, square specimens, no periodic sampling)
- Oil mass loss calculation; acid number change; viscosity change

## Methods / results / takeaways
Metal washer specimens (6.35 mm ID × 19.05 mm OD × 0.81 mm) are stacked on the air tube in a prescribed order (Al, Ag, bronze, low-carbon steel, M50, Mg, Ti). Specimens must be polished to 400-grit finish, degreased, and weighed to 0.1 mg before testing. The air supply must be dry (−68°C dew point) or controlled-humidity (10±1 mg/L). Periodic 10 mL samples are drawn for acid number (D664) and viscosity (D445) at 40 and 100°C. Precision (at 175°C, 96 h, Standard vs. Alt 1): repeatability for viscosity change ≈ 1.6–2.0%, reproducibility ≈ 2.7–3.2%; for acid number change, reproducibility ≈ 0.62–1.53 mg KOH/g depending on range.
