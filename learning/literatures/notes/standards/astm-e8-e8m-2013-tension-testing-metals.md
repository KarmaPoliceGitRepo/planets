## Overview
The primary ASTM standard for tension testing of metallic materials at room temperature (10–38°C). It covers determination of yield strength, tensile strength, elongation, and reduction of area for metals in any product form. E8 uses 4D gauge length (inch-pound), E8M uses 5D gauge length (metric). The standard has been in continuous use since 1924 and is referenced in virtually all metallic material product specifications.

## Unique contribution
Specifies two parallel unit systems (E8 inch-pound with 4D gauge length; E8M metric with 5D gauge length) that are not numerically equivalent and must be used independently. Provides standard and small-size proportional round specimen dimensions for both systems (5 specimen sizes per series), plus flat sheet/strip specimen provisions. Defines specific wedge-grip and pin-grip end configurations for different product forms.

## Key concepts
- Yield strength (offset method, 0.2% offset; also upper/lower yield point)
- Tensile strength (ultimate tensile strength)
- Elongation after fracture; elongation at fracture
- Reduction of area
- Gauge length (4D for E8, 5D for E8M)
- Discontinuous yielding; Lüders band
- Specimen size: Standard 12.5 mm [0.500 in.] round; small-size proportional specimens
- Extensometer classification (Practice E83)
- Strain rate control; crosshead speed

## Methods / results / takeaways
Standard round specimens have gauge length of 50.0±0.1 mm (E8) or 62.5±0.1 mm (E8M) with 12.5±0.2 mm diameter. Reduced section must be coaxial within specified tolerances; surface finish 63 µin (1.6 µm) or better. Testing machines must conform to E4. Strain rate in the elastic range should be 0.005 in./in./min. The 0.2% offset yield strength is determined graphically or by autographic recording. Elongation is measured after fracturing and fitting the pieces together (elongation after fracture), distinguished from elongation at fracture. Reduction of area is calculated from minimum cross-section dimensions. Five convenient diameter sizes (0.505, 0.357, 0.252, 0.160, 0.113 in.) give cross-sectional areas that yield simple stress calculation multipliers.
