## Overview
Comprehensive review of air-coupled ultrasonic techniques for materials characterisation, covering transducer technologies, matching layer approaches, measurement configurations, and application examples in NDE of composites, plates, and layered structures.

## Unique contribution
Provides a broad survey of the state of the art in air-coupled NDE as of 2014, synthesising decades of development in transducer materials (PVDF, 1-3 composites, CMUTs), matching layers (aerogel, porous polymers), and measurement methods (through-transmission, leaky Lamb waves, C-scan imaging).

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Reviews three main transducer technologies: PVDF (low impedance, broadband), 1-3 composites (tunable impedance, high coupling), CMUTs (broadband, microfabricated)
- Matching layer state of the art: silica aerogel best single-layer material; multi-layer designs improve bandwidth
- Through-transmission C-scan: standard for composite delamination detection; requires accurate alignment
- Leaky Lamb wave inspection: allows determination of elastic constants from air-coupled measurements; sensitive to in-plane stiffness
- Air absorption limits practical frequency to <2 MHz for propagation distances >100 mm; water-mist coupling extends range
- Applications reviewed: aerospace composites (CFRP, GFRP), wood, paper, ceramics, food inspection
- CMUT advantages: batch fabrication, flat frequency response, good air coupling at low frequencies
- 1-3 composites with engineered pillar geometry achieve ZA ~2–5 MRayl, significantly reducing PZT-to-air mismatch
