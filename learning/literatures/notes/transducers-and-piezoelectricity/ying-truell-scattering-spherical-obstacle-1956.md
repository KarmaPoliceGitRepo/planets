# Scattering of a Plane Longitudinal Wave by a Spherical Obstacle in an Isotropically Elastic Solid

- **Source:** Ying Truell scattering spherical obstacle 1956
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Hu6khyf54sdpmPEFvA7MVtbNMK-uvqk7/view
- **Type:** paper (Journal of Applied Physics 27(9), 1956)
- **Author/Year:** C. F. Ying, Rohn Truell / 1956
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Derives analytical expressions for the scattering cross section of a plane longitudinal ultrasonic wave by a spherical obstacle in an isotropically elastic solid, for three cases: an elastic sphere, a spherical cavity, and a rigid sphere. Applies spherical harmonic expansion with boundary conditions (displacement and stress continuity) and derives Rayleigh scattering limits (obstacle much smaller than wavelength). Compares results with the known fluid-sphere-in-fluid case. Motivated by need to calculate ultrasonic attenuation from scattering in polycrystalline metals and alloys with precipitates.

## Unique contribution
First derivation of scattering cross section for obstacles in elastic (solid) media, extending the well-known fluid-sphere-in-fluid result to include shear wave coupling. Identifies that the solid case has an extra term in the scattering coefficient ge (vs fluid gf) due to rigidity of the matrix — the shear wave contribution absent in fluids. Shows that for Rayleigh scattering: γ ∝ k₁⁴a⁶ (cross section scales as sixth power of radius, inverse fourth power of wavelength) for all three obstacle types. Rigid sphere in solid has anomalous dependence (cross section proportional to a² at high ka, independent of wavelength) unlike elastic sphere or cavity.

## Key concepts
- Scattering cross section γ for plane longitudinal wave in elastic solid
- Three obstacle types: elastic sphere, spherical cavity, rigid sphere
- Spherical harmonic expansion: coefficients Aₘ, Bₘ from boundary conditions (displacement + stress continuity)
- Rayleigh scattering (la ≪ 1): γ = (4π/9)gₑk₁⁴a⁶ for elastic sphere; γ = (4π/9)gₒk₁⁴a⁶ for cavity
- ge includes extra term from rigidity vs fluid case gf; ge can be >> gf for proper material choice
- Rayleigh scattering in Kₗ/k₁ space: scattering cross section increases with velocity ratio
- kₗ/K₁ ratio (longitudinal to transverse velocity) for common metals: ~1.7–3.1

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Boundary conditions: stress tensor components σᵣᵣ and σθᵣ continuous at sphere surface
- Scattering cross sections: γ(elastic sphere) = (4π/9)gₑk₁⁴a⁶; γ(cavity) = (4π/9)gₒk₁⁴a⁶
- For aluminum (Kₗ/kₗ = 2.0): γ(rigid sphere)/γ(cavity) depends on particle size and frequency
- Rigid sphere in solid: cross section ∝ a² for ka ≫ 1 (unlike elastic sphere/cavity which follow Rayleigh law)
- Attenuation coefficient a related to scattering cross section: I = I₀ exp(−2αx)
- Table I: K₁/k₁ ratios for common metals (Ag: 2.3, Al: 2.0, Cu: 2.1, Fe: 1.8, Pb: 3.1, W: 2.1, Rubber: 39)
- Application: ultrasonic attenuation in polycrystalline metals and alloys with precipitates at MHz frequencies
