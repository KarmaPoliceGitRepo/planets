# Compressive and Ultrasonic Properties of Polyester/Fly Ash Composites

- **Source:** Rohatgi polyester fly ash composites 2009
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Uy2OBfqMPFKblf09ogmynF-3ZXDrxYDb/view
- **Type:** paper (Journal of Materials Science 44, 2009)
- **Author/Year:** P. K. Rohatgi, T. Matsunaga, N. Gupta / 2009
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Fabricates and characterises polyester/fly ash cenosphere syntactic foam composites (0–29.5 vol% cenospheres, 45–75 μm) using a gravity-settling functionally graded synthesis method. Measures compressive modulus/strength and ultrasonic longitudinal/shear velocities and attenuation as a function of cenosphere volume fraction. Shows that elastic modulus increases with cenosphere loading (1.33 → 2.1 GPa at 0–29.5 vol%), yield strength decreases, ultrasonic velocity increases, and attenuation decreases.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that the relative normalized modulus from ultrasonic and quasi-static compression methods agree within 10%, despite absolute ultrasonic modulus being significantly higher than static values (viscoelastic effect). First systematic characterisation of polyester/fly ash cenosphere composites by both methods. Shows specific modulus E/ρ improves by up to 110% while strength decreases only 22%.

## Key concepts
- Syntactic foam: hollow particle-filled polymer composite; density reduction while maintaining/improving stiffness
- Cenospheres: hollow fly ash particles; density ≈ 600 kg/m³; cost-effective waste by-product of coal combustion
- Ultrasonic modulus vs static modulus: ultrasonic gives dynamic modulus (higher than quasi-static due to viscoelasticity)
- Attenuation decreases with increasing cenosphere volume fraction (cenosphere attenuation < polyester attenuation)
- Specific modulus E/ρ: key metric for lightweight structural applications

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Physical Acoustics Ultrapac immersion (longitudinal) + Technisonic contact shear transducers; 5 MHz; 500 MS/s
- Neat polyester: VL = 2321 m/s, VS = 1128 m/s; 29.5 vol% composite: VL = 2753 m/s, VS = 1356 m/s
- Compressive modulus: 1.41 GPa (neat) → 2.10 GPa (29.5 vol%); yield strength: 87.8 → 71.7 MPa
- Ultrasonic Young's modulus: 4.14 GPa (neat) → 5.06 GPa (29.5 vol%) — much higher than static values
- Attenuation decreases from ~1200 to ~300 dB as cenosphere content increases 0→29.5 vol%
- Relative modulus (composite/matrix) matches within 10% between ultrasonic and compression methods
- Application context: backing composite design, lightweight transducer-integrated structural components
