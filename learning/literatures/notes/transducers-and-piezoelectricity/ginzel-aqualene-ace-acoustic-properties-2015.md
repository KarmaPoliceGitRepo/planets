# Acoustic Properties of the Elastomeric Materials Aqualene and ACE

- **Source:** Ginzel Aqualene ACE acoustic properties 2015
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1N_02x6Rs7OpbIFOu4vb4iq7K5sXjNN8i/view
- **Type:** paper (e-Journal of Nondestructive Testing, Vol.20 No.12, Dec 2015)
- **Author/Year:** Ed Ginzel, Rick MacNeil, Robert Ginzel, Mario Zuber, A.N. Sinclair / 2015
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Characterises acoustic velocity (2–50 MHz) and attenuation (2–20 MHz) of multiple formulations of two elastomeric polymer materials — Aqualene (thermoset divinyl olefin) and ACE (Acoustic Coupling Elastomer, thermoplastic) — developed by Innovation Polymers for industrial ultrasonic applications. Both materials have acoustic velocities near water (1525–1625 m/s) and acoustic impedances near water (≈1.5 MRayl). Provides comparative tables against common NDT polymers (PMMA, PEEK, HDPE, polyurethane, LDPE, polystyrene). Temperature coefficient for Aqualene: dV/dT ≈ −3 m/s/°C.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first systematic multi-formulation acoustic database for Aqualene and ACE elastomers, covering 9 Aqualene and 10 ACE variants. Identifies three distinct attenuation groups: Aqualene (lowest), high-attenuation ACE, and standard ACE. Demonstrates that velocity and attenuation are closely coupled to mechanical properties (Shore A hardness, density, elongation) — optimising one property inevitably affects others. Extends prior single-formulation characterisations.

## Key concepts
- Aqualene (thermoset, EPR series): c ≈ 1548–1580 m/s, Z ≈ 1.45–1.49 MRayl, attenuation 0.06–0.49 dB/mm @ 5 MHz; Shore A 30–54
- ACE (thermoplastic, EPM series): c ≈ 1528–1624 m/s, Z ≈ 1.41–1.53 MRayl, attenuation 0.99–4.7 dB/mm @ 5 MHz; Shore A 30–83
- Both materials: near-water acoustic impedance; lower attenuation than most elastomers used in NDT
- Temperature coefficient: Aqualene dV/dT ≈ −3 m/s/°C (5–35°C range)
- Comparison polymers: only Rexolite has lower attenuation than Aqualene M320; water attenuation is >1 order of magnitude lower than all polymers
- Applications: dry couplant wedge/delay line material, refracting wedge for low-velocity plastics NDE, medical phantom tissue-mimicking

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Phase velocity from phase-slope technique; attenuation from through-transmission amplitude comparison (diffraction correction omitted as negligible)
- Matched transducer pairs at 5–50 MHz; immersion in water at 23°C
- Aqualene EPR-M320 (35 Shore A): lowest attenuation in class; c = 1565–1568 m/s (2–20 MHz)
- ACE EPM-M400 (35 Shore A): reference ACE formulation; c = 1529–1552 m/s; α = 0.99–8.4 dB/mm (2–20 MHz)
- High-attenuation ACE variants: α > 10 dB/mm @ 10 MHz — suitable for absorptive backing or isolation layers
- Mechanical-acoustic coupling: forming process (injection vs compression moulding) also affects properties
