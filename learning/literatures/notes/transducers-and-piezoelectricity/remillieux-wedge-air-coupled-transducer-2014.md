# Improving Air Coupling of Bulk Piezoelectric Transducers with Wedges of Power-Law Profiles

- **Source:** 2014 - Improving the air coupling of bulk piezoelectric transducers with wedges of power-law profiles - (2014) MC Remillieux.pdf

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
FE numerical study showing that attaching a PZT element to a steel plate with a power-law profile wedge significantly enhances air coupling efficiency. The acoustic black hole effect traps flexural wave energy near the wedge tip, creating a compact source that radiates efficiently into air.

## Unique contribution
Applies the acoustic black hole concept (wedges of power-law profile) to air-coupled ultrasound transducer design for the first time. FE simulations show ~8.5× near-field and ~5.5× far-field pressure increase over isolated PZT, and ~9× gain at the time-reversal focus — superior to a thin plate of uniform thickness.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Wedge of cubic profile (m=3) attached to bulk PZT-5A element modelled in 2D COMSOL with full fluid-structure interaction
- For m ≥ 2 in vacuum (or m ≥ 5/3 in fluid), wave phase integral diverges → energy concentrates near wedge tip without reflection
- Wedge extends operating bandwidth: 6 resonances within 8 dB over 40 kHz range vs. single resonance for thin plate
- Near-field pressure gain: ~8.5× over isolated PZT; far-field gain: ~5.5×
- In TRM simulation: wedge gives 9× improvement in focal amplitude; side lobes reduced substantially
- At 43 kHz resonance (continuous wave, 30 V peak-peak): max pressure 220 Pa at 12 mm from wedge tip
- Simulation operated at 50 kHz (10–100 kHz range); device scalable to higher frequencies
- PVDF MUT comparison: wedge at 43 kHz delivers 33 dB more SPL than equivalent-frequency MUT
- Concept proposed for phased arrays and parametric arrays; 3D validation still needed experimentally
