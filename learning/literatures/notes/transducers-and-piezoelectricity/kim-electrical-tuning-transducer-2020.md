## Overview
Uses the KLM equivalent circuit model to simulate and optimise electrical tuning networks (L-tuning and LC-tuning) for an ultrasonic transducer. Compares the electrical impedance, pulse-echo waveforms, and frequency spectra of untuned, L-tuned, and LC-tuned transducers both in simulation and experiment. Validates KLM model predictions against fabricated transducer measurements at approximately 10–12 MHz.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that LC-tuning of a transducer's electrical port significantly improves -6 dB bandwidth and sensitivity compared to L-tuning or untuned operation, and shows the KLM simulation can reliably predict the effect of different tuning topologies before fabrication.

## Key concepts
- KLM equivalent circuit model
- Electrical tuning network (L-tuning, LC-tuning)
- Impedance matching via series inductance / resonant circuits
- Pulse-echo transducer characterisation
- -6 dB bandwidth measurement
- Piezoelectric transducer frequency response

## Methods / results / takeaways
- KLM model implemented with separate electrical tuning circuit block (R1, C1, L1, C0 components)
- Electrical tuning types compared: untuned, L-tuned, LC-tuned — each gives different impedance characteristic and pulse echo shape
- Experimental validation: KLM simulation closely matches fabricated transducer in both pulse echo amplitude and frequency spectrum at ~10–12 MHz
