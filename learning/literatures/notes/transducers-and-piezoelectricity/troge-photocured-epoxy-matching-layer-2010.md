## Overview
Characterises photocured (UV-cured) epoxy resin as a matching layer material for piezoelectric ultrasonic transducers. Measures acoustic velocity, impedance, and attenuation of the cured resin and evaluates suitability as a single or first matching layer.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates UV-cured epoxy as a rapid, low-temperature process for forming matching layers, with controllable thickness via photomasking. Provides acoustic property data for photocured resin formulations used in prototype transducer fabrication.

## Key concepts
- Photocured (UV-cured) epoxy
- Matching layer material
- Acoustic impedance of epoxy
- Attenuation in epoxy
- Single-step matching layer fabrication
- Transducer matching
- Thickness control via photomask

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Photocured epoxy offers rapid curing (seconds to minutes under UV) compared to thermally cured systems
- Acoustic impedance of cured resin in the range ~3–4 MRayl depending on formulation and cure conditions
- Attenuation moderate (~1–2 dB/cm/MHz) — higher than glassy epoxies but acceptable for single matching layers
- Thickness control achievable via photomasking and cure depth; uniform layers possible
- Useful for transducer prototyping where rapid iteration is needed
- Performance compared to standard Araldite/Vantico epoxy systems; photocured formulation gives slightly lower Z and higher attenuation
