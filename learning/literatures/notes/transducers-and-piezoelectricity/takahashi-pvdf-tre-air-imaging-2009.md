# Air-Coupled Reflection Acoustic Microscopy at 2 MHz Using P(VDF/TrFE)

- **Source:** Takahashi 2009 - air imaging at 2 MHz.pdf

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops a reflection-mode air-coupled acoustic microscope operating at 2 MHz using a P(VDF/TrFE) concave transducer. Achieves 150 µm lateral resolution with ~80 dB two-way insertion loss. Demonstrates imaging of surface and near-surface features on solid specimens without liquid couplant.

## Unique contribution
Extends earlier 2.93 MHz work to 2 MHz, trading resolution for reduced air attenuation, enabling imaging of larger areas and more attenuating specimens. Provides systematic characterisation of the instrument sensitivity and resolution as a function of focusing geometry.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Concave P(VDF/TrFE) transducer at 2 MHz; focal length determined by transducer curvature radius
- Two-way insertion loss: ~80 dB (includes air path loss, conversion loss at transducer face, and receiver noise)
- Lateral resolution: 150 µm (-6 dB) at 2 MHz with concave focusing
- Air absorption: empirically ~1.6 × 10⁻¹² × f² dB/cm at 25°C (Takahashi data consistent with this formula)
- Reflection images of metal and ceramic surfaces demonstrate sensitivity to surface topology and sub-surface delaminations
- Comparison to 2.93 MHz system: lower resolution but better penetration through air gaps and more attenuating targets
- P(VDF/TrFE) selected for kt ~0.27 and film-casting reproducibility; allows custom concave shapes
- System noise floor limited by pre-amplifier; 40 dB gain pre-amp with low noise figure required
