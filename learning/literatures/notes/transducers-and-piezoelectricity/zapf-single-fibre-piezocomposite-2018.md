## Overview
Describes the development of a new transducer array design for the third-generation KIT 3D Ultrasound Computer Tomography (USCT) system for breast imaging, based on single-fibre PZT piezocomposite transducer discs (PTDs). Covers the design rationale, manufacturing process, and characterisation of the piezocomposite transducers in terms of centre frequency (~2.2 MHz), bandwidth (~2.0 MHz at -6 dB), and opening angle (~50° at -6 dB).

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that embedding individual PZT fibres (spun from Sonox P505 powder via polysulfone process) in epoxy to form single-fibre piezocomposite discs allows control of the transducer's opening angle, frequency, and bandwidth independently of the array geometry. This enables wider opening angles (~50°) compared to the previous octagonal dice-and-fill design (~24°), which is critical for tomographic reconstruction quality.

## Key concepts
- Single-fibre piezocomposite transducer disc (PTD)
- 3D USCT (ultrasound computer tomography) for breast imaging
- PZT fibre fabrication (polysulfone spinning, Sonox P505)
- Transducer opening angle (directivity) vs frequency
- Piezocomposite: PZT fibre + epoxy matrix
- Centre frequency, -6 dB bandwidth, -6 dB opening angle
- KIT USCT system (Karlsruhe Institute of Technology)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- New PTD design: 18 PZT fibres (diameter 0.45 mm) embedded in EpoTek 301-2 epoxy, disc ~20 mm diameter, thickness ~0.75 mm (first gen) → 0.6 mm (second gen)
- Performance: centre frequency ~2.2 MHz, -6 dB bandwidth ~2.0 MHz, opening angle ~50° (smooth, far-field) — significant improvement over previous USCT 2.0 design (~24° opening angle)
- KLM model used for transducer characterisation and manufacturing quality monitoring
