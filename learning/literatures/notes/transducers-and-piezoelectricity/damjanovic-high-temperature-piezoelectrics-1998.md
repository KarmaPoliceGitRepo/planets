## Overview
Reviews piezoelectric materials suitable for operation at elevated temperatures (above 200°C). Covers single-crystal materials (langasite, LiNbO3, quartz, GaPO4, Li2B4O7) and polycrystalline ferroelectric ceramics (bismuth titanate family, lead titanate, strontium niobate). Discusses fundamental limitations including phase transitions, depoling, conductivity, and domain-wall effects.

## Unique contribution
Provides a concise comparative review of high-temperature piezoelectric options as of 1998, highlighting the breakthrough demonstration of langasite SAW devices operating at 1000°C. Identifies Nb-doped bismuth titanate as a promising ceramic for 300–400°C applications via domain-wall pinning.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Quartz: excellent stability but limited to <573°C (α→β transition); low coupling; ferrobielastic twinning
- LiNbO3 / LiTaO3: high coupling but high conductivity and pyroelectric effect at high T; decomposition at 300°C
- Langasite (LGS): no phase transition to melting (1470°C); SAW device demonstrated at 1000°C; full property data only available to 80°C; twinning and softness are concerns
- GaPO4: similar to quartz but β-phase transition at 930°C; very good temperature stability of piezoelectric coefficients; lower mechanical strength than quartz
- Li2B4O7: zero TCF orientations available; phase transition near 500°C discovered — reduces interest
- AlN thin film: no phase transition; operation demonstrated to 1150°C; high resistivity; works in N2 atmosphere; ideal for high-temperature thin-film sensors
- Bismuth titanate (Bi4Ti3O12): Tc = 685°C; high p-type conductivity limits use; Nb-doping reduces conductivity by 3 orders of magnitude but introduces hysteresis; n-type Nb compositions show domain-wall pinning → linear response
- Lead titanate modifications and strontium niobate family also reviewed
- Conclusion: langasite and AlN best for stringent high-temperature applications; Nb-doped BTO for 300–400°C range
