## Overview
Studies PZT rod-polymer 1-3 composites (5–30 vol% PZT, 0.28 mm and 0.45 mm rods) for ultrasonic transducer applications in the 1–10 MHz range. Identifies three principal resonance modes: planar mode, thickness mode, and lateral (periodicity) modes from rod array regularity. Uses electrical impedance spectroscopy and laser probe dilatometry to map resonance behaviour. Shows that lateral interactions through the epoxy matrix create additional resonance modes that can interfere with or enhance thickness-mode performance.

## Unique contribution
First systematic investigation of PZT rod-polymer composites at megahertz frequencies (vs prior sub-40 kHz hydrophone work). Demonstrates using laser probe dilatometry that lateral coupling through epoxy creates periodicity-related resonances. Establishes that composite microstructure periodicity scale relative to acoustic wavelength determines frequency behaviour — essential design criterion for medical imaging transducers. Investigates temperature dependence of electromechanical properties.

## Key concepts
- 1-3 connectivity PZT rod-polymer composite: rods continuous in thickness direction, polymer in lateral
- Three resonance types: planar mode, thickness mode, lateral periodicity modes
- Lateral modes: arise from regular rod spacing in composite lattice; can obscure thickness resonance
- 5–30 vol% PZT; rod diameters 0.28 mm and 0.45 mm; epoxy matrix
- Impedance spectroscopy + laser dilatometry for resonance mapping
- Temperature dependence of composite electromechanical properties
- Application range: 1–10 MHz (medical diagnostic, NDE) vs prior <40 kHz hydrophones

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Materials: PZT rods in epoxy matrix; compositions: 5%, 10%, 20%, 30% vol PZT
- Observations: lateral modes due to periodic rod array become problematic when rod spacing ≈ acoustic wavelength
- Laser dilatometry: shows spatial distribution of displacement amplitude across composite; confirms mode structure
- Temperature effect on coupling coefficient and resonance frequencies measured
- Design implication: rod spacing must be chosen to push periodicity modes away from operating frequency
- Origin of current transducer design rules for 1-3 composites in medical imaging established in this paper
