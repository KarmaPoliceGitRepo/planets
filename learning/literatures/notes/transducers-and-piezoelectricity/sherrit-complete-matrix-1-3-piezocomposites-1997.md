# Complete Matrix of the Piezoelectric, Dielectric, and Elastic Material Constants of 1-3 Piezoelectric Ceramic/Polymer Composites

- **Source:** Sherrit 1997 complete matrix 1-3 piezocomposites
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1q6GpcieHP4lT_ObplNDDQYljNqeQITr8/view
- **Type:** paper (conference, SPIE Smart Materials, Structures, and Integrated Systems 1997; invited)
- **Author/Year:** S. Sherrit, H. D. Wiederick, B. K. Mukherjee / 1997
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Characterises the effective material constant matrix [S^E, d, ε^T] for two PZT-5H/hard-polyurethane 1-3 piezoelectric composites at 15 vol% and 30 vol% PZT (injection-moulded by Materials Systems Inc.) using five resonance modes. Presents complete reduced complex matrices for both compositions. Identifies limitations of applying bulk resonance analysis to composite materials, particularly for thickness shear and length extensional modes where aspect ratio and rod-count constraints are hardest to satisfy.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that the 1-3 connectivity selectively suppresses the thickness shear mode (k₁₅ ≈ 0.015–0.008 for 15%) relative to other modes due to lateral shear compliance of the polymer matrix. Provides Banno-model theoretical estimates for thickness shear and lateral permittivity and shows quantitative agreement with measured values. Identifies that length extensional d₃₃ is underestimated due to limited aspect ratio and single-rod unit cell boundary conditions; Berlincourt (200 Hz) measurement gives d₃₃ up to 477 pC/N for 30% vs 406 pC/N from LE resonance.

## Key concepts
- 1-3 connectivity PZT-5H/polyurethane composite: ceramic rods in polymer matrix
- Injection moulding: MSI (Materials Systems Inc.); two compositions: 15 vol% (ρ=2200 kg/m³), 30 vol% (ρ=3190 kg/m³)
- Five resonance modes: RADE, TE, LTE, LE, TSE
- Effective material constants: treat composite as homogeneous if many rods and correct aspect ratios
- Banno model for lateral permittivity and thickness shear compliance of 1-3 composite
- s₁₃^E determination by iterative matrix inversion (matching c^D₃₃ from TE mode)
- Berlincourt d₃₃ at 200 Hz vs LE resonance d₃₃: frequency dispersion comparison

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Poisson ratio 0.45–0.49 for both compositions: lateral dimension controlled by polymer elastic properties
- TE mode: rod periodicity resonances obscure the thickness resonance for 15% samples; only f_p used to extract c^D₃₃
- LTE and RADE material constants in general agreement; differences attributed to frequency dispersion (LTE at 4–6 kHz vs RADE at 20–50 kHz)
- Key results (Table I): 15%/30% PZT: s^E₁₁ = 2.20/1.75×10⁻¹⁰; d₁₃ = −124/−168 pC/N; d₃₃ (LE) = 282/406 pC/N; d₃₃ (Berlincourt) = 336/477 pC/N; ε^T₃₃ = 4.84/8.98×10⁻⁹ F/m; k_p = 0.239/0.286; k₁₃ = 0.119/0.126
- Thickness shear (30% only): d₁₅ = 0.035 pC/N (very small), ε^T₁₁ = 0.095×10⁻⁹ F/m; 15% shear resonances not reproducible
- Hydrostatic coefficients: LE-based d₃₃ underestimates true hydrostatic performance; Berlincourt d₃₃ more appropriate for sonar/hydrophone design
- Caution: proper aspect ratios (≥5:1 for LE) and ≥many rods required; single-rod unit cell gives different strain boundary conditions
