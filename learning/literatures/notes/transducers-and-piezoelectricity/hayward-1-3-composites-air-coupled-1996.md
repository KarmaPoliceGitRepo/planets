# 1-3 Connectivity Piezocomposite Transducers for Air-Coupled Ultrasonics

- **Source:** Hayward 1996 - 1-3 composites air coupled.pdf

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Investigates 1-3 piezocomposite transducers (PZT pillars in polymer matrix) as transmitters and receivers for air-coupled ultrasonic NDE. The reduced acoustic impedance of composites (compared to monolithic PZT) improves coupling to air, while the design retains strong piezoelectric activity.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that 1-3 composites with appropriately chosen polymer fill can achieve acoustic impedances in the range 5–12 MRayl, bridging the gap between PZT (~33 MRayl) and air, enabling air-coupled through-transmission with significantly better SNR than monolithic PZT transducers.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- 1-3 composite structure: PZT pillars embedded in soft polymer (e.g., Spurrs epoxy, polyurethane); pillar volume fraction controls impedance
- Effective impedance of composite: approximately Z_eff ≈ V_f × Z_PZT + (1-V_f) × Z_polymer (simplified rule of mixtures)
- Coupling coefficient kt of composite exceeds monolithic PZT in thickness mode due to lateral stress relief
- Fabrication: dice-and-fill technique (precision sawing of PZT block, then polymer infiltration)
- Demonstrated at 400 kHz in air; transmitted through 25 mm CFRP test blocks
- Receive sensitivity improvement of ~20 dB over monolithic PZT at same frequency for air coupling
- Matching layers still beneficial but single-layer suffices due to reduced base mismatch
- Results show 1-3 composites are practical for industrial air-coupled NDE of composites
