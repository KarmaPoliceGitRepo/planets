## Overview
Review of ultrasonic sensors and sensor systems for continuous process monitoring in the chemical, pharmaceutical, food, petrochemical, and water/waste-water industries. Covers applications in level measurement, flow measurement, concentration determination, and reaction monitoring. Discusses advantages, limitations, and commercial examples of ultrasonic process sensors.

## Unique contribution
Provides a structured overview of industrial ultrasonic sensor applications with discussion of competing sensor types and the specific advantages of ultrasonic methods for process monitoring. Covers both contact and non-contact configurations. Discusses acoustic microsensors alongside conventional transducers.

## Key concepts
- Process industry applications: level, flow, concentration, conversion monitoring
- Ultrasonic sensors vs other process sensor types (temperature, pressure, flow)
- Speed of sound and sound attenuation as measurable quantities for process parameters
- Non-invasive measurement: key advantage for industrial process sensors
- Acoustic microsensors (quartz resonators) for liquid property measurement
- Industries covered: chemical, petrochemical, pharmaceutical, food/beverage, biotechnology, water/waste-water

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Market context: flow sensors most important process measurement (by value), followed by level, pressure, temperature
- Requirements: high accuracy, low cross-sensitivity, high reliability, high turndown ratio, non-invasive
- Ultrasonic methods applicable to: liquid level (echo time-of-flight), flow (Doppler/transit time), concentration (speed of sound)
- Challenges: acoustic impedance mismatch at vessel walls, temperature effects on sound velocity, multi-phase flow complications
- Commercial examples presented for each application category
