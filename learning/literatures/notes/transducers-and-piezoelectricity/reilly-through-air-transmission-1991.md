## Overview
Describes an air-coupled ultrasonic NDE system using PVDF polymer transducers for through-transmission testing. The system avoids the use of coupling fluids by exploiting PVDF's lower acoustic impedance relative to PZT ceramics, making it more compatible with air.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates practical air-coupled through-transmission C-scan imaging using PVDF transducers with matching layers, achieving usable SNR without liquid couplant. Provides design guidelines for PVDF-based air transducers including focusing and bandwidth.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PVDF has acoustic impedance ~4 MRayl, much closer to air (0.0004 MRayl) than PZT (~33 MRayl), reducing mismatch by roughly 8x
- Even so, air/PVDF mismatch is still large (~10,000:1 in intensity), requiring matching layers or focusing
- System used focused PVDF transducers to increase signal amplitude through geometrical gain
- Through-transmission mode used: separate transmitter and receiver aligned coaxially
- C-scan images of test samples (e.g. CFRP composites) demonstrated defect detection capability
- Bandwidth of PVDF transducers is inherently broad due to low Q, aiding pulse resolution
- Identified practical constraints: alignment sensitivity, SNR limitations in highly attenuating samples
