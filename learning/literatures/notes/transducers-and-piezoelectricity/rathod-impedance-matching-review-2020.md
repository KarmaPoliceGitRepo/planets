## Overview
A comprehensive review of acoustic impedance matching strategies for piezoelectric transducers, covering both electrical and acoustic matching techniques. Reviews single-layer and multi-layer quarter-wavelength matching layers, gradient-index matching, and electrical tuning approaches across a broad frequency range.

## Unique contribution
Consolidates several decades of matching layer design into a single reference, covering materials, design equations, and applications from low-frequency NDE to high-frequency medical imaging. Includes novel approaches such as metamaterial matching and air-coupled transducer matching.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Quarter-wave matching layer design: optimal impedance Z_m = (Z_piezo × Z_load)^0.5; thickness = λ/4 at center frequency
- Multi-layer matching (Chebyshev, binomial tapering) extends bandwidth; two layers can achieve >10:1 impedance ratio with good bandwidth
- Gradient matching layers (e.g., aerogel, porous ceramics) act as continuous impedance graded interface, reducing reflection over broad band
- Electrical matching: L-C networks transform transducer impedance to match drive electronics; affects sensitivity vs. bandwidth
- For air-coupled transducers: impedance mismatch between PZT (~33 MRayl) and air (0.0004 MRayl) is ~82,500:1 in impedance ratio
- Silica aerogel (Z ~0.08 MRayl) and Kapton composite layers used as matching materials for air coupling
- Review covers medical, NDE, SONAR, and process monitoring applications
- Backing material selection: high-impedance backing (tungsten-epoxy) maximizes damping; low-impedance backing preserves sensitivity
