## Overview
Comprehensive review of industrial applications of piezoelectric polymer transducers (primarily PVDF and its copolymers) covering non-destructive testing, underwater acoustics, medical imaging, and industrial sensing. Reviews transducer design principles and compares polymer-based devices to ceramic alternatives, highlighting the niche markets where PVDF and derivatives are the material of choice (e.g., miniature underwater hydrophones, broadband NDE probes).

## Unique contribution
Provides a consolidated survey of PVDF/P(VDF-TrFE) transducer applications from 1969 discovery through mid-1990s, identifying emerging NDE niche markets. Reviews literature from Ferroelectrics special issue (1984) through 1994, covering structure, properties, and industrial deployments. Links piezoelectric/pyroelectric polymer properties to specific application requirements.

## Key concepts
- PVDF and derivatives: piezoelectric activity first reported 1969; research maturation by mid-1990s
- Applications: underwater hydrophones, NDE probes, biomedical imaging, industrial sensing
- Advantages over PZT ceramics: low acoustic impedance (better water matching), flexibility, large area, low cost
- Niche market: miniature underwater hydrophones — polymer has become material of choice
- Pyroelectric properties also relevant for thermal sensing

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Review covers structure-property relationships, fabrication, and device design for polymer transducers
- NDE field: 'niche market' emerging for PVDF-based transducers in specific high-resolution applications
- Comparison to ceramics: polymers favoured where impedance matching to water/soft tissue is critical
- Publication context: Department of Instrumentation and Analytical Science, UMIST, Manchester
