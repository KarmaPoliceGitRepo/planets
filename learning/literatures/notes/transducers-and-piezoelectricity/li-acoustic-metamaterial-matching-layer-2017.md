# Broadband Gradient Impedance Matching Using an Acoustic Metamaterial for Ultrasonic Transducers

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Introduces an anisotropic cone-structured acoustic metamaterial as a broadband impedance matching layer for piezoelectric transducers. The metamaterial consists of pyramidal silica fibre cones in epoxy resin, providing a continuous gradient impedance from ~11.4 MRayl (piezo side) to ~3.0 MRayl (tissue side). Achieves -6 dB bandwidth of 107% experimentally.

## Unique contribution
First practical acoustic metamaterial matching layer for medical ultrasound transducers. Demonstrates that a silica cone array (etched optical fibres) can be integrated with PZT to give >100% bandwidth — far exceeding the 57–60% achievable with single quarter-wave matching layers. Enables full utilisation of high-coupling PMN-PT single crystal materials.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Fabrication: silica fibres (125 µm diameter) etched with HF to form pyramidal cones of length ~610 µm; voids filled with EPO-TEK 301 epoxy; sliced to thickness
- Gradient: silica volume fraction decreases from ~90% (PZT side) to near 0% (tissue side); impedance gradient 11.4 → 3.0 MRayl
- Theory: ISO-STRAIN composite theory used to derive Z(t) along thickness; impedance transfer equation predicts transmission ≥90% for f > first resonance
- With 610 µm layer: -6 dB bandwidth from ~3–8 MHz (simulation 100%, measured 107%)
- Traditional λ/4 single layer: measured 58%, simulated 58.2% — more than 40 percentage points improvement
- Porous PZT (ρ = 6300 kg/m³, vl = 3200 m/s, ZA = 20 MRayl) used to reduce PZT-metamaterial mismatch
- Simulated PMN-PT single crystal with Si-epoxy metamaterial: predicted -6 dB bandwidth of 116%
- Applications: next-generation broadband medical imaging and harmonic imaging transducers
