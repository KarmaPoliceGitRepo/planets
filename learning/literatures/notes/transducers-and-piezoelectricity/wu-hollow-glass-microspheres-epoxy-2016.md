# Hollow Glass Microsphere Epoxy Composite as Matching Layer for Air-Coupled Ultrasonic Transducers

- **Source:** Wu hollow glass microspheres epoxy 2016
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1f32OnuRgzNqg9RQMCK_DE7Ck_x5JzFbc/view
- **Type:** paper (2016 Symposium on Piezoelectricity, Acoustic Waves, and Device Applications, SPAWDA)
- **Author/Year:** S. Wu et al. / 2016
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Fabricates and characterises a single quarter-wave matching layer made from hollow glass microsphere (HGM) filled epoxy composite for air-coupled PZT transducers operating at 700 kHz. Achieves ρ = 665 kg/m³ and c = 2862 m/s (Z = 1.91 MRayl) by adjusting HGM volume fraction. Measures 29.6 dB improvement in received voltage amplitude compared to an unmatched transducer, with the theoretical prediction being 30.5 dB — within 1 dB of ideal.

## Unique contribution
Provides complete fabrication and characterisation procedure for HGM-epoxy at 700 kHz — higher frequency than previous HGM matching layer work. Demonstrates that Z = 1.91 MRayl is achievable with controlled HGM loading, and that attenuation α = 350 Np/m does not significantly degrade performance at 700 kHz for λ/4 thickness (≈ 1 mm). Closes the gap between Tone 1984 (silicone-microsphere at 1 MHz) and modern transducer design practice.

## Key concepts
- HGM-epoxy material: ρ = 665 kg/m³; c = 2862 m/s; Z = ρc = 1.91 MRayl; α = 350 Np/m at 700 kHz
- Quarter-wave thickness: λ/4 = c/(4f₀) = 2862/(4×700×10³) ≈ 1.02 mm
- Transducer: PZT (ZC ≈ 33 MRayl); target medium: air (ZT = 0.000429 MRayl)
- Optimal single ML impedance: Z_opt = (ZC × ZT)^(1/2) ≈ 0.12 MRayl — HGM-epoxy at 1.91 MRayl is far above ideal but still improves transmission
- Improvement mechanism: any layer reduces mismatch; not at theoretical optimum but practically useful
- Measured improvement: 29.6 dB increase in received voltage amplitude
- Theoretical improvement: 30.5 dB (1D acoustic transmission model)
- Agreement: theory and experiment within 0.9 dB
- Test setup: two matched 700 kHz transducers in pitch-catch mode; separation 40 mm
- Attenuation in ML: 350 Np/m × 0.001 m = 0.35 Np ≈ 3 dB — partially offsets coupling gain

## Methods / results / takeaways
- HGM type: 3M Scotchlite glass bubbles; mixed with epoxy resin; cured in mold
- Volume fraction: adjusted to achieve target density; Z = 1.91 MRayl selected for sub-optimal single ML
- Characterisation: pulse-echo immersion technique; sound speed from TOF; density from mass/volume; attenuation from spectral decay
- Bonding: ML bonded to PZT front face with thin epoxy layer; λ/4 at 700 kHz
- Pitch-catch experiment: 700 kHz tone burst; 40 mm air gap; oscilloscope amplitude measurement
- Results: 29.6 dB (measured) vs 30.5 dB (theory); excellent agreement
- Limitation: Z = 1.91 MRayl is 16× higher than optimal Z_opt = 0.12 MRayl; a second outer low-Z layer would give further improvement
- Comparison to Tone 1984: similar concept but at higher frequency and with characterisation data
- Comparison to Yano 1987: complementary — Yano used microporous polyolefin (Z=0.24 MRayl) as outer ML
- Application: air-coupled NDE at 700 kHz; first matching layer in a two-layer design
