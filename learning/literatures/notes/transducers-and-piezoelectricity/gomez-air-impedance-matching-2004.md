## Overview
Comprehensive study of matching layer materials and designs for coupling piezoelectric transducers to air. Investigates filtration membranes, aerogels, and porous polymers as matching layers in single and multi-layer configurations, demonstrating transmission improvements across the 0.1–1 MHz range.

## Unique contribution
Reports best achieved insertion loss of -24 dB at 0.45 MHz using optimised single or multi-layer filtration membrane matching, substantially better than unmatched PZT performance. Surveys commercially available membrane filter materials (polycarbonate, PVDF, polysulfone) as convenient low-impedance matching layers.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Filtration membranes (polycarbonate track-etched, PVDF, polysulfone) characterised as potential matching layers
- Best result: -24 dB two-way insertion loss at 0.45 MHz — significant improvement over unmatched (~-82 dB)
- Membrane porosity determines effective acoustic impedance; track-etched polycarbonate offers controllable ZA
- Multi-layer configurations (two or three matching layers) provide broader bandwidth matching
- Aerogel included as reference material; membranes offer better mechanical robustness and bondability
- Performance degrades at higher frequencies due to membrane surface roughness and scattering losses
- Practical advantage of membrane filters: off-the-shelf availability, flat geometry, consistent porosity
