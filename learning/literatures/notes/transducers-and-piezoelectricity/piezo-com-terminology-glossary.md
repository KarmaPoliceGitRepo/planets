## Overview
A concise glossary of piezoelectric terminology from the piezo.com website, defining the key coefficients and parameters used to characterise piezoelectric materials and devices.

## Unique contribution
Quick-reference definition sheet for piezoelectric constants (d, g, k, K, Y), including their physical meanings and interrelationships. Useful as a primer for interpreting datasheets and literature.

## Key concepts
- Piezoelectric d coefficient
- Piezoelectric g coefficient
- Electromechanical coupling coefficient k
- Relative permittivity K (dielectric constant)
- Young's modulus Y
- Pyroelectric coefficient
- Strain vs. stress definitions

## Methods / results / takeaways
- d (strain coefficient): strain developed / applied electric field = short-circuit charge density / applied stress (units: m/V = C/N)
- g (voltage coefficient): open-circuit electric field / applied mechanical stress (units: Vm/N)
- k (coupling coefficient): k = √(electrical energy stored / mechanical energy applied)
- K = permittivity of material / permittivity of free space (dimensionless)
- Y = stress / strain (elastic modulus, Pa)
- Pyroelectric coefficient E(pyro) = p·ΔT / (K₃·ε₀) — voltage generated per unit temperature change
- Density ρ = mass / volume
- These definitions provide the framework for interpreting all PZT and PVDF datasheet values
