# Performance Enhancement of an Ultrasonic Power Transfer System Through a Tightly Coupled Solid Media Using a KLM Model

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Applies the KLM model to design and optimise an ultrasonic power transfer (UPT) system that transmits electrical energy through a solid metal wall using two 1-3 composite piezoelectric transducers (emitter and receiver). Derives emission and reception transfer functions from KLM transfer matrices, characterises transducer properties by fitting impedance measurements to the KLM model, and validates optimal electrical loading predictions against experiment for aluminium and POM (polyoxymethylene) walls.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that for a broadband medium (aluminium, impedance close to transducer), the maximum received power occurs at the parallel resonance frequency (not the series resonance frequency), whereas for a narrowband medium (POM, large mismatch) maximum power is at series resonance. Provides a practical workflow: KLM impedance fitting → material parameter extraction → optimal load prediction → experimental validation.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic power transfer (UPT) through metal walls
- KLM model for emitter and receiver transducers
- ABCD transfer matrix cascade for combined transducer-medium system
- Emission transfer function HE(p) = F_front/V_input
- Reception transfer function HR(p) = V_output/F_incident
- Two-way transfer function HER(p) = HE(p) × HR(p)
- Complex material parameter extraction from impedance measurements
- Optimal electric load for maximum power transfer
- 1-3 composite piezoelectric transducer (80% fill factor)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- 1-3 composite transducer: Z₀=25.6 MRayl, ZB=1.55 MRayl Neoprene backing; aluminium ZL=13.6 MRayl, POM ZL=3.4 MRayl
- Material parameters extracted by KLM impedance fitting: kt=0.58−0.008j (in air), changes to 0.57−0.013j when mounted; stiffness changes from (102+2.4j) to (97+9.9j) kN/mm²
- Maximum output power: aluminium → occurs near parallel resonance (due to broadband response from low impedance mismatch); POM → occurs at series resonance (narrowband)
- Optimal load prediction: simulated load magnitudes agree with measurement; small discrepancies attributed to undefined back acoustic boundary condition and shear wave effects (1D model limitation)
- Coupling medium: glycerin for repeatable clamped contacts without permanent bonding; avoids air gaps
- Key limitation: 1D KLM model does not capture shear wave contributions at medium-transducer interface
