# Theory of Propagation of Elastic Waves in a Fluid-Saturated Porous Solid. II. Higher Frequency Range

- **Source:** Biot porous solid high frequency 1956
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1B3iDScuzPRr2oWcIR8lnvL1DD7Mv3NKz/view
- **Type:** paper (Journal of the Acoustical Society of America 28(2), 179–191, 1956)
- **Author/Year:** M. A. Biot / 1956
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Extension of Biot (1956a) to the full frequency range. Derives the complex viscosity correction factor F(K) for Poiseuille friction breakdown at high frequency, treating two pore geometries: two-dimensional slits (Kelvin functions) and circular ducts (Bessel functions). Introduces a "structural factor" β that combines pore sinuosity and shape effects. Replaces the static viscosity coefficient b with bF(K) in the propagation equations. Plots group velocity, phase velocity, and attenuation per cycle for rotational and two dilatational waves vs f/fc for six parameter cases.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first rigorous treatment of the frequency-dependent friction factor in porous media (the high-frequency boundary layer correction). Demonstrates that for both slit-like and circular pores, the correction functions have the same shape — only the frequency scale changes — justifying a universal correction F(K) with a single structural factor β. Shows that attenuation per unit distance increases as √f at high frequency (compared to f² at low frequency). Provides asymptotic formulas and group velocity curves.

## Key concepts
- Frequency correction function F(K): replaces static viscosity b with bF(K); K = a√(ω/ν)
- F(K→0) = 1 (Poiseuille limit); F(K→∞) ∝ (1+i)/√2 × K (boundary layer regime)
- Pore geometry: slit-like (uses Kelvin functions F₁(K₁)); circular (uses Bessel/Kelvin F(K))
- Structural factor β: accounts for sinuosity and pore shape; β = √8 chosen as representative average
- Two extreme cases: circular pores β = (8γ)^½; slit-like pores β = (16/3)^½
- At high frequency: friction ∝ √f, 45° phase shift between force and velocity (boundary layer)
- All three wave types: attenuation per cycle has maximum near f/fc; vanishes as 1/√f at high f

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Slit geometry: Kirchhoff oscillatory friction; result: F₁(K₁) = (i^½K₁/3)tanh(i^½K₁)/(1 − tanh correction)
- Circular duct: Bessel differential equation; result: T(K) = Kelvin function ratio
- Structural factor β links pore size a to characteristic frequency fc via β² = a²fc × 2π × ρ₂/b
- Phase velocity of rotational wave: increases from V_T = (N/p)^½ to higher asymptote; dispersion peaks near fc
- Group velocity of dilatational first kind: non-monotonic, with a peak near f/fc ≈ 1–2
- Application: extension of Biot theory to ultrasonic NDE frequencies in water-saturated rock, sintered ceramics
