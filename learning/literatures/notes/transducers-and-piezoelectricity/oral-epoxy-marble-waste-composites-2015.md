# Ultrasonic Properties of Epoxy Resin/Marble Waste Powder Composites

- **Source:** Oral epoxy marble waste composites 2015
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1JyLRWwPd9_sl0cBfIQqyT0nsRxAzsM6T/view
- **Type:** paper (Polymer Composites 36, 2015)
- **Author/Year:** Imran Oral / 2015
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Characterises epoxy resin (ER) composites filled with marble waste powder (MWP) extracted from wastewater using different coagulants (sepiolite, zeolite, pumice) at different dosages. Measures longitudinal (20 MHz) and shear (5 MHz) ultrasonic velocities, density, and derives full elastic constants (L, G, K, E, ν, Z) for each composite formulation using pulse-echo contact method with glycerin couplant.

## Unique contribution
First study using different coagulants to produce marble waste powder and characterising the resulting composites by ultrasonic methods. Shows that coagulant chemistry strongly affects microstructure and ultrasonic properties: sepiolite at 4 g/500 mL (S4.0) gives highest longitudinal velocity (2654 m/s), bulk modulus (5.89 GPa), and acoustic impedance (2.78 MRayl), while zeolite at 0.5 g (Z0.5) gives lowest values.

## Key concepts
- Marble waste powder (MWP): fine calcium carbonate filler; environmental recycling application
- Coagulant type (sepiolite > zeolite/pumice at same dosage) controls MWP particle chemistry and dispersion
- Pulse-echo elastic constants: L = ρVL², G = ρVS², K = L − (4/3)G, E from L and G, ν = (L−2G)/[2(L−G)], Z = ρVL
- Pure ER (reference): VL = 2160 m/s, VS = 1048 m/s, ρ = 1164 kg/m³, Z = 2.51 MRayl, E = 3.44 GPa, ν = 0.34

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Panametrics V116 (20 MHz longitudinal) + V155 (5 MHz shear) contact transducers; Epoch XT flaw detector
- Glycerin couplant (longitudinal); SWC couplant (shear); 10 repeated measurements per sample
- S4.0 (sepiolite 4 g): VL = 2654 m/s, E = 3.14 GPa, Z = 2.78 MRayl (highest acoustic impedance in set)
- Z0.5 (zeolite 0.5 g): VL = 2015 m/s (lower than neat ER); lowest acoustic impedance 2.05 MRayl
- Poisson's ratio ranges 0.29–0.41 across all composites; pure ER = 0.34
- MWP with higher MgO content (sepiolite) gives higher longitudinal modulus vs silica-rich coagulants
- Application: construction material recycling; method demonstrates ultrasonic NDE for polymer composite characterisation
