## Overview
A review paper covering the range of piezoelectric materials used in ultrasonic transducers, including PZT ceramics, single crystals (PMN-PT, PZN-PT), PVDF polymer films, and composite structures. Discusses key material parameters and their influence on transducer performance.

## Unique contribution
Provides a comparative framework for material selection, including coupling coefficients, acoustic impedances, bandwidth, and temperature stability across the major piezoelectric families. Useful as a reference for matching material choice to application requirements.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PZT ceramics: kt ~ 0.45–0.55, ZA ~ 30–35 MRayl; workhorse material for most transducer applications
- PMN-PT single crystals: kt ~ 0.60–0.70, d33 > 1000 pC/N; far superior coupling but more fragile and expensive
- PZN-PT: similar to PMN-PT; both near morphotropic phase boundary
- PVDF: kt ~ 0.12–0.15, ZA ~ 4 MRayl; low coupling but excellent acoustic match to water/tissue; broadband
- 1-3 Composites: kt ~ 0.55–0.65, ZA tunable 5–25 MRayl by adjusting volume fraction
- Temperature stability: PZT degrades above Curie temperature (~200–350 °C); PVDF limited to <80 °C
- Lead-free alternatives (KNN, BNT-BT) discussed as emerging materials; coupling still below PZT
- Selection guide: medical imaging favors PMN-PT or 1-3 composites; NDE favors PZT or composites; air coupling favors PVDF or low-impedance composites
