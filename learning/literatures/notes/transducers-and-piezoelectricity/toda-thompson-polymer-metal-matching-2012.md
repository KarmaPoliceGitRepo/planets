# Detailed Investigations of Polymer/Metal Multilayer Matching Layer and Backing Absorber Structures for Wideband Ultrasonic Transducers

- **Source:** 2012-D~2.PDF
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1oLO3ByKT_yaFka3_4gJsvH0UuF00pfh0/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Minoru Toda, Mitch Thompson / 2012
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Validates the polymer/metal multilayer matching and backing absorber design theory introduced in a companion 2010 paper. Characterises constituent material properties (polyimide, copper tape adhesive) by fitting Mason's model to impedance angle curves of P(VDF-TrFE) resonators with bonded test layers. Applies the design to a double matching layer on PZT5H (11 μm PVDF / 22 μm Cu / 75 μm polyimide) achieving 3.1 MHz bandwidth centred at 6.4 MHz. Also characterises multilayer copper-tape absorbers: 10 layers at 3 MHz give 4 MRayl impedance, 220 dB/cm attenuation, 890 m/s velocity.

## Unique contribution
Provides rigorous parameter identification for thin-film matching layers using acoustic resonance spectroscopy on a P(VDF-TrFE) resonator platform, enabling accurate Mason's model simulations. Demonstrates that sub-λ/15 polymer/metal bilayer structures can realise arbitrary intermediate acoustic impedances, and that stacked copper-tape layers provide high-attenuation backing absorber behaviour comparable to tungsten-filled polymer composites — using only commercially available adhesive-backed metal tape.

## Key concepts
- Polymer/metal multilayer impedance matching (non-monotonic impedance ordering)
- Acoustic resonance spectroscopy for thin-film parameter identification
- Mason's model with complex impedance for matching layer simulation
- Sub-quarter-wavelength polymer-metal matching structure (mass-spring model)
- Multilayer copper-tape backing absorber (adhesive as spring, copper as mass)
- P(VDF-TrFE) copolymer transducer characterisation
- Effective acoustic impedance Z_MK = √(M·K)
- Quarter-wave polyimide outer matching layer

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Material parameter identification: P(VDF-TrFE) at 4.8 MHz (v=2235 m/s, Z≈4.2 MRayl, Q=5.1), polyimide (v=2100 m/s, Z=3.1 MRayl), copper tape adhesive (Q=3.5 at 6 MHz), copper (v=5010 m/s, Z=44.9 MRayl)
- Double matching layer design (for PZT5H Z=36.2 MRayl → water Z=1.5 MRayl): Z₁=9.25 MRayl realised by 11 μm PVDF + 22 μm Cu; Z₂=2.36 MRayl realised by 75 μm polyimide
- Experimental pulse-echo result: 3.1 MHz bandwidth at −6 dB, centred at 6.4 MHz; agrees well with Mason's model prediction
- Non-traditional layer ordering: polymer (low Z) close to PZT, metal (high Z) on outer side — effective impedance governed by √(M·K) with M=metal areal mass, K=polymer spring constant
- Copper tape absorber (50–90 layers stacked): velocity 928 m/s (theory 889 m/s); attenuation 222 dB/cm at 3 MHz (theory 342 dB/cm, discrepancy from Q frequency dependence)
- 10-layer copper tape stack: effective Z≈4 MRayl at 3 MHz; confirmed by impedance angle comparison on loaded PZT plate
- High-impedance absorber (38 μm stainless / 2.5 μm spray adhesive): theoretical Z_MK=14.7 MRayl, measured ≈10 MRayl — comparable to tungsten-powder/epoxy backings
- Back matching layer (50 μm polyimide + 50 μm brass) between PZT and absorber: Z_MK=7.2 MRayl, converts absorber impedance to ≈11 MRayl at PZT back face
- Non-uniform adhesive thickness in absorber expected to increase bandwidth (beneficial for practical production)
