# Representation of Dielectric, Elastic, and Piezoelectric Losses by Complex Coefficients

- **Source:** 1967 - Representation of Dielectric, Elastic, and Piezoelectric Losses by Complex Coefficients - R Holland.pdf

- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** R. Holland / 1967
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Establishes the formal framework for representing piezoelectric material losses as negative imaginary parts of the complex material coefficient matrix (s^E, d, ε^T). Uses the electromechanical Poynting vector to separate loss into three components: purely dielectric, purely elastic, and a cross-product piezoelectric loss. Shows that positive loss requires the imaginary part of the material coefficient matrix to be positive definite, placing strict constraints on the permitted values of the loss tangents.

## Unique contribution
First complete rigorous treatment of all three loss components in piezoelectrics using complex coefficients and Poynting vector formalism. Derives necessary and sufficient conditions on the loss tangents for a passive (lossy, non-amplifying) material. Gives explicit constraints for ferroelectric ceramics with their simplified (6mm symmetry) coefficient matrices. Standard reference for complex material constant formalism.

## Key concepts
- Complex piezoelectric material coefficients (s^E*, d*, ε^T*)
- Electromechanical Poynting vector
- Power dissipation density in piezoelectrics
- Passive material constraints (positive definiteness)
- Dielectric, elastic, and piezoelectric loss tangents
- Ferroelectric ceramic coefficient matrix (6mm symmetry)
- Imaginary part sign convention

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Power dissipation density derived from divergence of generalised Poynting vector: Pd = (ω/2) Im(E·D* + T:S*)
- Three independent loss terms: (1) purely elastic loss ∝ s^E'' |T|² ; (2) purely dielectric loss ∝ ε^T'' |E|² ; (3) cross-term piezoelectric loss ∝ d'' |E||T|
- Sign convention: imaginary parts of s^E, d, ε^T must be negative for positive loss (physical passive material)
- Positive definiteness of Im(M_pq) matrix gives: s^E_ii'' ≥ 0, ε^T_ii'' ≥ 0, and cross-term constraints involving d'' and the other loss tangents
- For ferroelectric ceramics (6mm symmetry): explicit inequalities derived relating s^E_11'', s^E_33'', ε^T_11'', ε^T_33'', and d_15'', d_33''
- Foundation paper for all subsequent work on complex piezoelectric constants (Sherrit, Smits, etc.)
