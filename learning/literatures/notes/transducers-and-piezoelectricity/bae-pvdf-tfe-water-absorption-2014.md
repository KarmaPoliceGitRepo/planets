# Effect of Water Absorption on the Mechanical Properties of P(VDF-TrFE) Copolymer Films

- **Source:** Bae PVDF TrFE water absorption 2014
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/12_v0UjhAlciU8qdsBbGbaHgOr26ZrGpl/view
- **Type:** paper (Polymer Engineering & Science 54, 2654–2659, 2014)
- **Author/Year:** K. Bae, S.-H. Moon, K.-Y. Suh, J.-H. Lee, S. Cho / 2014
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Investigates the effect of water absorption on mechanical properties of P(VDF-TrFE) 75/25 mol% films (120 μm thick) submerged in water at 30°C for up to 4 weeks. Measures tensile properties (Young's modulus, tensile strength, elongation-to-break), water content by mass, XRD d-spacing, and creep compliance. Finds that water absorption is moderate (≈5 wt% after 4 weeks), fully reversible upon annealing at 50°C, and unexpectedly stiffens the film — contrary to the behavior of most polymers.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that water absorption in P(VDF-TrFE) increases Young's modulus (+9.33%), tensile strength (+36.66%), and strain-to-break (+66.23%) simultaneously — a stiffening effect unusual among polymers. Explains this through hydrogen bonding between absorbed water molecules and the polymer chain, which restricts chain mobility. Shows creep rate decreases with water uptake (anti-plasticizing behavior). Provides XRD evidence: d₁₁₀ spacing increases from 4.46 Å (dry) to 4.51 Å after 4 weeks, indicating water intercalation in crystalline interstitial sites.

## Key concepts
- P(VDF-TrFE) 75/25 mol%: semicrystalline ferroelectric copolymer; films cast from DMF solution
- Water absorption: ≈5 wt% after 4 weeks at 30°C; absorption rate follows Fickian diffusion
- Reversibility: annealing at 50°C fully restores original (dry) properties; confirmed by mass recovery
- Young's modulus: 1.05 GPa (dry) → 1.148 GPa (4 weeks wet) = +9.33% increase
- Tensile strength: 14.53 MPa (dry) → 19.85 MPa (4 weeks) = +36.66% increase
- Strain-to-break: 19.3% (dry) → 32.1% (4 weeks) = +66.23% increase
- XRD: d₁₁₀ spacing increases 4.46 → 4.51 Å; indicates water in crystalline interstitial sites
- Creep: compliance decreases with immersion time (F-H bonding restricts chain mobility → anti-plasticizing)
- Mechanism: H-F hydrogen bonds between water and PVDF fluorine atoms restrict chain mobility at inter-lamellar regions

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Films: P(VDF-TrFE) 75/25 mol%, 120 μm thick, corona-poled
- Immersion: distilled water, 30°C, up to 4 weeks (168 h); mass weighed periodically
- Tensile tests: Instron at 10 mm/min crosshead speed; five specimens per condition
- XRD: CuKα radiation; (110)/(200) reflections tracked for d-spacing changes
- Creep: constant stress applied; creep compliance J(t) measured vs time
- Saturation water content: ≈5 wt% at 4 weeks; no further increase after 3 weeks
- Drying recovery: 24h at 50°C fully restores original mass, modulus, and XRD pattern
- Application: design of P(VDF-TrFE) sensors/transducers in humid/wet environments; confirms material is robust to incidental water exposure but properties change during immersion
