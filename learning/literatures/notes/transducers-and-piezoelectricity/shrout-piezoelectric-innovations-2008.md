## Overview
Concise review of piezoelectric material innovations driven by medical ultrasound transducer demands. Covers PZT-5H ceramics for 3–7 MHz imaging, high-permittivity PMN-PT polycrystals for array impedance matching, Relaxor-PT single crystals (PMN-PT, PIN-PT) for >90% coupling and broad bandwidth, lead-free alternatives, and fabrication methods for high-frequency (20–100 MHz) transducers including tape casting, sol-gel, lost-mold, and DRIE microfabrication.

## Unique contribution
Provides a concise comparative overview across material families and fabrication methods, framing each innovation against the specific transducer performance requirement driving it (coupling, permittivity, frequency, resolution). Highlights DRIE (deep reactive ion etching) as an enabling technology for 1-3 crystal-polymer composites at >60 MHz, well beyond conventional dicing limits.

## Key concepts
- PZT-5H ceramic for 3–7 MHz medical imaging (k33 ≥ 75%, εr ≈ 3400)
- PMN-PT polycrystalline ceramics (high permittivity εr > 7000, lower Tc <150°C)
- Relaxor-PT single crystals PMN-PT, PIN-PT (k33 > 90%, ultra-high bandwidth)
- Lead-free piezoceramics (BaTiO3, KNN, NBT) — lower k and εr, limited utility
- Fine-grain PbTiO3 and PMN-PT for 20–100 MHz single elements and annular arrays
- Fabrication: dice and fill, tape casting, sol-gel lost-mold, DRIE microfabrication
- 1-3 composite transducers (DRIE-fabricated at 60+ MHz)
- Electromechanical coupling coefficient k33, kt, keff
- Curie temperature Tc vs coupling / permittivity trade-off

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Material comparison table: PZT-5H (εr=3900, d33=690 pC/N, k33=0.74, Tc=210°C), PMN-PT polycrystal (εr=6000, Tc=150°C), PMN-32PT crystal (εr=8000, d33=2250 pC/N, k33=0.91, Tc=166°C)
- Lead-free ceramics show far lower permittivity and coupling than PZT — limited to single-element applications
- Dice-and-fill: limited by saw kerf width; fine features require alternative methods
- DRIE: mills 5 μm kerfs in bulk PMN-PT crystal → 1-3 composites with keff > 70% at >60 MHz
- For high-frequency arrays (>20 MHz): higher permittivity ceramics (εr > 7000) needed to maintain 50 Ω electrical impedance matching
- BiScO3-PbTiO3 system: Tc > 400°C — potential for harsh environment NDE applications
