## Overview
Describes fabrication and characterization of micromachined capacitive transducers (electrostatic/CMUT precursors) for use in air-coupled ultrasound. The devices use micro-etched silicon membranes over a conductive backplate to create capacitive transducer elements operable in air without liquid coupling.

## Unique contribution
Early demonstration of silicon micromachining (plasma etching) to produce capacitive air-coupled transducers competitive with piezoelectric devices. The approach achieves broad bandwidth and reasonable sensitivity in air by exploiting the compliance of thin silicon membranes.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Fabrication: silicon wafer etched (plasma/KOH) to create thin membrane arrays over a conductive backplate
- DC bias applied to create electrostatic attraction; AC signal excites membrane vibration
- Frequency range demonstrated: 100 kHz to >1 MHz in air
- Bandwidth much broader than piezoelectric resonators due to absence of piezoelectric resonance
- Key advantage: acoustic impedance of thin silicon membrane is much closer to air than bulk PZT
- Through-transmission NDE demonstrated on thin plates and composites
- Sensitivity limited by capacitance (small gap, small area); SNR lower than contact piezoelectric but workable for many NDE tasks
- Precursor technology to modern CMUTs (capacitive micromachined ultrasonic transducers)
