## Overview
Comprehensive tabulation of acoustic properties (longitudinal velocity, acoustic impedance, attenuation) for a wide range of isotropic materials including metals, plastics, rubbers, epoxies, biological tissues, liquids, and gases. Measurement methodology described for immersion tank echo-amplitude methods.

## Unique contribution
Provides a single-source reference for acoustic material properties covering hundreds of materials — a standard reference for ultrasonic transducer and NDE system design. The measurement method (immersion tank, echo amplitude ratios against reference) is practical and transferable.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Measurement method: immersion tank at room temperature; echo amplitude compared to calibrated reference reflector to derive impedance and attenuation
- Extensive tables cover: metals (aluminium, steel, brass, copper, tungsten), plastics (Perspex/PMMA, polyethylene, polystyrene, Araldite, PTFE), rubbers, epoxies, biological tissues (fat, muscle, liver, bone), liquids (water, glycerol, alcohols), gases
- Selected values: water ZA = 1.48 MRayl; PMMA ZA = 3.22 MRayl; steel ZA = 45.7 MRayl; tungsten ZA = 101 MRayl; air ZA = 0.000429 MRayl
- Attenuation varies widely: metals ~0.001–0.01 dB/cm/MHz; rubbers ~1–5 dB/cm/MHz; biological tissue ~0.3–1 dB/cm/MHz
- Many values are approximate (±5–20%) due to composition variability and measurement conditions
- Reference widely cited in transducer design literature for choosing matching layer, backing, and coupling fluid materials
