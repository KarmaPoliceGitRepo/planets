# Method for Acoustic Characterization of Materials in Temperature

- **Source:** Cadot acoustic characterization temperature 2016
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1s1XUuEzqz6nhwitjDeth-WIRMJUjAg0E/view
- **Type:** paper (conference, 19th World Conference on Non-Destructive Testing 2016)
- **Author/Year:** Christophe Cadot, Jean-François Saillant, Bernard Dulmet / 2016
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Presents an ultrasonic transmission method for characterising longitudinal and shear wave velocity and attenuation in solid materials from 40°C to 180°C, using an oil bath (hydrogenated coconut oil) as the coupling medium. Characterises Rexolite 1422 (40–100°C) and PEEK 1000 (40–170°C) for the first time over these temperature ranges. Derives both normal-incidence (longitudinal) and oblique-incidence (shear, mode-converted) measurements from the same setup.

## Unique contribution
First complete acoustic characterisation of Rexolite and PEEK at elevated temperatures (to 180°C), filling a gap for harsh-environment NDE transducer design (nuclear plant safety, oil/gas extraction). Uses cooking oil bath to reach temperatures beyond water's practical range. Identifies glass-transition effects on acoustic properties (Rexolite: onset near 90°C/Tg=114°C; PEEK: strong transition near 150°C/Tg=143°C). Shear measurement via oblique incidence between first and second critical angles.

## Key concepts
- Oil bath immersion: hydrogenated coconut oil calibrated vs temperature for speed and attenuation
- Longitudinal measurement: normal incidence, differential arrival time vs reference (no sample)
- Shear measurement: oblique incidence between critical angles — mode-converted shear only
- Rexolite 1422 (crosslinked polystyrene): longitudinal c=2340 m/s, shear c=1140 m/s at room temp; stable to ~90°C
- PEEK 1000 (polyether ether ketone): longitudinal c=2555 m/s at 5 MHz, 37°C; strong glass transition near 150°C
- Glass transition signature: speed decreases more steeply, attenuation increases above Tg

## Methods / results / takeaways
- 2.3 MHz homemade high-temperature transducers; Panametrics 5900PR pulser; 1024-average acquisition
- Oil calibration: longitudinal speed = 292.7×10⁻³·T² − 3.419·T + 1465.7 m/s (T in °C)
- Rexolite 40–100°C: acoustic properties stable, consistent with supplier data at room temperature
- PEEK 40–170°C: speed decreases from ~2570 m/s (40°C) to ~2300 m/s (150°C); attenuation rises sharply above 150°C; semi-crystalline nature means thermal history affects results
- Limitation: shear attenuation values are upper bounds (beam deviation correction not exact)
- Practical use: design data for NDE transducer materials in nuclear and petrochemical applications
