## Overview
Compares twelve theoretical models for ultrasonic velocity in solid-in-solid particulate composites against experimental data from the literature. Models include multiple scattering, self-consistent, and incremental approaches. No single model works best in all cases, but self-consistent and incremental Foldy models perform best across the broadest range of systems.

## Unique contribution
Provides a systematic benchmark of twelve theoretical models against a wide range of solid-solid composite systems (iron/PMMA, CaCO3/polypropylene, W/gold, W/vinyl, hydroxyapatite/HDPE, etc.). Identifies models that fail to predict the velocity minimum and models suitable for high concentration.

## Key concepts
- Solid-solid composite ultrasonic velocity
- Multiple scattering models (Foldy, Waterman-Truell, Lloyd-Berry)
- Self-consistent models (Budiansky, Hill, Berryman, Sabina-Willis)

- Kuster-Toksoz model
- Hashin-Shtrikman bounds
- Particle volume fraction
- Scattering amplitudes
- Particulate composite acoustics
- Long-wavelength limit

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Twelve theoretical models tested on 15+ experimental datasets (longitudinal and shear velocities vs. volume fraction)
- Key finding: Foldy self-consistent model performs poorly and should be discarded
- Models failing at high concentration (>20%): simple Foldy (eq. 1), Devaney-Levine, Willis-Acton, Kuster-Toksoz group at high fraction
- Models valid for wide range: self-consistent (Sabina-Willis), incremental Foldy, Kerr model (dynamic Kuster-Toksoz)
- Critical test: velocity minimum at intermediate concentration for iron-in-PMMA — only 4 models reproduce this
- Self-consistent Foldy fails to converge when shear moduli of components are equal
- Dynamic models (Waterman-Truell, Kerr) needed for ka > 0.1; static models unreliable above this
- Segregated network composites (non-random distribution) not predicted by any model — require separate approach
- Conclusion: no universal model; for practical transducer backing and composite design, incremental or Kerr-model preferred
