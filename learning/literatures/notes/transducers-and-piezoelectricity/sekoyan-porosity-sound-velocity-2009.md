# Effect of the Porosity and Particle Size of Materials on Sound-Wave Velocity

- **Source:** Sekoyan porosity sound velocity 2009
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/12bbgJYMhkEmcbwUhm7kKm-a8BGDqlrFd/view
- **Type:** paper (Journal of Applied Mechanics and Technical Physics 50(4), 646–650, 2009)
- **Author/Year:** S. S. Sekoyan, V. R. Shlegel', S. S. Batsanov, et al. / 2009
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Measures longitudinal sound velocity CL in porous Zn–S pressed-powder samples (various densities) and KBr samples (various grinding durations, particle sizes, and pore contents). Uses two ultrasonic methods: (1) buffer-rod pulse transit method (5 MHz, 50 mm diameter samples), (2) direct pulse-echo on small disks (125 ns pulses). Shows that a porous body (λ >> pore size) behaves as a continuous medium with an intermediate sound velocity predictable by the Batsanov additive method.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates experimentally that pore content (vacuum, air, or liquid in pores) significantly affects ultrasound velocity even when the solid skeleton geometry is unchanged — proving that waves propagate through the whole medium, not just solid contacts. Shows that moisture removal from KBr pellets suppresses sound transmission completely (pore interfaces act as acoustic contacts). Provides experimental Λ-ratio CL/Cad ≈ 1.43 for Zn–S mixtures.

## Key concepts
- Additive method (Batsanov): 1/C = x/Cm + (1−x)/Ca where x is solid volume fraction; Cm = monolithic velocity, Ca = air velocity
- CL/Cad ≈ 1.43 for Zn–S (average above density ratio 0.755 = sphere packing density); CL/Cad ≈ 1.67 for KBr
- Sphere packing density 0.74 → establishment of rigid skeleton → onset of shear resistance
- Air in pores → CL higher than vacuum; toluene in pores (CL = 1328 m/s) → CL increases further
- Moisture: drying KBr pellet causes loss of sound transmission; re-humidification restores it (water bridges acoustic contacts)
- Grain grinding increases sound velocity (KBr): finer particles produce denser packing → higher rigidity

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Zn–S: CL increases from 975 m/s (ρ/ρ₀ = 0.708) to 2430 m/s (ρ/ρ₀ = 0.926); vs Cad from 856 to 1672 m/s
- Transverse velocity: CT = 1.233 km/s (Zn–S at ρ/ρ₀ = 0.9) from tangential disk resonance; bulk C₀ = 1.657 km/s
- KBr: CL increases with grinding time (0 → 4 h); impact compression sample has CL = 3.03 km/s
- KBr pore filling experiment: CL (air) = 1.999 → 2.132 km/s as toluene fills pores progressively
- Application: elastic moduli of porous ceramics, nanocrystalline materials, transducer backing characterisation
