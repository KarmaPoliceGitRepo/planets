## Overview
Comprehensive review and design guide for active piezoelectric polymer ultrasound transducers, specifically PVDF and P(VDF-TrFE). Covers seven design areas: materials selection, construction and packaging, materials characterisation and modelling, film thickness and active area design, electrode selection, backing material design, and front protection/matching layer design. Includes new derivations for optimum active area for maximum power transfer at resonance (extended to thin/shielded transducers) and a method for modelling non-linear pulser-receiver loading effects. Demonstrates a direct-deposition P(VDF-TrFE) on aluminium backing transducer achieving 37 dB insertion loss, 9.8–22 MHz bandwidth (71%). Mylar front layer improves bandwidth from 86% to 95%.

## Unique contribution
Systematically compares PVDF and P(VDF-TrFE) for transducer applications and identifies their unique challenges versus ceramics: low electromechanical coupling (k_t ≈ 0.2), high dielectric loss, low acoustic impedance (~4 MRayl), but wideband characteristics without backing. Derives optimum active area for maximum power transfer — not previously published for polymer transducers. Also discusses the impact of electrode thickness and material on parasitic resonances and broadband performance.

## Key concepts
- PVDF and P(VDF-TrFE) piezoelectric polymer properties
- Low coupling constant (k_t ≈ 0.2) and its implications
- Direct deposition of P(VDF-TrFE) onto aluminium backing (no adhesive layer)
- Active area optimisation for maximum power transfer at resonance
- Mylar quarter-wave matching layer for polymer transducers
- Modified Mason's model with dielectric loss (complex ε^S) and mechanical loss
- Electrode material effects on transducer performance
- Non-linear pulser-receiver loading model
- Backing material design for polymer films
- Immersion transducer construction and packaging

## Methods / results / takeaways
- P(VDF-TrFE) direct deposition on insulated Al backing: minimum insertion loss 37 dB; −6 dB bandwidth 9.8–22 MHz (71%)
- Mylar matching layer on P(VDF-TrFE) transducer: amplitude improvement 15% (+1.2 dB); bandwidth improvement from 86% to 95%
- Active area formula: optimum for maximum power at resonance depends on radiation resistance and electrical impedance; extended to thin/shielded geometry
- Low impedance of PVDF (~4 MRayl) means good match to water without matching layers; slight improvement possible with thin polymer layers
- Polymer films do not require heavy backing for broadband operation due to low k_t and low Z
