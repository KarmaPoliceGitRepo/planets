## Overview
Early systematic treatment of the acoustic impedance matching problem between solid piezoelectric transducers and gaseous media. Analyses single and multi-layer quarter-wavelength matching approaches, quantifying the theoretical transmission gain for various intermediate impedance values.

## Unique contribution
Provides foundational analysis of solid-to-gas ultrasonic matching at a time when the topic was largely unexplored. Establishes the theoretical framework and quantifies the impedance mismatch penalty (~82,500:1 for PZT to air) that later matching layer work aimed to overcome.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Theoretical analysis of plane-wave transmission through single and double quarter-wavelength matching layers
- PZT acoustic impedance ~30 MRayl; air ~0.000429 MRayl; ratio ~70,000:1 (intensity), pressure ratio ~264:1
- Without matching: pressure transmission coefficient ~0.008% through PZT-air interface
- Single quarter-wave layer at geometric mean impedance gives maximum theoretical transmission but intermediate impedance materials for this ratio (~0.113 MRayl) were unavailable in 1965
- Double-layer analysis shows potential for broader bandwidth matching
- Paper establishes the problem statement and theoretical bounds that motivated subsequent material development (aerogels, composites, etc.)
