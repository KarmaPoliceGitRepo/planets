## Overview
Theoretical and experimental study of tungsten-particle/Araldite-epoxy composite materials as ultrasonic transducer backing materials. Derives and validates models for acoustic velocity and attenuation as functions of tungsten volume fraction.

## Unique contribution
Shows that W/Araldite composites exhibit an initial velocity decrease then recovery as W volume fraction increases, explained by scattering theory. Establishes that approximately 60% W by volume is needed to match PZT acoustic impedance (~30 MRayl). Also characterises the frequency-dependent scattering loss.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- W particles in Araldite epoxy mixed at volume fractions 0–75%; specimens cast as cylindrical plugs
- Acoustic velocity: decreases initially as W fraction increases (mass loading effect), then increases at high W fractions (stiffness dominates) → non-monotonic
- Minimum velocity occurs around 20–30% W; above ~50% velocity rises significantly
- Attenuation: increases with W volume fraction and with frequency (scattering from W particles)
- Acoustic impedance: increases nearly linearly with W fraction; ~60% W needed to reach ~30 MRayl (PZT-match)
- Scattering contribution to attenuation follows f⁴ Rayleigh law at low frequency, transitions at higher f
- Model predictions (effective medium theory) agree well with measurements below particle resonance frequency
- Practical guidance: use 50–65% W by volume for PZT-matched backing; accept trade-off between impedance match and attenuation increase
