# The Design of Efficient Broad-Band Piezoelectric Transducers

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Presents a systematic design method for broadband piezoelectric thin-disk transducers using quarter-wave matching layers, founded on the KLM transmission line model. Shows that the piezoelectric element thickness must be accounted for in matching layer design. Derives criteria for optimal Gaussian-shaped passbands and demonstrates transducers achieving one-octave bandwidth with only 3.2 dB round-trip insertion loss.

## Unique contribution
Establishes the Desilets–Fraser–Kino matching layer impedance formula (Zc = Z0^(2/3) × ZL^(1/3) for a single layer, and extensions for two layers), derived from the KLM model rather than classical microwave quarter-wave theory. This corrected formula is now standard for ultrasonic transducer design and is cited in virtually every subsequent matching layer paper.

## Key concepts
- KLM transmission line model for transducer design
- Quarter-wave matching layer impedance optimisation
- Gaussian bandshape for optimal impulse response
- Electromechanical coupling coefficient importance
- PZT-5A with two-layer borosilicate glass and epoxy matching
- PZT-4 with tungsten-loaded epoxy backing
- Round-trip insertion loss
- Series inductance tuning

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Design based on KLM model; distinguishes from earlier Mason model which lacks transparent matching layer design guidance
- Key result: optimum single-layer matching impedance is Zc = Z0^(2/3) × ZL^(1/3), not the geometric mean Z0^(1/2) × ZL^(1/2) from microwave theory
- Two-layer design: ZC1 = Z0^(4/7) × ZL^(3/7), ZC2 = Z0^(1/7) × ZL^(6/7)
- High kt (electromechanical coupling) is critical for broad bandwidth
- PZT-5A transducer matched into water with glass + epoxy layers: predicted 65% 3-dB bandwidth, 3.2 dB insertion loss, confirmed experimentally
- Air-backed transducer with series inductance tuning achieves one-octave bandwidth with Gaussian impulse response
- PZT-4 with W-epoxy acoustic backing: good Gaussian bandshape, three half-cycle impulse response
