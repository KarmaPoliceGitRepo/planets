# Micromachined 1-3 Composites for Ultrasonic Air Transducers

- **Source:** Haller 1994 - micromachined 1-3 composites.pdf
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1_haller_file_id/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** M.I. Haller, B.T. Khuri-Yakub / 1994
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Describes fabrication of 1-3 piezoelectric composites using silicon micromachining techniques (reactive ion etching and plasma etching of PZT wafers) to create fine-scale pillar arrays filled with polymer. Targets high-frequency air-coupled applications where conventional dice-and-fill is impractical.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates micromachining as a fabrication route for 1-3 composites with pillar periodicities of 20–100 µm, enabling high-frequency (1–10 MHz) composite transducers not accessible with conventional sawing. The fine-scale structure also suppresses lateral modes.

## Key concepts
- Micromachined piezoelectric composites
- 1-3 connectivity
- Reactive ion etching (RIE)
- Plasma etching of PZT
- Fine-scale pillar array
- High-frequency transducers
- Lateral mode suppression
- MEMS fabrication
- Air-coupled ultrasonics

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PZT wafers etched using reactive ion etching to form pillar arrays with 20–100 µm pitch
- Pillars infiltrated with low-viscosity polymer (epoxy) to complete 1-3 structure
- Resulting composites showed impedance of 8–15 MRayl (vs. ~33 for bulk PZT), dependent on volume fraction
- High-frequency resonances achieved: 2–10 MHz range demonstrated
- Lateral resonance modes suppressed when pillar pitch << λ/2; micromachining enables this at MHz frequencies
- Electromechanical coupling kt comparable to bulk PZT (~0.45–0.50)
- Fabrication challenges: maintaining pillar integrity during etching; polymer infiltration at small scale
- Enables air-coupled NDE at higher frequencies (better resolution) than possible with conventional composite fabrication
