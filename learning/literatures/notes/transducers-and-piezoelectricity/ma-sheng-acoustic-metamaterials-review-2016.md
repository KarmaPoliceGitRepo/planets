# Acoustic Metamaterials: From Local Resonances to Broad Horizons

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Comprehensive review of acoustic metamaterials tracing the 15-year development from locally resonant sonic materials (Liu et al. 2000) to broad applications. Covers negative mass density, negative bulk modulus, double negativity, zero-index materials, transformation acoustics and cloaking, space-coiling and acoustic metasurfaces, superabsorption, and non-Hermitian / active metamaterials. Reviews elastic and mechanical metamaterials, graphene-inspired designs, and applications in noise control, imaging beyond diffraction limit, and acoustic wave manipulation.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive synthesis connecting the constitutive parameter framework (effective ρ and K, and their negative values) to diverse phononic functionalities. Introduces unified perspective: metamaterials arise from subwavelength resonances, making physical size much smaller than operating wavelength. Reviews three distinct loss pathways for absorption (friction/porous, resonance, membrane-type), and shows that impedance matching + critical coupling enables single-layer total absorption. Covers space-coiling as large phase delay for metasurface design.

## Key concepts
- Acoustic wave equation: ∂²p/∂t² = K∇²p/ρ; wave speed v = √(K/ρ)
- Negative ρ: from locally resonant heavy-core/soft-shell inclusions (Liu et al. 2000); monopole resonance
- Negative K: from Helmholtz resonators; dipole resonance
- Double negative: negative index n = −√(ρ/K) when both negative simultaneously
- Zero-index material (ZIM): ρ→0 or K→0; wavefront tunneling, phase-decoupled propagation
- Transformation acoustics: coordinate-transformation-mapped density tensor; cloaking device possible but requires extreme anisotropy
- Space-coiling: labyrinthine passages force acoustic path l >> exterior dimension a; phase delay Δφ = k₀l >> k₀a
- Acoustic metasurface: subwavelength-thin phase array; phase shifts 0–2π; generates non-specular reflection/refraction
- Superabsorption: impedance-matched membrane-type resonators; near-perfect absorption at subwavelength thickness
- Non-Hermitian / PT-symmetric: parity-time symmetry → unidirectional wave transport, exceptional points
- Elastic/mechanical metamaterials: negative mass density for elastic waves; pentamode materials; topological edge states

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Review covers ~150+ references spanning 2000–2016
- Experimental demonstrations of cloaking in water (Zhang et al.), near-zero index, and phase gradient metasurfaces
- Absorption: membrane-type (coated plate + ring mass) achieves ~100% at a single frequency; multi-cell design for broadband
- Gradient index: lens focusing below diffraction limit using resonant units with spatially varying resonant frequency
- Ongoing challenges: narrow-band character of resonance-based metamaterials; loss associated with resonance; 3D fabrication
- Application: noise control, architectural acoustics, acoustic holography, non-destructive testing, underwater sonar
