# Broadband Piezoelectric Transducer Using PVDF

## Overview
Applies KLM equivalent circuit modelling to the design of a broadband PVDF ultrasonic probe intended for NDE applications. The probe uses a Perspex backing and polystyrene shoe (matching layer) to optimize frequency response over 2–15 MHz.

## Unique contribution
One of the earlier systematic applications of the KLM model to PVDF (rather than PZT) probe design. Demonstrates that the low acoustic impedance of PVDF (~2.88 kgm⁻²s⁻¹ × 10⁶) and low Q allow very broad bandwidth without complex matching, at the cost of ~15 dB lower sensitivity than PZT.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PVDF acoustic impedance ZA = 2.88 × 10⁶ kgm⁻²s⁻¹ (2.88 MRayl); close to water/tissue, far below PZT (~30 MRayl)
- KLM model used to predict frequency response for different backing and front-face loading conditions
- Perspex (acrylic) backing with ZA ~3.2 MRayl provides reasonable damping without severe sensitivity loss
- Polystyrene shoe (ZA ~2.5 MRayl) used as front matching layer for water/tissue coupling
- Measured bandwidth 2–15 MHz (-6 dB) — approximately octave bandwidth from a simple geometry
- Sensitivity ~15 dB lower than equivalent PZT probe; acceptable for pulse-echo NDE where dynamic range is sufficient
- PVDF probe particularly useful for wideband spectral analysis of flaws and material characterisation
- Confirms KLM model predictions match measured responses well for PVDF
