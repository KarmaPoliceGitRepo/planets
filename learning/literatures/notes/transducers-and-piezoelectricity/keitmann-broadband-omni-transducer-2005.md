# Development of Broadband Omni-directional Transducers for NDE Applications

## Overview
Develops broadband (>50% bandwidth) omnidirectional (>120° opening angle) piezoelectric ultrasound transducers for imaging metallic surfaces in a water-oil emulsion under high pressure. Uses hemispherical PZT elements with two-layer matching (aluminium + perspex) and W/epoxy backing. Transducers withstand up to 400 bar static pressure.

## Unique contribution
Combines omni-directional spatial characteristics from hemispherical PZT elements with broadband matching layer design and high-pressure resistance (500 bar via complete epoxy encapsulation). Provides a recipe for building broadband omnidirectional transducers using standard materials.

## Key concepts
- Hemispherical PZT element
- Omni-directional transducer
- Broadband ultrasound
- Two-layer matching (Al + Perspex)
- W/epoxy backing
- High-pressure resistance (400 bar)
- Schlieren imaging
- NDE hydroforming inspection

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PZT hemispheres (12–20 mm diameter, 1–2 mm thick) from Ferroperm PZ26; convex face used as emitting aperture → spherical waves
- Backing: epoxy L285/H287 + 65% W powder; ZA = 16 MRayl; attenuation α(2 MHz) = 28 dB/cm
- Two-layer matching: aluminium (ZM1 = 17 MRayl, L1 = 0.24λ) + perspex (ZM2 = 3.2 MRayl, L2 = 0.28λ)
- Planar transducer bandwidth: 1.6 MHz at 2.5 MHz centre frequency (64–80% relative)
- Convex transducer: 1.0 MHz bandwidth at 1.2 MHz centre (83% relative); opening angle 160° (−6 dB)
- Pressure resistance: full housing filled with non-conductive epoxy → 400 bar withstand demonstrated
- Schlieren imaging confirms spherical wave generation from convex aperture
- Target application: ultrasonic imaging of sheet metal during high-pressure hydroforming
