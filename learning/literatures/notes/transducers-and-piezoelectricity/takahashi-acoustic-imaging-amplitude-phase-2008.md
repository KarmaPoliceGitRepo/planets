# Acoustic Imaging in Air Using Amplitude and Phase Information

## Overview
Demonstrates reflection-mode acoustic microscopy in air at 2.93 MHz using a P(VDF/TrFE) concave focused transducer. Combines amplitude and phase-difference imaging to achieve 20 µm lateral resolution and enhanced contrast for surface defect detection.

## Unique contribution
Shows that phase-difference imaging (comparing phase of reflected signal to reference) provides superior contrast for surface irregularities compared to amplitude-only imaging, particularly for features with similar reflectivity but different surface orientation.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Concave P(VDF/TrFE) transducer at 2.93 MHz; concave geometry provides focusing in air without acoustic lens
- System uses pulse-echo reflection mode; single transducer acts as both transmitter and receiver
- Amplitude image: standard reflectivity map of surface
- Phase-difference image: local phase deviation relative to plane reference — highlights surface slope and sub-surface features
- Lateral resolution: ~20 µm at 2.93 MHz focused geometry
- Two-way insertion loss in air path is large (~80 dB range); low-noise receiver with careful shielding required
- Results show defects invisible in amplitude image revealed by phase-difference contrast
- P(VDF/TrFE) chosen for high kt (~0.27) relative to plain PVDF and good film quality at thin gauges
- Applicable to non-contact inspection of delicate surfaces (e.g., semiconductor wafers, printed circuits)
