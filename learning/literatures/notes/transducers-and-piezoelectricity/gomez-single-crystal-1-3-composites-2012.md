## Overview
Designs, fabricates, and characterises air-coupled transducers (~1 MHz) using 1-3 connectivity PMN-PT single crystal piezocomposites. Compares performance to 1-3 PZT ceramic fiber composites. Both achieve similar peak sensitivities (-23 dB) but PMN-PT single crystal composites offer wider bandwidth (up to 89%).

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that PMN-PT 1-3 single crystal composites provide significantly broader frequency bandwidth than PZT 1-3 fiber composites for air-coupled applications while maintaining similar sensitivity, leveraging the higher electromechanical coupling of single crystals near the morphotropic phase boundary.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PMN-PT single crystal 1-3 composites fabricated by dice-and-fill: 25–50% volume fraction, ZA = 9.97–15.27 MRayl, fc ≈ 0.8–1.5 MHz
- PZT ceramic fiber composites: ZA = 7.3–18.4 MRayl for comparison; all ~1 MHz centre frequency
- Transducer design: external λ/4 polymeric membrane (ZA ~0.05 MRayl); 2–4 intermediate matching layers cascaded according to geometric mean formula
- Optimal: three matching layers tuned to (fm+fn)/2 for maximum bandwidth; two layers tuned to fm for maximum sensitivity
- Best pulse-echo sensitivity: −32 dB for both SC and CF composites (three matching layers tuned to fm)
- Best pulse-echo bandwidth: 89% for SC-45% with three matching layers tuned to (fm+fn)/2
- Best through-transmission sensitivity: −22 dB (CF-65%) and −23 dB (SC-45%)
- Best through-transmission bandwidth: 86% for SC-45% composite
- Air attenuation formula used: α [Np/m] = 1.84 × 10⁻¹⁰ × f² [Hz] (consistent with Bass 1995)
- Adding a fourth matching layer does not improve performance consistently — fabrication tolerances increase
