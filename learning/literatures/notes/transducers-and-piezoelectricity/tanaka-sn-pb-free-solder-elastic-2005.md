# Elastic Properties of Sn-Based Pb-Free Solder Alloys Determined by Ultrasonic Pulse Echo Method

- **Source:** Tanaka Sn Pb-free solder elastic 2005
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1QiQYRynpCgIirR7RGy7D313rcFUc3QUc/view
- **Type:** paper (Materials Transactions 46(6), 2005)
- **Author/Year:** H. Tanaka, L. F. Qun, O. Munekata, T. Taguchi, T. Narita / 2005
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Measures Young's modulus E and Poisson's ratio ν of Sn-based lead-free solders (Sn–3.5Ag, Sn–58Bi, Sn–9Zn) and Sn–37Pb reference using ultrasonic pulse echo method, over −50 to 100°C. Uses 1.6 MHz longitudinal and 5 MHz shear PZT contact transducers with glycerin couplant. Derives E and ν from longitudinal (Vl) and shear (Vs) wave velocities and density. Provides polynomial temperature dependence equations.

## Unique contribution
First systematic temperature-dependent elastic property database for Pb-free solders from −50 to 100°C using ultrasonic method. Identifies anomalous rapid decrease of Young's modulus and rapid increase of Poisson's ratio for Sn–58Bi above 80°C, correlated with internal friction data suggesting atomic motion along dislocations/grain boundaries. Provides polynomial fits for thermomechanical simulation use.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic pulse echo: E = f(ρ, Vl, Vs); ν = f(Vl, Vs) for isotropic materials
- Sn–37Pb: E decreases, ν increases linearly with temperature
- Sn–9Zn: similar trend to Sn–37Pb; E(T) = 55.1 − 7.34×10⁻²T + 3.69×10⁻⁵T² GPa
- Sn–37Pb: E(T) = 42.2 − 4.93×10⁻²T − 4.64×10⁻⁵T² GPa
- Sn–58Bi: anomalous Young's modulus drop above 80°C; internal friction (shear) increases rapidly above 80°C
- Room temperature values (25°C): Sn–3.5Ag E = 51 GPa, ν = 0.36; Sn–9Zn E = 53.3 GPa, ν = 0.35; Sn–37Pb E = 40.9 GPa, ν = 0.37

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Toshiba Tungaloy UMS-L system; 1.6 MHz longitudinal + 5 MHz shear sensors; glycerin coupling
- Sample dimensions: 30 mm diameter × 10 mm height; Archimedes density measurement
- Room-temperature results agree with Lau & Pao literature data within a few percent
- Internal friction for Sn–58Bi: shear wave friction increases rapidly above 80°C → dislocation/grain boundary motion
- Application: mechanical property data for thermomechanical simulation of solder joint reliability
- Relevance to transducer work: ultrasonic pulse echo as standard technique for elastic constants of structural materials
