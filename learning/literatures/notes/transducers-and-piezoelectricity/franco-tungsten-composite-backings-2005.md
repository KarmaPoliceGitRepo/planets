## Overview
Experimental characterisation of tungsten/epoxy and tungsten/polyurethane composite materials for ultrasonic transducer backing applications. Measures acoustic velocity, impedance, and attenuation across a range of W volume fractions for both binder systems.

## Unique contribution
Directly compares epoxy and polyurethane matrices for W composites, finding that polyurethane composites are much more highly attenuating (advantageous for backing), while epoxy composites achieve higher impedance but lower attenuation per unit thickness.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- W/Epoxy: maximum impedance ~8 MRayl at 29.5% W by volume; increasing beyond this causes cracking and processing difficulties
- W/Epoxy at 56–60% W required to reach ~30 MRayl for PZT matching (consistent with Sayers 1984)
- W/Polyurethane: lower achievable impedance but very high attenuation coefficient — valuable for absorbing backing with less emphasis on impedance match
- Attenuation in W/PU composites exceeds W/Epoxy by factor ~3–5× at same volume fraction
- Both composites show frequency-dependent attenuation; PU composites show stronger frequency dependence
- Practical recommendation: use W/Epoxy for high-impedance backing where reflection suppression from acoustic match is primary; use W/PU when high attenuation within thin layers is priority
- Fabrication notes: PU composites easier to process (lower viscosity binder); epoxy requires degassing to avoid voids
