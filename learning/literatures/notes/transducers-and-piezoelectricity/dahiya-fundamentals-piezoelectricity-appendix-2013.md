## Overview
Appendix A from "Robotic Tactile Sensing" (Dahiya & Valle, Springer 2013). Provides an introductory tutorial on the fundamentals of piezoelectricity including historical background, polarisation mechanisms in dielectric/ferroelectric/piezoelectric materials, and the basic mathematical formulation of direct and converse piezoelectric effects. Covers PVDF and PVDF-TrFE polymer piezoelectrics as well as conventional ceramics. Targeted at readers who need background before using piezoelectrics in sensors and transducers.

## Unique contribution
Accessible tutorial covering the historical milestones from the Curie brothers (1880) through to Lippmann's converse effect prediction (1881) and crystal resonator applications, with simplified but mathematically grounded treatment of the piezoelectric constitutive equations.

## Key concepts
- Direct and converse piezoelectric effect
- Polarisation mechanisms (dielectric, ferroelectric, piezoelectric)
- PVDF and PVDF-TrFE polymer piezoelectrics
- Piezoelectric constitutive equations (d, g, e, h forms)
- Crystal symmetry and piezoelectric activity
- Historical development (Curie brothers 1880, Lippmann 1881)
- Piezoelectric ceramics and single crystals
- Sensors and actuators based on piezoelectricity
