# Modifying the Acoustic Impedance of Polyurea-Based Composites

- **Source:** Nantasetphong 2013 polyurea composites
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1rqWWuDcOJF2uKQ5RCxlnt0W1Zu-K9cNj/view
- **Type:** paper (conference, SPIE)
- **Author/Year:** Wiroj Nantasetphong, Alireza V. Amirkhizi, Zhanzhan Jia, Sia Nemat-Nasser / 2013
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops polyurea/phenolic-microballoon composites whose acoustic impedance can be tuned toward water impedance (1.5 MRayl) by varying hollow particle volume fraction. Uses ultrasonic measurements (1 MHz, contact, two-thickness method) to determine longitudinal wave speed and attenuation, calculates storage/loss moduli, and computes acoustic impedance. Validates against an analytical model for dilute, randomly distributed hollow spherical inclusions.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates a practical route to custom-impedance matching materials using a military-grade elastomer (polyurea, used for blast protection) as the matrix — dual-use potential for acoustic impedance matching layers and stealth/camouflage applications. Confirms that adding phenolic microballoons to polyurea (Z_pure ≈ 1.65 MRayl > water) decreases impedance predictably toward water Z.

## Key concepts
- Polyurea matrix with hollow phenolic microballoon inclusions
- Acoustic impedance tuning by particle volume fraction
- Dilute-randomly-distributed hollow spherical inclusions model (Hashin-type)
- Two-thickness transmission method for wave speed and attenuation at 1 MHz
- Storage and loss longitudinal moduli L', L'' from wave speed and attenuation
- Archimedes principle for composite density measurement
- Air-coupled / water-coupled NDE context

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Matrix: polyurea from Isonate 143L + Versalink P-1000 (density 1.1 g/cm³)
- Filler: hollow phenolic microballoon particles (shell density 1.28 g/cm³)
- Measurement: Matec TB-1000 toneburst at 1 MHz, two Panametrics V103 transducers, two sample thicknesses; Archimedes density measurement
- Modelling: dilute randomly distributed hollow spherical inclusions — effective moduli from energy change due to insertion of inhomogeneities
- Results: impedance decreases monotonically as microballoon fraction increases; calculated and measured densities agree well
- Key limitation: dilute assumption — model accuracy decreases at high volume fractions where particle interactions become significant
- Application context: sonar impedance matching/stealth for military structures submerged in water; air-coupled NDE
