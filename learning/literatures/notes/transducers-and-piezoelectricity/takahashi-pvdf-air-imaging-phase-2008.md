# A System Using a Piezoelectric Polymer Transducer for Acoustic Imaging of Amplitude and Phase Differences in Air

- **Source:** Takahashi PVDF air imaging phase 2008
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1GXBrHwCFuUKWTN_0at05wZVTe6-AI5tO/view
- **Type:** paper (Acoustical Science and Technology 29(6), 394–396, 2008)
- **Author/Year:** S. Takahashi, H. Ohigashi / 2008
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Short communication describing a single-transducer pulse-echo P(VDF/TrFE) acoustic imaging system operating in air at 2–3 MHz, producing both amplitude and phase-difference images. Three P(VDF/TrFE) films (95 μm, parallel-connected) form a semi-spherical concave transducer (140° aperture, f = 10 mm). Shows amplitude image of commemorative coin (150 μm resolution) and phase image of 2000-yen currency bill (sub-μm height sensitivity). Also shows 2.9 MHz phase image of ROM IC chip with 20-μm bonding wire resolution.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration of single-transducer (pulse-echo) air-coupled P(VDF/TrFE) imaging at MHz frequencies, eliminating the noise problem of the earlier two-transducer (separation-type) setup. Phase-difference imaging enables surface height detection with sub-micrometer sensitivity in air. Proves P(VDF/TrFE) is viable for high-resolution air-coupled acoustic microscopy at 2–3 MHz.

## Key concepts
- P(VDF/TrFE) 75 mol% VDF: kt = 0.3 (highest among piezoelectric polymers), Z_acoustic << PZT
- Air-coupled imaging: air attenuation ∝ f² (3 orders of magnitude > water at 1 MHz)
- Multilayer parallel stack: 3 × 95 μm films; electric impedance matching improved by 3²= 9
- Two-way loss: 96 dB at 1.87 MHz and 103 dB at 2.93 MHz (including air path losses)
- Excess loss ≈ 20 dB over Mason circuit prediction (attributed to insufficient inter-layer bonding)
- Phase-difference imaging: detects surface height from phase shift; resolution < 1 μm

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Concave transducer: 140° aperture, f = 10 mm, three 95 μm P(VDF/TrFE) films, parallel-connected
- 3 MHz transducer: two films serial type, 65° aperture, f = 7.5 mm
- Amplitude image: coin at 2 MHz, 150 μm transverse resolution (theory 115 μm; limited by bonding + stepping)
- Phase image: 2000-yen bill printed surface (10×25 mm) at 1.83 MHz; height = 22 μm confirmed by interference microscope
- ROM IC chip phase image: 20 μm bonding wires resolved at 2.9 MHz with 20 μm/step scanning
- Through-transmission measurement: silicone rubber (3 mm thick) at 2 MHz; c = 951 m/s, α = 4.5 dB/cm
- Future target: insertion loss < 60 dB by improved bonding; quarter-wavelength silicone rubber matching layer (+4 dB)
