## Overview
Materials database compiled by the Centre for Ultrasonic Engineering (CUE), University of Strathclyde. Tabulates longitudinal and shear velocity, density, Poisson's ratio, Young's modulus, acoustic impedance, elastic constants (c11, c44, s11, s44), and longitudinal and shear attenuation for a wide range of epoxy, silicone rubber, and metal samples.

## Unique contribution
Provides frequency-dependent (0.25–5 MHz) acoustic property data for a comprehensive set of engineering epoxies (Araldite formulations, Stycast, Duralco), silicone rubbers (RTV664, Ward Bekker), and Aqualene — all measured by immersion at the same facility, making them internally consistent and directly comparable.

## Key concepts
- Epoxy acoustic properties (Araldite, Stycast, Vantico)
- Silicone rubber acoustic properties
- Aqualene acoustic coupler
- Frequency-dependent velocity and attenuation
- Acoustic impedance of matching layer materials
- Elastic constants (c11, c44, s11, s44)
- CUE Strathclyde database
- Immersion measurement

## Methods / results / takeaways
- All measurements by immersion; velocity from phase; attenuation from insertion loss
- Hard Set epoxy HY1300/CY1301: vl ≈ 2500–2560 m/s; ZA ≈ 2.86–2.92 MRayl; α(2.25 MHz) = 608 dB/m
- Araldite 20/20: vl ≈ 2490–2577 m/s; ZA ≈ 2.81–2.91 MRayl; frequency-dependent attenuation
- Araldite MY750/HY956EN: vl ≈ 2629–2686 m/s; ZA ≈ 3.01–3.08 MRayl — slightly higher impedance
- Alumina-filled Araldite: ZA up to 5.6–8.2 MRayl (50–73 wt% alumina) — useful for intermediate matching layers
- Stycast 2850-FT (alumina-filled): vl ≈ 3400 m/s; ZA ≈ 7.7–8.2 MRayl — high impedance backing/matching material
- RTV664 silicone: vl ≈ 1000 m/s; ZA ≈ 1.28 MRayl; highly attenuating (very high shear losses)
- Aqualene: vl ≈ 1595 m/s; ZA ≈ 1.47–1.50 MRayl; moderate attenuation — useful as acoustic coupler
- Perspex: vl ≈ 2705–2775 m/s; ZA ≈ 3.20–3.28 MRayl; low attenuation ~130–350 dB/m longitudinal
- Data provided at 0.5, 1.0, 2.25 (and sometimes 5.0) MHz to show frequency dependence
