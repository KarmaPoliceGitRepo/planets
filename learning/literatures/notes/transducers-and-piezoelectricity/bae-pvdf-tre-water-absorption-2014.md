## Overview
Investigates how water absorption affects the mechanical properties of P(VDF-TrFE) copolymer films. Water molecules intercalate into the polymer crystal structure, producing significant changes in Young's modulus, tensile strength, and elongation at break.

## Unique contribution
Quantifies the mechanical property changes in P(VDF-TrFE) due to water exposure and demonstrates that these changes are reversible upon drying. Provides data relevant to transducer longevity and calibration stability when deployed in humid or aqueous environments.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- P(VDF-TrFE) films immersed in water for varying durations; mass uptake measured gravimetrically
- Young's modulus increases by ~9% after full water saturation (stiffer film)
- Tensile strength increases by ~37% in saturated condition
- Elongation at break increases by ~66% (more ductile)
- Water molecules intercalate into amorphous regions and between crystalline lamellae without disrupting β-phase crystallinity
- Changes are reversible: properties return to baseline values after drying at room temperature
- Piezoelectric d33 and kt coefficients not significantly degraded by water absorption at room temperature
- Practical implication: P(VDF-TrFE) transducers used in humid or liquid environments should be characterised wet; re-calibration after drying may be needed
