# Solution-Cast P(VDF/TrFE) Transducers for High-Frequency Air Imaging

- **Source:** Takahashi 2012 - solution casting method.pdf

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Reports solution-cast P(VDF/TrFE) transducers operating at 4, 6, and 10 MHz in air for high-resolution reflection imaging. The casting method allows custom thicknesses and concave shapes not achievable with commercial stretched films.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that solution-cast P(VDF/TrFE) achieves kt = 0.27 and competitive insertion loss performance (57 dB at 6.2 MHz), enabling sub-100 µm resolution air-coupled imaging. Provides the empirical air absorption formula α = 1.6 × 10⁻¹² × f² dB/cm applicable to these high-frequency experiments.

## Key concepts
- P(VDF/TrFE) solution casting
- Air-coupled ultrasound
- High-frequency transducer (4–10 MHz)
- kt electromechanical coupling
- Insertion loss
- Air absorption coefficient
- α = 1.6 × 10⁻¹² × f² dB/cm
- Sub-100 µm resolution
- Thin-film transducer fabrication

## Methods / results / takeaways
- P(VDF/TrFE) dissolved in methyl ethyl ketone (MEK); cast onto curved metal mandrel; dried, poled and electroded
- Film thickness controlled by solution concentration and spin/dip parameters; target thicknesses ~13–55 µm for 4–10 MHz operation
- kt = 0.27 achieved; consistent with VDF/TrFE 75/25 molar composition
- Insertion loss at 6.2 MHz: 57 dB (round trip in air, focused geometry)
- Air absorption formula: α [dB/cm] = 1.6 × 10⁻¹² × f² [Hz] — empirically confirmed at 25°C, dry air
- Lateral resolution: ~50–80 µm at 6 MHz and 10 MHz with concave focusing
- 4, 6, 10 MHz transducers all demonstrated; 10 MHz used for finest surface feature imaging
- Repeatability of casting confirmed across multiple batches; variation in kt < 5%
- Applications: non-contact surface inspection, semiconductor wafer quality control
