# Modeling and Characterization of Electrostrictive Ceramics

- **Source:** Sherrit 1998 electrostrictive ceramics
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1GiQKonVvbXv_FbGu2rYLWi1cE9OYEXzd/view
- **Type:** paper (conference, SPIE Smart Structures)
- **Author/Year:** S. Sherrit, S. P. Leary, B. P. Dolgin, Y. Bar-Cohen / 1998
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops a nonlinear theoretical framework for electrostrictive ceramics (PMN-PT-La 0.9/0.1/1%) based on Taylor series expansion of the elastic Gibbs function to third and higher order in polarisation. Shows that a DC bias field induces effective linear piezoelectricity (d₃₃ proportional to bias E), and that higher-order terms produce saturation of d₃₃ and permittivity at large fields. Also identifies that remanent polarisation D₀ creates a switching piezoelectric-like component in the S–D hysteresis data.

## Unique contribution
Provides a thermodynamic derivation of why PMN-PT behaves linearly (electrostrictively) at low fields but saturates at high fields, and why a bias field induces an apparent piezoelectric response. Demonstrates that the remanent D₀ contribution (if present) generates an odd-function S–E component that mimics conventional piezoelectricity — important for separating true electrostrictive response from ferroelectric remnant effects. Connects Mason nonlinear model to measured S–D and D–E curves with quantitative fit.

## Key concepts
- Electrostriction: S = Q·D² (quadratic strain vs polarisation); not piezoelectric
- Elastic Gibbs function Taylor expansion to 3rd+ order: G = G₀ + (1/2χ)D² + (1/2s)T² + Q·D²·T + higher
- Bias-field-induced piezoelectricity: d₃₃(E_bias) ≈ 2ε·Q·E_bias (linear in E_bias at small fields)
- Saturation: higher-order permittivity terms reduce d₃₃ at large E
- Remanent polarisation D₀: induces switching odd-function component in S–D; mimics piezoelectric hysteresis
- PMN-PT-La (0.9PMN / 0.1PT / 1% La): relaxor ferroelectric near morphotropic phase boundary
- Mason nonlinear model extended to electrostrictive regime

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PMN-PT-La composition selected for near-zero hysteresis at low fields (relaxor behaviour)
- S–D curves: quadratic at low D; become linear with slope ≈ 2Q·D₀ when remanent D₀ ≠ 0
- d₃₃ vs E_bias: linear region up to ~0.3 MV/m, saturation above; agrees with truncated Taylor series
- Permittivity vs bias field: peaks near E=0, decreases with applied field — higher-order term signature
- Practical implication: for actuator applications, bias field selects operating point; linearity range limited to low-field regime
- Thermal depoling resets D₀ to zero; subsequent cycling re-establishes remanent component
- Comparison with PZT: PMN-PT has larger electrostrictive coefficient Q but no spontaneous polarisation without bias
