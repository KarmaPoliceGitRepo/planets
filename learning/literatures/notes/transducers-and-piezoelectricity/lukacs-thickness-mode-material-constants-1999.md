# Thickness Mode Material Constants of Piezoelectric Films

- **Source:** Lukacs - thickness mode material constants 1999.pdf
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1_lukacs_file_id/view

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Presents a systematic method for extracting the thickness-mode piezoelectric material constants of thin piezoelectric films and bulk ceramics from electrical impedance measurements combined with Levenberg-Marquardt nonlinear regression. The extracted constants include kt, ZA, and loss parameters.

## Unique contribution
Provides a rigorous, self-consistent extraction procedure for thickness-mode constants using impedance spectroscopy and iterative fitting. Applicable to both traditional PZT ceramics and novel materials including sol-gel deposited films, providing a path to material characterization without destructive testing.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Electrical impedance measured as a function of frequency across the thickness resonance
- KLM or Mason equivalent circuit used as the fitting model; parameters are kt, ZA, series/parallel resonance frequencies, and mechanical/dielectric Q
- Levenberg-Marquardt (nonlinear least squares) algorithm minimizes residual between measured and modeled impedance
- Demonstrated on PZT-5H, PZT-5A, and sol-gel PZT films
- Extracted kt values: ~0.47 for PZT-5H, consistent with literature
- Method can resolve small differences in coupling efficiency between samples, useful for process optimization
- Loss parameters (mechanical Q, dielectric loss tan δ) extracted simultaneously, important for transducer efficiency
- Applicable to frequency range 1–50 MHz depending on film thickness
