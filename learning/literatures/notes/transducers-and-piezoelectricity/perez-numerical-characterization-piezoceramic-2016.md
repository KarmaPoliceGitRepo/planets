## Overview
Review paper on numerical characterisation of 6mm symmetry piezoceramic materials (e.g. PZT) using electrical impedance resonance curves combined with Finite Element Method (FEM) and iterative optimisation. Explains that a fully lossy 6mm material requires 20 independent complex material constants (5 elastic + 3 piezoelectric + 2 dielectric). Reviews recent advances where FEM models are iteratively fitted to experimental impedance curves to extract all material constants. Discusses validation strategies and provides worked examples for common PZT ceramics.

## Unique contribution
Reviews the combined FEM + iterative optimisation approach for full complex material constant determination of piezoceramics — going beyond the simplified analytical resonance methods (IEEE standard, Smits, Sherrit series) to handle all 20 complex parameters simultaneously. Addresses the challenge that theoretical Q values from single-mode analysis are insufficient for accurate transducer modelling.

## Key concepts
- 6mm symmetry class piezoceramics (PZT)
