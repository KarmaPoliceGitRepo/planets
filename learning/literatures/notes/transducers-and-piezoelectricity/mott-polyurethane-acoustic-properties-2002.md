## Overview
Reports sound speed and attenuation measurements in DeSoto PR1547 polyurethane rubber at 12.5–75 kHz and 3.9–33.6 °C, combined with dynamic mechanical shear data. Combines acoustical and mechanical measurements to derive the complex dynamic bulk modulus. Also documents practical rules-of-thumb for acoustic measurements in small tanks.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first documented derivation of the bulk modulus master curve for PR1547 polyurethane by combining high-frequency acoustic data with low-frequency mechanical data. Establishes practical working rules for acoustic measurements in small immersion tanks using large-area PVDF hydrophones.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Sound speed at 43 kHz: 1654 m/s at 3.9 °C, 1497 m/s at 33.6 °C (strong temperature dependence)
- Attenuation: linear with frequency (dB/cm ∝ f); slope ~105 × 10⁻⁶ Np/m/s at 3.9 °C, decreasing with temperature
- Kramers-Kronig relation verified: consistent internal relationship between measured velocity dispersion and attenuation
- Dynamic shear modulus measured 10⁻⁴–2 Hz, −36 to 34 °C; WLF equation parameters: C1=7.39, C2=119.6, T0=34°C
- Bulk modulus B ~ 100× shear modulus G (nearly incompressible); shift factors for bulk and shear deformations agree
- PVDF hydrophone (three-layer, dual bimorph) used as large-area directional receiver for low-noise measurements
- Tank size rules: sample must be ≥4λ wide for valid insertion loss; 3λ with directional transducers
- Low-frequency limit for time window: 12 kHz for 250 µs window (three-wave rule)
- Applications: material characterisation, underwater acoustic panel evaluation
