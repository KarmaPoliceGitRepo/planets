# Properties and Characteristics of P(VDF/TrFE) Transducers Manufactured by a Solution Casting Method for Use in the MHz-Range Ultrasound in Air

- **Source:** Takahashi PVDF TrFE casting air transducer 2012
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/16DQ6qjvblWZxfRppUZ38DFPgtDSiY_Nx/view
- **Type:** paper (Ultrasonics 52, 422–426, 2012)
- **Author/Year:** S. Takahashi / 2012
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops P(VDF/TrFE) air-coupled transducers at 4, 6, and 10 MHz by casting polymer solution directly onto the backing aluminum plate — eliminating the adhesive layer that caused 20 dB excess loss in previous bonded transducers. Characterises material properties (kt = 0.27 at 6 MHz), measures air absorption loss from 1–10 MHz, and demonstrates 6 MHz air-coupled acoustic imaging of a ROM IC chip (20 μm lateral resolution) using a bonded concave transducer.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration that P(VDF/TrFE) cast directly on metal electrode achieves insertion loss matching Mason circuit predictions (57 dB at 6.2 MHz), eliminating the 20 dB bonding loss penalty. Provides empirical air absorption law α = 1.6×10⁻¹² f² dB/cm (f in Hz, T = 25°C, P = 996 hPa) validated from 1–10 MHz. Extends practical air-coupled P(VDF/TrFE) imaging to 6 MHz in air, equivalent to ~30 MHz in water for resolution.

## Key concepts
- Solution casting method: P(VDF/TrFE) dissolved in DMF (20 wt%), cast on 0.3 mm Al plate, annealed at 145°C/2h
- Adhesive problem: 6 μm epoxy layer → resonance shift + 20 dB insertion loss increase (from bonding issues, air bubbles, kissing bonds)
- Air absorption law: α = 1.6×10⁻¹² f² dB/cm at 25°C, 996 hPa (matches Bass/Bond theoretical formulas)
- At 10 MHz in air: α ≈ 163 dB/cm (extreme attenuation limits practical range to a few mm)
- P(VDF/TrFE) parameters: kt = 0.27, ρ = 1.89 g/cm³, c_L = 2400 m/s, ε/ε₀ = 4.5, tan δ_e = 0.1, tan δ_m = 0.05
- Lateral resolution in air: D = 1.2λf_focal/D_aperture; at 6 MHz equivalent to ~30 MHz in water

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Cast film thickness ≈ 98 μm; poling field Ep ≥ 75 MV/m; 1/4λ Al backing 300 μm thick
- Mason circuit fitting: kt = 0.27 (slightly below the theoretical 0.30 for high-crystallinity film)
- Planar transducer insertion loss at 6.2 MHz: 57 dB (matches Mason prediction; vs 80 dB for bonded version)
- Concave transducer (f = 6.3 mm, 2θ = 152°): ROM IC chip image with 20 μm bonding wires resolved at 6 MHz
- Image scanning: 20 μm/step; D_theoretical = 36 μm
- Air absorption table: 2 MHz → 6.7 dB/cm; 4 MHz → 27 dB/cm; 6 MHz → 59 dB/cm; 10 MHz → 163 dB/cm
- kt improvement to 0.3 via higher crystallinity P(VDF/TrFE) → projected insertion loss ≤ 52 dB
