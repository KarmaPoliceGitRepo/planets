## Overview
Short communication showing that particle resonance in a random particulate composite leads to anomalous dispersion and attenuation near the resonance frequency of the inclusion. Uses Waterman-Truell multiple scattering theory for a dilute suspension of elastic spheres in an elastic matrix. Shows that near the natural resonance frequency of individual inclusions, the effective wave speed can drop sharply (anomalous dispersion) and attenuation peaks. This is an early paper connecting inclusion resonance to metamaterial-like behaviour.

## Key concepts
- Material system: dilute suspension of elastic spherical inclusions in elastic matrix
- Theory: Waterman-Truell multiple scattering for effective medium
- Key result: near resonance frequency of inclusions → anomalous dispersion (wave speed drops) + strong attenuation peak
- Physical mechanism: same as locally resonant acoustic metamaterials (Ma & Sheng 2016) — inclusion resonance couples to propagating wave
- Relevance: cited as precursor to acoustic metamaterial locally resonant structures (Liu et al. 2000, Ma & Sheng 2016)
- Note: this is an early, short mechanics communication — full analytical derivation required reading physical paper
