## Overview
NPL technical guide on measurement and analysis of ferroelectric hysteresis loops (P-E and S-E curves) for piezoelectric ceramics. Developed under the DTI CAM7 programme. Covers hardware implementation for polarisation-field (P-E) and strain-field (S-E) measurements at high driving voltages, software algorithms for analysis, and practical guidance for setting up a hysteresis loop measurement facility. Aims to support characterisation under application-relevant high-stress/high-field conditions.

## Unique contribution
Practical NPL reference guide (not a research paper) for establishing a high-voltage P-S-E measurement system. Provides example hardware implementations, choice of HV amplifiers, displacement measurement options (fibre optic probe, capacitance probe, laser interferometry, strain gauges), and analysis software. Relevant for characterising materials for high-power acoustic generation and high-load actuation beyond small-signal regime.

## Key concepts
- P-E loop measurement: polarisation vs electric field under high drive; reveals remanent polarisation and coercive field
- S-E loop measurement: strain vs electric field; butterfly hysteresis from irreversible domain wall motion
- High-field characterisation: relevant for high-power transducers and actuators operating beyond linear regime
- Domain wall contributions: irreversible 180° and 90° domain switching at high field
- Measurement systems: Sawyer-Tower circuit for P-E; fibre optic, capacitance, or interferometer for displacement
- DTI CAM7 programme context (UK National Measurement System)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Setup: HV power supply → amplifier → sample; P via charge integration; S via displacement sensor
- Key measurements: permittivity, dielectric loss, and piezoelectric displacement as function of drive voltage
- Analysis features: loop integration, harmonic analysis, effective material parameters under bias
- Software included (analysis features only, not instrument control)
- Context: characterising piezoelectric materials operated at ever-increasing stresses in devices
