# Ultrasonic Imaging Using Air-Coupled P(VDF/TrFE) Transducers at 2 MHz

- **Source:** Takahashi PVDF TrFE air imaging 2009
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1E-tAoENU6lPU1ytE046DI-ntWL4iF9wU/view
- **Type:** paper (Ultrasonics 49, 495–498, 2009)
- **Author/Year:** S. Takahashi, H. Ohigashi / 2009
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Full journal article (expanded from 2008 AST conference paper) describing the air-coupled P(VDF/TrFE) acoustic microscope at 2 MHz. Concave transducer composed of three 90 μm P(VDF/TrFE) films in alternating polarization parallel stack (electrode metallized on each film surface). Two-way transducer insertion loss = 80 dB at 1.83 MHz (20 dB above Mason circuit prediction). Shows amplitude image of coin (150 μm resolution), phase image of currency bill printed surface (22 μm height, sub-μm detection), and through-transmission measurement of silicone rubber (c = 951 m/s, α = 4.5 dB/cm at 2 MHz).

## Unique contribution
Full characterisation of the first air-coupled P(VDF/TrFE) transducer with measured insertion loss, Mason circuit comparison, and through-transmission capability. Demonstrates that the 20 dB excess loss (vs prediction) is from insufficient inter-film bonding. Establishes P(VDF/TrFE)'s unique advantage for air-coupled MHz-range imaging: Z ≈ 4.5 MRayl vs PZT ≈ 30 MRayl, so impedance mismatch loss is lower, and flexibility enables wide-aperture concave transducers.

## Key concepts
- P(VDF/TrFE) properties: kt = 0.3, ρ = 1.89 g/cm³, c = 2400 m/s, ε/ε₀ = 4.5, Z ≈ 4.5 MRayl
- Concave transducer: three 90 μm films, alternating polarity (parallel electrical), 140° aperture, f = 10 mm
- Improved electrical impedance matching: three-film parallel stack → 9× capacitance increase
- Two-way insertion loss (2TL): defined as output acoustic power / input electric power ratio
- Through-transmission mode: silicone rubber plate (3 mm); c = 951 m/s, α = 4.5 dB/cm at 2 MHz
- Phase resolution: ultrasound wavelength at 1.83 MHz in air = 0.190 mm → c_air = 348 m/s

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Three P(VDF/TrFE) films (90 μm each), alternating polarization, electrodes on each film surface
- Measured 2TL = 80 dB at 1.83 MHz (20 dB worse than Mason circuit) due to bonding layer problems
- Amplitude image: coin at 2 MHz, 150 μm transverse resolution (theoretical 115 μm)
- Phase image: 2000-yen bill, printed area height 22 μm confirmed by interference microscope
- Silicone rubber quarter-wavelength matching layer (at 2 MHz): expected +4 dB sensitivity gain
- Future goal: P(VDF/TrFE) transducer with insertion loss ≤ 60 dB; higher voltage drive (800 Vpp)
- Air attenuation: 3 orders of magnitude higher than in water at 1 MHz; ∝ f²
