# Frequency and Temperature Dependence of Acoustic Properties of Polymers Used in Pulse-Echo Systems

- **Source:** Carlson 2003 polymer acoustic properties temperature
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1jcshTgA7Wgsqe_Q0OqVAbpqig4YX0WUo/view
- **Type:** paper (conference, IEEE Ultrasonics Symposium 2003)
- **Author/Year:** J. E. Carlson, J. van Deventer, A. Scolan, C. Carlander / 2003
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Measures acoustic attenuation, speed of sound, and density of PMMA and PEEK buffer-rod polymers as functions of both frequency (5 and 10 MHz) and temperature (5°C, 20°C, 37°C) using a pulse-echo immersion technique. Provides quantitative data tables for both materials. Shows that speed of sound disperses with frequency (group delay method required), while density is frequency-independent. Temperature dependence documented for use in buffer-rod transducer systems.

## Unique contribution
First documented measurement combining both frequency and temperature dependence of acoustic properties for PMMA and PEEK under identical conditions. Demonstrates that the standard cross-correlation technique for speed-of-sound is biased due to dispersion — group delay estimation must be used. Reports that density estimated from the three-echo method varies slightly between 5 and 10 MHz transducers (attributed to transducer misalignment causing oblique reflection errors).

## Key concepts
- Buffer-rod polymers: PMMA (polymethylmethacrylate) and PEEK (polyetherether-ketone) used between transducer and unknown medium
- Three-echo immersion method: echoes x₁(t), x₂(t), x₃(t) from water/polymer interfaces; solves for z₂ and α₂
- Group delay estimator for speed of sound: avoids cross-correlation bias from dispersion
- Frequency and temperature dependence: both must be known for accurate buffer-rod corrections
- Acoustic impedance: z = ρ·c (derived simultaneously with attenuation from echo amplitudes)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PMMA at 20°C, 5 MHz: z = 3.28 MPa·s/m, α = 60.84 Np/m, c = 2750 m/s, ρ = 1192 kg/m³
- PEEK at 20°C, 5 MHz: z = 3.24 MPa·s/m, α = 52.59 Np/m, c = 2586 m/s, ρ = 1252 kg/m³
- PMMA: speed decreases slightly with increasing temperature; attenuation increases with temperature; density appears to increase (likely measurement artefact from misalignment)
- PEEK: similar trends; all values consistent with van Deventer et al. literature data
- Speed of sound is not constant with frequency (dispersive); pulse shape changes when propagating through material
- PVDF and POM attempted but failed: reflection/attenuation too high to record three distinct echoes at test frequencies
