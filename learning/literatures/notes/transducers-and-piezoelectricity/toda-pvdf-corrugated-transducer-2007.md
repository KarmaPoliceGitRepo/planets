## Overview
Describes a corrugated PVDF transducer operating at 200 kHz for air ultrasonic ranging applications. The corrugated (pleated) geometry increases the effective radiating area and reduces the resonance frequency relative to flat PVDF films, enabling efficient air coupling.

## Unique contribution
Derives analytical design equations for the corrugated PVDF geometry, linking corrugation pitch, depth, and film thickness to resonance frequency and bandwidth. Achieves single-transducer transmit/receive capability via a T/R switch, with ~33% fractional bandwidth and narrow (±7°) directivity.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Corrugation lowers effective stiffness of PVDF film → resonance frequency reduced from MHz range to ~200 kHz suitable for air ranging
- Design equations: resonance frequency scales with corrugation depth and pitch; derived from membrane mechanics
- Demonstrated prototype: 200 kHz centre frequency, ~33% fractional bandwidth (-6 dB)
- Directivity: ±7° half-angle beam pattern (narrow) — suitable for precision ranging
- Single transducer TX/RX operation achieved with TR switch; avoids need for separate transmit and receive elements
- Air coupling efficiency improved vs. flat PVDF due to better impedance transformation from the corrugated structure
- Target application: robot sensing, automotive parking sensors, indoor localisation at short–medium range
- Fabrication: corrugated geometry formed by pressing PVDF film over a mold under heat
