## Overview
An extensive review article (Soviet Physics Uspekhi, vol. 13 no. 6, 1971) covering all major nonlinear acoustic phenomena in solids: harmonic generation, Raman scattering of sound by sound, nonlinear resonance, and phonon-phonon interactions. Provides the theoretical foundation (nonlinear elasticity, five-constant theory) and reviews experimental results in detail, including specific experimental setups, materials tested, and quantitative results. Covers isotropic solids, cubic and lower-symmetry crystals, Rayleigh surface waves, hypersonic waves, and optical (Bragg diffraction) methods. Also discusses piezosemiconductors, the role of dislocations and residual internal stresses in generating otherwise-forbidden harmonics, and stimulated Mandel'shtam-Brillouin scattering.

## Unique contribution
The most comprehensive review of nonlinear acoustics in solids available at the time (1971), covering both theoretical framework and experimental results across dozens of materials. Provides consolidated tables of third-order elastic moduli for isotropic solids (Murnaghan notation: l, m, n / Landau notation: A, B, C) and for crystals of all symmetry classes (Brugger notation C_ijk), serving as a key reference compilation. Distinguishes geometrical vs. physical nonlinearity, synchronous vs. non-synchronous effects, and provides quantitative estimates of harmonic amplitudes in representative alloys. Introduces the acoustic Reynolds number Re = ρωu₀λ/η as the key dimensionless measure of nonlinearity. Demonstrates that even small dislocations and residual internal stresses can unlock nominally forbidden interactions (e.g., transverse second harmonic).

## Methods / results / takeaways

### Theory
- Strain tensor exact (nonlinear) form, Eq.(1); invariants I₁, I₂, I₃, Eq.(2)
- Internal energy expanded to third order; five-constant elasticity (λ, μ, l, m, n for isotropic; C_ij, C_ijk for crystals using Brugger's [Ref 13] notation)
- Murnaghan ↔ Landau conversion: n = −4m = −2A − 4B; l = −4m = −2A − 4B [Eq.(4)]
- Generalized nonlinear Hooke's law (Murnaghan stress tensor), Eq.(6)
- Nonlinear wave equation (8): solved by successive approximation; first approximation = linear wave equation; second approximation = driven inhomogeneous equation
- Second longitudinal harmonic amplitude (no damping): grows linearly with distance x, Eq.(14); u₂ ∝ βᵢ k₁² u₀² x / 2
- With damping: harmonic reaches maximum at stabilization distance x_s = ln2/2αᵢ, Eq.(16); at x_s, (u_x)_max/u₀ ~ Re
- Transverse wave alone → no transverse second harmonic in ideal isotropic solid (symmetry argument); shear wave does drive a non-synchronous longitudinal double-frequency wave (Eq.(17)), but with spatial period Δx = λᵢ/2(α−1) ~ λ_T, extremely small amplitude
- 54 types of three-wave interaction in isotropic solid (Table V): 36 forbidden, 10 only co-propagating, 8 for intersecting (Raman scattering) waves
- Synchronism (phase-matching) conditions Eq.(20): ω₁ ± ω₂ = ω₃ and k₁ ± k₂ = k₃ (equivalent to phonon energy/quasimomentum conservation)
- Non-linear acoustic detection in a rod: amplitude of low-frequency detected signal depends on carrier frequency, giving triple "resonance" pattern, Eq.(21)

### Experimental results
- **Pulsed spectral method (ultrasonic)**: Mg-Al alloy at 5 MHz; second harmonic (10 MHz) grows then decays with distance; stabilization distance ~44 cm at low damping. Fundamental amplitude u₀ ~ 10⁻⁷ cm at ~1000 V on transducer; (u_x)_max/u₀ ~ 4.3×10⁻⁴ (Fig. 3 & 4).
- **Hypersonic pulsed-spectral method (400 MHz, LiNbO₃)**: Both longitudinal and transverse second harmonics at 800 MHz observed (oscillogram Fig. 6). Second-harmonic amplitude (0.12–0.35)% of fundamental. Nonlinear parameter Γᵢ = 1.4 ± 0.9. Third harmonic at 1200 MHz also observed at higher power.
- **Transverse harmonic generation**: In Mg-Al alloy, Al, duraluminum, single crystals of Al (99.95%), Cd, Zn with propagation in [100]: a second shear harmonic is observed, 1–2 orders below the longitudinal harmonic (Figs. 7 & 8). Amplitude sensitive to weak external forces (due to dislocations/internal stresses, not lattice nonlinearity). LiF crystals: dislocation density varies by 4 orders of magnitude but transverse harmonic increases only ~2×.
- **Raman scattering of sound by sound**: Intersecting waves T(ω)+T(ω)→L(2ω) at 3 MHz in polycrystalline aluminum; combination signal ~ 10⁻³–10⁻⁴ of single-wave signal (Fig. 9 setup). Agrees with 54-interaction theory.
- **Angular dependence of Raman scattering**: In fused quartz at 300 and 20.6 MHz, angle between waves varied via goniometer; amplitude of difference-frequency wave vs. angle agrees with theory (Fig. 11). Relevant to almost-collinear phonon absorption explaining longitudinal-wave absorption anomaly (LR theory predicts αᵢ ≪ αᵢ but experiments find αᵢ ~ αᵢ; almost-collinear correction resolves this).
- **Rayleigh surface wave harmonics**: Second harmonic of 11 MHz surface wave in X-cut quartz along Y axis; grows linearly up to ~10 cm, ratio u₂/u₁ ~ 0.5% at saturation; Eq. uₓ = 0.18 u₀² k² x (Fig. 12). Rayleigh wave absorption α_R ~ ωT⁴ same as volume waves.
- **Nonlinear resonance / modulation method**: High-Q aluminum rod modulated at carrier ω and modulation Ω (natural frequency); detected Ω amplitude shows triple-resonance pattern as function of ω, matching Eq.(21) with Re ~ 0.05, Q ~ 10⁵ (Fig. 14). Very sensitive method.
- **Bragg-diffraction optical method**: LiNbO₃ at 562 MHz; second harmonic reaches 20% of fundamental at stabilization distance. Harmonics up to 5th observed in quartz at 708 MHz (Figs. 16 & 17). After reflection from free surface, second harmonic decreases (phase reversal), confirming theory.
- **SMBS (stimulated Mandel'shtam-Brillouin scattering)**: At room temperature, max hypersound power in fused quartz from 80 MW/cm² Q-switched laser is only 10⁻³ W — nonlinear effects negligible. At helium temperature, nonlinear effects significant; sawtooth waveform formation proposed as damage mechanism. One experiment (Brewer 1965) observed second harmonic at f₂ = 2×5×10⁹ = 10¹⁰ Hz in SMBS.

### Key numerical estimates
- Nonlinear effects observable at stress amplitudes of "several atmospheres" (well within linear acoustics range)
- Harmonic nonlinear parameter β = −(3 + 3B/A + C/A) for longitudinal waves in isotropic solid (from combining Table I moduli with formula; e.g., for Mg-Al alloy εᵢ ~ 6)
- Nonsynchronous transverse-driven longitudinal harmonic: (u_x)_max/u₀ ~ 10⁻⁵ (two orders below synchronous second harmonic)
- In piezosemiconductors, effective nonlinearity ~ liquids (practical applications possible)
