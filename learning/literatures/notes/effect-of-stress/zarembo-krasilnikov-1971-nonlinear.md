## Overview
An extensive review article (Soviet Physics Uspekhi) covering all major nonlinear acoustic phenomena in solids: harmonic generation, Raman scattering of sound by sound, nonlinear resonance, and phonon-phonon interactions. Provides the theoretical foundation (nonlinear elasticity) and reviews experimental results. Covers both isotropic and crystalline solids, and discusses the role of third-order elastic moduli, dislocations, and residual stresses in nonlinear effects.

## Unique contribution
The most comprehensive review of nonlinear acoustics in solids available at the time (1971), covering both theoretical framework and experimental results across dozens of materials. Provides consolidated tables of third-order elastic moduli for isotropic solids (Murnaghan notation: l, m, n / Landau notation: A, B, C) and for crystals (Brugger notation C_ijk), serving as a key reference compilation. Discusses geometrical vs. physical nonlinearity and their experimental separation.

## Key concepts
- Nonlinear elasticity
- Acoustic harmonic generation
- Phonon-phonon interaction
- Third-order elastic moduli (Murnaghan: l, m, n; Landau: A, B, C; Brugger: C_ijk)
- Geometrical vs. physical nonlinearity
- Acoustic Raman scattering
- Nonlinear acoustic resonance
- Lattice anharmonicity
- Residual internal stresses (affect nonlinear effects)
- Piezosemiconductors (enhanced nonlinearity via electron-phonon coupling)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Theoretical framework: strain tensor exact form; internal energy expanded to third order; five-constant elasticity (λ, μ, l, m, n for isotropic; C_ij, C_ijk for crystals)
- Murnaghan ↔ Landau conversion: n = −4m = −2A−4B; l = −4m = −2A−4B; see Eq.(4) in paper
- Table I: TOE constants (×10¹¹ N/m²) for isotropic solids including polystyrene, Armco iron, nickel steel, C1018 steel, aluminium alloys, fused quartz, Pyrex
- Table II: TOE constants for cubic and other crystal classes (NaCl, KBr, KCl, Ge, Si, Cu, Ag, Au, MgO, YIG, quartz) in both Brugger and Landau notation
- Amplitude of nonlinear acoustic effects: detectable at stress amplitudes of "several atmospheres" (well within "linear" acoustics range)
- Harmonic generation coefficient: β = −(3 + 3B/A + C/A) for longitudinal waves in isotropic solid
- Phonon-phonon interactions: selection rules same as for thermal phonons; nonlinear conversion efficiency low in most solids
- Exceptions: piezosemiconductors have effective nonlinearity comparable to liquids due to electron-phonon coupling
- Rayleigh surface waves: nonlinear effects more pronounced than bulk waves; harmonic generation observable at lower amplitudes
- Residual stresses modify the effective third-order constants; can produce nominally "forbidden" interactions
