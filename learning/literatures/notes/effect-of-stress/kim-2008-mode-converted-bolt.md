# Bolt Axial Stress Measurement Using TOF Ratio of Mode-Converted Ultrasound

- **Source:** Kim 2008 mode-converted ultrasound
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1QB3WagOdBmGXIoNCK8PaW99G4AvTzJMv/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Kim, 2008
- **Coverage:** full

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Uses time-of-flight ratio γ = TOF_LL / TOF_LT (longitudinal/longitudinal vs. longitudinal/transverse round-trip times) as the measurement variable
- γ is linearly proportional to axial stress; eliminates bolt length dependency since both TOF values change proportionally
- Acoustoelastic factor M = 13.79 TPa⁻¹ measured experimentally vs. 13.94 TPa⁻¹ theoretical (within 1%)
- Elastic constants (λ, μ, l, m, n) measured from the bolt head itself (not from a separate specimen)
- Maximum error: 5% over practical stress range
- Applicable to bolts already installed; no need for pre-stress reference measurement

## Overview
Demonstrates a TOF-ratio method for measuring bolt axial stress using longitudinal and mode-converted waves. The ratio γ eliminates the need to know the exact bolt geometry and is linearly proportional to stress through the acoustoelastic factor M.

## Unique contribution
In-situ elastic constant measurement from the bolt head (rather than material coupon) enables material-specific calibration without additional specimens. The TOF ratio approach with M measured per bolt provides <5% error.

## Key concepts
- TOF ratio (γ = TOF_LL / TOF_LT)
- Mode-converted ultrasound
- Acoustoelastic factor M
- In-situ material characterization
- Bolt axial stress measurement
- Acoustoelastic effect in fasteners
