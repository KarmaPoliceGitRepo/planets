# A Comparison of Two Theories of Acoustoelasticity

- **Source:** Comparison two theories of acoustoelasticity
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1T-T64AS92YYOCgRZNNAEZbvfpHSMsPiQ/view

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Compares two theories for describing acoustic birefringence in slightly orthotropic materials under plane stress: the theory of Iwashimizu & Kubomura (IK, one acoustoelastic constant) and Okada's theory (three acoustoelastic constants). The paper generalizes the IK theory to include anisotropy in third-order moduli, derives a birefringence equation with three constants matching Okada's form, and shows that Okada's approach is equivalent to the rigorous theory with a specific relationship between constants. Verified experimentally on 2024-T351 aluminium.

## Unique contribution
Resolves the "paradox" that Okada's less-rigorous optical-analogy theory gave better experimental agreement than the more rigorous IK theory. Proves that the two theories are formally equivalent when the third-order anisotropy is properly accounted for, and establishes the correspondence between the Okada stress-acoustic constants and the physically derived acoustoelastic constants mв‚Ѓ, mв‚‚, mв‚ѓ. First rigorous three-parameter birefringence theory for slightly orthotropic plates.
