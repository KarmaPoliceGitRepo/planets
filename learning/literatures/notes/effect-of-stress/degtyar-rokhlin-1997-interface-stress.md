# Stress Effect on Ultrasonic Wave Propagation Through Solid-Solid and Liquid-Solid Plane Interface

- **Source:** Stress Effect Ultrasonic Wave Solid-Solid
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/10U-ZeJs59ovsBJEoXpds7t4ZDyccNidH/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** A. D. Degtyar & S. I. Rokhlin, 1997
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Presents a unified computational procedure for solving the reflection-transmission problem at a plane interface between two stressed, generally anisotropic solids (or fluid and stressed solid). Uses the Man-Lu acoustoelastic constitutive equation with stress-dependent elastic constants. Demonstrates stress effects on energy redistribution, refraction angle, and shear wave interference patterns for Ti/SiC, water/plexiglass, and water/textured aluminium interfaces.

## Unique contribution
First rigorous treatment of the boundary conditions and computational procedure for wave propagation through the interface between two prestressed anisotropic media, including fluid-solid configurations. Shows that stress modifies not only bulk wave velocities but also boundary conditions and interfacial energy redistribution. Demonstrates that shear wave interference patterns in textured materials are strongly modified by stress, offering a potential measurement approach.

## Key concepts
- Man-Lu acoustoelastic model
- Stress-dependent elastic constants C_ijkl(stress)
- Reflection-transmission at interfaces
- Christoffel equation (stressed medium)
- Boundary conditions for stressed media
- Ti/SiC composite residual stress
- Shear wave birefringence at interface
- Shear wave interference
- Snell's law in stressed anisotropic media

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Constitutive equation (Man-Lu): σ_ij = σ°_ij + C_ijkl(σ°)·ε_kl + σ°_ij·u_j,k·v_k (first Piola-Kirchhoff form)
- Christoffel equation in stressed medium: (G_ij − ρV²δ_ij)P_j = 0 where G_ij is the generalized Christoffel tensor incorporating stress-dependent C_ijkl
- Modified boundary conditions: continuity of displacement + traction at interface; traction includes term σ°_ij·u_j,k·v_k (absent in unstressed case)
- Ti/SiC interface (composite residual stresses: 483 MPa compression normal, 400 MPa tension along interface in Ti, 970 MPa compression in SiC):
  - Stress effect on energy conversion coefficient near critical angles: up to ~10% change
  - Refraction angle also shifts near critical angles
  - Overall energy redistribution small except near critical angles
- Water/plexiglass (20 MPa uniaxial, 45° to incident plane):
  - Stress causes birefringence: one shear wave splits into two (fast/slow) in plexiglass
  - Velocity difference very small; interference over significant path needed to detect
- Water/textured aluminium (1% orthotropy, 100 MPa uniaxial):
  - Shear wave amplitude ratio vs. azimuthal angle: stressed state destroys destructive interference minimum present in unstressed state
  - Interference pattern strongly sensitive to stress
- Practical implication: the stress effect on the interference of transmitted shear waves may be a more sensitive stress indicator than velocity shift alone
- Limitation: calculation examples only; no experimental validation presented in this conference paper
