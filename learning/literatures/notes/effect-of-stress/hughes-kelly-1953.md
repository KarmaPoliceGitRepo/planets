## Overview
The foundational paper that established the acoustoelastic equations for isotropic solids. Hughes and Kelly applied Murnaghan's finite-strain energy function (with third-order elastic constants l, m, n) to derive seven explicit expressions relating ultrasonic wave velocity to applied stress (uniaxial tension or hydrostatic pressure) in isotropic materials. They verified these experimentally on polystyrene, Armco iron, and Pyrex glass.

## Unique contribution
The first rigorous derivation of the seven principal-wave acoustoelastic equations for isotropic solids, providing the theoretical backbone used by virtually all subsequent work. Includes experimental determination of Murnaghan TOE constants (l, m, n) from ultrasonic velocity-stress measurements, and shows that third-order constants are typically an order of magnitude larger than second-order Lam√© constants and generally negative.

## Key concepts
- Murnaghan strain-energy function
- Third-order elastic (TOE) constants: l, m, n
- Second-order elastic (SOE) / Lam√© constants: őĽ, őľ
- Acoustoelastic effect
- Principal ultrasonic waves (nine possible)
- Hydrostatic pressure dependence
- Uniaxial stress dependence
