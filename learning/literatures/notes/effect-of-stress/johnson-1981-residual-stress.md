# Acoustoelastic Applicability to Residual Stress Measurement in Aluminium Alloys

- **Source:** Johnson (undated) residual stress applicability
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Wrkwp4GVlyDLnSya9MxlYkwrrwXSxqn8/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Johnson, ~1981
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Evaluates the suitability of three aluminium alloys (2024-T351, 6061-T651, 7075-T651) for residual stress measurement by acoustoelasticity. Key question: does the ultrasonic velocity return to its original value after yielding, or does it retain a permanent offset that would confound residual stress measurement?

## Unique contribution
Establishes practical alloy-selection criteria for acoustoelastic residual stress measurement. Shows that 2024-T351 is suitable (velocity returns to baseline after yield), 6061-T651 is not suitable (permanent ~0.08% velocity drop), and 7075-T651 is marginal. This addresses a critical practical limitation of acoustoelastic stress measurement that is often glossed over.

## Key concepts
- Residual stress measurement by ultrasonics
- Velocity change through yield point

- Aluminium alloy suitability
- Acoustoelastic coefficient
- Dislocation effects on velocity

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method: measure ultrasonic velocity (longitudinal and/or shear) as function of applied uniaxial stress; load into plastic region; unload; check if velocity returns to initial value
- 2024-T351 Al: velocity change ≈ 0 after plastic flow → suitable for residual stress by acoustoelasticity; permanent dislocation effects negligible
- 6061-T651 Al: velocity drops ~0.08% during yield and does not fully recover → NOT suitable; permanent velocity offset masks residual stress signal
- 7075-T651 Al: ~0.07% velocity drop during yield → marginal; may be usable but requires careful characterization
- Physical interpretation: alloys with stronger age-hardening precipitate structures (6061, 7075) pin dislocations differently; 2024 microstructure allows dislocations to reorganize without permanent velocity change
- Practical implication: before applying acoustoelastic residual stress measurement, verify that the specific alloy/temper does not exhibit permanent velocity change at yield
