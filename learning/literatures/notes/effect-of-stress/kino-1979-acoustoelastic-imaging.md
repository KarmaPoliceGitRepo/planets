# Acoustoelastic Imaging of Stress Fields

- **Source:** Kino 1979 acoustoelastic imaging
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1UeB4t2fOmh3Sl9jIenKFqx-Ij4VCoawQ/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** G. S. Kino et al., 1979
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops an acoustic imaging system that maps the two-dimensional stress distribution in specimens by scanning with a focused ultrasonic transducer. Uses a double-pulse-echo phase detection technique to measure local velocity changes of ultrasonic waves and converts these to stress values via acoustoelastic constants. Demonstrates the technique on 6061-T6 aluminium and EPTI steel specimens under applied loads.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration of scanning acoustic imaging of a 2D stress field, producing contour maps of principal stress difference that match theoretical solutions. The double-pulse-echo phase technique achieves very high phase resolution. Provides quantitative acoustoelastic constants B for two practical engineering materials.

## Key concepts
- Acoustoelastic imaging
- Double-pulse-echo phase technique

- Focused transducer scanning
- Principal stress difference mapping
- Birefringence imaging

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Double-pulse-echo technique: two echoes from specimen surfaces; phase difference between them proportional to velocity change in the material between the reflectors
- Phase resolution: ~0.1° → velocity change resolution ~10⁻⁶; far better than conventional pulse-echo
- Acoustoelastic constant B (relating phase shift to stress):
  - 6061-T6 Al: B = 18×10⁻⁶ MPa⁻¹
  - EPTI steel: B = 2.0×10⁻⁶ MPa⁻¹
- Steel is ~9× less sensitive than aluminium due to higher elastic moduli
- Specimens: notched aluminium plate under tension; stress concentration around notch imaged
- Scanned contour maps of principal stress difference agreed closely with theoretical solutions (stress concentration factor consistent with theory)
- Shear wave birefringence mode also used: difference in phase velocities of orthogonally polarized shear waves maps principal stress difference
- Spatial resolution: limited by transducer focal spot size (~0.5 mm at 10 MHz in aluminium)
- Practical limitation: requires good coupling and flat specimen geometry; not easily applied to curved surfaces
