# Large-Area PVDF Film Sensors for Ultrasonic Lamb Wave Detection

- **Source:** Rathod 2010 PVDF thin film
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1iyw8O9q1nysQ93991wc9yT7c1La3O7C-/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** V. T. Rathod, 2010
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Reports the fabrication of large-area β-phase PVDF (polyvinylidene fluoride) piezoelectric films via hot-pressing and hot-stretching, and their use as broadband ultrasonic sensors for detecting Lamb waves. Demonstrates that PVDF films can simultaneously capture both the antisymmetric (A0) and symmetric (S0) Lamb wave modes without the resonance artifacts that complicate PZT sensors at high frequencies.

## Unique contribution
Shows that hot-press + hot-stretch processing achieves high-quality β-phase PVDF with measured piezoelectric coefficient d₃₁ ≈ −37.69×10⁻¹² m/V, and that the film operates effectively for broadband Lamb wave sensing up to high ultrasonic frequencies (>1 MHz) where PZT ceramics exhibit resonance interference. Demonstrates simultaneous A0 + S0 mode capture on aluminum plates.

## Key concepts
- PVDF (polyvinylidene fluoride)
- β-phase piezoelectric polymer
- Hot-press and hot-stretch processing
- Piezoelectric coefficient d₃₁
- Lamb waves (A0 antisymmetric, S0 symmetric modes)
- Broadband ultrasonic sensing
- Structural health monitoring (SHM)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Processing: PVDF pellets hot-pressed to film, then hot-stretched at elevated temperature to induce β-phase crystallinity; poling in strong electric field
- β-phase confirmed by FTIR spectroscopy and X-ray diffraction
- Measured d₃₁ = −37.69×10⁻¹² m/V (comparable to commercial PVDF film)
- Frequency response: flat from low kHz to beyond 1 MHz; no piezoelectric resonance artifacts in this range
- Lamb wave experiments on 1 mm aluminum plate: PZT excitation at various frequencies; PVDF film reception
- Both A0 and S0 modes clearly resolved in time-domain waveforms at multiple frequencies
- PZT sensors at high frequencies showed frequency-dependent phase distortion; PVDF did not
- Large-area PVDF patch can integrate over the wavefront, giving improved SNR for guided waves
- Practical advantage for SHM: can be embedded in or bonded to structures without requiring coupling fluid
