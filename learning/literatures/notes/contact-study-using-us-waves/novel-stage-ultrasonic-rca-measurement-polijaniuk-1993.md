## Overview
A Journal of Testing and Evaluation paper describing a specialized measurement stage for real-time ultrasonic measurement of real contact area (RCA) during quasi-static loading. The counter-specimen itself serves as both a rigid flat and an ultrasonic sending/receiving probe, enabling simultaneous contact and ultrasound measurement. The paper uses the transverse (shear) wave energy coefficient T² as a measure of RCA and validates against bearing ratio P' from surface profilometry of plastically deformed surfaces.

## Unique contribution
The counter-specimen design — where the flat acts as an integrated ultrasonic probe (piezo permanently coupled) — eliminates coupling variability and enables true real-time RCA monitoring during loading, unlike earlier setups with external transducers. The use of *transverse* (shear) waves is novel for RCA measurement; the paper argues shear waves are less affected by normal stress and ambient air/liquid layers than longitudinal waves. Demonstrates frequency selection strategy matching wave/obstacle geometry for optimal sensitivity.

## Key concepts
- Real contact area (RCA) measurement
- Transverse (shear) ultrasonic waves for contact measurement
- Energy transmission coefficient T² as RCA proxy
- Babinet's principle (acoustic scattering by obstacles)
- Bearing ratio P' (surface topography parameter)
- Frequency selection (wavelength vs. contact geometry matching)
- Surface roughness Ra, surface layer deformation state
- Curvature maxima K in T²(q) curve marking convergence to RCA
- Nominal contact pressure q (quasi-static)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Setup:** Specimen a (rough) presses axially against counter-specimen b (smooth, rigid ring sector, nominal contact area 193.7 mm²). Counter-specimen b has permanently bonded piezoelectric transducer generating shear waves. A 4.6×23 mm cylinder within the contact serves as the active probe area (examination area 16 mm²). Load measured by strain gauge bridge; microcomputer records in real time.
- **Wave-obstacle frequency matching:** Contact spot diameters range from ~0 to ~31 μm; inter-spot distances ~3–300 μm. For Ra ~ 1 μm and pressures up to 400 MPa, the frequency range 5–10 MHz satisfies both the Babinet approximation condition (Ac < 1) and sufficient obstacle sensitivity.
- **RCA determination from T²:** Repeated T²(q) measurements show a curvature maximum K at a low load q', interpreted as convergence to the RCA of the plastically deformed surface. T²(q') values compared against bearing ratio P' (from profilometry of unloaded deformed surface).
- **Validation results:** T² and P' measurements correlate well across 32 surface specimens (Ra = 0.3–22 μm, face-turned and ground, 45% C steel). Best agreement at Ra ≈ 0.63 μm, q ≤ 400 MPa. Total estimated RCA measurement error: ±5%.
- **Load class resolution:** Four load classes (0–50, 150, 800, 1000 MPa) allow selection of appropriate range; maximum pressure errors ±0.3, 0.5, 3, 10 MPa respectively.
- **Key takeaway:** The integrated-probe design enables direct, real-time RCA(q) characteristics for a wide range of technical surfaces without specialized lab equipment, making it practical for engineering use. Shear waves provide reliable RCA information unperturbed by fluid coupling or applied normal stress.
