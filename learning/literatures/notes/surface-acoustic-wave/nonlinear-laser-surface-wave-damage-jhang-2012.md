## Overview
A conference paper demonstrating a fully non-contact method to evaluate surface micro-damage in aluminium 6061-T6 alloy using narrowband laser-generated Rayleigh waves and second harmonic generation (acoustic nonlinearity). A pulsed Nd:YAG laser with a line-arrayed slit mask generates a narrowband 2.5 MHz surface wave; a 5 MHz resonant transducer receives the signal and the relative nonlinear parameter β' = A₂/A₁² is computed from fundamental and second-harmonic amplitudes. Correlation between β' and tensile strain level is demonstrated.

## Unique contribution
Adapts the established acoustic nonlinearity parameter (second harmonic generation) to a fully non-contact configuration using laser generation with a slit mask, allowing measurement on complex surfaces without couplants. Shows clear monotonic correlation between the nonlinear parameter and accumulated tensile plastic deformation, validating the approach for NDE of surface damage.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The nonlinear parameter β = (8A₂)/(k²A₁²x) where x is propagation distance; β' (relative) is used for comparison between specimens.
- The slit mask spatial period controls the fundamental frequency; slit width 0.6 mm chosen to suppress intrinsic 2nd harmonic from the mask itself.
- Array function G(f) = N·H(f)·S(f): N openings, single-beam response H(f), and array factor S(f).
- Center frequency 2.5 MHz generated at slit interval 1.168 mm; 5 MHz transducer receives 2nd harmonic.
- Specimens: Al 6061-T6, tensile strained to 0–20% plastic strain. β'/β'₀ (normalized to as-solutionized baseline) increased monotonically with tensile strain.
- Good correlation suggests laser-ultrasonic acoustic nonlinearity can serve as a damage index for early-stage surface fatigue/plasticity.
