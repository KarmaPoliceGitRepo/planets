## Overview
A short theoretical paper developing an approximate analytical model for Rayleigh wave reflection and transmission at the edge of an elastic wedge. The novel approach treats the wedge as two coupled surface-wave waveguides; the incident Rayleigh wave is decomposed into symmetric (S₀-like) and antisymmetric (A₀-like) Lamb modes using a WKB approximation. Oscillatory behaviour of the reflection and transmission coefficients as a function of wedge angle is explained by the phase difference accumulated between these two modes during propagation to and from the tip.

## Unique contribution
Previous approximate theories failed to explain the experimentally observed oscillations of |R| and |T| with wedge angle θ and the possibility of nearly total reflection or transmission at small angles. This paper provides the first theory that correctly predicts these oscillations using the coupled-waveguide representation, with good agreement to published Duralumin data.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The wedge is modelled as two coupled surface waveguides; for small angles, local plate modes approximate the wedge modes.
- The reflection coefficient |R|² + |T|² = 1 (energy is conserved between reflected and transmitted Rayleigh waves) holds in the theory, while experiments show |R|² + |T|² < 1 for larger angles due to bulk wave scattering.
- Explicit formulas for |R| and |T| involve a dimensionless integral δ ≈ 2.75 for Duralumin, encapsulating the elastic properties.
- Both the oscillation period decreasing with smaller θ and the correspondence of reflection maxima with transmission minima are correctly predicted.
- Under oblique incidence the effective path difference is multiplied by 1/cos(α), shifting the oscillation pattern — a new prediction not previously reported.
- Results are relevant to SAW delay lines/filters where wedge corners are used as reflectors or bulk-to-surface wave converters.
