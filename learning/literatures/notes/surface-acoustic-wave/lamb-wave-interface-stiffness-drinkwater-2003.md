# Measurement of A0 and S0 Lamb Wave Attenuation to Determine Normal and Shear Stiffnesses of a Compressively Loaded Interface

## Overview
An experimental and modelling study showing that the attenuation (not phase velocity) of the A₀ and S₀ Lamb modes in a glass plate is uniquely sensitive to the normal and shear spring stiffnesses of a solid–solid interface when an elastomer half-space is compressively loaded against the plate. A multilayered continuum model (surface impedance method) is used to predict dispersive attenuation, and experiments validate that A₀ attenuation at 300 kHz tracks normal stiffness KN, while S₀ attenuation at 120 kHz tracks shear stiffness KT. Measured interfacial stiffnesses agree well with independent 1 MHz bulk-wave measurements.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates for the first time that combined A₀ and S₀ Lamb wave attenuation measurements provide a practical route to measuring both KN and KT of a rough solid–solid contact independently — a task that is extremely difficult with bulk waves alone (shear stiffness changes bulk-wave reflection coefficient by less than 1.5%). The result extends solid–solid interface characterisation beyond qualitative inspection.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- A₀ displacement at the surface is dominated by out-of-plane motion → sensitive to KN; S₀ is mostly in-plane → sensitive to KT.
- At low frequency-thickness: A₀ at 300 kHz (1.17 MHz·mm) depends on KN; S₀ at 120 kHz (0.47 MHz·mm) depends on KT.
- Phase velocity is virtually unchanged by the elastomer loading; attenuation changes by >300%.
- Spring model interface: KN = C₁₁/h, KT = C₆₆/h where h is interface layer thickness.
- Extracted values at max load (0.42 MPa): KT ≈ 3.3×10¹² N/m³, KN ≈ 4.3×10¹² N/m³.
- Loading–unloading hysteresis observed (contact adhesion); consistent with prior metal–metal contact work.
- A₀ and S₀ attenuation is relatively insensitive to ±12% errors in elastomer velocity, making the method robust.
