## Overview
A short theoretical paper published in Defence Science Journal (India) that extends earlier work by Kaliski on transverse surface waves (Love-type waves) propagating at the interface between two elastic half-spaces with elastic constraint (spring-like coupling) at the contact surface. The novelty is the inclusion of constant (pre-existing static) biaxial stresses in both half-spaces and the analysis of how these stresses alter the phase velocity and the existence conditions for such waves.

## Unique contribution
Derives a dispersion equation for interface transverse waves under biaxial constant stress and shows that: (1) the elastic constant of constraint Ro is always influenced by the background stress; (2) tensile stresses of order σ restrict wave existence to Ro < 1/2 while compressive stresses open up a wider range Ro > 1/2; (3) at a specific compressive magnitude (σ₃₃ = –μ) the constraint has no effect on velocity. This precise threshold behaviour is not in prior literature.

## Key concepts
- Transverse surface waves (Love waves at interface)
- Elastic constraint at contact surface
- Constant (initial) stress biaxial loading
- Dispersion equation
- Slip boundary condition
- Elastic constant of constraint Ro
- Phase velocity dependence on stress

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Two elastic half-spaces (z > 0 and z < 0) with different Lamé constants; wave propagates in x-direction, decays exponentially with |z|.
- Boundary condition: shear stress = R × slip (displacement discontinuity); R is the elastic constraint parameter.
- General dispersion equation derived; analysed in the symmetric case (two similar media) with σ₃₃ ≠ 0.
- Key result: velocity ratio ξ = c/b is a function of both Ro = R/ρbk and the stress ratio σ₃₃/μ. Increasing tensile stress reduces propagation range of Ro; compressive stress broadens it.
- When σ₃₃ = –μ (compressive equal to shear modulus), stress effect on Ro vanishes entirely.
- Practical relevance: models interface bonding quality between structural elements under preload.
