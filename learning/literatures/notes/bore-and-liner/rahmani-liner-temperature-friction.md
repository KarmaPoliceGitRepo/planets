# The Effect of Cylinder Liner Operating Temperature on Frictional Loss and Engine Emissions

- **Source:** Rahmani 2017 liner temperature effect (Applied Energy 191, 2017)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1WYz7M_x-2OLiP5SJ4t5jT1bFxpxb8W2F/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Rahmani, Rahnejat, Fitzsimons, Dowson / 2017 (Applied Energy 191, pp. 568–581; Loughborough University / Aston Martin)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Numerical study of how cylinder liner operating temperature affects friction, power loss, fuel consumption, and emissions for the top compression ring in a high-performance 12-cylinder engine. Simulations at liner temperatures of 40, 80, and 120 °C (representing cold start, transient, and hot steady-state NEDC conditions) at engine speeds of 1500 and 2500 rpm. Uses an integrated EHD lubrication + mixed/boundary asperity contact model with Rayleigh-Ritz lubricant film approach.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates existence of an optimum liner temperature (~80 °C) for minimum cyclic average frictional power loss, arising from a trade-off between viscous friction (dominant at low temperature/high viscosity) and boundary friction (dominant at high temperature/low viscosity). Shows liner temperature has negligible effect on film thickness once boundary regime is reached at TDC, implying optimum conditions are independent of engine speed.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Surface parameters: liner σ₁ = 0.26 µm, ring σ₂ = 0.235 µm, combined skewness factor (σ/j)c = 0.192.
- At 1500 rpm: for most of engine cycle, HD lubrication prevails; near TDC reversal regime becomes mixed/boundary, especially at 80 and 120 °C.
- MOFT reduction: 40→80 °C drops film by 40–55% at mid-stroke; at TDC, film thickness insensitive to temperature rise once boundary regime prevails.
- Oil mass flow rate at ring: increasing temp 40→80 °C reduces flow by ~50%; 80→120 °C reduces by ~42% (speed-independent).
- Peak total friction occurs in power stroke near detonation point (DP, ~23–47° after TDC) due to high combustion pressure loading. Peak friction values: 41 N (40 °C), 93 N (80 °C), 125 N (120 °C).
- Cyclic average power loss minimum at 80 °C liner temperature: 28.6% less than at 40 °C and 16.3% less than at 120 °C for 1500 rpm.
- Fuel energy lost to top ring friction: ~3–4% of input fuel (consistent with Richardson's estimate for full ring pack ~5–8%).
- Thermal loss reduction: 10 °C liner temperature rise reduces power stroke heat loss by ~8–12%, offsetting some friction gains.
- Conclusion: optimum liner temperature ~80 °C minimises total power loss; liner cooling control is a viable strategy for NEDC compliance.
