# Summary of Results of Development and Validation of Hot Honing System

- **Source:** 2002 Hot Honing System (SAE 2002-01-3299)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1DPWYHTUwpnwYrg8dzRLozGaLBH4Mhe9K/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** McCormick, Meara / 2002 (SAE Technical Paper 2002-01-3299; C-K Technologies / Sunnen Products)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Development and validation study of hot honing — honing a cylinder bore while the engine block and hone plate are heated to simulate operating temperature — to produce a bore that is round at actual engine operating conditions rather than at room temperature. Studies conducted on GM SB-2 cast iron block and Brodix aluminum block. Covers bore distortion measurement (PAT Incometer), Fourier harmonic analysis of bore shape, bore surface finish parameters (Rk, Rpk, Rvk), and their effects on ring-cylinder kit performance (blowby, oil consumption, friction).

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that conventional torque-plate honing (even at elevated temperature) produces a bore that re-distorts at operating temperature, and that only heating both the block AND the hone plate to operating temperature (~220°F / 104°C) and using a "Flow-Through" torque plate achieves a round bore at operating conditions. Introduces Rk, Rpk, Rvk surface finish parameters as superior to Ra for cylinder bore characterisation.

## Key concepts
- Hot honing; cylinder bore geometry at operating temperature
- Bore distortion; cylindricity; Fourier harmonic orders (0th to 4th)
- PAT Incometer bore geometry gage; circumferential measurement (72 points / 5°)
- Flow-Through Torque Plate; heated block and hone fluid
- Surface roughness parameters: Rk (core roughness), Rpk (reduced peak height), Rvk (reduced valley depth), MR1, MR2 (bearing ratio)
- Ring conformability; orifice/accumulator cylinder kit model
- Blow-by; reverse blow-by; oil consumption; ring pack friction
- Nikasil-type liner surfaces

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Standard bore gage (2–3 point) cannot resolve bore roundness; PAT Incometer (fixed-centre reference) required for shape measurement.
- Bore shape changes significantly from room temperature to operating temperature (~220°F); end cylinders in Siamese iron block distort more than centre cylinders; centre cylinders of aluminium block distort more.
- Requirements for reproducible hot honing at 220°F: (1) both hone plate AND block must be heated; (2) hone fluid at same temperature as block; (3) special Al₂O₃ stones with sulfur.
- Hot honed bore cylindricity: 0.000275 in vs standard 0.001181 in (4.3× improvement).
- Cylinder kit model at 9800 rpm: hot honed bore reduces friction loss 51% (0.432 vs 0.876 kW/cycle) and blowby 47% vs standard bore.
- Rk/Rpk/Rvk recommended ranges for hot honed surfaces: Rpk 0.3–0.5 µm, Rk 0.6–0.9 µm, Rvk 0.6–0.9 µm, MR2 > 83%.
- Nikasil surfaces show higher Rvk (~24 µm) than cast iron (~17 µm), with broader variation.
- 1–2% improvement in horsepower achievable in a well-developed high-performance engine through hot honing optimisation.
- Oil consumption correlated with Rvk and MR2: IOC = 397.0 + 1.673·(Rvk(100-MR2)) + ... for cast iron engines.
- Thermal distortions dominate over mechanical (head clamping) distortions in well-designed cast iron block with aluminium head (consistent with Abe & Suzuki 1995).
