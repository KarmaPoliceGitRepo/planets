# A Review of Surface Texturing in Internal Combustion Engine Piston Assembly

- **Source:** International Journal of Integrated Engineering 12(5), pp. 146–163 (2020), DOI: 10.30880/ijie.2020.12.05.018
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1aTDBf82kfTIg1jlO_hy0qqTsnIh0PlQR/view
- **Type:** paper (review)
- **Author/Year:** Azmi, M.A.M.; Wan Mahmood, W.M.F.; Ghani, J.A.; Rasani, M.R.M. (Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia) / 2020 (Int J Integr Eng 12(5))
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Review of surface texturing for piston assembly tribology in ICEs, covering micro-dimple fabrication methods, mechanisms of hydrodynamic lift generation, effects of dimple geometric parameters on friction, and surface texturing effects on heat transfer. Three main fabrication methods reviewed: laser surface texturing (LST), electrochemical/electrodischarge machining (ECM/EDM), and micro-mechanical cutting. Finds that textured piston rings reduce friction 20–50% vs un-textured; partial LST achieves ~25% friction reduction; 4% lower fuel consumption demonstrated with LST rings; laser-textured piston skirt showed 60% compression improvement, 8 dB noise reduction in an old engine.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive review of micro-dimple fabrication technologies as applied to piston assembly surfaces (rings and skirt), integrating tribological mechanism (lubrication reservoir, hydrodynamic lift, wear debris entrapment) with manufacturing process guidance; bridges surface engineering and engine tribology.

## Key concepts
- Surface texturing mechanisms: lubrication reservoir, hydrodynamic lift generation (micro-bearing), wear debris entrapment, stiction reduction
- LST: pulsed laser (Nd:YAG, CO2, UV), flexible, fast, complex shapes; heat-affected zone concern
- ECM/EDM: non-contact; ECM lacks residual stress, no tool wear; TMEMM, PDMS mask approaches; EDM for hard materials
- Micro-mechanical cutting: turning, milling, drilling; cost-effective; tool wear issues
- Dimple parameters: shape (circular, ellipse, diamond), depth, area density, distribution — must be optimised or can increase friction
- Partial LST piston rings: 25% friction reduction (Ryk & Etsion 2006); 4% fuel saving (Etsion & Sher 2009)
- Textured piston skirt: 60% compression improvement; 8 dB piston slap noise reduction
- Heat transfer: texturing affects oil film temperature → viscosity → secondary motion; adiabatic shear heating
- Piston skirt: 3% fuel energy for piston-liner assembly friction (Littlefair et al. 2013); piston ring 4%

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Review methodology: structured survey of tribological literature 2005–2020.
- LST advantages: flexible, fast, no damage, optical control, various wavelengths.
- ECM advantages: no residual stress, large area; PDMS mask for cylindrical surfaces (relevant for bore/liner).
- EDM: micro-scale features; 27% oil film thickness increase observed for piston ring/cylinder.
- Friction reduction mechanisms: micro-dimples act as reservoirs in starved conditions; generate hydrodynamic pressure (micro-bearings); entrap wear debris.
- Warning: wrong dimple design (size, depth, density) can increase friction — optimisation required.
- Heat transfer: textured surfaces improve heat transfer between piston and liner → reduced oil temperature → better viscosity retention.
- Gap identified: optimisation of micro-dimple parameters for piston skirt application and combined effect on engine tribology and heat transfer needs more study.
- Bore-liner relevance: surface texturing is a complementary intervention to bore distortion control; understanding texturing mechanisms informs liner surface design for reduced ring-bore friction under non-ideal (distorted) bore conditions.
