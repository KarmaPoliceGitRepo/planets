# An Experimental Study on Relationship between Lubricating Oil Consumption and Cylinder Bore Deformation in Conventional Gasoline Engine

- **Source:** SAE Int. J. Engines, Vol. 2, Issue 1 (2009), pp. 106–113, SAE Technical Paper 2009-01-0195
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1E_4x1g_q1jBAqEL05yLI-PVvc0lrKyG1/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Iijima, Naoki; Sakurai, Takeo; Takiguchi, Masaaki (Musashi Institute of Technology); Harigaya, Yasuo (Utsunomiya University); Yamada, Takeshi; Yoshida, Hideki (RIKEN Corp.) / 2009 (SAE Int J Engines 2(1))
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Experimental study combining rotary-piston bore deformation measurement + sulfur tracer oil consumption (LOC) measurement per cylinder, for an inline 4-cylinder naturally aspirated gasoline engine (2L, 89 mm bore). Rotary piston method: crown rotatable via spindle with inductance sensor to scan 360°. Sulfur tracer method: SO2 in exhaust per cylinder, intake air flow per cylinder (hot-wire + laminar flow). Cylinders #3 and #4 measured for bore deformation; all 4 for LOC. Standard and low-tension rings tested. Results: deformation increases with load (thermal); inner cylinder #3 deforms oval (thrust direction constrained laterally); end cylinder #4 deforms triangular (unconstrained rear). Despite different bore shapes, #3 and #4 have nearly equal LOC; ring conformability model confirms oil flow into combustion chamber (inertial mechanism, not evaporation) is dominant LOC path.

## Unique contribution
Combines rotary-piston cylinder deformation measurement with per-cylinder sulfur-tracer LOC measurement simultaneously; demonstrates experimentally that different bore deformation shapes (oval vs triangle) yield similar LOC; ring conformability model with deformed bore shows that inertial oil flow (qi) is the dominant mechanism; lower tension ring → reduced conformability → thicker oil film → more inertial oil flow → higher LOC (evaporation relatively insensitive to ring tension).

## Key concepts
- Engine: 4-cylinder NA gasoline, 2L, 89 mm bore, 80.3 mm stroke, 108 kW @ 6000 rpm, 198 Nm @ 4000 rpm; aluminium open-deck block; 4 head bolts per cylinder
- Rotary piston method (Fujimoto/Furuhama concept): split piston (crown + skirt); crown rotatable 360° by spindle/gears from outside; inductance clearance sensor in crown
- Sulfur tracer method: SO2 analyzer per cylinder exhaust; intake air per cylinder via hot-wire flow meter; intake manifold divided
- 3000 rpm full load used for bore deformation measurements; LOC measured 2000–4000 rpm full load
- Cylinder #3 (inner): constrained thrust/antithrust → oval deformation (2nd order); TH–ATH direction
- Cylinder #4 (end): unconstrained rear → triangular deformation (3rd order) at full load
- Ring conformability model: "theorem for three moments" (Timoshenko beam theory); 360 ring elements; gas pressure, ring tension, oil film pressure applied; Furuhama boundary conditions
- Oil flow mechanisms: qv (sliding of ring) + qi (inertial force upward near ETDC where gas pressure < second land pressure); qi causes oil to enter combustion chamber
- STD ring vs low-tension ring: low tension → less conformability → thicker film → larger qi → higher LOC
- Evaporative oil: nearly unchanged between STD and low-tension ring; unsteady 1D heat conduction model for oil film temperature

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Bore deformation results: no load → small deformation; full load → large (thermal); cylinder #3 oval at UPR/MID/LWR; cylinder #4 triangular at UPR.
- LOC: cylinders #3 and #4 nearly equal despite different bore shapes; LOC increases similarly with lower tension ring for both.
- Ring oil film calculation: thicker where bore protrudes (outward), thinner where bore indents; physically reasonable conformance pattern.
- Low-tension ring: uniform oil film thickening → larger qi → more oil blown into combustion chamber near ETDC.
- Evaporation rate: small increase with low-tension ring; not dominant LOC driver.
- Conclusion: LOC increase from lower tension ring primarily due to reduced conformability → thicker oil film → higher inertial flow rate qi.
- Bore-liner relevance: experimentally connects bore deformation shape (oval vs triangular, due to cylinder position) with ring conformability and oil consumption; demonstrates that bore topology determines local oil film non-uniformity; inertial oil flow near TDC is the key consequence of ring non-conformity.
