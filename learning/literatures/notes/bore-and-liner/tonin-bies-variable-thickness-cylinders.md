# Free Vibration of Circular Cylinders of Variable Thickness

- **Source:** Journal of Sound and Vibration 62(2), pp. 165–180 (1979)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/13Aju8ULBpZWzs0hXger4L2QtmIDcw-zM/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Tonin, Bies / 1979 (J Sound Vib 62(2), pp. 165–180; University of Adelaide)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Rayleigh-Ritz analysis of flexural vibrations of finite-length circular cylinders with circumferentially (symmetric) varying wall thickness and shear-diaphragm end conditions. Thickness variation described as Fourier series; vibration described as sum of uniform-cylinder modes. Applied to a non-concentric bore cylinder (inner bore eccentric to outer surface). Validated against time-averaged holographic measurement of mode shapes.

## Unique contribution
First application of Rayleigh-Ritz with multi-mode coupling for circumferentially variable-thickness cylinders, validated holographically. Demonstrates that small eccentricity (e = 0.5 mm for 39 mm radius shell) splits degenerate mode pairs into distinct symmetric/asymmetric frequencies and generates superposed mode amplitudes proportional to distortion magnitude. Theory agrees with experiment to within 2% on frequencies.

## Key concepts
- Rayleigh-Ritz method; circumferentially varying wall thickness
- Fourier series description of thickness variation (inner and outer surfaces)
- Superposed modes; mode coupling due to distortion
- Symmetric and asymmetric vibration modes
- Non-concentric bore (inner bore circular but eccentric to outer surface)
- Time-averaged holographic interferometry for mode shape measurement
- Shear-diaphragm boundary conditions; Arnold-Warburton elasticity equations

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Formulation: displacements as sum over (m, n) modes of uniform cylinder; mass matrix Q and stiffness matrix D from Rayleigh-Ritz with thickness integration terms.
- Thickness as Fourier series: outer surface a⁺(θ), inner surface a⁻(θ); h⁺ₚ and h⁻ₚ coefficients evaluated numerically for non-concentric bore.
- Model cylinder: steel; a = 39.29 mm, a⁺ = 40.75 mm, a⁻ = 37.83 mm; eccentricity e = 0.5 mm; L = 398.8 mm; N = 6 circumferential modes adequate.
- Frequency predictions: agree with experiment to within 2% for modes (1,2) through (3,4).
- Symmetric mode frequencies decrease with increasing eccentricity e; asymmetric mode frequencies increase with e — mode splitting increases with e.
- Mode shapes: theory shows superposed mode amplitudes; higher circumferential modes contribute proportionally to eccentricity-induced coupling. Agreement with holograms "excellent" for all modes except (1,2) lowest mode.
- Bore-liner relevance: provides mathematical framework for how cylinder wall thickness variation (from out-of-round machining or distortion) couples vibration modes — relevant to liner NVH and bore distortion vibration studies.
