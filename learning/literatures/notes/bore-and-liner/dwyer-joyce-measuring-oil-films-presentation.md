## Overview
Presentation slides from Rob Dwyer-Joyce's group summarising ultrasonic oil film measurement methods, covering basic principles of light, electrical, and acoustic measurement techniques, then focusing on the reflected-ultrasound spring-model approach. Shows results from a modified TE-77 bench rig (ring-liner simulator) and a lawnmower engine, including Stribeck curve construction and piston ring / skirt film measurements.

## Unique contribution
Concise pedagogical overview of the acoustic impedance mismatch, quasi-static spring model, and practical implementation on both bench rig and fired engine, including direct Stribeck curve construction from ultrasonic OFT vs friction coefficient data.

## Key concepts
- Optical interference film measurement (Cameron–Gohar, LIF)
- Electrical film measurement (capacitance, resistance)
- Acoustic impedance mismatch; reflection coefficient
- Quasi-static spring model (Tattersall)
- Bulk modulus and film stiffness
- Stribeck curve from ultrasonic data
- Lambda ratio (λ = hmin/σc)
- Piston ring / skirt imaging
- PZT piezoelectric sensors

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Spring model stiffness K = B/h; reflection coefficient R relates to film stiffness; film thickness extracted from FFT of reflected vs reference pulse.
- TE-77 results: load ramp (40–180 N) and speed ramp (2.5–17.5 Hz) experiments with S20 and 15W40 oils; Stribeck curve assembled from friction coefficient and OFT data; λ ranges from ~0.5 (boundary) to ~3 (mixed).
- Engine results (lawnmower, ~2500 rpm, loaded): compression ring, both oil-ring rails, and skirt film imaged; skirt shows repeatable piston-slap oscillation during the power stroke.
- Main challenge noted: spatial resolution (sensor footprint vs. ring width).
