# Increase in Cylinder Bore Distortion Due to Head Gasket Installation and Its Treatment

- **Source:** Journal of the Japan Institute of Marine Engineering (JIME) Vol. 43, No. 4 (2008), pp. 585–590
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1BCGLL_vIgsrr8F5JXb5TTFEtI5CNvWK_/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Udagawa, Ko / 2008 (J JIME 43(4); article primarily in Japanese with English abstract/title)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Experimental study identifying the gasket bore seal face pressure concentration as the primary cause of cylinder bore distortion amplification during head gasket installation. Compares closed-deck and open-deck engine structures. Shows that bore distortion at the top of the cylinder (10–30 mm from deck) is directly linked to gasket seal pressure distribution. Proposes a partial reinforcement plate in the gasket at the inter-bore region to correct bore distortion in lightweight engines.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration that gasket bore seal face pressure non-uniformity (not just bolt forces) controls bore distortion direction and magnitude, and that targeted partial reinforcement plates in the gasket can correct bore distortion by adjusting local seal pressure — applicable to lightweight aluminium open-deck engines.

## Key concepts
- Head gasket bore seal pressure concentration; open-deck vs closed-deck engine
- Bore distortion amplification: mainly in top 10–30 mm from deck face
- Closed-deck: 4th order distortion (4 bolts/cylinder); open-deck: 2nd order (outer wall high-pressure points)
- Face pressure measurement: pressure-sensing plate with strain-gauge sensors
- Incometer bore distortion measurement; circumferential 8-point measurement
- Partial reinforcement plate (shim) in gasket at inter-bore region to equalise pressure
- Metal laminate gasket (MLS); bore seal load allocation ~60% for diesel vs ~50% for gasoline

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Measurement: Incometer measures bore shape at 20 mm depth from deck; face pressure measured by instrumented plate (strain-gauge spring sensors at 8 circumferential positions).
- With gasket vs without: significantly higher bore seal pressure and larger bore distortion when gasket installed; face pressure non-uniformity is key.
- Closed-deck: bolt-adjacent positions (b,d,f,h) have highest face pressure → 4th order (cloverleaf) distortion at middle cylinders; top part of cylinder contracts (like crushed pot).
- Open-deck: outer wall positions (a,e) have highest face pressure → 2nd order (oval) distortion; top part expands outward (like flared glass).
- Gasket shim thickness: thicker reinforcing shim → higher bore seal pressure → larger distortion; face pressure and distortion magnitude are directly correlated.
- Inter-bore region: lowest face pressure area in lightweight aluminium engines → inward (negative) bore distortion; causes ring/piston friction increase and scuffing risk.
- Correction: partial reinforcement plate (0.15–0.20 mm shim with elliptical cut-out) inserted in gasket at inter-bore region increases local face pressure → bore distortion corrected from inward to outward at that region.
- Bore-liner relevance: establishes that gasket seal pressure distribution is as important as bolt preload for bore distortion control; provides gasket design strategy to reduce inter-bore inward distortion in lightweight engines.
