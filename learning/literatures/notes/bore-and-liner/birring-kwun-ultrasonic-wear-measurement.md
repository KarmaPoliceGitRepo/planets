# Ultrasonic Measurement of Wear

- **Source:** Tribology International 22(1) (1989), pp. 33–37
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1tWtxdSvbKYgoyJkWTanZ1dqYoE6Y8u2r/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Birring, Kwun (Southwest Research Institute) / 1989 (Tribol Int 22(1), pp. 33–37)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Feasibility study of applying the ultrasonic phase-comparison technique to measure liner wear in locomotive diesel engines to sub-micron accuracy. A custom wear probe (lead metaniobate PZT crystal + platinum RTD, ferrite plug) is installed in the liner. Laboratory tests on a grinding machine demonstrate 0.75 µm accuracy. Engine tests reveal that temperature changes and instrument drift substantially degrade accuracy; counterbalancing procedures are proposed.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates the first application of ultrasonic phase-comparison interferometry to continuous in-service engine liner wear measurement, achieving 0.75 µm laboratory accuracy, and identifies the dominant error sources (thermal expansion, velocity change with temperature, instrument drift) that must be corrected for practical engine use.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic phase-comparison technique; interferometry; frequency-shift method
- Phase change Δφ = 2πf·Δt; frequency change Δf to maintain fixed phase → Δt/t = −Δf/f
- Lead metaniobate PZT transducer; platinum RTD temperature compensation
- Ferrite plug (97% Fe + 3% Si): hardness 78 Rb (softer than liner 90 Rb); low attenuation
- Temperature effects: velocity change with T (α = −0.37 m/s/°C), thermal expansion
- Instrument drift; echo number N (Nth echo × N accuracy improvement)
- Diesel engine liner; locomotive application; Alternative Fuels programme (AAR/SwRI)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Principle: wear Δd = v·Δt/2; phase-comparison measures Δt to ±5×10⁻¹¹ s → theoretical accuracy 0.015 µm; practical limit 0.75 µm due to temperature and drift errors.
- Frequency: 10 MHz; accuracy improves proportional to f and to echo number N.
- Probe design: ferrite plug screwed into 9 mm hole in liner; crystal soldered to plug; RTD adjacent for temperature correction; sealed with stycast epoxy rated to 260°C.
- Laboratory results: wear vs time agreement with micrometer to within ±0.75 µm over 400-minute grinding tests.
- Engine results: errors increased significantly due to non-uniform temperature gradient along sound path and instrument drift; measurements possible only if temperature corrections applied at consistent temperature intervals (~daily).
- Counterbalancing: (1) take readings at same temperature post-shutdown; (2) use reference control probe not subject to wear for drift compensation; (3) design probe to resist vibration/temperature fluctuation.
- Bore-liner relevance: foundational reference for ultrasonic-based liner wear and thickness monitoring; precursor to oil film thickness ultrasonic methods; demonstrates temperature compensation requirement for in-engine ultrasonic measurements.
