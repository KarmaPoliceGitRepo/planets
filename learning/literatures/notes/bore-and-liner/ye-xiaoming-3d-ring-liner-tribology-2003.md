# Three-Dimensional Analysis of Tribological Performance and Heat Transfer in Piston and Cylinder Liner System

- **Source:** ASME ICEF2003-735 (Internal Combustion Engine Division Fall Technical Conference 2003)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1iku8i1evvULjk6a1eUTbaD9Oh3pU2UQj/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Ye Xiaoming, Li Guiqian, Cui Yaohui / 2003 (ASME ICEF2003-735)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
3D mixed lubrication model for the piston ring-liner system incorporating bore out-of-roundness (elliptical distortion). Uses average Reynolds equation with Patir-Cheng flow factors for rough surface lubrication and Greenwood-Tripp asperity contact model. Couples a FEM heat transfer model to predict bore surface temperature distribution. Applies to a gasoline engine with 1 µm elliptical liner distortion.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that even a 1 µm elliptical (2nd order) bore distortion significantly increases friction loss due to reduced hydrodynamic film and increased asperity contact, and quantifies the asymmetric axial heat flux distribution showing that friction heat from rings mainly affects temperature near the top ring groove.

## Key concepts
- 3D mixed lubrication model; average Reynolds equation (Patir-Cheng)
- Flow factors (pressure and shear); rough surface lubrication
- Greenwood-Tripp asperity contact model; composite roughness
- Bore out-of-roundness (elliptical, 2nd order); 1 µm amplitude
- FEM heat transfer model; bore surface temperature distribution
- Friction heat flux distribution; heat mainly near top ring groove
- Piston ring pack (compression ring + oil ring); oil film thickness
- Minimum film thickness; friction force; asperity contact pressure

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Reynolds equation: 2D average form with Patir-Cheng pressure and shear flow factors; boundary conditions: JFO (Swift-Stieber) at film exit; flooded inlet.
- Contact model: Greenwood-Tripp; asperity contact pressure as function of composite roughness and separation; elastic modulus combination.
- Geometry: elliptical bore described by 2nd order Fourier term; amplitude 1 µm; remaining harmonics zero.
- Heat transfer FEM: steady-state; heat flux input from ring friction; convective boundary conditions on coolant side; structured mesh.
- Results: With 1 µm elliptical distortion, minimum oil film thickness reduced and asperity contact increases over most of piston stroke — friction loss increases.
- Temperature distribution: friction heat mainly deposited near top ring groove region (highest gas pressure, highest friction); middle and lower liner zones less affected.
- Bore distortion finding: even small (1 µm) elliptical distortion has measurable effect on tribological performance; higher distortion amplitudes would have proportionally larger impact.
- Bore-liner relevance: demonstrates 3D ring-liner lubrication methodology explicitly including bore out-of-roundness; links bore distortion directly to friction and temperature.
