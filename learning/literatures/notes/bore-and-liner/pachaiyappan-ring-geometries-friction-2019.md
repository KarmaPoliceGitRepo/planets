# Effect of Piston Ring Geometries on Lubrication and Friction in Diesel Engine

- **Source:** IOP Conference Series: Materials Science and Engineering 1240 (2022), 012003

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Parametric study of piston ring geometry effects on lubrication and friction using AVL EXCITE simulation software for a 1.9 L diesel engine. Investigates tangential force (ring tension), barrel height, axial width, closed gap, and ring twist. Compares symmetric vs asymmetric barrel profiles and positive vs negative twist. Identifies optimal geometry combinations for friction reduction.

## Unique contribution
Quantifies the individual and combined effects of five ring geometry parameters on mixed lubrication friction using a commercial tribodynamics code, showing that asymmetric barrel profile reduces asperity contact friction by ~50% and that positive ring twist improves ring-liner contact stability compared with negative twist.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Software: AVL EXCITE Power Unit; mixed lubrication model with average Reynolds equation; Greenwood-Tripp asperity contact.
- Engine: 1.9 L diesel; 80×92.8 mm; 4-cylinder; compression ring studied.
- Tangential force: increasing ring tension increases conformability but also increases friction; optimum balance between sealing and friction.
- Barrel height (crown height): affects wedge angle and hence hydrodynamic film buildup; too low reduces film; too high reduces conformability.
- Axial width: narrower ring increases pressure per unit area but enhances wedge effect; reduces friction at optimal width.
- Closed gap: affects end-gap leakage; influence on ring end-gap region lubrication.
- Ring twist: positive twist (toe-in at top) provides more stable contact and lower minimum film thickness variation vs negative twist; reduces ring flutter tendency.
- Asymmetric barrel profile: shifts pressure peak away from TDC/BDC reversal regions; reduces asperity contact friction by approximately 50% compared to symmetric barrel.
- Combined optimum: asymmetric barrel + positive twist + optimised axial width gives best friction reduction.
- Bore-liner relevance: directly applicable to ring design optimisation for bore-liner friction reduction; geometry parameters studied are those adjusted in production ring specifications.
