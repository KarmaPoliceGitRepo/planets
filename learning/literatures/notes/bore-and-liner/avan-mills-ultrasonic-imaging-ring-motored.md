# Ultrasonic Imaging of the Piston Ring Oil Film during Operation in a Motored Engine

- **Source:** 2010 - Ultrasonic Imaging of the Piston Ring Oil Film during operation
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1OvI7vHLifogaIgixqbyoAG57iTLfcVxi/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Avan, Mills, Dwyer-Joyce / 2010 (SAE Int. J. Fuels & Lubricants, 3)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Early demonstration of non-invasive piston ring imaging using a single external 5 MHz contact transducer magnetically clamped to a motored 4-cylinder diesel engine (Perkins 1100 series, 105 mm bore, cast-iron block). Reflection coefficient data show all three rings distinctly at engine speeds 600–840 rpm; approximate oil film thickness values (5–10 µm) are computed from the spring model, but spatial resolution is acknowledged as a limitation.

## Unique contribution
First published attempt to image the ring pack non-invasively from the exterior of a cylinder block, without bore penetration, on a motored multi-cylinder engine. Discusses three practical sensor strategies to improve spatial resolution (smaller transducer, focusing immersion transducer, piezoelectric thin-film deposition).

## Key concepts
- Non-invasive ring imaging; reflection coefficient
- Quasi-static spring model (Tattersall)
- Acoustic impedance; pulse-echo mode
- Motored engine; ring pack detection
- Spatial resolution limitation
- Cavitation; thin oil layer on liner wall

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Setup: 5 MHz straight-beam contact transducer; LeCroy oscilloscope at 500 MHz/500 Msps; trigger from shaft encoder at TDC, BDC, or mid-stroke; 370 segments per trigger.
- All three rings clearly resolved (oil ring, 2nd ring, top ring) at 840 rpm; R values at ring passages range 0.88–0.97.
- Minimum OFT at BDC is 5.5–7.5 µm; OFT increases toward mid-stroke as expected from hydrodynamics; weaker speed dependence than expected, attributed to temperature-driven viscosity change without cooling system.
- Key limitation: sensor diameter (5 MHz transducer) larger than ring width → mixed reflection from ring and wall-air boundary; OFT is only approximate.
- Recommended solutions: transducer smaller than ring width, focused immersion transducer, or thin-film AlN piezoelectric deposition.
