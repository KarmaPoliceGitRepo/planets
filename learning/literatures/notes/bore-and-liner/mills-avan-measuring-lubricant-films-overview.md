# Measuring Lubricant Films at the Piston-Cylinder Contact: An Overview of Current Technologies

- **Source:** 2013 Measuring Lubricant Films overview (SAE 2013-01-0294)

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Review paper surveying techniques for measuring lubricant film thickness between the piston and cylinder: optical (LIF), electrical (inductance, resistance, capacitance), and ultrasonic. After reviewing each method's principles, advantages, and disadvantages, presents ultrasonic measurements from a 15-sensor array on a Honda CRF450R fired engine measuring both compression ring and piston skirt oil film thickness.

## Unique contribution
First comprehensive comparison table of documented OFT measurements across all techniques (LIF, inductance, capacitance, resistance, ultrasound), with the ultrasonic method highlighted for being non-invasive and not requiring cylinder penetration. Demonstrates OFT measurement at both ring and skirt locations simultaneously in a fired engine using 10 MHz PZT transducers.

## Key concepts
- Laser induced fluorescence (LIF) for OFT measurement
- Inductance, resistance, capacitance electrical OFT methods
- Ultrasonic OFT method; quasi-static spring model; reflection coefficient
- PZT transducer array; multiplexed acquisition
- Compression ring OFT vs piston skirt OFT
- Honda CRF450R test engine
- Friction FMEP; piston-cylinder interactions
- Non-invasive measurement; road-going applicability

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Ultrasonic method: R = reflection coefficient from FFT of pulse-echo signals; thin film spring model K = f(ω, Zi); h = B·ρ/K from bulk modulus. Calibration-free (uses bulk material properties).
- 15 sensors (10 MHz, 7.1×1 mm) per side (thrust, anti-thrust, neutral) bonded to exterior of Honda CRF450R. Multiplexed at 80 kPulse/s; each sensor active for 2 s/cycle set.
- Compression ring results at 3200 rpm, 90% load: film increases from ~2.3 µm near TDC to ~5 µm at mid-stroke (reducing ring load + increasing entrainment speed).
- Skirt results at 3000 rpm, 90% load (sensors at 21, 27.5, 39.5 mm from bore top): thin film (~3.5 µm min) on anti-thrust during compression and thrust during expansion, consistent with kinematics; cycle-to-cycle variation sub-500 nm.
- Optical (LIF) range: 400 nm–4.5 µm (ring); inductance range: up to 250 µm (skirt clearance); capacitance: 0.2–60 µm; ultrasonic: ~0.1–20 µm practical range.
- Main limitations of ultrasonic method: spatial resolution currently > 1 mm; temperature/pressure effects on acoustic properties of lubricant at measurement location.
