# Some New Results for the Vibrations of Circular Cylinders

- **Source:** Journal of Sound and Vibration 29(2), pp. 189–205 (1973)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1OHtHKPQQ8LrmWkh88Eqc9ZBYJhcEyvsW/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Dym / 1973 (J Sound Vib 29(2), pp. 189–205; Carnegie-Mellon University)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Thorough examination of circular cylinder free vibrations using Sanders' "best" first-order shell theory. Derives bi-cubic frequency equation for simply-supported shells; examines effects of axial and circumferential wave numbers and thickness ratio. Compares Sanders theory with Donnell, Flügge, and Timoshenko shell theories. Derives all limiting cases (axisymmetric torsion, longitudinal, breathing; beam modes; ring vibrations; plate modes).

## Unique contribution
First comprehensive vibration analysis using Sanders shell theory to show it is the most consistent first-order theory (satisfies reciprocity, coordinate invariance, rigid body modes), and systematically compares it with three other theories over wide parameter ranges. Demonstrates that Donnell theory fails to capture rigid body beam mode (n=1) while Sanders and Flügge correctly give zero frequency.

## Key concepts
- Sanders' shell theory; Donnell theory; Flügge theory; Timoshenko theory
- Bi-cubic frequency equation; frequency factor K
- Radial (low) frequency; axial (middle); circumferential (high) frequency triplet
- Circumferential wave number n; axial wave number λ; thickness parameter H = (h/R)²/12
- Membrane (extensional) energy; bending energy; strain energy partition
- Axisymmetric limiting cases: torsion, longitudinal, breathing modes
- Extensional and inextensional ring vibration frequencies
- Beam modes (n=1, λ→0); plate modes (λ→∞); vibration-buckling analogy

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Frequency equation: bi-cubic in K² = ρR²ω²(1−ν²)/E; three roots for each (λ, n) pair.
- Radial frequency: sensitive to thickness ratio H; in-plane frequencies nearly insensitive to H.
- Modal displacement ratios (A/C, B/C): insensitive to thickness variations.
- Strain energy: pure membrane at n=0; transition to mostly bending for large n (bending energy ∝ H·n⁴ dominates over membrane at high n).
- Theory comparison: Donnell overestimates radial frequency (up to 30% for thin shells at low n); Flügge and Timoshenko agree with Sanders to within 0.5% over all (n, λ) combinations.
- Limiting cases: torsional frequency < longitudinal frequency < breathing frequency for λ < 1 (axisymmetric case), unlike n≥1 where ordering is different.
- Ring vibrations: Sanders gives zero frequency for n=1 (rigid body); Donnell does not.
- Euler column formula recovered from shell theory via vibration-buckling analogy.
- Bore-liner relevance: foundational reference for cylindrical shell acoustic and structural analysis; used as structural mechanics background for cylinder liner vibration, NVH, and piston slap studies.
