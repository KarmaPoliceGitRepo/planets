## Overview
Comprehensive literature survey of piston ring tribology covering over 150 references, intended as a reference volume for IC engine tribology with emphasis on diesel engines. Covers piston ring functions, design and materials, mechanical and thermal loads, contact pressure, sealing action, blow-by, lubrication conditions, friction, wear, and surface technology (coatings and surface treatments).

## Unique contribution
Provides a systematic cross-disciplinary synthesis of piston ring tribology (mechanical, thermal, lubrication, surface science) with particular emphasis on diesel engines and large-bore applications. Covers surface coating deposition techniques (plasma spray, galvanic, nitriding, HVOF) in detail.

## Key concepts
- Piston ring sealing functions; blow-by; gas erosion
- Ring-liner contact pressure; ring conformability
- Lubrication conditions: EHD, mixed, boundary at TDC reversal
- Lambda ratio; oil film thickness
- Friction coefficient; friction mean effective pressure
- Ring and liner wear mechanisms: adhesion, abrasion, delamination, fatigue
- Break-in wear; steady-state wear
- Surface coatings: plasma-sprayed Mo, Cr, carbide; nitriding; HVOF Cr2C3
- Thermal and chemical loads; combustion products; ZDDP
- Cylinder liner anti-thrust vs thrust side wear asymmetry

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Survey structure: ring history → design/materials → loads → lubrication → friction → wear → surface technology.
- Break-in wear rate ~12× higher in first hour than at 3.5 h steady-state; strongest wear near TDC reversal.
- Liner wear higher on anti-thrust side due to thrust force distribution across engine cycles.
- Plasma-sprayed Mo and Cr coatings standard for large-bore diesel rings; resist hot gas erosion.
- HVOF Cr2C3 coatings show potential for scuff and wear resistance.
- FFS (stainless steel + Ni-BN) and M-1P (Fe-FeO-C) porous coatings reduce friction coefficient.
- Wear mechanisms in unlubricated (simulated boundary) conditions: truncation, adhesion, delamination, ploughing.
- Oil film thickness changes with surface roughness; Abbot bearing area curve used for wear quantification.
- Note: survey does not present new experimental data; synthesises existing literature up to 2002.
