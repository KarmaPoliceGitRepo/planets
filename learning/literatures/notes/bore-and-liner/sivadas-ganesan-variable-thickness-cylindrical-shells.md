# Free Vibration of Circular Cylindrical Shells with Axially Varying Thickness

- **Source:** Journal of Sound and Vibration 147(1), pp. 73–85 (1991)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1sLcqZYu_X7FmFEJ2h6-el7V4_7HQlWc1/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Sivadas, Ganesan / 1991 (J Sound Vib 147(1), pp. 73–85; Indian Institute of Technology Madras)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Semi-analytical finite element analysis (using Love's first-approximation shell theory) for natural frequencies of thin circular isotropic cylindrical shells with linear or quadratic axial thickness variation. Studies two boundary conditions (SS-SS and CC-CC) and examines how thickness distribution (symmetric or asymmetric about mid-length) affects natural frequencies. Total shell mass held constant for comparison.

## Unique contribution
Systematic study of how axial thickness taper affects the full spectrum of circumferential mode natural frequencies (n = 1–20), showing that symmetric thickness variation about mid-length gives the largest improvement in lowest natural frequency, and that short shells benefit most.

## Key concepts
- Cylindrical shell free vibration; Love's first approximation shell theory
- Axially varying thickness (linear and quadratic); symmetric vs asymmetric taper
- Semi-analytical finite element method; ring elements; 15 elements
- Simply-supported (SS) and clamped-clamped (CC) boundary conditions
- Circumferential mode number n; axial mode number m
- Lowest natural frequency optimization by thickness distribution
- Thickness variation parameter β (ratio of max to min thickness)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Formulation: displacement functions as product of trigonometric functions in x (axial) and θ (circumferential); 8-DOF ring element; 15 elements standard.
- Convergence: 15 elements adequate; 64 equations, band width 8.
- Validation: results agree with Bert et al. (1969) for SS constant-thickness shells; agree with Smith (1968) for CC constant-thickness shells.
- Short shells (µ = l/a ≤ 1): increasing thickness taper parameter β raises lowest natural frequency for both SS and CC; symmetric taper gives larger improvement than asymmetric.
- Long shells (µ > 2): SS frequencies show decreasing trend with increased β; CC frequencies show increasing then plateau trend.
- Quadratic taper shows larger frequency changes than linear taper for same β.
- Lowest natural frequency occurs at higher circumferential mode number n for shells with thickness variation, and the n at which minimum occurs shifts as β changes.
- Bore-liner relevance: marginally relevant as background for understanding cylindrical shell acoustics/dynamics when bore varies axially (e.g., tapered liner); used as structural mechanics reference in bore-liner context.
