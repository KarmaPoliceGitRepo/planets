# Piezo-electric Sensors to Monitor Lubricant Film Thickness at Piston-Cylinder Contacts in a Fired Engine

- **Source:** 2012 - Piezoelectric sensors to monitor lubricant film thickness at piston-cylinder contacts
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1o7VVS3GLHRb15mOLbZm3wYwULfohIr6y/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Mills, Avan, Dwyer-Joyce / 2012 (Proc IMechE Part J)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
First demonstration of non-invasive ultrasonic oil film measurement on a fired engine (5 hp Briggs & Stratton single-cylinder, air-cooled, 68 mm bore). Seven strip-cut 10 MHz PZT sensors bonded to the outside of the cylinder without penetrating the bore. An array monitors compression ring MOFT at multiple axial locations during loaded and idle conditions; the skirt film is also captured and shows evidence of piston slap.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that the ultrasonic technique can be deployed on a production engine running outside a test cell, with no bore penetration. Resolves individual oil-control ring rails (0.8 mm wide) and detects piston-slap transients in the skirt film. Provides MOFT data from a small fired gasoline engine comparable to larger diesel data in prior literature.

## Key concepts
- Non-invasive ultrasonic oil film measurement
- PZT piezoelectric strip sensors (10 MHz, 7.1 × 1.5 mm)
- Quasi-static spring model (Tattersall)
- Reflection coefficient; acoustic impedance
- Minimum oil film thickness (MOFT)
- Piston slap; piston secondary motion
- Oil control ring rails
- Cycle-to-cycle variability
- Multiplexing; pulse-echo mode

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Sensor system: 7 sensors, 40K pulses/s, 100 MSamples/s; local cycle-by-cycle reference signal removes temperature drift.
- Loaded condition (2230 rpm, 7 Nm): compression ring MOFT increases with piston speed (3.2–5.8 µm from 2.2 to 7 m/s), indicating hydrodynamic wedge formation; standard deviation < 0.3 µm.
- Idle condition: thicker films (up to 11 µm) with larger cycle-to-cycle variation, attributed to engine hunting from the speed governor.
- Skirt film during power stroke: 4–7 µm; during pumping strokes: 7–22.5 µm. The large variance and the observed transient oscillation suggest piston slap.
- Bulk modulus pressure-dependence (10–15% error at 8–50 MPa) and spatial resolution (sensor area vs. ring width) are identified as the main measurement limitations.
