# Cylinder Bore Distortion: A Look at Static and Dynamic Cylinder Bore Shape Issues

## Overview
Practitioner trade article covering static and dynamic cylinder bore distortion sources, measurement methods, and mitigation strategies for high-performance engine builders. Topics: PAT Incometer bore shape measurement; static distortion from head bolt clamping; dynamic distortion from thermal and coolant-pressure effects; torque plate (deck plate) honing; hot honing methodology; gasket selection (MLS vs wire ring); bolt load measurement (Bidwell ultrasonic gauge); bore finish parameters (Rk, Rpk, Rvk); clamp-load distribution at end cylinders. Draws on MAHLE Clevite seminar materials, Sunnen, KW Products hot-hone data, and literature (Abe & Suzuki, Bird & Gartside, Koch et al.).

## Unique contribution
Accessible synthesis of bore distortion knowledge for practitioner engine builders; covers hot honing system implementation (heated block + heated hone plate + pressurized coolant + heated honing fluid); links head gasket type (active MLS vs stopper MLS vs wire ring) to bore distortion severity; provides bore size change estimates (0.002–0.004" for 4" bore, 65°F–210°F); introduces Rk/Rpk/Rvk surface finish parameters for bore characterisation.

## Key concepts
- PAT Incometer: ~$175,000 bore shape gage; measures bore shape via shaft + probe referenced from fixed centerline; 3D isometric + radial display
- Static distortion from head bolts: upper 0.5–1.5" of bore; torque plate simulates this; actual distortion 0.0005–0.001" at bolt locations
- Thermal bore size change: 0.003–0.004" for 4" bore between 65°F and 210°F; non-linear and non-uniform → barrel shape with hot + oval/cloverleaf from differentials
- Cooling system pressure (10–14 psi) partially offsets barrel effect (hourglass compression ~0.0005")
- Hot honing: block + head plate both must be heated; hone fluid at same temperature; special Al2O3 stones with sulfur
- KW Products Hot Hone 2000: self-contained system; adjustable thermostat; automotive filler; stainless pump; brass tank
- Head gasket comparison: wire ring = highest clamp load, 10–25% relaxation, highest peak bore load; active MLS = lowest bore distortion, spring-function layers absorb head motion; stopper MLS = high combustion sealing but distorts bore
- Bolt load measurement: Bidwell ultrasonic bolt gauge; torque/angle gives 25–30% load variation; ultrasonic accurate to bolt stretch
- Bore finish: Rk (core roughness depth), Rpk (peak height above bearing surface, relates to run-in), Rvk (valley depth, relates to oil retention); Ra insufficient alone
- IOC equation: IOC = 239.7 + 1.673·Rvk·(100–MR2)/100 + … (for cast iron automotive engine)
- End cylinders over-clamped in Siamese blocks; smaller-diameter end studs recommended for racing
- Clamp load general rule: 3× lift-off force → ~7500 psi combustion sealing stress
- Thermal distortions in aluminum block more severe and higher-order than cast iron → ring conformability harder

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Measurement procedure: PAT Incometer, 72 points/level (5° spacing), 6 levels; bore cylindricity from Sunnen GR-2000.
- Honing assembly: install deck plate(s), torque to measured (not nominal) bolt loads, hone; uses actual cold assembly-state bolt loads from data-bearing engine.
- SB-2 cast iron block study: intake manifold installation has minor effect; end cylinders more distorted thermally; standard torque plate without hot honing fails to achieve round bore at operating temperature.
- Brodix aluminum block: more severe and higher-order distortion than cast iron; center cylinders more distorted in aluminum (vs end cylinders in cast iron).
- Hot honing with Flow-Through Torque Plate at 220°F provides round bore at that temperature; returns to out-of-round at room temperature (shape cylindricity = what was round at temperature).
- Optimization conclusion: 1–2% HP improvement from optimized bore geometry + ring orifice/land volumes; aluminum block may improve more.
- Cast iron Rk/Rpk/Rvk targets (hot honed): Rpk 0.3–0.5 µm; Rk 0.6–0.9 µm; Rvk 0.6–0.9 µm; MR2 > 83%; MR1 > 6%.
- Bore-liner relevance: practitioner-level overview of bore distortion mechanisms, measurement tools, and mitigation by hot honing; connects block material, gasket type, and bolt load strategy to achievable bore geometry at operating temperature; surface finish guidance (Rk/Rpk/Rvk) directly applicable to liner specification.
