## Overview
Comprehensive review chapter on piston assembly lubrication covering ring geometry and mechanics, Reynolds equation formulation for ring-liner contact, appropriate boundary conditions, inter-ring gas pressure (orifice-volume method), multi-ring pack analysis, ring lift/flutter, piston skirt lubrication, and effects of bore distortion. Provides the theoretical background and governing equations used in the field.

## Unique contribution
Authoritative reference chapter that systematically assembles the complete analytical framework for piston assembly lubrication — from ring face geometry through Reynolds equation, JFO/half-Sommerfeld boundary conditions, inter-ring pressure dynamics, ring pack interactions, and distorted bore effects — serving as the standard theoretical foundation for subsequent computational studies.

## Key concepts
- Piston ring geometry: face profile (symmetric/asymmetric barrel, flat, stepped), ring twist, back-clearance
- Reynolds equation for ring-liner contact; squeeze film; wedge film
- Boundary conditions: Reynolds (JFO); half-Sommerfeld; Swift-Stieber; flooded vs starved inlet
- Ring mechanics: tangential load (radial pressure); gas pressure behind ring; ring end gap
- Inter-ring gas pressure: orifice-volume method; inter-ring volumes; blow-by
- Ring lift/flutter conditions; ring stability; ring inertia
- Multi-ring pack analysis; oil transport between rings
- Piston skirt lubrication; secondary motion coupling
- Bore distortion effects on ring conformability and oil film thickness
- Abbott-Firestone curve; Rk, Rpk, Rvk surface parameters

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Ring face geometry: barrel profile described as parabolic or polynomial; crown height affects peak film thickness and friction; asymmetric barrel shifts minimum film thickness location.
- Reynolds equation: 1D form for narrow ring in axial direction; includes squeeze term (dh/dt) and wedge term (U·dh/dx); integrated to give pressure distribution.
- Boundary conditions: half-Sommerfeld sets sub-ambient pressures to zero; JFO (Reynolds/Swift-Stieber) satisfies mass conservation at film rupture and reformation — preferred for accurate friction and oil transport prediction.
- Inter-ring pressures: orifice-volume model treats ring end gaps as orifices; differential equations for each inter-ring volume; required for accurate ring load distribution and blow-by prediction.
- Ring lift: occurs when gas pressure behind ring exceeds oil film support; flutter when ring loses contact with groove face — detrimental to sealing.
- Distorted bore section: discusses Fourier harmonic representation of bore distortion; summarises conformability criteria; notes that high harmonics (n ≥ 4) require ring stiffness to follow bore; low harmonics (n = 2, 3) governed by tangential load.
- Piston skirt: squeeze and wedge action from secondary motion; thermal and mechanical distortion of skirt shape important for film thickness.
- Bore-liner relevance: foundational theoretical reference for ring-liner lubrication analysis; defines governing equations and boundary conditions used in all subsequent computational studies in this collection.
