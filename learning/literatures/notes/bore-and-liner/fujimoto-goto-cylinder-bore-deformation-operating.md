# Measurement of Cylinder Bore Deformation During Actual Operating Engines

- **Source:** SAE Technical Paper 910042
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1gZyrg0-6QNWhDAu1BDCxjvxmaKtOtzav/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Fujimoto, Yoshihara (Musashi Institute of Technology), Goto (Nissan), Furuhama (Musashi) / 1991 (SAE 910042; Int Congress Detroit)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops and demonstrates a novel method to measure actual cylinder bore deformation during live engine operation by embedding an inductance gap sensor in a piston that can be rotated circumferentially while the engine runs. Applied to a Nissan L4 cast-iron block engine (83×83.6 mm) at conditions up to 5000 rpm full load. Measures bore shape at three axial positions (TDC, mid-stroke, BDC) under different operating conditions.

## Unique contribution
First method to measure the complete 2D circumferential cylinder bore shape continuously during actual engine operation by rotating the sensing piston, enabling characterisation of combined static (head bolt) and dynamic (thermal + pressure) bore distortion effects at up to 5000 rpm full load. Key finding: thermal load generates consistent ovalling in the thrust direction regardless of axial position; gas pressure causes nearly uniform circumferential expansion.

## Key concepts
- In-situ bore deformation measurement; inductance gap sensor in rotating piston
- Rotating piston mechanism: internal gear + external drive rod (square cross-section)
- Static deformation: head bolt tightening dominant; oval towards #2 cylinder side
- Dynamic deformation: thermal load → oval towards thrust direction; gas pressure → uniform expansion
- Axial position effect: deformation shape similar at TDC, mid-stroke, BDC positions
- Temperature effect at 5000 rpm full load: #2 cylinder wall 70°C hotter than thrust side
- Nissan L4 engine: cast iron block + aluminium head; siamese bores; closed-deck

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method: piston crown with sensor on second/third land rotated circumferentially at ~1 rpm; clearance δ measured over 360° at each axial position during one engine cycle.
- Mechanism: bevel gear → rod (square section through bearing) → pinion → internal gear on piston. Allows continuous rotation without disrupting primary motion up to 5000 rpm.
- Gap sensor: heat-resistant inductance type; inorganic materials; stable to 300°C; aged with thermal cycling.
- Head bolt effect (static): second cylinder side deformation dominates, creating asymmetric oval.
- Operating at 1600 rpm no-load: bore shape almost same as head-bolt tightened static case — confirms measurement accuracy.
- At 5000 rpm full load: bore expands thermally toward thrust direction; oval in T/AT direction; uniform across axial positions.
- Gas pressure effect (explosion TDC vs intake TDC): uniform circumferential expansion proportional to peak cylinder pressure; expansion rises sharply above ~5 MPa.
- Bore-liner relevance: fundamental experimental reference establishing that in-service cylinder bore distortion has a strong thermal ovalling component in addition to static bolt-induced distortion; motivates all subsequent FEA-based bore distortion analysis.
