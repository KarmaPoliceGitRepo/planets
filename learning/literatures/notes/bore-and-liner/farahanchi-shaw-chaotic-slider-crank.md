# Chaotic and Periodic Dynamics of a Slider-Crank Mechanism with Slider Clearance

- **Source:** Journal of Sound and Vibration 177(3), 307–324 (1994)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1BxA1SXq-1wv8XNtGn3cT2ayupstW4NHF/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Farahanchi, Shaw / 1994 (J Sound Vib 177(3), pp. 307–324; Michigan State University / University of Michigan)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Investigates the nonlinear dynamics of a planar slider-crank mechanism with clearance at the prismatic (sliding) joint. Studies how clearance gap size, bearing friction, crank speed, and impact restitution coefficient affect system response. Identifies three response types: chaotic, transient chaos, and periodic. Uses Poincaré maps and impact location statistics to characterize wear implications.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that chaotic motion occurs predominantly at high crank speeds and/or low bearing friction with near-elastic impacts, while periodic response occurs at low speeds and low restitution. Shows that chaotic motions produce uniform wear distributions while periodic motions produce highly localized impact wear — practical implication for piston-cylinder clearance management.

## Key concepts
- Slider-crank mechanism with prismatic joint clearance
- Chaotic dynamics; periodic dynamics; transient chaos
- Poincaré maps; impact location statistics
- Coefficient of restitution; bearing friction
- Slider clearance gap size effects
- Wear distribution (uniform vs localized impact patterns)
- System unpredictability over wide parameter ranges

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: 2D rigid-body slider-crank; clearance modelled as gap between slider and guide; impacts with restitution coefficient e; Coulomb friction at bearing.
- Three response regimes: (1) chaotic at high speed / low friction / elastic impact; (2) transient chaos (eventually periodic); (3) periodic at low speed or low restitution.
- Poincaré maps: chaotic response shows strange attractors; periodic shows fixed points.
- Impact location distribution: chaotic → statistically uniform over slider surface (uniform wear); periodic → highly concentrated at one or two locations (severe localised wear).
- Unpredictability: accurate prediction of long-term behaviour impossible over wide parameter ranges, even with known initial conditions.
- Relevance to bore-liner topic: piston-liner clearance creates analogous impact dynamics (piston slap); chaotic vs periodic slap has different wear and noise implications; high crank speed / low damping → chaotic slap with diffuse wear; low speed / high damping → periodic slap with localised contact.
- Note: this paper is more broadly relevant to piston secondary motion and slap than to ring/liner tribology directly.
