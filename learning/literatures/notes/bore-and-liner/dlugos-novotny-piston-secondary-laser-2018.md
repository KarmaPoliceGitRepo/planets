# Piston Secondary Motion Measurement by Laser Displacement Sensors

- **Source:** Vibroengineering Procedia 18 (May 2018), pp. 170–175, DOI: 10.21595/vp.2018.19937
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1eILO2LshqYEzadPLJ67bpOG-Vr3akb94/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Dlugoš, Jozef; Novotný, Pavel (Brno University of Technology) / 2018 (Vibroeng Proc 18)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Short conference paper describing a non-contact laser displacement sensor method for measuring piston secondary motion (tilt and lateral translation) in a motored Briggs & Stratton side-valve engine with a transparent plexiglass cylinder head. Three sensors aimed at the piston crown: two for tilt angle, one (at 45°) for lateral motion. Tilt angle results are physically reasonable and highly repeatable (Spearman correlation 0.61–0.87 across 7 test positions). Lateral displacement results are unreliable (measured amplitude 0.267 mm vs nominal bore clearance 0.053 mm — piston would penetrate liner); cause unresolved.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates feasibility of external (non-piston-mounted) laser displacement method for piston secondary motion using a side-valve engine with a transparent head; provides repeatability analysis via Spearman rank correlation; identifies key limitation (lateral motion corrupted, possibly by engine vibration or laser beam corruption through combustion gases) and highlights need for further investigation.

## Key concepts
- Three laser displacement sensors aimed at piston crown; SV engine (side-valve) with plexiglass cylinder head
- Piston tilt angle formula: α = arctan((L1-L1⁰)-(L2-L2⁰))/s
- Piston lateral motion formula: u = (L3-L3⁰) corrected for tilt angle
- Motored engine at 1000–1200 rpm; 100 working cycles averaged; defective cycles excluded
- Spearman rank correlation for repeatability: most pairs 0.71–0.87; measurement #7 lower due to warmer engine
- Nominal radial clearance 0.053 mm; measured lateral amplitude 0.267 mm → unreliable
- Tilt angle results: within physical limits; repeatable
- Briggs & Stratton Series 500: 158 cm3, 65.1 mm bore, 47.7 mm stroke, 6 Nm torque
- Method avoids need to mount sensors on piston or use linkage mechanism

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Test rig: SV engine driven by electric motor via belt; both engine and motor mounted to ground on separate brackets; laser sensor bracket attached to cylinder head with 6-DOF adjustable joints.
- Tilt angle measured at 360 crank positions per cycle; all 100 cycles averaged; ±1σ shown.
- Tilt angle: physically reasonable; higher variability near BDC (end of expansion stroke); sensor dropout suspected.
- Lateral motion: amplitude 0.267 mm >> nominal clearance 0.053 mm → unphysical; investigated causes: piston/liner deformation, laser corruption through transparent head, engine vibration — none confirmed as cause.
- Repeatability: 7 measurement sessions with sensor repositioning; Spearman rank correlation matrix shows strong–very strong correlation for tilt angle; measurement in warmer engine gives higher amplitudes.
- Bore-liner relevance: establishes non-contact laser method for piston secondary motion measurement applicable to bore-liner interaction studies; limitation in lateral motion measurement highlights challenge for non-piston-mounted sensor approaches; useful negative result for methodology development.
