# Measurement of Bore Distortion in a Firing Engine

- **Source:** SAE Technical Paper 2002-01-0485, SAE 2002 World Congress, Detroit, March 4–7, 2002
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/15fsHxBgRZOHa51ZbO4AIowgyBVPinpwF/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Bird, Lawrence E.; Gartside, Robert M. (Federal-Mogul Corporation) / 2002 (SAE 2002-01-0485)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Experimental study measuring bore distortion in a firing 2.0L inline-4 gasoline engine (siamesed bores, cast iron block, aluminium head, 85 mm bore) using 10 Micro-Epsilon eddy-current transducers in an invar carrier mounted at the piston undercrown. Signals transmitted via a 2-beam mechanical linkage. Bore distortion measured at speeds up to 6000 rpm and compared at static cold/clamped/motoring/firing conditions. Maximum bore distortion 86 µm at 6000 rpm full load. Dominant distortion is 2nd order (thermal, siamesed bore effect) which increases with speed/load; 4th order (bolt clamping) is near-maximum at cold clamped and does not increase much with speed; combustion pressure contributes only ~7 µm.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates with 10 transducers (first to exceed 8-point FEV technique) that siamesed-bore 2.0L engines are dominated by 2nd-order thermal bore distortion (up to 37 µm RMS) far exceeding the bolt-induced 4th order (≤5 µm RMS at speed); argues that dominant 2nd-order distortion may actually be less harmful for ring conformability than equivalent-amplitude higher-order distortions (rings conform to 2nd order more easily), and that siamesed bores may not necessarily hurt sealing efficiency.

## Key concepts
- 10 inductive eddy-current sensors (Micro-Epsilon) in invar carrier on piston undercrown; 36° spacing; probes emerge through 2nd ring land apertures (ring removed)
- 2-beam mechanical linkage for cable routing; big-end attached; operates to 6000 rpm
- Invar carrier: minimises thermal expansion; carrier temperature monitored by 2 thermistors
- Calibration: BDC reference grooves (0.185 mm depth); static calibration ring at room temperature; in-engine static check vs Incometer (agreement ≤1.8 µm)
- Fourier orders 0–4: 0 = diameter change, 1 = eccentricity, 2 = oval, 3 = 3-lobe, 4 = 4-lobe
- Siamesed bores: no inter-bore coolant → thermal constraint → expansion absorbed as 2nd order (oval) at 90° to longitudinal axis
- 4th order from bolt pattern; 3rd order from intermediate thermal effects; combustion pressure contribution ~7 µm
- Ring conformability: rings more easily conform to 2nd order than to same-amplitude 3rd or 4th order

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Engine: 2.0L I4, siamesed bores, 85 mm bore, cast iron block, aluminium head, DOHC 4-valve; cylinder 3 instrumented; cylinder 2 thermocoupled (9 TCs) for thermal modelling.
- Static free state: 5 µm peak-to-peak (from transducers), consistent with Incometer (6.8 µm range).
- Cold clamped: 20 µm p-p; 4th order 3.34 µm RMS; 2nd order 5.58 µm RMS.
- Motoring (50 rpm with hot fluids): 23.6 µm range; 3rd order increases.
- 3000 rpm half load: 48.5 µm range; 2nd order 18.2 µm RMS.
- 4500 rpm full load: 83.8 µm range; 2nd order 35.1 µm RMS.
- 6000 rpm full load: 86.1 µm range; 2nd order 37.0 µm RMS; 4th order 4.84 µm RMS.
- Combustion pressure effect: TDC firing minus TDC non-firing at 6000 rpm = ~7 µm; smaller than expected because head clamping stiffens bore wall against pressure.
- 4th order at clamped: 3.34–3.55 µm RMS; minimal further increase at speed — bolt clamping only, temperature-independent.
- 3rd order: increases with bulk temperature then decreases as 2nd order dominates at high speed.
- Key interpretation: siamesed bores produce large 2nd-order thermal ovality because the rigid inter-bore wall forces thermal expansion to occur perpendicular to engine axis; rings have best conformability to 2nd order → siamesed bores may not be detrimental to ring sealing despite large absolute distortion.
- Bore-liner relevance: most comprehensive fired bore distortion dataset for a Siamese-bore production engine; establishes relative magnitudes of thermal (2nd), bolt-clamping (4th), and combustion pressure contributions; critical reference for ring conformability and bore distortion measurement methodology.
