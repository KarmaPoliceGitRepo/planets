## Overview
Experimental and analytical study of piston ring friction behaviour in a small utility two-stroke engine, specifically accounting for piston secondary motion. Investigates how lateral piston displacement and tilt affect the ring–liner friction characteristics.

## Unique contribution
Applies piston secondary motion analysis to a small utility two-stroke engine context, which is less commonly studied than automotive four-stroke engines. Provides experimental friction measurements coupled with secondary motion modelling.

## Key concepts
- Piston secondary motion (lateral and tilt)
- Piston ring friction; two-stroke engine
- Ring-liner tribology
- Frictional losses in small engines

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Combines floating-liner friction measurement with piston secondary motion modelling.
- Results show secondary motion has measurable effect on ring friction, particularly at reversal points.
