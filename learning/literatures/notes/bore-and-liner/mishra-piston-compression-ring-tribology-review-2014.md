# A Review of Piston Compression Ring Tribology

- **Source:** Tribology in Industry 36(3), pp. 269–280 (2014)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Cb_9NeEkfjBANtPwZGnf3HQpyl2sqc4v/view
- **Type:** paper (review)
- **Author/Year:** Mishra, P.C. (KIIT University, Green Engine Technology Center) / 2014 (Tribol Ind 36(3))
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Literature review of piston compression ring tribology covering simulation and experimental methods for ring-liner contact friction analysis. Structured around: ring dynamics and deformation (elastic ring beam theory, natural frequencies, 3D distortions); ring-bore conformability (Dunaevsky, Hill/Newman, Tomanik criteria); lubrication principles (Reynolds equation, piezo-viscosity, EHD); mixed regime and surface texture effects; experimental methods. Includes the author's own tribodynamic model results showing 57% friction loss in power stroke, minimum film thickness 0.5 µm at TDC, rough liner reduces friction through micro-conjunctions.

## Unique contribution
Broad survey consolidating ring tribology literature from 1983–2014 into a single accessible reference; includes author's EHD model formulation (Reynolds equation, Dowson-Higginson viscosity-pressure, density-pressure, friction force integration) with results showing stroke-by-stroke friction and film thickness; identifies gap between simulation and experiment in ring tribology.

## Key concepts
- Compression ring: 80% of piston subsystem friction; sealing + sliding; outward-springing split-ring
- Ring dynamics: vibration of incomplete circular ring (natural frequency, mode shapes); Lang, Den Hartog, Archer, Love
- Piston secondary motion: eccentric gudgeon pin offset reduces TDC slap
- Ring-bore conformability: Dunaevsky criterion; Hill & Newman piston ring design for friction; Tomanik SAE 960356; Abe & Suzuki 950541
- Lubrication: Reynolds equation (2D transient average flow); piezo-viscosity (Dowson-Higginson); density-pressure correlation; cavitation (Reynolds/Swift-Stieber BC)
- Mixed lubrication: Patir-Cheng average flow model; stochastic roughness; rougher bore → less viscous friction (micro-conjunctions)
- Surface texturing: laser dimpling (Etsion, Kligerman); plateau honing; DLC coatings
- EHD result: max film ~5 µm compression/power; minimum ~0.5 µm at TDC; viscosity increases 200% via piezo-viscous action in power stroke
- Friction loss by stroke: power 57%, compression 26%, combined 80% of total cycle friction
- Rough liner: more oil in suction/exhaust, less friction in power/compression than smooth liner

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Reynolds equation solved via FDM with Newton-Raphson relaxation; Swift-Stieber exit boundary condition; load convergence with film relaxation and damping factor.
- Piezo-viscosity: Roelands-Dowson formula; density-pressure: Dowson-Higginson correlation.
- Friction force: integration of shear stress over contact area; power loss F×U.
- Simulation for all 720° crank locations; comparison of smooth vs rough liner.
- Rough liner: higher oil accumulation in suction/exhaust but less friction in power/compression due to micro-conjunction effect reducing viscous shear.
- Ring conformability: signed pressure criterion (positive everywhere = full conformability; negative = seal breach at that location).
- Review gap: experimental validation of ring tribology models is limited; non-contact and nano-sensing measurement methods not yet implemented.
- Bore-liner relevance: survey of ring-liner contact mechanics, conformability criteria, and lubrication regime theory applicable to bore distortion effects on ring performance; connects Dunaevsky/Tomanik conformability with lubrication consequences.
