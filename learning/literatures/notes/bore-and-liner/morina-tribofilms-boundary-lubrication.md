# Surface and Tribological Characteristics of Tribofilms Formed in the Boundary Lubrication Regime

## Overview
Tribological and surface-analytical study of ZDDP antiwear tribofilm formation under boundary lubrication using a reciprocating pin-on-plate (cast iron pin, bearing steel plate) tribometer. Tests vary temperature (20–100 °C), lambda ratio (0.02–1.25), and lubricant formulation (with/without ZDDP, no friction modifier). Post-test characterisation by AFM, EDX and XPS reveals layered tribofilm structure and how ZDDP interacts with Ca detergents.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that starting lambda ratio and temperature jointly control ZDDP tribofilm thickness and composition; at low λ, ZDDP thickens friction but reduces wear; at high λ, ZDDP produces thicker Ca-phosphate-rich films with lower friction than no-AW oil. Shows that the standard λ metric is incomplete without accounting for temperature-driven film kinetics.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Low λ (0.02–0.126), 100 °C, 8 h: ZDDP film develops progressively (Zn, P, S content increases with time), friction rises with time due to surface roughening; no-AW oil shows lower friction but higher wear.
- High λ (0.74–1.25): ZDDP film thicker (Ca phosphate incorporated from detergent), friction lower than no-AW oil—interaction with Ca detergent is key.
- Temperature is critical: more film at 100 °C than 50 °C or 20 °C; Ca only detected by XPS after etching at low λ but prominent at high λ.
- AFM confirms ZDDP film is rougher than no-AW film, consistent with friction increase at low λ.
- Practical implication for engine: lubricant additive package performance is regime-dependent; boundary contacts at TDC reversal are the most critical for ZDDP effectiveness.
