# Tribology of Compression Ring-to-Cylinder Contact at Reversal

- **Source:** 2005 Tribology of compression ring-to-cylinder
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1KFFzygkrXn8KS0dw3UIUG6TdTXH_K4aw/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Mishra, Balakrishnan, Rahnejat / 2008 (Proc IMechE Part J, 222(7))
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Lubrication analysis of the compression ring–cylinder contact during piston reversal at TDC, coupling global in-plane ring deformation (Okamoto–Sakai beam model) with local EHD contact analysis (quasi-static Reynolds equation series). Applied to a Honda CRF450R single-cylinder racing engine. Results show that EHD pressures are insufficient to cause significant local deformation but alter lubricant viscosity by an order of magnitude; minimum film thickness falls to 0.04–0.09 µm in the reversal window, suggesting mixed/boundary lubrication.

## Unique contribution
Integrates global ring radial deformation (including ring end-gap changes with combustion pressure) with local EHD analysis for the first time in a consistent quasi-static framework. Validates in-plane deformation model against both Okamoto–Sakai literature data and in-situ CMM measurements of fitted ring shape.

## Key concepts
- Piston ring global deformation (in-plane, beam model)
- EHD lubrication; squeeze film effect
- Compression ring tribology at TDC reversal
- Ring conformability; ring end-gap
- Piezo-viscous rigid regime
- Lambda ratio (λ = hmin/σ) at reversal
- Ring tension; combustion pressure
- Okamoto–Sakai ring deformation model

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Global ring deformation: end-gap closes from 10 mm free to 0.1 mm fitted; changes by ~1 µm under combustion pressure variation.
- Film thickness at TDC reversal: 0.04–0.09 µm; composite roughness σ = 0.16 µm → λ < 1 → mixed/boundary lubrication.
- Viscosity rises by ~10× under EHD pressure (Roelands equation), making contact effectively piezo-viscous rigid.
- Squeeze film provides only a small supplementary film at TDC; entraining velocity zero at reversal.
- Conclusion: significant asperity interactions occur at TDC over ~25% of ring face-width in circumferential direction.
- Future extensions needed: asperity model, thermal effects, surface features (texturing) to promote boundary film retention.
