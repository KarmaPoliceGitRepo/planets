# The Effect of Three-Dimensional Deformations of a Cylinder Liner on the Tribological Performance of a Piston Ring–Cylinder Liner System

- **Source:** Proc IMechE Part J: J Engineering Tribology 228(10), pp. 1080–1087 (2014)
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1jqvN7codiDWo665OH0-5kfECfRaVwjVz/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Söderfjäll, Isaksson, Spencer, Almqvist, Larsson / 2014 (Proc IMechE Part J 228(10), pp. 1080–1087; Luleå University of Technology / Scania CV AB)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Numerical simulation study of a twin-land oil control ring running against an out-of-round cylinder liner in a heavy duty diesel engine (130 mm bore, 160 mm stroke). Uses a full 3D COMSOL model of the ring with the Luleå Mixed Lubrication Model (LMLM) incorporating real surface topography, homogenized Reynolds equation, and Boussinesq-type asperity contact. Studies orders 2–5 of bore out-of-roundness and how friction and ring-liner separation change with ring tension.

## Unique contribution
First full 3D oil control ring model (not beam elements) coupled with real surface topography correction factors and 3D bore distortion. Shows that oil control ring friction is 78% higher with standard acceptable out-of-roundness than a perfectly round bore — quantifying the friction penalty of bore imperfection. Provides friction reduction potential table for reducing bore out-of-roundness.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Engine: HDD 130 mm bore, 160 mm stroke; mid-stroke 1100 rpm (piston speed 9.6 m/s); oil 10W30 at 95°C, µ = 0.0104 Pa·s.
- Ring geometry: twin-land oil control ring; 250 µm land width; parabolic profile, 1.2 µm crown height; fully flooded assumption; half-Sommerfeld cavitation.
- Order effects: 2nd and 3rd order have negligible effect on separation for amplitudes up to 50 µm; 4th order causes significant separation increase; 5th order causes even larger effects (ring loses conformity at reduced tension).
- Friction decomposition: as bore out-of-roundness increases, hydrodynamic friction decreases (larger gaps at concave regions) but boundary friction increases (higher contact at convex regions) — net increase in total friction.
- Maximum recommended distortion (AVL Glide): order 2 = 32.5 µm; order 3 = 20.8 µm; order 4 = 15.6 µm; order 5 = 7.8 µm; order 6 = 5.2 µm; order 8 = 3.9 µm.
- Key result: with standard out-of-roundness (100% of AVL limits), friction is 78% higher (16.4 N vs 9.2 N for perfectly round bore) to maintain 1.5 µm average separation.
- Friction reduction potential: reducing out-of-roundness to 20% of AVL limits allows ring tension reduction sufficient to cut friction to 9.4 N (from 16.4 N) — a 43% friction reduction.
- Practical guidance: bore roundness improvement is one of the most effective levers for oil control ring friction reduction alongside ring tension reduction.
