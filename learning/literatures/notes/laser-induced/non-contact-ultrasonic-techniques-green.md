## Overview
A review article from Johns Hopkins University surveying the landscape of non-contact acoustic and ultrasonic techniques developed and applied at the author's center over roughly 40 years. The paper covers optical interferometric detection, holographic imaging, electromagnetic acoustic transducers (EMATs), air-coupled systems, and hybrid laser-generation / air-coupled-detection configurations, illustrated with applications ranging from artistic paintings to railroad infrastructure and nuclear reactor steel.

## Unique contribution
Provides a multi-decade practitioner's perspective on real industrial deployment of non-contact ultrasound, with detailed examples for: full-field holographic wavefront imaging in composite panels, railroad rail and wheel inspection using laser/air-coupled hybrid systems, and a novel prospective application — remote, in-situ ultrasonic detection of neutron irradiation embrittlement in nuclear reactor pressure vessels.

## Key concepts
- Laser generation of ultrasound (thermoelastic, ablation, radiation pressure)
- Optical interferometric detection
- Holographic interferometry (ultrasonic wavefront imaging)
- Electromagnetic acoustic transducers (EMATs)
- Air(gas)-coupled ultrasonic transducers
- Hybrid laser-generation / air-coupled detection
- Spatial modulation (toneburst narrowband surface waves)
- Railroad rail and wheel inspection (non-contact)
- Acoustic emission (optical detection)
- Ultrasonic attenuation (radiation embrittlement monitoring)
- Capacitive air-coupled transducer

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Three generation mechanisms ranked by efficiency: radiation pressure (negligible) < thermoelastic (non-destructive, moderate amplitude) < ablation (highest amplitude, slight surface damage acceptable in some cases).
- Holographic pulsed interferometry: full-field image of propagating wavefront revealed anisotropic layup and a delamination in a graphite-epoxy composite panel.
- EMATs: work on electrically conducting materials only; efficiency drops with lift-off; better as detectors than generators.
- Laser generation / air-coupled detection for composite tape-lay processing: optical fibre-delivered Nd:YAG (10 ns, 20 Hz); spatial modulation mask controls toneburst frequency (penetration depth); capacitive transducer 100 kHz–2 MHz; detected two-ply-deep poor consolidation in thermoplastic composite.
- Railroad inspection: laser/air-coupled system operated several inches from rail surface, immune to rivets and protrusions; successfully demonstrated at US government rail test track. Separate system for wheel flange cracking using 0°/90° laser generation and detection geometry.
- Radiation embrittlement: historical correlation between ultrasonic attenuation and neutron irradiation in steel established since 1950; proposed new laser technique for in-situ, remote, non-contact attenuation measurements during irradiation.
- Key advantage across all applications: non-contact, standoff distance, usable in hostile environments (hot, corrosive, radioactive, vacuum).
