## Overview
A standard undergraduate textbook on mechanics of materials (also called strength of materials), covering the fundamental relationships between stress, strain, and deformation in structural members. Includes both US customary and SI unit treatments. The 6th edition is widely used in mechanical, civil, and aerospace engineering programmes worldwide.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive and pedagogically polished treatment with worked examples, extensive problem sets, and clear physical motivation. The conversion table front matter alone (U.S. to SI) is a practical reference. Covers classical topics from axial load and torsion through beam bending, deflection, columns, and combined loading — unified by Hooke's law and energy methods.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Presents derivations from first principles with careful attention to sign conventions and boundary conditions.
- Covers thin-walled pressure vessels (hoop and axial stress), thick-walled cylinders (Lamé equations), and stress concentration factors.
- Includes conversion factors between USCS and SI throughout; e.g., 1 psi = 6890 Pa, 1 ksi = 6.89 MPa.
- The failure theories section covers maximum-normal-stress, maximum-shear-stress (Tresca), and von Mises criteria.
- Buckling: Euler critical load Pcr = π²EI/L_e², with effective length L_e depending on end conditions.
- Strain energy methods lead to Castigliano's theorem for deflection calculations in complex structures.
- Practical gotcha: the book reinforces that the flexure formula σ = My/I is only valid within the elastic range and for prismatic beams with symmetric cross-sections.
