## Overview
The second part of a two-volume introduction to mechanical engineering for UK undergraduate students. Builds on Part 1 with more advanced treatments of fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, solid mechanics, and electromechanical drive systems. The solid mechanics unit (Unit 3) is the most relevant to this folder: it covers combined loading, yield criteria, beam deflection by multiple methods, elastic-plastic behaviour, elastic instability, thick cylinders, shear stresses in beams, asymmetrical bending, strain energy, fatigue, fracture mechanics, and thermal stresses.

## Unique contribution
Integrates structural, thermal, and materials content into a single pedagogical volume targeting second-year undergraduate level. The solid mechanics unit is more advanced than Part 1, covering elastic-plastic analysis (residual stresses after yielding), fracture mechanics fundamentals (stress intensity factors, LEFM), and fatigue assessment — topics usually treated as standalone advanced courses. The electromechanical drive systems unit (Unit 4) is unusual for a general mechanical engineering text, covering DC motors, inverter-fed induction motors, gearing, and rectified supplies.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Combined loading: principal stresses via Mohr's circle or eigenvalue approach; stress on oblique planes using transformation equations.
- Yield criteria: Tresca (maximum shear stress) τ_max = (σ₁ − σ₃)/2 ≤ σ_y/2; von Mises (distortion energy) σ₁² − σ₁σ₂ + σ₂² ≤ σ_y² (in 2D biaxial).
- Elastic-plastic beams: plastic hinge forms when full cross-section yields; moment at full plasticity M_p = σ_y × Z_p (plastic section modulus); M_p = 1.5 M_y for rectangular sections.
- Fracture mechanics: K_I = σ√(πa)·Y where Y is geometry factor; fracture when K_I = K_IC (material toughness). Mode I (opening), Mode II (sliding), Mode III (tearing).
- Fatigue: Goodman criterion S_a/S_e + S_m/S_u = 1 where S_a = alternating stress, S_m = mean stress, S_e = endurance limit, S_u = UTS.
- Thick cylinders section covers same Lamé derivation as companion document in this folder, now embedded in the broader curriculum context.
