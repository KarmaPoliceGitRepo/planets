## Overview
This open-access paper from Jönköping University measures the coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE) and density of Compacted Graphite Iron (CGI) and Spheroidal Graphite Iron (SGI) in the temperature range 25–500 °C (600 °C for cycling tests) using a push-rod dilatometer. The study investigates how microstructural variables — graphite morphology (nodularity), graphite area fraction, and pearlite/ferrite ratio — affect thermophysical properties, with motivation from automotive powertrain components requiring tight dimensional tolerances.

## Unique contribution
Develops an empirical multivariate regression equation for CTE as a function of nodularity (N), graphite area fraction (G), temperature (T), and pearlite/ferrite ratio (R_P/F), with R² = 0.87. Also documents thermal cycling effects on volume stability: ferritic SGI shows volume decrease due to carbon filling of graphite pores, while pearlitic SGI shows initial volume increase from local graphitization of cementite bridges, with behaviour eventually stabilising after ~15 cycles. Provides uncertainty budget (GUM method) for CTE measurements: combined standard uncertainty ~6.39 × 10⁻⁸ K⁻¹.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Five alloys tested: three SGIs (one mixed ferritic-pearlitic, two fully ferritic with different Si contents) and two CGIs (both fully ferritic with high Si).
- Dominant factor for CTE in ferritic matrices: graphite area fraction (higher graphite → lower CTE, since graphite CTE << matrix). In ferritic-pearlitic matrices: ferrite/pearlite ratio dominates over graphite amount.
- CTE equation: CTE = 1.38×10⁻⁵ + 5.38×10⁻⁸N − 5.85×10⁻⁷G + 1.85×10⁻⁸T − 2.41×10⁻⁶R_P/F − 1.28×10⁻⁸NG − 2.97×10⁻⁷GR_P/F + 4.65×10⁻⁹TR_P/F + 1.08×10⁻⁷G² − 4.80×10⁻¹¹T²
- Room temperature densities: SGI 7.02–7.11 g·cm⁻³, CGI 7.04–7.06 g·cm⁻³ (density decreases with temperature due to thermal expansion).
- Contrary to literature, measured CGI CTE was sometimes higher than SGI — attributed to non-comparable compositions between sample sets.
- Practical implication: CGI with high Si content may offer more robust manufacturing but presents complex Si-CTE interaction; thermal conductivity trade-offs remain an open research question.
