## Overview
An introductory undergraduate textbook for mechanical engineering, covering the core disciplines in Part 1: thermodynamics, solid mechanics (stress, strain, beam bending, torsion), fluid mechanics, and electrical/electronic systems. Published by Hodder Education, it is designed to accompany a first-year UK mechanical engineering degree course.

## Unique contribution
Integrates four main engineering disciplines (solid mechanics, thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, electrical systems) into a single coherent Part 1 text, explicitly bridging basic physics to engineering analysis. Covers design analysis and materials selection in the opening chapter before diving into mechanics — a curriculum-aligned approach that links "why" to "how" earlier than most textbooks.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Solid mechanics sections start with uniaxial tension test definitions: σ = F/A₀, ε = δL/L₀, E = σ/ε (linear elastic regime), followed by Poisson's ratio ν = −ε_lateral/ε_axial.
- Beam bending derivation: neutral axis at centroid for symmetric sections; flexure formula σ = My/I; shear stress τ = VQ/Ib.
- Torsion: τ = Tr/J where J = πd⁴/32 for circular shaft; angle of twist θ = TL/(GJ).
- Thin-walled vessels: hoop stress σ_h = Pr/t; axial stress σ_a = Pr/2t; applies for r/t > 10.
- Thermodynamics: covers first and second laws, entropy, Carnot efficiency η_max = 1 − T_cold/T_hot; specific coverage of steam cycles (Rankine) and gas turbines (Brayton).
- Fluid mechanics: laminar vs. turbulent flow, drag force F_D = C_D × ½ρv²A; pipe friction using Darcy-Weisbach equation.
- Design analysis chapter introduces material property charts (Ashby-style) for material selection — linking performance indices to geometry and loading scenarios.
