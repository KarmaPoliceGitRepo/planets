## Overview
A conference paper from the University of Windsor's Industrial Research Chair in Applied Solid State Physics, presenting pulse-echo ultrasonic monitoring of epoxy adhesive cure in real time. The study tracks longitudinal velocity, elastic modulus, and density changes throughout the curing reaction, and also uses acoustic microscopy to visualize microstructural evolution at the adhesive-metal interface. The effect of varying the epoxy resin/hardener ratio on cure dynamics is quantified.

## Unique contribution
Combines bulk acoustic wave monitoring (15 MHz focused transducer in pulse-echo mode) with high-resolution acoustic microscopy C-scans of the adhesive interface, providing both real-time bulk property data and spatial visualization of microstructural changes — including detection of overcured and undercured regions. The study explicitly quantifies how off-ratio mixing (excess or deficient hardener) degrades final acoustic parameters and adhesion strength.

## Key concepts
- Pulse-echo ultrasonic monitoring
- Longitudinal sound velocity in curing polymers
- Elastic modulus from acoustic velocity
- Gelation point
- Postgel crosslinking

- Acoustic microscopy / C-scan imaging
- Ultrasonic attenuation as cure state indicator
- Transmission-mode imaging of cured vs. uncured zones
- Shrinkage monitoring via time-of-flight

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Test cell:** 20 mm diameter × 3 mm thick reaction chamber; PET film separates coupling medium (water-glycerin) from epoxy; spherically focused 15 MHz immersion transducer in pulse-echo mode.
- **Velocity calculation:** longitudinal velocity derived from sample thickness (corrected for shrinkage via t1 signal drift) and time-of-flight difference between PET-film and adhesive-metal interface echoes.
- **Cure dynamics:** velocity follows a sigmoidal curve; gelation is marked by near-complete disappearance of the metal-interface echo and a change in the velocity-time slope.
- **Ratio effects (1/1, 1/2, 1/0.5 epoxy/hardener):**
  - Control (1/1): cure ~20 min, final v ≈ 2230 m/s, E ≈ 5.47 GPa, gelation ~12 min.
  - Excess hardener (1/2): earlier gelation (~10 min), lower final velocity (2165 m/s) and modulus (5.16 GPa).
  - Deficient hardener (1/0.5): slower cure (~25 min), intermediate final values.
- **In-situ cure at 180°C:** velocity peaks around 22 min and plateaus — used to define complete cure.
- **Attenuation imaging:** transmission C-scan shows 30% amplitude contrast between cured and uncured zones; time-of-flight maps less sensitive.
- **Acoustic microscopy of interface:** uncured adhesive shows no structured interface image; 5 min cure produces granular structure; 30 min gives full network; overcuring (200°C) produces fragmented image with elevated reflection amplitudes indicating microdefects.
