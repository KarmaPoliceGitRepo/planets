## Overview
Studies the effect of coupling layer thickness on the reproducibility of ultrasonic TOF measurements. Uses a 1D transducer model combined with Fresnel–Kirchhoff diffraction theory and a viscoelastic couplant model to predict waveforms as a function of coupling layer thickness, and validates with experiments.

## Unique contribution
Identifies a critical coupling layer thickness (~80–90 µm) below which multiple reflections within the couplant layer severely distort the waveform and cause large TOF errors; above this threshold, a reproducibility of 1:20,000 is achievable. Also quantifies the effect of transducer tilt on TOF shift.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: 1D piezoelectric transducer model → Fresnel-Kirchhoff diffraction integral → viscoelastic layer propagation.
- First zero-crossing of received waveform used as TOF reference (more reproducible than peak or overlap methods for this setup).
- Below ~80–90 µm coupling layer: reflections within layer create interference → large, non-monotonic TOF errors.
- Above ~80–90 µm: TOF becomes smooth and reproducible; ratio 1:20,000.
- Transducer tilt <1° with 100 µm coupling layer gives <0.2 ns TOF shift for shear waves.
- Practical recommendation: ensure coupling layer thickness is controlled above critical threshold for precision measurements.
