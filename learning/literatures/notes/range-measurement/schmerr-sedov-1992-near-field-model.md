# A Near-Field Measurement Model for Ultrasonic Reference Standards

## Overview
Derives an analytical near-field pulse-echo measurement model for ultrasonic inspection in fluid media. The model superimposes plane wave contributions using the T-matrix framework and applies the high-frequency stationary phase approximation to express the received voltage as a sum of four far-field scattering amplitude terms. The far-field limit reproduces the standard Thompson-Gray model.

## Unique contribution
Extends the Thompson-Gray far-field measurement model into the near field by including diffraction effects through a stationary phase integration over the angular spectrum. Derives explicit results for cylindrical and spherical rigid and Kirchhoff scatterers.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: ⟨vz⟩ = sum of 4 terms, each proportional to a far-field scattering amplitude f(0,n) evaluated at different angles and weighted by beam diffraction factors.
- Stationary phase approximation applied to angular spectrum integral → retains accuracy in near field without full numerical integration.
- Far-field limit: reduces to Thompson-Gray model (single scattering amplitude f(0,0)).
- Special cases derived analytically: cylindrical scatterer (rigid, Kirchhoff), spherical scatterer (rigid, Kirchhoff).
- Model useful for calibrating inspection setups where transducer is in the near field of reference reflectors.
- Shows that standard far-field models over-predict or under-predict signal amplitude when transducer–reflector distance is in the near field.
