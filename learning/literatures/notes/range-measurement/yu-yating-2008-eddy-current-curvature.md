# Research on Effect of Measured Surface Curvature on Eddy Current Evaluation

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
A finite element method (FEM) study of how target surface curvature (convex and concave spheroidal and cylindrical shapes) affects the impedance response of an eddy current sensor coil, for both non-magnetic and magnetic conductive materials.

## Unique contribution
Provides FEM-derived quantitative threshold curves showing at what radius-to-coil-size ratio the curvature effect on ECS impedance becomes negligible, separately for convex and concave geometries and for magnetic vs. non-magnetic materials.

## Key concepts
- Eddy current sensor (ECS)
- Surface curvature effect
- FEM (finite element method) for ECS
- Non-magnetic conductive target
- Magnetic conductive target
- Convex surface / concave surface
- Coil impedance (resistance, inductance)
- Spheroidal geometry
- Cylindrical geometry

## Methods / results / takeaways
- FEM model of coil over curved target; varied: surface radius, coil outer radius, material type.
- **Non-magnetic material — convex surface**: increasing convexity → coil resistance increases, inductance decreases.
- **Non-magnetic material — concave surface**: opposite trend (resistance decreases, inductance increases).
- **Magnetic material**: convex vs. concave show different behaviour from non-magnetic.
- Curvature effect becomes negligible when:
  - Convex spheroid: R_surface / R_coil_outer > ~15
  - Concave spheroid: R_surface / R_coil_outer > ~30
  - Cylindrical surfaces: similar thresholds to spheroid.
- Below these thresholds, curvature must be corrected for accurate distance/displacement measurement on curved parts.
