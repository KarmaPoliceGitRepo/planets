# Characteristics of a Pneumatic Proximity Sensor Using a Swirling Jet

- **Source:** 1996 - Characteristics of pneumatic proximity sensor using a swirling jet - Hisaki Shimizu.pdf
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1uFnF-hKcgOkOoskuRI1Q8ZPmWhjDURKr/view

- **Author/Year:** Hisaki Shimizu / 1996
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Describes the B-type pneumatic proximity sensor, which adds a swirl chamber to the standard A-type sensor nozzle to extend the effective sensing range and improve pressure output. The paper characterises the sensing range, output pressure, linearity, and air consumption against the A-type baseline.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that adding a swirl chamber extends the jet attachment range from 2 mm to 5 mm and increases output pressure by 1.5×, while combining with a Low-Pressure Amplifier (LPA) achieves a total supply pressure of only 12.4 kPa — about 1/8 of commercial sensors — with air consumption 1/7 of commercial designs.

## Key concepts
- Pneumatic proximity sensor

- Air consumption
- Non-contact sensing

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **A-type (baseline)**: jet extends to ~2 mm effective sensing range.
- **B-type (swirl chamber added)**: jet extends to ~5 mm; output pressure 1.5× higher.
- Linear measurement range: 0.5–4 mm.
- Combined B-type + LPA (pressure gain ~12): total supply pressure = 12.4 kPa (vs. ~100 kPa for commercial sensors).
- Air consumption: 79 × 10⁻⁶ m³/s (1/7 of commercial).
- Frequency cutoff (dynamic response): ~15 Hz.
- Practical for applications requiring low-pressure, extended-range pneumatic proximity sensing.
