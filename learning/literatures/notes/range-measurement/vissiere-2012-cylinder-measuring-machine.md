## Overview
Presents the design and mathematical methods for a novel cylinder-form measuring machine developed at LNE (French national metrology laboratory) targeting nanometric accuracy. The machine uses the 'dissociated metrological technique' principle with multiple reference probes and a reference cylinder to separate spindle motion errors from the true form error of the artefact.

## Unique contribution
Implements a modified multi-step error separation method applied to a new machine architecture that dissociates the metrological frame from the support structure, aiming for 5 nm measurement uncertainty on cylindrical and spherical artefacts — beyond the ~25 nm repeatability limit of conventional air-bearing spindle machines.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Conventional roundness machines limited by spindle repeatability (>25 nm for air bearing spindles).
- New machine architecture: metrological loop does NOT pass through support structure → isolates form measurement from vibration and thermal effects.
- Two probe sets: one sensing the artefact, one sensing the reference cylinder.
- Form errors extracted by mathematical combination (modified multi-step separation) of both probe signals.
- Target: 5 nm measurement uncertainty for cylindrical and spherical artefacts.
