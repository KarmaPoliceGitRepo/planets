# Acoustic Characterisation of Liquid Foams with a Non-Contact Method

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Presents an air-coupled ultrasonic method using capacitance transducers to characterise the acoustic properties (phase velocity and attenuation as functions of frequency) of liquid foams without contact. An inversion method extracts the complex wavenumber k from the measured transmission coefficient T.

## Unique contribution
Non-contact air-coupled measurement of liquid foam acoustics over 60–600 kHz using thin-walled PET-cell samples; resolves a 2π phase ambiguity in the inversion by requiring zero phase shift at zero frequency; tracks foam bubble size evolution over time through acoustic dispersion.

## Key concepts
- Air-coupled ultrasound
- Capacitance transducers
- Liquid foam acoustics
- Complex wavenumber inversion
- Phase velocity dispersion
- Attenuation dispersion
- Wood's law (low-frequency limit)
- Bubble size characterisation
- Phase ambiguity (2π shift)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducers: BAT-1 and MicroAcoustic capacitance transducers, bandwidth 60–600 kHz.
- Sample cell: thin PET walls (3 µm thickness) to minimise wall effects.
- Inversion: solve for k from T = T_exp(k) by minimising residual; phase ambiguity resolved by requiring k→0 as f→0.
- Fresh foam (small bubbles ~13 µm): velocity ~40 m/s across frequency range (Wood's law regime), dispersive attenuation.
- Aged foam (larger bubbles ~65 µm at t = 120 min): dispersive velocity (Wood's law breaks down).
- Method tracks average bubble size evolution via acoustic dispersion curve shape.
- Demonstrates feasibility of non-contact, non-invasive foam acoustics characterisation.
