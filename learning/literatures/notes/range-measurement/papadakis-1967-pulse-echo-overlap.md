## Overview
A detailed treatment of the pulse-echo-overlap (PEO) method for precision ultrasonic phase velocity measurement. The technique drives an oscilloscope's x-axis at a frequency equal to the reciprocal of the round-trip travel time, causing successive echoes to overlap. The paper extends the method by deriving and applying diffraction phase corrections that account for the phase shift introduced by beam spreading near the transducer.

## Unique contribution
Derives analytic diffraction phase corrections for the PEO method and demonstrates that applying them reduces the standard deviation of phase velocity measurements by ~30%. Shows that correct cyclic matching (not leading-edge matching) is essential for accuracy, and provides the governing formula incorporating these corrections.

## Key concepts
- Pulse-echo-overlap (PEO) method
- Ultrasonic phase velocity
- Diffraction phase correction
- Cyclic matching vs. leading-edge matching
- Fresnel diffraction (near-field)
- Fused quartz, silicon as reference materials
- Transit-time precision

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Oscilloscope x-axis driven at frequency f = 1/(2t), where t is the one-way travel time, to superimpose echoes.
- Formula: T = pΔ − p′γ/360f + n/f, where p and p′ are integers, Δ is a time interval read from the overlap, γ is the diffraction phase correction in degrees, and n is a cycle-count integer.
- Proper matching is cyclic (matching waveform crests), not leading-edge matching, to avoid cycle-count errors.
- Diffraction phase corrections are significant in the near field; ignoring them introduces a systematic velocity error.
- Applying corrections reduces standard deviation of measured velocity by ~30%.
- Demonstrated on fused quartz and silicon specimens.
- Timing accuracy: ~0.2 ns.
