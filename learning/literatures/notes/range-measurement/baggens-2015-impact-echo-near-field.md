## Overview
Studies near-field effects on the Impact-Echo (IE) method for thickness estimation of concrete plate-like structures. Using FEM simulations (COMSOL) and field data, the paper identifies how near-field P-wave and Rayleigh-wave velocity deviations cause the IE method (and the combined MASW/IE method) to systematically underestimate thickness by 5–15%.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first systematic FEM-based quantification of near-field error in IE thickness estimation, showing that the near field creates a zone around the source where first-arrival velocity cannot be linked directly to the theoretical P-wave velocity — and that this zone size depends on plate properties and source frequency.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- IE method: h = βVP / (2fr), where fr = thickness resonance frequency (S1-ZGV), β ≈ 0.96 for concrete.
- Three plate parameters: VS, ν, h; Lamb wave theory needed for exact solution.
- **Near-field problem**: first arriving wave near source is a mix of P-wave and Rayleigh wave → picked first arrival velocity is lower than true VP.
- FEM (COMSOL) confirms: near source, VP and VR both appear lower than theoretical values.
- S1-ZGV frequency estimated accurately even in near field.
- Combined MASW/IE and conventional IE both give systematic thickness underestimation of ~5–15% depending on ν, measurement setup, and source pulse.
- Practical implication: receivers must be placed outside the near-field zone, or near-field correction must be applied.
- Validated against experimental data from ATREL test site (Univ. of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign).
