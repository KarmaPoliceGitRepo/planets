## Overview
A comprehensive review of optical techniques for non-contact distance and displacement measurement, covering the full range from interferometric (sub-nm) to triangulation and time-of-flight (meters) methods.

## Unique contribution
Broad systematic survey of optical ranging and displacement methods classified by operating principle, with coverage of practical tradeoffs in range, resolution, and target type requirements.

## Key concepts
- Interferometry (homodyne, heterodyne)
- Laser triangulation
- Time-of-flight (optical)
- Phase-shift measurement
- Confocal chromatic sensing
- Speckle methods
- Fringe projection / structured light
- Optical coherence tomography (OCT)
- Autofocus methods
- White-light interferometry

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Interferometry**: sub-nm resolution; limited range (requires fringe counting); sensitive to vibration.
- **Laser triangulation**: µm-to-mm resolution; range ~mm to ~1 m; depends on target surface reflectivity and angle.
- **Time-of-flight (optical)**: ns-to-ps timing; range cm to km; resolution limited by timing electronics.
- **Phase-shift**: hybrid of TOF and interferometry; unambiguous range set by modulation wavelength.
- **Confocal chromatic**: axial chromatic aberration used for Z-ranging; nm resolution; very short range.
- **White-light interferometry**: coherence gating for absolute depth; ~nm resolution; surface topography maps.
- Selection criteria: required range, resolution, surface type, speed, and environmental conditions.
