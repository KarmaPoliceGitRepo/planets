# A New Comparator for Measurement of Diameter and Form of Precision Cylindrical and Spherical Parts

## Overview
Describes a precision comparator instrument developed at PTB (Germany's national metrology institute) for simultaneous diameter and form measurement of cylindrical and spherical precision parts. The instrument applies the Abbe principle at each probing point and uses laser interferometers for displacement measurement.

## Unique contribution
Combines diameter and form measurement in a single setup with uncertainty approaching 10 nm over 100 mm diameter, using Zerodur (low-expansion glass-ceramic) form references and oil-damped probing systems with 1 nm resolution.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Two probing systems per measurement point, arranged to satisfy the Abbe (collinearity) principle.
- Measurement volume: 160 × 100 × 120 mm.
- Displacement measured by laser interferometers: 1.25 nm resolution.
- Probing systems: oil-damped, 300 Hz eigenfrequency, ±0.2 mm range, 1 nm resolution.
- Form references: Zerodur cylinders and spheres (near-zero thermal expansion).
- Verified uncertainty: ±20 nm on outer diameter per 100 mm.
- Target uncertainty: 10 nm per 100 mm outer diameter.
- Simultaneous diameter and form measurement eliminates re-chucking errors.
