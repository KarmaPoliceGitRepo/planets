- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Rigorous numerical study of the pressure field radiated by a circular piston transducer within the near field. Computes on-axis and off-axis pressure patterns for a/λ = 1 to 20 (radius/wavelength) and derives corrected boundaries for the near-field–far-field transition and the minimum spot size at focus.

## Unique contribution
Overturns the traditional near-field distance formula (Z_nf = a²/λ) by showing the actual boundary where the off-axis -3 dB beam becomes regularly collimated is Z = 0.75a²/λ, not a²/λ. Also shows minimum spot size is a/4, not a (as traditionally stated), and locates the last on-axis pressure maximum.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Numerical integration of Rayleigh–Sommerfeld integral for circular piston over a/λ = 1–20.
- Traditional near-field boundary (Z = a²/λ) is incorrect as a practical criterion.
- Actual boundary where -3 dB beam becomes regularly collimated: Z = 0.75 a²/λ.
- Last on-axis pressure maximum at Z_max ≈ 1 × a²/λ (limiting value as a/λ → ∞).
- Last on-axis pressure zero at Z_zero ≈ 0.5 × a²/λ.
- Minimum spot size: a/4 (at Z ≈ 0.75 a²/λ), not a.
- For a/λ > 13.3: far-field regular beam behaviour requires z > 10a.
- For a/λ < 13.3: far-field behaviour can be observed at closer distances.
- Results verified for a broad range of a/λ ratios; paper is standard reference for near-field characterisation.
