## Overview
Investigates how transducer diffraction effects cause systematic errors in the transit-time measurements of ultrasonic flow meters (USMs) used for natural gas custody transfer. Uses a plane-piston model and FEM of piezoelectric transducers to quantify the magnitude of diffraction-induced transit-time errors as a function of operational parameters, and evaluates consequences for volumetric, mass, and energy flow measurement accuracy.

## Unique contribution
Shows that diffraction corrections based on simplified models are insufficient for high-accuracy USMs, and that FEM-based transducer models are needed. Demonstrates that flow calibration can reduce volumetric flow errors from diffraction but NOT the velocity-of-sound (VOS) errors, which directly affect mass and energy flow accuracy.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Diffraction effects depend on: pressure, temperature, path length, gas composition (VOS), transducer diameter, frequency, vibration pattern, bandwidth, and time-detection method.
- Two detection regimes studied: transient (start of signal) and stationary (middle of signal) — diffraction corrections differ between them.
- FEM used to model realistic transducer vibration and radiation patterns (rather than ideal piston).
- For small-size meters (4"–12"): diffraction effects are highly significant; improved control strongly recommended.
- Flow calibration can compensate diffraction errors in volumetric flow measurement, but NOT in VOS measurement.
- VOS accuracy requirement: ±0.3 m/s (≈ ±0.075%) for density/calorific value determination.
- Methods for improved diffraction control are described (modified dry calibration procedures).
