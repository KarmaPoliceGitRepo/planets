## Overview
A comprehensive reference booklet for Cambridge's Engineering Tripos, collecting key material properties, formulae, equilibrium phase diagrams, heat treatment data, and process attribute charts. Serves as an exam and course reference for mechanical engineering students covering the full materials science curriculum.

## Unique contribution
Combines formulae (stress/strain, fracture mechanics, fatigue, creep, diffusion, heat flow), material property charts (Ashby maps: E vs. density, strength vs. density, etc.), phase diagrams for 9 binary alloy systems, TTT/Jominy hardenability curves for two steels, and process attribute charts (mass, section thickness, surface roughness, tolerance, batch size) — all in one compact reference.

## Key concepts
- Stress-strain (true and nominal), Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio
- Elastic moduli (G, K, E relationships)
- Composite stiffness and strength (Voigt/Reuss bounds)
- Dislocation mechanics and yield stress
- Fast fracture (stress intensity factor K, toughness G_IC)
- Weibull statistics of fracture
- Fatigue (Basquin, Coffin-Manson, Goodman, Miner, Paris)
- Power-law creep
- Diffusion (Fick's law, error function)
- Thermal diffusivity and Fourier heat flow
- Ashby material property charts
- Phase diagrams (Cu-Ni, Pb-Sn, Fe-C, Al-Cu, Al-Si, Cu-Zn, Cu-Sn, Ti-Al, SiO2-Al2O3)
- TTT diagrams and Jominy hardenability curves
- Process attribute charts (CES Granta Design database)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Material properties vary widely with composition and treatment — data is for initial selection only; use manufacturer data for final design.
- Ashby maps allow material index optimization: e.g., E^(1/2)/ρ for stiffness-to-weight, σ_f^(2/3)/ρ for strength-to-weight in bending.
- Error function table provided for 1D heat/diffusion problems.
- Characteristic diffusion distance: x ≈ √(Dt); heat flow distance: x ≈ √(at).
- Process charts from CES educational database (Level 2) — suitable for preliminary process selection; not for final specifications.
- Physical constants, unit conversions (stress, energy, power), and atomic data for selected elements included.
