## Key concepts
- Clearance fit (loose, running, slide/medium)
- Interference fit (shrink/heavy force, medium force, tight/press)
- Transition fit (push/snug, force/shrink, wringing)
- Hole-basis system vs. shaft-basis system
- Upper and lower limits, tolerance zone
- Unilateral tolerance vs. bilateral tolerance
- Geometrical tolerances (flatness, straightness, cylindricity, circularity)
- Tolerance of position (parallelism, perpendicularity, concentricity, symmetry)
- Angular tolerances

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Clearance: positive (hole > shaft) before assembly; interference: negative (shaft > hole); transition: tolerance zones overlap.
- Hole-basis system: lower deviation of hole = 0; shaft size is varied. More common because standard drills/reamers have fixed sizes.
- Shaft-basis system: upper deviation of shaft = 0; used when ground/drawn bars are used directly.
- Unilateral tolerance is preferred for interchangeable manufacture because Go gauges can be standardized.
- Worked example: H7/p6 interference fit at 50 mm basic size gives maximum interference of 42 µm (shaft HL – hole LL).
- Geometrical tolerances are specified in rectangular tolerance frames in addition to dimensional tolerances.
