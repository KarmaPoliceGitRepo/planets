## Overview
The full text of ASME Y14.5M-1994 — the primary US national standard for Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) on engineering drawings. Establishes symbols, definitions, rules, and conventions for expressing and interpreting tolerances of form, orientation, location, and runout.

## Unique contribution
This is the normative source document for GD&T in the United States. It codifies Rule #1 (Taylor Principle: form is controlled by size at MMC), Rule #2 (RFS as default), datum reference frames, material condition modifiers (MMC, LMC, RFS), and composite positional tolerancing. It is the reference for approximately 90% of major US corporations.

## Key concepts
- Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T)
- Rule #1 (envelope/Taylor principle)
- Maximum Material Condition (MMC), Least Material Condition (LMC), Regardless of Feature Size (RFS)
- Datum reference frame (primary, secondary, tertiary)
- Tolerance of form: flatness, straightness, circularity, cylindricity
- Tolerance of orientation: parallelism, perpendicularity, angularity
- Tolerance of location: true position, concentricity, symmetry
- Tolerance of runout: circular runout, total runout
- Profile tolerances (surface and line)
- Composite positional tolerancing
- Feature control frame
- Basic dimensions

## Methods / results / takeaways
- ASME Y14.5M-1994 uses a functional approach to tolerancing, with abundant illustrated examples throughout.
- Single comprehensive document (~232 pages) updated approximately every 10 years.
- Key difference from ISO: ASME defaults to Rule #1 (envelope principle for size/form control); ISO defaults to independency (size and form are separate).
- ASME uses third-angle projection; ISO uses first-angle.
- The standard includes specific rules for non-rigid parts (free-state variation), screw threads (pitch diameter rule), and statistical tolerancing.
- Full content was not extracted (file too large for tool); the above reflects the visible introduction and structure.
