## Overview
A concise technical guide introducing roundness metrology: why roundness matters, the limitations of diameter-based measurement, methods of measurement, reference circle types, roundness parameters, harmonic analysis, and Taylor Hobson instrument product overviews (Talyrond 100, 365, 450 series).

## Unique contribution
Provides a clear conceptual explanation of why diameter measurement alone is insufficient for roundness evaluation (using the British 50-pence coin example), and a practical table mapping harmonic content (undulations per revolution) to likely manufacturing causes — essential for process debugging.

## Key concepts
- Roundness (RONt, RONp, RONv)
- Reference circles: LSCI (Least Squares), MCCI (Minimum Circumscribed), MZCI (Minimum Zone), MICI (Maximum Inscribed)
- Vee-block (3-point) method vs. rotational datum method
- Undulations per revolution (upr) and filters (15, 50, 150, 500, 1500 upr)
- Eccentricity (ECC) and concentricity (CONC)
- Runout (TIR)
- Cylindricity (CYLt), flatness (FLTt), squareness (SQR), coaxiality (COAX)
- Harmonic analysis
- ISO roundness standards (ISO 12181, 12180, 12780, 12781, 1101)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- A component is round when all cross-section points are equidistant from a common center; measurement therefore requires spindle rotation and radial displacement sensing.
- Vee-block method gives unreliable results because it is sensitive to lobe spacing, vee angle, and phase — it cannot detect certain numbers of lobes at all.
- Internationally accepted filter cut-offs: 15, 50, 150, 500, and 1500 upr.
- Harmonic content guide: 0 upr = radius; 1 = eccentricity; 2 = ovality/tilt; 3–5 = clamping/manufacturing forces; 6–20 = chatter from lack of rigidity; 20–100 = process/tool marks; >100 = material effects.
- LSCI is the most commonly used reference circle; MCCI is used as a ring-gauge reference; MICI as a plug-gauge reference.
- Taylor Hobson instruments have been available since 1951 and range from shop-floor (Talyrond 100) to large-component (Talyrond 450, up to 1000 mm height / 1000 kg load).
