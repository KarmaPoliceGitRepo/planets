## Overview
Part A of NSK's comprehensive rolling bearing technical information catalog. Covers all aspects of rolling bearing selection and application: types and features, bearing selection, arrangement selection, size selection (including load ratings and life calculations), speed limits, boundary dimensions/identifiers, bearing tolerances, fits and internal clearances, preload, friction, lubrication, bearing materials, and shaft/housing design.

## Unique contribution
A definitive engineering reference for rolling bearing application. Distinguishes rolling bearings from plain bearings by low starting torque, low sensitivity to interruptions in lubrication, ability to support combined loads, and ease of interchangeability. Provides the NSK numbering system, tolerance classes, and fit selection guidance aligned with ISO standards.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Rolling bearings have low and consistent starting torque compared to plain bearings; this enables repeatable machine accuracy.
- Bearing life L10 (90% survival) is calculated from dynamic load rating C and applied load P: L10 = (C/P)^p × 10^6 rev.
- Internal clearance changes with fit (interference on shaft reduces clearance); selection must account for both thermal expansion and fit effects.
- Preloaded bearings (angular contact pairs) improve rigidity for precision machine tools but reduce life if over-preloaded.
