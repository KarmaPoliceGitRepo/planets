## Overview
A comprehensive material specification table for DEL Piezo's full product line of piezoelectric ceramic materials. The sheet covers 27 distinct material grades spanning Lead Metaniobate, Hard PZT, and Soft PZT families (DL-10 through DL-61HD), providing quantitative data on dielectric, piezoelectric, elastic, and time/temperature stability properties.

## Unique contribution
Provides a side-by-side comparison of all DEL Piezo grades in tabular form, making it easy to select a material based on coupling coefficients, piezoelectric charge/voltage constants, mechanical quality factors, and long-term stability. The HD (high-density) variants extend the range to exceptionally high dielectric constants (e.g., DL-61HD: ε33T = 6600).

## Key concepts
- Relative dielectric constant (free and clamped, planar and shear modes)
- Dielectric loss factor (tgδ)
- Coupling coefficients (Kp, K33, Kt, K31, K15)
- Piezoelectric charge constants (d33, d31, d15, dh)
- Piezoelectric voltage constants (g33, g31, g15, gh)
- Elastic piezoelectric constants (e, h)
- Frequency constants (Np, N33, Nt, N15, Nc, N31)
- Mechanical quality factors (Qpm, Qtm)
- Elastic compliance and stiffness constants (S, C, Y)
- Poisson's ratio
- Curie temperature
- Acoustic impedance
- Time stability and temperature stability of key parameters

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Hard PZT grades (DL-40 to DL-54HD) offer moderate d33 (145–730 pC/N) with high Qpm (65–1800), suited for sonar and resonant applications.
- Soft PZT grades (DL-60HD, DL-61HD) push d33 to 750–810 pC/N and ε33T up to 5500–6600 but with lower Qpm (10–15) and higher losses.
- Lead Metaniobate (DL-10, DL-20) has exceptionally high Qtm (650–3500) and low dielectric constant, useful for broadband transducers where mechanical damping is important.
- Bismuth Titanate (DL-21) shows high Curie temperature (480°C) making it suitable for high-temperature applications.
- Time stability shows that dielectric constant drifts on the order of –0.5 to –3.4% per decade for most grades.
