## Overview
A set of educational slides on fits and tolerances for mating mechanical parts. Covers the three general types of fit (clearance, transition, interference), ISO/ANSI tolerance-grade charts, preferred fit and range-of-fit tables, and preferred clearance fit tolerances. The content appears to be a companion to a mechanical design or metrology course.

## Unique contribution
Provides a compact visual reference for fit selection, complementing the more detailed Unit-3-62 textbook notes. The tolerance-grade chart and preferred-fit tables are practical tools for design engineers.

## Key concepts
- Clearance fit
- Transition fit
- Interference fit
- Tolerance grade (IT grades)
- Hole-basis and shaft-basis fit systems
- Preferred fits (running, sliding, locating, force)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Three main fit categories: clearance (positive difference), interference (negative difference), and transition (overlap of tolerance zones).
- ISO tolerance grades define allowable variation by fundamental deviation and IT grade number.
- Preferred clearance fits are tabulated for common hole-basis and shaft-basis systems.
- Slides are minimal in text; primary value is as a visual summary — detailed worked examples are in the Unit-3-62 companion document.
