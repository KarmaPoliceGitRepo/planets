## Overview
A one-page reference table of ultrasonic acoustic properties (longitudinal velocity, shear/transverse velocity, and acoustic impedance Z) for a wide range of common materials: metals, polymers, miscellaneous solids, composite materials, liquids, and gases.

## Unique contribution
Compact, comprehensive single-page reference covering over 50 materials spanning from air (Vl = 0.356 mm/µs) to beryllium (Vl = 12.878 mm/µs). Values are given in both in/µs and mm/µs alongside acoustic impedance in MRayl, enabling immediate transducer design calculations without unit conversion.

## Key concepts
- Longitudinal (compression) wave velocity
- Transverse (shear) wave velocity
- Acoustic impedance (Z, in MRayl)
- Material categories: metals, polymers, composites, liquids, gases

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Metals: aluminum ~6.2–6.4 mm/µs, steel ~5.8–6.0 mm/µs, titanium 6Al-4V ~6.17 mm/µs; lead is slowest at 2.16 mm/µs.
- Polymers: acrylics 2.67–2.77 mm/µs; rubber (silicone) only 0.94 mm/µs; teflon shear 6.35 mm/µs (anomalously high — possibly an error in source data).
- Ceramics/solids: alumina 10.85 mm/µs; tungsten carbide 6.66 mm/µs; sapphire 11.91 mm/µs.
- Composites: graphite/epoxy (60 v/o) 2.97 mm/µs, Z = 4.65 MRayl.
- Liquids: water (20°C) 1.473 mm/µs, Z = 1.48 MRayl; glycerin 1.93 mm/µs, Z = 2.42 MRayl.
- Many polymers and liquids support only longitudinal waves (no shear component listed).
