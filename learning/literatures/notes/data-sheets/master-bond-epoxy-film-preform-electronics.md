## Overview
A technical whitepaper describing epoxy adhesive films and preforms — solid-form adhesives used in electronics manufacturing for die attach, lid sealing, heat sink attach, and substrate bonding. Covers two main types (premixed frozen films and B-staged films), their process advantages over liquid adhesives, and application guidelines.

## Unique contribution
Explains the B-staging process in depth: partial curing is arrested before crosslinking, creating a solid that can be applied at one stage or facility, then fully cured later. This enables supply-chain flexibility not possible with liquid adhesives, along with superior bond line uniformity and near-zero squeeze-out.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Premixed frozen films require –40°C storage and dry-ice shipping; B-staged films are stable at ambient with longer refrigerated shelf life.
- B-staged films fully cure in ≤1 hour at 250–300°F (121–149°C).
- Advantages over liquids: no mix-ratio issues, no sag/slump, minimal squeeze-out, compatible with large-area bonding.
- Thermally and/or electrically conductive variants available through filler engineering; fiberglass-reinforced variants also available.
- Custom preforms via CAD/laser cutting reduce assembly labor and improve yield.
- Key caution: premixed frozen films are fragile and require reinforcement during shipping and handling.
