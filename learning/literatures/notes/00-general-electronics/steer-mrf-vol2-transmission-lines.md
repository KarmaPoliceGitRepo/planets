## Overview
Volume 2 of the Steer RF/microwave series, dedicated entirely to transmission-line theory and practical applications. Covers the distributed-parameter model, lossless and lossy lines, reflection and impedance transformation, Smith chart usage, microstrip design, and broadband matching techniques. Oriented toward microstrip implementations for cellular and planar circuit design.

## Unique contribution
Places transmission-line theory in the context of microstrip design for modern planar circuits, with extensive design examples and case studies. Distinguishes itself from purely mathematical treatments by connecting formulas to practical substrate parameters (εr, h, W) and design rules.

## Key concepts
- Distributed-parameter model (RLGC per unit length)
- Characteristic impedance Z0, propagation constant γ = α + jβ
- Reflection coefficient, SWR, return loss
- Lossless line impedance formula: ZIN = Z0(ZL + jZ0 tan βl)/(Z0 + jZL tan βl)
- Smith chart usage for matching
- Microstrip line design (effective permittivity, width-to-height ratio)
- Quarter-wave transformer, single-stub matching, double-stub matching
- Broadband impedance matching

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Microstrip effective permittivity εeff < εr accounts for field fringing; it determines both Z0 and wavelength shortening.
- Single-stub matching: place a short/open stub at a distance from the load where the normalized conductance = 1, then the stub cancels the residual susceptance.
- Quarter-wave transformer achieves matching at exactly one frequency; bandwidth is improved by using multiple sections (Chebyshev or binomial taper).
