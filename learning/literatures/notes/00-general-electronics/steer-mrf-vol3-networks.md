## Overview
Volume 3 of the Steer series covering microwave network theory. Topics include scattering parameters (S-parameters), signal flow graphs, multiport networks, directional couplers, power dividers/combiners, hybrids, and microwave filters. The S-parameter formalism is developed as the primary language for characterizing microwave circuits.

## Unique contribution
Provides a rigorous but application-oriented treatment of S-parameters with emphasis on how they are measured (with a VNA) and how they are used in amplifier and filter design. Connects abstract network theory to practical microwave circuit components.

## Key concepts
- Scattering parameters (S-parameters): S11, S21, S12, S22
- Signal flow graphs, Mason's rule
- Multiport networks, power waves
- Directional couplers, hybrid junctions (90° and 180°)
- Wilkinson power divider
- Microwave filter design (Butterworth, Chebyshev, coupled-resonator)
- Return loss, insertion loss, isolation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- S-parameters are defined with respect to reference impedance Z0 (typically 50 Ω); they are measured with all other ports terminated in Z0.
- Signal flow graphs allow cascaded S-parameter network analysis without matrix inversion at each stage.
- Directional couplers extract a small fraction of power traveling in one direction with high isolation from the opposite direction.
- Filter design typically starts with a lowpass prototype (Chebyshev or Butterworth) and applies frequency and impedance transformations for the actual specification.
