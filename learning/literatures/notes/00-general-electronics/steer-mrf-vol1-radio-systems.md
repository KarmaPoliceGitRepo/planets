## Overview
Volume 1 of a 5-volume open-access textbook series on microwave and RF design, taking a modern "systems-first" approach. This volume covers the radio system context: modulation, wireless standards, RF link budgets, noise, and the overall architecture of communication and radar systems. It is designed for upper-level undergraduate and graduate students with an emphasis on cellular communications and practical design decisions.

## Unique contribution
Distinguishes itself by starting from system-level requirements (bandwidth, SNR, link budget) before diving into component design, giving readers context for why specific component specifications matter. The open-access CC BY-NC 4.0 license and NC State University publication make it freely available.

## Key concepts
- Systems-first approach to RF/microwave design
- Radio link budget, signal-to-noise ratio (SNR)
- Modulation schemes (AM, FM, digital: QAM, QPSK)
- Noise figure, noise temperature
- Cellular communication systems, frequency bands
- Radar fundamentals
- RF spectrum management

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The series emphasizes that system-level decisions drive component specs — an engineer must understand both layers.
- Volume 1 contextualizes why transmission lines, matching networks, and amplifier designs matter for real wireless links.
- Covers the frequency bands from VHF through millimeter wave, with emphasis on the microwave range (1–100 GHz).
- Companion volumes cover Transmission Lines (Vol. 2), Networks (Vol. 3), Modules (Vol. 4), and Amplifiers/Oscillators (Vol. 5).
