## Overview
A small personal survey spreadsheet at the root of the LITERATURES folder, logging findings from
papers, organized as "Speed of sound vs <variable>" tables with columns: Reference, Stress type,
Survey (summary of finding), Critical review (owner's critique).

## Unique contribution
The owner's own critical reading log — it captures judgements not in the papers themselves
(e.g. doubts about whether authors actually achieved the claimed torsion angle).

## Key concepts
- speed of sound vs stress (acoustoelastic effect)
- speed of sound vs temperature
- ultrasonic attenuation under cyclic loading
- dislocation density and fracture onset

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Logged finding (from "Hystereses of Mechanical and Acoustic Parameters of Metals under High
  Torsional Strain"): under cyclic torsional (shear) loading, speed of sound decreases; attenuation
  behaves similarly until fracture onset, where dislocation multiplication causes a change; speed of
  sound *increases* just before failure — a potential failure precursor signature.
- Owner's critique: path length changed only 0.0001% yet failure occurred at θ=3.5°; skeptical the
  authors torsioned the bar to the stated 30°.
- A "Speed of sound vs temp" section is started but appears empty — survey was left incomplete.
