## Overview
A 2019 paper addressing temperature compensation in guided wave SHM, focusing specifically on accounting for temperature-dependent changes in both phase and wave velocity to improve damage detection reliability in composite and metallic structures.

## Unique contribution
Separates and independently compensates for phase changes and velocity changes caused by temperature variation in guided waves, providing a more complete compensation strategy than methods that address only velocity (time stretching) or only phase.

## Key concepts
- Temperature compensation
- Guided waves
- Phase compensation
- Velocity compensation
- Structural health monitoring (SHM)
- Lamb waves
- Piezoelectric transducers

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Temperature affects guided waves in two ways: (1) changes wave velocity (equivalent to time stretch of the signal); (2) changes the phase of the signal independently.
- Most existing methods (BSS, scale transform) address velocity change only.
- This paper addresses both effects: velocity change corrected by time warping; residual phase shift corrected separately.
- Compensation validated on experimental data from composite and/or metallic plates with PZT transducers.
- Improved damage detection sensitivity after dual compensation compared to velocity-only compensation.
