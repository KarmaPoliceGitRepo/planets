## Overview
Published in ASME Journal of Applied Mechanics 69, 2002. Develops a theoretical framework for elastic wave propagation in the circumferential direction of anisotropic cylindrical curved plates using a Fourier series expansion to solve the coupled governing ODEs, with dispersion curve computation and weighted residuals validation.

## Unique contribution
Extends guided wave dispersion theory from isotropic to fully anisotropic curved plates by introducing the Fourier series expansion approach for the coupled governing equations, introduces the angular wave number concept, and validates against the weighted residuals method.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Governing equations for elastic waves in anisotropic curved plate geometry involve coupled ODEs in the thickness direction.
- Fourier series expansion in the circumferential coordinate converts ODEs to a matrix eigenvalue problem.
- Angular wave number plays the role of the wave number in flat-plate theory.
- Dispersion curves: phase and group velocity vs. frequency×thickness computed for different anisotropy orientations.
- Weighted residuals method used as an independent verification of the Fourier series results.
- Results show anisotropy significantly affects wave speeds and mode shapes compared to isotropic case.
- Relevant for guided wave inspection of curved structures such as pipes and cylinders.
