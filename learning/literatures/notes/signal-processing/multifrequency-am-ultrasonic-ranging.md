## Overview
Published in IEEE Transactions on Instrumentation and Measurement 43(6), 1994. Describes an air-coupled ultrasonic ranging system using three-frequency amplitude-modulated (AM) signals to achieve high-accuracy distance measurement via a vernier principle, with harmonic compensation.

## Unique contribution
Applies the vernier principle to extend the unambiguous range of phase-based distance measurement: a 44 kHz carrier is amplitude-modulated with 4.4 kHz and 440 Hz tones; phase comparison at three frequencies resolves range ambiguity. Harmonic distortion compensation is included.

## Key concepts
- Amplitude modulation (AM) ranging
- Phase shift measurement
- Vernier principle
- Harmonic compensation
- Air-coupled ultrasonic transducer
- Unambiguous range

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Three-frequency AM: carrier at 44 kHz, modulation at 4.4 kHz (10:1 ratio) and 440 Hz (100:1 ratio from carrier).
- Distance computed from phase difference of demodulated envelope signals at the two modulation frequencies.
- Vernier principle: coarse range from low-frequency (440 Hz) envelope phase; fine range from high-frequency (4.4 kHz) phase; combined to resolve ambiguity.
- Harmonic compensation: nonlinearity in the transducer/amplifier chain produces harmonics; correction algorithm applied in post-processing.
- Reported accuracy: approximately 1% over the measured range (several metres).
- System suitable for robotic navigation, industrial distance measurement.
