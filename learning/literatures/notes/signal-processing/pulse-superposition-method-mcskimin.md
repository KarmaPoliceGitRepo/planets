## Overview
Published in the Journal of the Acoustical Society of America 34(5), 1962. Critically analyses the pulse superposition method for measuring ultrasonic wave velocities in single crystals as a function of temperature and pressure, with particular attention to the coupling (bond layer) effect and its compensation.

## Unique contribution
Derives analytical expressions for the coupling (bond layer) phase correction in the pulse superposition resonance condition, enabling precise absolute velocity measurements in single crystals at extreme temperatures (down to −195.8°C) and pressures (up to 30,000 psi). Provides graphical design curves for practitioners.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Pulse superposition: apply RF pulses at repetition rate T ≈ p × (round-trip delay δ); superposed echoes reach maximum when T/p = δ + phase correction.
- Key equation: δ = (T/p) + (γ/360f); γ is phase angle from transducer coupling.
- Coupling effect: bond layer acts as a mass load at the transducer resonance; introduces a phase shift γ independent of temperature/pressure if operated at resonance.
- Operating at transducer resonance (f = fr) makes γ essentially constant, so measurements of T give direct ΔT → Δδ → Δv.
- Velocity measurements: longitudinal waves along X-direction of quartz and XY shear waves; data at 25°C and −195.8°C (liquid N₂); pressure up to 30,000 psi.
- Appendix: graphical curves of ΔT vs. bond thickness and phase shift, useful for applying method to arbitrary specimens.
- Accuracy for p=2 operation: ratios of delay accurate to a few parts in 10⁵.
