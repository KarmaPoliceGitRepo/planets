## Overview
Published in the Journal of the Acoustical Society of America 79(2), 1986. Presents a gated FFT method using short broadband pulses to isolate and identify Rayleigh and Whispering Gallery resonance modes of submerged elastic cylinders (aluminum wires).

## Unique contribution
Uses time-gating of the received pulse-echo waveform before applying FFT to isolate specific resonance contributions, allowing separation of geometrically reflected and resonance-scattered components in the frequency spectrum.

## Key concepts
- Resonance spectrum
- Elastic scattering

## Methods / results / takeaways
- A short broadband pulse insonifies a submerged aluminum wire; the backscattered signal contains both geometrical reflection and resonance contributions.
- Time gating selects a specific time window of the scattered signal before FFT, suppressing the direct reflection and isolating resonance modes.
- Resonance peaks in the frequency spectrum correspond to Rayleigh surface wave modes and Whispering Gallery modes.
- Method identifies resonance frequencies by matching peaks with theoretical predictions.
- Practical approach: broadband pulse provides wide frequency coverage in a single shot; gating is flexible and can be adjusted to target different resonance families.
