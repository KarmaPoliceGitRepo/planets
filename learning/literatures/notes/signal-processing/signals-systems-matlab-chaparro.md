## Overview
A standard signals and systems textbook by Chaparro (Elsevier 2019) covering continuous-time and discrete-time signals and systems: Laplace transform, Fourier series and transform, Z-transform, state-space representation, and filter design, with MATLAB examples throughout. The book is split into 22 separate PDF files (Chapters 0–12, Appendix A, Bibliography, Copyright, Dedication, Front Matter, Index, Preface).

## Unique contribution
Provides unified treatment of analog and digital signals and systems theory with MATLAB implementation, emphasising duality between continuous-time (Laplace/Fourier) and discrete-time (Z/DTFT) domains; pedagogically strong introduction with worked examples.

## Key concepts
- Continuous-time signals and systems
- Laplace transform
- Fourier series, Fourier transform
- Discrete-time signals, DTFT
- Z-transform
- State-space representation
- Sampling theorem
- FIR and IIR filter design
- MATLAB signal processing toolbox

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Book follows the dual analog/digital track: paired chapters on CT and DT systems share the same conceptual framework.
- Chapter 0: review / introduction; Chapters 1–5: CT signals and systems (Laplace, Fourier, convolution, BIBO stability); Chapters 6–12: DT signals and systems (Z-transform, DTFT, DFT/FFT, digital filters).
- State-space: both CT and DT state-space models covered; modal analysis, observability/controllability.
- MATLAB used throughout for simulation, filter design, and Fourier analysis.
- Appendix A: mathematical background (complex numbers, matrix algebra, partial fractions).
