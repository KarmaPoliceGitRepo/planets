## Overview
A 2010 paper proposing a combined guided wave approach for monitoring corrosion in structures, combining different guided wave modes or inspection strategies to improve detection sensitivity and reliability.

## Unique contribution
Combines multiple guided wave measurement modes or frequencies to increase the probability of detecting corrosion-related wall thinning, leveraging complementary sensitivity of different wave modes to damage at different locations or depths.

## Key concepts
- Guided waves
- Corrosion monitoring
- Wall thinning
- Structural health monitoring (SHM)
- Multi-mode guided wave inspection
- Pipe inspection

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Corrosion causes localised wall thinning; different guided wave modes have different sensitivity to thickness changes at different depths.
- Combined approach: uses two or more guided wave modes or configurations; corrosion features visible in one mode may be obscured in another.
- Demonstrated on metallic pipe/plate specimens with artificial corrosion defects.
- The combined method improves detection reliability compared to single-mode inspection.
- Temperature effects likely considered given the broader context of the containing folder.
