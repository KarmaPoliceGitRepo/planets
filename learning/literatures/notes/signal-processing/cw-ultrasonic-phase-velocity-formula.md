## Overview
Published in Journal of Applied Physics 52(5), 1981. Derives an improved analytical formula — based on transmission line theory for a compound resonator — for measuring absolute ultrasonic phase velocity in continuous-wave (CW) experiments, more accurate than the Bolef-Menes and Ringermacher formulas.

## Unique contribution
Derives a cubic equation from transmission line theory that accounts for the transducer bond layer effect, providing a more accurate formula than previous linear approximations. Includes systematic analysis of bond layer contributions to resonance frequency shifts.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- CW resonance method: a crystal-transducer bonded to a specimen exhibits composite resonance frequencies; phase velocity derived from resonance spacing.
- Previous formulas (Bolef-Menes, Ringermacher) are first-order approximations that neglect or underestimate the bond layer contribution.
- New cubic formula: derived from exact transmission line impedance equations for a three-layer resonator (transducer / bond / specimen).
- Bond layer: even a "thin" bond layer introduces a measurable frequency shift proportional to its acoustic impedance and thickness.
- The cubic equation is solved numerically; results agree better with experiment than earlier approximations.
- Particularly important for precise measurement of small velocity changes (e.g., temperature or stress dependence of elastic moduli).
