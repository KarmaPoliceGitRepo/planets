## Overview
NASA Conference Publication 2383 collects papers from a workshop on analytical ultrasonics for materials research. Topics include microstructure characterization, texture measurement, residual stress, fatigue damage assessment, and grain size determination using advanced ultrasonic methods.

## Unique contribution
Captures the state-of-the-art in materials characterization ultrasonics as of 1984, bringing together contributions on grain scattering theory (including early Stanke-Kino unified theory results), velocity measurements for texture, and backscatter measurements for microstructure — a key reference document for the field's maturation.

## Key concepts
- Grain scattering theory
- Ultrasonic texture measurement
- Backscattering coefficient
- Residual stress ultrasonics
- Fatigue monitoring
- Effective medium theory
- NASA materials inspection

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Multiple papers on grain scattering: Stanke-Kino formalism presented; Rayleigh/stochastic/diffusive regimes discussed.
- Velocity anisotropy used to determine crystallographic texture (ODF expansion in terms of wave speed Fourier coefficients).
- Backscatter techniques: figure of merit (FOM) for grain noise; relates to inspection capability.
- Fatigue papers: acoustic emission and velocity changes track dislocation accumulation and micro-crack formation.
- Residual stress: acoustoelastic coefficients for steel and aluminum measured; third-order elastic constants used.
- Applications to titanium alloy billets for aerospace: grain size and texture critical for sonic inspection.
