# Dispersion in Metal Rods Above the Rayleigh-Lamb Transition

- **Source:** Hueter JASA 22 514 1950 (letter to editor)

## Overview
A short letter reporting the observation of higher-mode dispersion in aluminum rods above the bulk Rayleigh-Lamb transition frequency. Identifies multiple dispersion branches (modes a, b, c) through nodal radius measurements on the end face of the rod, showing that multiple modes simultaneously propagate in the high-frequency regime.

## Unique contribution
First direct identification of multiple co-propagating higher Pochhammer-Chree modes in a metallic rod by nodal pattern measurement, providing experimental evidence that rod wave dispersion cannot be characterized by a single effective velocity above the fundamental cut-off.

## Key concepts
- Rod wave dispersion
- Pochhammer-Chree modes

- Nodal pattern measurement

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Aluminum rod; frequencies chosen to excite modes above first cutoff frequency.
- Nodal radii on end face measured using sand (Chladni) pattern technique at different frequencies.
- Three distinct modes (a, b, c) identified; each has different nodal structure and phase velocity.
- Phase velocity of each mode measured from time-of-flight; compared with theoretical Pochhammer curves.
- Key observation: above ~1.5·f_cutoff, multiple modes present simultaneously; pulse measurement gives superposition → apparent dispersion.
- Implication: broadband pulse measurements on rods must account for multi-mode interference above the transition.
