# Variational Principles for Inhomogeneous Non-linear Media

## Overview
A theoretical paper extending variational principles (Hashin-Shtrikman type) to nonlinear elastic composites. Develops upper and lower bounds on effective strain energy density for composites with nonlinear constituents, providing a framework for estimating effective nonlinear elastic moduli of two-phase materials.

## Unique contribution
First systematic derivation of Hashin-Shtrikman-type variational bounds for nonlinear composites, generalizing previous linear variational bounds. The framework handles finite-strain nonlinearity and is applicable to fibre-reinforced composites and polycrystals with nonlinear single-crystal behavior.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Variational formulation in terms of strain energy functional; comparison medium method used (linear reference medium with optimized moduli).
- Upper bound: strain prescribed uniformly in matrix reference; lower bound: stress prescribed uniformly.
- For power-law hardening materials: explicit bounds expressed in terms of single-crystal exponents and volume fractions.
- Applied to fibre composites: bounds tighter than Reuss-Voigt for transverse loading.
- Applied to polycrystals: self-consistent estimate obtained as stationary point of variational functional.
- Connection to Willis variational estimates for wave propagation: effective moduli from this paper feed into wave scattering calculations.
