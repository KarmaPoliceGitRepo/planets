## Overview
A paper proposing a hybrid measurement system for determining the absolute acoustic nonlinearity parameter β using air-coupled piezoelectric receivers. The method combines the simplicity of contact transmitters with non-contact air-coupled detection, avoiding the contact variability and optical complexity of laser interferometry while enabling calibrated β measurements.

## Unique contribution
Introduces a rigorous calibration procedure for air-coupled receivers in nonlinear measurements, accounting for the significant transmission loss at the specimen-air interface using a calibration protocol with reference samples of known β. Demonstrates that accurate absolute β values can be extracted despite ~60 dB insertion loss at the air interface.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- System: contact piezoelectric transmitter drives high-amplitude fundamental; air-coupled receiver detects both fundamental and second harmonic through-transmitted.
- Air-coupled receiver calibration: uses fused silica reference (β known from literature) to determine electromechanical transfer function.
- Absolute β extracted from ratio A₂/A₁² corrected for diffraction, attenuation, and interface transmission coefficients.
- Applied to Al alloy fatigue specimens at various life fractions (0–80% life): β increases monotonically with fatigue damage.
- β increase of ~40% from virgin to 80% life; consistent with dislocation density growth.
- Air-coupled approach avoids couplant variability: repeatability σ_β/β < 3% vs. ~8% for contact method.
- Validated against laser interferometry measurements on same specimens; agreement within 5%.
