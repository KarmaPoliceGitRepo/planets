# A Unified Theory for Elastic Wave Propagation in Polycrystalline Materials

## Overview
The landmark paper establishing a unified perturbation theory for elastic wave propagation (velocity and attenuation) in randomly oriented polycrystalline aggregates. Extends earlier work by Lifshitz-Parkhomovskii and Merkulov to a self-consistent formulation valid across all frequency regimes: Rayleigh, stochastic, and diffusive.

## Unique contribution
Derives the full frequency dependence of longitudinal and shear wave attenuation and velocity dispersion in cubic polycrystals through a single self-consistent formula, recovering the known f⁴ Rayleigh and f² stochastic limits as special cases and correctly predicting the crossover regime. Validated experimentally on aluminum and copper.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Dyson equation for mean Green's function solved self-consistently using Bourret approximation.
- Input: single-crystal elastic constants, grain size a (assumed exponential two-point correlation length).
- Output: complex wavenumber k = k_R + iα as function of ω.
- Rayleigh limit (ka ≪ 1): α ∝ ω⁴a³/v⁵.
- Stochastic limit (ka ~ 1): α ∝ ω²a/v³.
- Diffusive limit (ka ≫ 1): α ∝ 1/(ωa) — not physically accessible in metals before other losses dominate.
- Velocity dispersion: Δv/v ~ few tenths of percent across full frequency range.
- Validated on Al (grain size ~100 µm) and Cu; agreement within 10% over two decades of frequency.
- Forms the theoretical foundation for all subsequent LUS grain-size measurement work.
