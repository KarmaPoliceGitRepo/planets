## Overview
Part II of a two-paper series on elastic wave propagation in composite media, giving explicit results for isotropic matrix with spherical inclusions and for cubic polycrystals. Applies the variational perturbation theory of Part I to compute attenuation and velocity dispersion, using Hashin-Shtrikman bounds and self-consistent estimates. Numerical examples for Cu, α-Fe, and Ni polycrystals.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first explicit comparison between Hashin-Shtrikman bounds and self-consistent estimates for wave attenuation in polycrystals, showing that both are tighter than the earlier Lifshitz-Parkhomovskii result, and quantifying the bounding gap for real materials including Cu, Fe, and Ni.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Matrix+spheres composite: upper/lower bounds derived for effective moduli; attenuation bounded by choosing optimal reference medium.
- Cubic polycrystal: treated as matrix+spheres with grains as inclusions; structure factor A from spatial correlation.
- Self-consistent estimate: reference medium = effective medium; gives unique estimate between bounds.
- For Cu: HS bounds tight (factor ~1.5 apart); self-consistent within 10% of experimental data.
- For α-Fe: bounds wider (higher anisotropy); self-consistent still within ~20% of experiment.
- For Ni: intermediate; experimental data between upper and lower bounds as expected.
- Structure factors A, A' related to grain spatial distribution (via radial distribution function g(r)); can be measured from metallography.
