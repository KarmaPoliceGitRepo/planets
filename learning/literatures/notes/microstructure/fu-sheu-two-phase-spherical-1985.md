# Scattering of Elastic Waves by a Spherical Elastic Inhomogeneity

## Overview
A theoretical paper solving the problem of elastic wave scattering by a single spherical inclusion embedded in an isotropic elastic matrix, using exact partial-wave (T-matrix) series expansion. Derives scattering cross-sections and backscattering amplitudes as functions of ka (wavenumber × radius) for both longitudinal and shear incident waves.

## Unique contribution
Provides exact (not Born-approximation) results for the spherical scatterer case valid across all ka regimes, enabling accurate computation of scattering from inclusions at intermediate frequencies where the Born approximation fails. Results include mode-converted shear backscattering contributions.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Exact solution via spherical harmonic expansion; boundary conditions (continuity of displacement and traction) at sphere surface yield mode-coupling coefficients.
- Scattering cross-section computed by optical theorem from forward scattering amplitude.
- Results compared with Born approximation: Born accurate for δC/C ≲ 0.2 and ka ≲ 0.3.
- At ka ~ 1, resonance-like features appear (geometric scattering regime onset); Born overestimates for large contrast inclusions.
- Mode conversion (L→S scattering) significant at intermediate ka; contributes ~30–50% of total cross-section.
- Long-wavelength limit recovers Rayleigh scattering ∝ (ka)⁴; short-wavelength limit approaches geometric cross-section 2πa².
