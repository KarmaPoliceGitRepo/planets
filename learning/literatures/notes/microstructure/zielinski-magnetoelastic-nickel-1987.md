# Magnetoelastic Attenuation and Velocity in Nickel Single Crystals

## Overview
An experimental study measuring magnetoelastic (magnon-phonon) attenuation and velocity in Ni single crystals over 10–250 MHz as functions of applied magnetic field, temperature, and field direction relative to crystallographic axes. Develops a domain wall damping theory to interpret the results.

## Unique contribution
Provides systematic measurements on Ni single crystal of magnetoelastic attenuation as a function of field direction, revealing a sharp attenuation peak for field along [001] attributed to 71° Bloch wall contribution; distinguishes quantitatively between 90°, 71°, and 109° domain wall mechanisms by their angular dependence.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Pulse-echo method; Ni crystals oriented for [100], [110], [111] propagation directions; B applied along various axes.
- Attenuation peak at intermediate fields (coercive field region) where domain walls are most mobile.
- For [001] field direction: sharp additional peak attributed to 71° Bloch walls intersecting {110} planes at 45°.
- Temperature dependence: attenuation decreases toward Curie temperature (358°C for Ni) as domain contrast decreases.
- Frequency dependence: α ∝ f² in magnetized state (residual viscous damping); domain wall contribution ∝ f at low fields.
- Domain wall damping model: viscous drag η_w relates wall velocity to applied stress; attenuation = η_w × (dM/dε)² × f.
- Velocity: ΔV/V ≈ −0.1% between fully magnetized and demagnetized states (ΔE effect).
