## Overview
A paper introducing the spectrum analysis method using a buffer rod for broadband ultrasonic attenuation measurement. The buffer rod separates the transducer from the specimen, allowing single-ended contact measurement and calibration. Derives new diffraction correction formulas applicable to the buffer rod geometry and validates against direct crystal bond method.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first complete treatment of diffraction correction for the buffer rod configuration in broadband spectral attenuation measurements, including new analytical near-field correction formulas valid for the composite buffer+specimen path. Enables non-destructive broadband attenuation measurement with a single transducer using the spectrum ratio method.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method: metal specimen bonded to end of buffer rod; echoes from front and back faces of specimen provide ratio for α(f).
- Diffraction correction formulas for combined buffer+specimen path derived; accounts for different acoustic velocities in buffer and specimen.
- Transducer characterization: frequency response extracted from echo in buffer without specimen.
- Validated against direct crystal bond (Papadakis 1964 method): agreement within ±0.05 dB/cm.
- Applied to steel, aluminum, titanium specimens at 1–30 MHz.
- Key advantage: no bonding of crystal directly to specimen; usable in harsh environments; works at elevated temperature with appropriate buffer material.
- Limitation: bond quality between buffer and specimen still matters; coupling correction needed.
