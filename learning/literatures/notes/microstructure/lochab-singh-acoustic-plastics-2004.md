# Acoustic Properties of Plastics

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
An experimental study measuring longitudinal and shear ultrasonic wave velocities and attenuation in a range of common engineering plastics (polyethylene, nylon, PTFE, PVC, polystyrene, etc.) at room temperature over the 1–10 MHz frequency range. Derives elastic constants from the velocity data.

## Unique contribution
Provides a systematic tabulated database of acoustic properties (vL, vS, density, elastic moduli, Poisson's ratio) for multiple plastics measured under consistent conditions, filling a gap in the literature for engineering design with polymer components in ultrasonic systems.

## Key concepts
- Longitudinal wave velocity
- Shear wave velocity
- Acoustic impedance
- Elastic moduli of polymers
- Attenuation in plastics
- Polyethylene, nylon, PTFE, PVC, polystyrene

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Pulse-echo technique with contact transducers; 2.25, 5, and 10 MHz transducers used.
- Velocities measured by time-of-flight; attenuation from amplitude ratios of successive echoes.
- Results: vL ranges from ~1800 m/s (PTFE) to ~2800 m/s (nylon); attenuation generally higher in softer plastics.
- Attenuation typically 1–5 dB/cm at 5 MHz for most plastics; PTFE and PE highest due to semi-crystalline structure.
- Elastic moduli consistent with mechanical measurements; Poisson's ratios 0.3–0.45.
- Temperature dependence noted qualitatively: velocities decrease significantly above glass transition.
