# Acoustic Properties of Human Cancellous Bone

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
An experimental study characterizing ultrasonic properties (speed of sound and attenuation) of human cancellous bone samples from the calcaneus (heel), femoral head, and vertebra over the 0.2–2 MHz frequency range. Investigates correlations between ultrasonic parameters and bone mineral density and trabecular architecture.

## Unique contribution
Provides systematic multi-site characterization of cancellous bone acoustics with simultaneous microstructural measurement (porosity, density), demonstrating that broadband ultrasonic attenuation (BUA) correlates with porosity rather than mineral density alone, suggesting attenuation reflects structural organization beyond density.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Through-transmission measurement at 0.2–2 MHz; BUA computed as slope of attenuation vs. frequency.
- BUA ranges: 5–20 dB/cm/MHz for osteoporotic to 20–50 dB/cm/MHz for normal calcaneus.
- SOS (speed of sound): 1450–1700 m/s in cancellous bone; correlates with apparent density (r² ~ 0.7).
- BUA correlates with trabecular connectivity and anisotropy as well as density; BUA is sensitive to early structural changes.
- Significant site-to-site variation (calcaneus vs. femoral head vs. vertebra); each site requires separate calibration.
- BUA measurement reproducibility: CV ~ 3–5% in vitro; in vivo higher due to soft tissue effects.
