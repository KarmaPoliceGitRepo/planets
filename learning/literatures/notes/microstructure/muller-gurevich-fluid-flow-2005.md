## Overview
A theoretical paper on seismic wave attenuation and velocity dispersion in fluid-saturated porous rocks with heterogeneous (patchy) fluid saturation. Derives analytical expressions for attenuation as a function of frequency using the Biot framework with spatial fluctuations in fluid compressibility (patchy saturation model).

## Unique contribution
Develops a unified model relating mesoscopic scale fluid flow (at patch scale, not pore scale) to bulk acoustic attenuation and dispersion, bridging the gap between Biot's global flow theory and the Gassmann low-frequency limit. Provides explicit formulas for the transition frequency between low- and high-frequency regimes.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: two-fluid patches (gas/water) of characteristic size l embedded in rock; pressure gradients between patches drive viscous flow and dissipation.
- Attenuation peak at transition frequency f_c ~ κ/(ηl²) where κ = permeability, η = fluid viscosity.
- Below f_c: Gassmann limit (low-frequency); above f_c: high-frequency Biot limit; between: strong attenuation and dispersion.
- Closed-form expressions for α(f) and v(f) in terms of Biot moduli and patch geometry.
- Applied to sandstone with partial gas saturation; good agreement with laboratory measurements.
- Implication: seismic frequencies (10–100 Hz) often in transition regime; velocity dispersion ~100 m/s between seismic and ultrasonic measurements in partially saturated rock.
