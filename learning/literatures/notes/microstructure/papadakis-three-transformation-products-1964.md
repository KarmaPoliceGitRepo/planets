# Ultrasonic Attenuation Caused by Scattering in Three Transformation Products of Steel

- **Source:** Papadakis 1964 - three transformation products of steel
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1XxRUB98.../view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** E. P. Papadakis / 1964
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Experimental paper measuring ultrasonic attenuation in plain carbon steel specimens heat-treated to produce three microstructural states: pearlite (lamellar), bainite (acicular), and martensite (tempered). Compares measured attenuation vs. frequency with grain scattering theory predictions, extracting effective scattering "grain sizes" for each microstructure.

## Unique contribution
One of the first systematic comparisons of attenuation from the three main transformation products of steel (pearlite, bainite, martensite), revealing that the effective scattering unit is the prior austenite grain for martensite/bainite but the pearlite colony for pearlitic steel, and quantifying the frequency dependence differences.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Pulse-echo immersion technique; frequencies 15–75 MHz; steel bars heat-treated to controlled microstructures.
- Pearlitic steel: scattering unit = pearlite colony (~50–150 µm); attenuation ∝ f⁴ at low f, crosses into stochastic regime.
- Bainitic steel: scattering unit = bainite packet (~20–50 µm); lower attenuation than pearlite at same frequency.
- Martensitic steel: scattering unit = prior austenite grain; martensite laths too fine to scatter at these frequencies.
- Elastic anisotropy factor W determines magnitude: W(pearlite) > W(bainite) > W(martensite-tempered).
- Diffraction correction (Papadakis correction) applied to separate geometric spreading from material attenuation.
- Conclusion: heat treatment can shift attenuation coefficient by factor ~5 at 30 MHz.
