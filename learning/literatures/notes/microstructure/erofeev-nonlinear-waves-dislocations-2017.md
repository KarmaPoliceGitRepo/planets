## Overview
A theoretical paper on nonlinear elastic wave propagation in solids containing mobile dislocation populations. Develops continuum mechanics models coupling the acoustic wave equation to dislocation density dynamics (Granato-Lücke type), deriving conditions for modulational instability, soliton formation, and nonlinear attenuation.

## Unique contribution
Derives explicit amplitude-dependent wave speed and attenuation arising from dislocation string vibrations in the nonlinear regime, extending the linear Granato-Lücke model. Shows that at high acoustic amplitudes, dislocation breakaway produces irreversible attenuation distinct from the linear hysteresis damping mechanism.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: dislocation line treated as a vibrating string pinned between nodes; acoustic field drives transverse displacement; back-reaction on stress field gives attenuation and velocity dispersion.
- In linear regime: recovers standard Granato-Lücke result (attenuation ∝ frequency²·dislocation density).
- Nonlinear regime: amplitude-dependent contribution ∝ A²; at critical amplitude, dislocations break away from pinning points leading to irreversible plastic damping.
- Soliton solutions found for specific parameter ranges: localized strain pulses propagate without dispersion.
- Numerical examples show shock formation in pre-strained metals.
- Practical implication: acoustic measurement of nonlinear parameter β can track dislocation density changes with fatigue.
