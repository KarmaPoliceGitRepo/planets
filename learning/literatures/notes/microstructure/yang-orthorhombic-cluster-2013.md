## Overview
A theoretical paper developing an ultrasonic scattering model for polycrystals composed of orthorhombic crystallites arranged in orientation clusters (microtexture regions, MTRs). Both the individual crystallites and their clustering arrangements are treated as ellipsoidal; the crystallite texture within clusters is described by a generalized Gaussian ODF with three texture parameters. Targeted at titanium alloys with MTRs.

## Unique contribution
Extends scattering theory from cubic to orthorhombic symmetry crystallites and adds the clustered MTR microstructure level, giving a two-scale model that captures both grain-level and cluster-level scattering contributions to attenuation and backscattering — essential for titanium billet inspection where MTRs dominate scattering noise.

## Key concepts
- Orthorhombic crystal symmetry
- Microtexture regions (MTRs)
- Orientation distribution function (ODF)
- Two-scale scattering
- Titanium alloy microstructure
- Ellipsoidal grain/cluster shape
- Backscattering coefficient
- Texture parameter

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Effective elastic constants of cluster computed by volume-averaging over crystallite ODF.
- Cluster-scale scattering then computed treating clusters as effective scatterers in an effective matrix.
- Model gives attenuation and backscattering as functions of cluster size, aspect ratio, crystallite ODF texture strength, and frequency.
- Analytical Rayleigh and stochastic limits derived explicitly.
- Results show: strong texture within clusters (low ODF width) gives higher cluster-scattering; clustering significantly increases backscattering FOM vs. same grain volume fraction without clusters.
- Comparison with Ti-6Al-4V measurements: reasonable agreement when MTR size ~200–400 µm.
- Key insight: MTR-induced backscattering can exceed random-grain backscattering by an order of magnitude.
