# The Effective Field Method in the Theory of Waves in a Composite Medium: Part I

## Overview
Part I of a two-paper series developing a general variational framework for elastic wave propagation in statistically homogeneous composite media. Derives a stochastic stationary principle for the complex wavenumber, yields perturbation expansions for the effective wavenumber in the long-wave limit, and gives formulas for effective moduli L (velocity correction) and complex perturbation Q (attenuation) and Q' (dispersion).

## Unique contribution
Establishes a rigorous variational (Hashin-Shtrikman-type) upper and lower bound framework for wave propagation — not just static moduli — in composite media, linking the bounds explicitly to the two-point correlation function of the composite and demonstrating that Lifshitz-Parkhomovskii is a special case.

## Key concepts
- Variational principle for waves
- Effective wavenumber
- Perturbation theory composite
- Two-point correlation function
- Effective moduli L
- Attenuation perturbation Q
- Dispersion perturbation Q'
- Statistically homogeneous composite

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Framework: start from stochastic elastodynamic equation; define comparison medium; expand perturbation in powers of δC (moduli fluctuations).
- Variational principle: stationary condition for functional involving effective wavenumber.
- Leading order: Q ∝ ∫S(k)δC_eff² dk where S(k) = power spectrum of moduli fluctuations.
- For polycrystals: Q relates directly to elastic anisotropy (W factor) and grain size distribution through grain correlation function.
- Provides both upper bound (stiff comparison) and lower bound (soft comparison) depending on sign of δC.
- Long-wave limit: L recovers Voigt/Reuss bounds on static moduli; Q gives first frequency-dependent attenuation correction.
- Part II applies these formulas to specific isotropic and polycrystalline cases.
