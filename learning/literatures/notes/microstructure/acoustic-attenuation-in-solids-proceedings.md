## Overview
A conference proceedings volume collecting short papers ("SESSIONS") on acoustic attenuation in solids from an international symposium (~2001, based on internal citation dates). Topics span nonlinear behavior of solids, magnetoelastic attenuation in ferromagnetics, surface acoustic wave attenuation, HBAR (high-overtone bulk acoustic resonator) spectroscopy, fractional calculus viscoelastic models, and acoustic nonlinearity in rocks/metals.

## Unique contribution
Provides a broad contemporary snapshot (early 2000s) of attenuation mechanisms across multiple material classes in a single volume: nonlinear crystal measurements (Breazeale), pulsed technique measurement (Djelouah), magnetoelastic attenuation, SAW roughness scattering, piezoelectric HBAR methods, and hysteron models for rocks — useful as a survey of active research directions.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Breazeale contribution: nonlinearity parameter β for cubic crystals (Cu, Si, Ge, LiNbO₃, GaAs, graphite-epoxy); β ~ 3–15 for single crystals; higher for composites with geometric effects.
- Djelouah contribution: pulsed technique for α in viscous fluids with small target; plane wave isolation using time gating avoids diffraction corrections.
- Magnetoelastic section: magnetization-strain coupling yields frequency-dependent attenuation peak at coercive field; domain wall viscous drag model.
- SAW roughness: Gaussian roughness with λ_SAW >> correlation length; attenuation ∝ σ²f² (diffuse limit).
- HBAR spectroscopy: thin-film resonator stacked on substrate; attenuation extracted from Q of individual resonance peaks; applied to ZnO films.
- Fractional calculus model: generalized Maxwell/Kelvin-Voigt with fractional derivative; complex dispersion relations for viscoelastic wave propagation.
- Hysteron model for rocks/metals: accounts for cyclic loading hysteresis in sound velocity; torsion hysterons explain velocity-stress loops.
