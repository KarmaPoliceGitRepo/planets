## Overview
A practical technical note comparing two dry/semi-dry ultrasonic couplants — Aqualene (a polyisobutylene gel) and ACE (Acoustic Coupling Emulsion) — for contact ultrasonic inspection. Evaluates acoustic coupling efficiency, frequency response, ease of use, and suitability for elevated-temperature testing.

## Unique contribution
Provides direct measured comparison of insertion loss and bandwidth for Aqualene vs. ACE vs. liquid couplant, giving practical guidance for field NDT applications where liquid couplant is undesirable (e.g., dry coupling on rough surfaces, elevated temperature, or where contamination must be avoided).

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Aqualene: polyisobutylene-based solid/gel couplant; works up to ~60°C; low viscosity variant for rough surfaces.
- ACE: water-based emulsion; better high-frequency transmission; cleaner removal.
- Insertion loss measurements: liquid couplant best; Aqualene introduces 2–4 dB additional loss at 5 MHz; ACE intermediate.
- Bandwidth: ACE preserves bandwidth better than Aqualene at high frequencies.
- Practical recommendation: ACE preferred for >5 MHz inspections; Aqualene for elevated temperature or where liquid is prohibited.
- Surface roughness significantly affects coupling; pressing force matters for Aqualene.
