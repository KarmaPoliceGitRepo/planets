## Overview
A short express letter (JASA) deriving compact closed-form expressions for ultrasonic attenuation in polycrystals whose single-crystal elasticity belongs to any of the 32 crystallographic point groups. Extends previous results (Stanke-Kino, Turner) that were mostly restricted to cubic and hexagonal symmetry.

## Unique contribution
Provides general attenuation formulae valid for all crystal symmetry classes (triclinic through cubic) using compact tensor notation, making it straightforward to compute attenuation for low-symmetry materials (orthorhombic, monoclinic) without re-deriving the scattering theory from scratch.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Derivation follows the Dyson equation / Born approximation path of Stanke-Kino and Turner.
- Key innovation: covariance tensor ⟨C'⊗C'⟩ evaluated in general for any symmetry using Voigt/Reuss averaging framework.
- Attenuation formula: α = (ω⁴/ρ²v⁵) × [scattering integral involving grain correlation function and elastic covariance].
- Explicit results tabulated for cubic, hexagonal, orthorhombic, tetragonal, trigonal, monoclinic, triclinic crystallites.
- Applied to α-Ti (hexagonal), steel (cubic), and calcite (trigonal) as demonstrations.
- Rayleigh limit (kD ≪ 1): α ∝ f⁴D³; stochastic limit (kD ~ 1): α ∝ f²D.
- Tensor contraction expressions given that are ready for numerical implementation.
