## Overview
A conference paper reporting industrial trials of laser ultrasonics (LUS) as an on-line microstructure monitor (grain size, texture, recrystallisation fraction, phase transformation) on a hot strip mill at SSAB. Uses the Stanke-Kino theory for grain size and the Poisson's ratio criterion to discriminate ferrite from austenite.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates real-world on-line application of LUS on an industrial hot strip mill, validating grain size measurements against post-rolling metallographic analysis, and introducing the Poisson's ratio diagnostic (ν = vP²/(2vS²) + 0.5·... ≈ 0.29 for ferrite vs. 0.324 for austenite) as a practical phase-transformation detector at high temperature.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- LUS system: CO₂ generation laser; interferometric receiver; applied to moving hot strip at 900–1200°C.
- Grain size from attenuation: Rayleigh α = Cr·D³·f⁴; Cr from single-crystal elastic constants of steel.
- Phase discrimination: v_P/v_S ratio gives Poisson's ratio ν; ferrite ν ≈ 0.29, austenite ν ≈ 0.324; clear step observed during γ→α transformation.
- Recrystallisation: velocity and attenuation shift during recrystallisation due to texture randomization.
- Grain size errors: ±20% in single-phase austenite; larger in mixed-phase regions.
- Industrial trial results: grain size predictions within 25% of final metallographic measurements.
- Proposed as real-time feedback for process control of rolling schedule.
