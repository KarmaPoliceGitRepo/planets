# Dispersion Measurement by Modulation Method

## Overview
Conference abstract describing a modulation method for measuring acoustic dispersion (d²k/dω²), in which a sinusoidally modulated tone-burst is used to convert phase dispersion into amplitude modulation, enabling sensitive measurement of very small velocity dispersions.

## Unique contribution
Introduces a modulation technique for dispersion measurement that converts difficult phase measurements into amplitude measurements, with sensitivity independent of small velocity fluctuations — useful for measuring tiny dispersions in metals where conventional phase methods are impractical.

## Key concepts
- Acoustic dispersion measurement
- Modulation method
- Group velocity dispersion
- d²k/dω² measurement
- Phase-to-amplitude conversion

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method: amplitude-modulated ultrasonic wave; group velocity dispersion converts AM to PM and back to AM distortion measurable in the envelope.
- Sensitivity: ∝ (propagation length)²; independent of first-order velocity fluctuations.
- Validated in water (near-zero dispersion confirmed) and thin metallic wire (dispersion consistent with dispersive wave theory for rods).
