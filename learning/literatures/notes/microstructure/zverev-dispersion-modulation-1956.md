## Overview
ASA Fifty-First Meeting abstract (FE4) describing a modulation method for measuring acoustic dispersion. The experiment directly yields D = d²k/dω² (k wave number, ω angular frequency) by exploiting the mutual transformation of phase modulation into amplitude modulation (and vice versa) as a modulated sine wave travels through a dispersive medium.

## Unique contribution
A dispersion measurement whose sensitivity depends **only on receiver noise level** and is independent of the unavoidable small random variations of sound velocity in the test medium — converting an impractical differential phase measurement into a robust amplitude (modulation-conversion) measurement.

## Key concepts
- Acoustic dispersion measurement
- Modulation method (PM ↔ AM conversion)
- Group velocity dispersion, d²k/dω²
- Waveguide dispersion in rods/wires
- Optical detection of ultrasound

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Carrier 1 Mc/s, modulation frequency 100 kc/s; tested on longitudinal waves in thin wires and in water.
- Thin wire: measured D in good agreement with the value calculated from theory of elasticity (rod waveguide dispersion); relative phase-velocity change over the 100 kc/s interval ≈ 10⁻⁴.
- Water: no dispersion observed, consistent with theory; a velocity variation of 1 cm/s over a 100 kc/s interval was detectable with ~1 kc/s receiver bandwidth — extreme sensitivity for 1956.
- An optical indicator of ultrasonic waves was used at the far end to avoid reflected-wave contamination (no electrical pickup, no standing waves).
- Context: the same abstract page (FE3) describes a differential two-vessel pulse apparatus measuring absorption-coefficient differences between liquids to Δα ≈ 0.01 cm⁻¹ per mA with 3–5 % accuracy.
