## Overview
A theoretical and experimental paper investigating the contribution of mobile dislocations to ultrasonic attenuation in NaCl single crystals. Develops a string-model theory and compares with measurements on plastically deformed crystals.

## Unique contribution
Extends dislocation string model to ionic crystals, showing that the point-defect pinning of dislocation lines in irradiated or impurity-doped NaCl gives measurable attenuation peaks at characteristic frequencies, providing a tool to characterize dislocation loop lengths.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: dislocation loop of length L₀ pinned at both ends vibrates under acoustic stress; resonance frequency ω₀ ∝ 1/L₀².
- Damping: viscous drag gives attenuation peak at resonance; height proportional to dislocation density Λ.
- Experiment: NaCl samples deformed to controlled plastic strains; attenuation measured 1–10 MHz.
- Loop length extracted from resonance frequency: L₀ ~ 0.5–2 µm for freshly deformed crystals.
- Aging at room temperature: loop length decreases as dislocation segments are re-pinned by diffusing defects.
