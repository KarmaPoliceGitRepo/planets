## Overview
Conference abstract reporting measurements of ultrasonic attenuation in ferromagnetic steels, separating the contributions of dislocation damping from magnetoelastic (domain wall) attenuation using an applied external magnetic field to suppress the ferromagnetic component.

## Unique contribution
Early experimental demonstration of the field-suppression technique for isolating dislocation attenuation in ferromagnetic metals — applying a DC magnetic field to saturate the specimen and eliminate domain wall contributions, leaving only dislocation and scattering terms.

## Key concepts
- Dislocation attenuation
- Magnetoelastic attenuation
- Ferromagnetic steel
- Domain wall suppression
- Applied magnetic field method
- Internal friction in steel

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method: measure α with and without applied DC magnetic field; difference = magnetoelastic contribution; residual = dislocation + scattering.
- Ferromagnetic contribution can be 50–200% of total attenuation at low MHz frequencies in unmagnetized steel.
- Dislocation contribution isolated and compared with Granato-Lücke theory predictions.
