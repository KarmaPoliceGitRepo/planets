## Overview
A theoretical paper extending the Stanke-Kino unified scattering theory to textured (non-random) polycrystalline materials. The crystallographic texture is represented through a set of Fourier coefficients of the ODF; effective elastic constants and the fluctuation covariance tensor are derived in terms of these texture descriptors. The Dyson equation is solved to give complex wavenumbers for all wave modes.

## Unique contribution
First systematic treatment of grain scattering in textured polycrystals using the Dyson equation framework, linking ODF texture descriptors directly to wave attenuation and velocity anisotropy. Provides the theoretical basis for extracting texture from angle-dependent attenuation measurements.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- ODF expanded in generalized spherical harmonics (Roe coefficients W_lmn).
- Effective elastic constants C̄_ijkl computed as volume averages over ODF.
- Fluctuation tensor δC_ijkl = C_ijkl − C̄_ijkl; covariance ⟨δC⊗δC⟩ expressed in terms of ODF coefficients.
- Dyson equation solved in Born approximation for each wave mode (qL, qT1, qT2 in anisotropic medium).
- Results: attenuation for L wave depends on all W_lmn with l ≤ 4; velocity depends on W_200, W_400.
- Applied to rolled steel (fiber texture): L-wave attenuation anisotropy up to ~30% between parallel/perpendicular to rolling.
- Enables both forward modeling and inversion of ODF from multi-directional attenuation measurements.
