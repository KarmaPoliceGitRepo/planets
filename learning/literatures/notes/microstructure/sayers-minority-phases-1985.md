# Attenuation of Ultrasound by Minority Phases in Polycrystalline Metals

- **Source:** Sayers Wave Motion 7 95 1985
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qyNeFmZuh5UaJfQGqd11gUOI29qq-vZz/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** C. M. Sayers / 1985
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
A theoretical paper investigating ultrasonic attenuation from minority phases (small volume fraction inclusions) in polycrystalline metals, using a spherical shell scattering model to represent grain boundary segregation. Applied to MnS inclusions in free-machining steel as a practical example.

## Unique contribution
Introduces the spherical shell scattering model for grain boundary films or segregates: instead of a solid inclusion, the scatterer is a shell of material coating grain boundaries. Shows that shell scattering gives α ∝ k⁴a⁴l² (l = shell thickness) at low frequencies, contrasting with ∝ k⁴a⁶ for solid spheres, allowing extraction of shell thickness from attenuation measurements.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: thin elastic shell of material coating outer surface of spherical grain; born approximation applied.
- Low-frequency result: α ∝ k⁴a⁴l²(δρ/ρ + δB/B)² where l = shell thickness, δρ, δB = density and bulk modulus contrast.
- For MnS in free-machining steel (sulphur content 0.3–0.5 wt%): predicted attenuation contribution ~5–15% of total grain scattering at 20 MHz.
- Comparison with measured attenuation on resulfurized vs. normal steel: sulphur addition increases attenuation by ~10% at 20 MHz, consistent with shell model.
- Key distinction: very thin shells contribute negligible scattering despite high specific surface area; scattering grows as (shell volume)² not shell volume.
- Practical implication: segregation-induced grain boundary films at the level of a few nm contribute negligibly to attenuation.
