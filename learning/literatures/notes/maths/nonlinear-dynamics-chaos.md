## Overview
The standard graduate/advanced undergraduate textbook on nonlinear dynamics and chaos. Covers 1D and 2D flows, bifurcations, limit cycles, the Lorenz equations, chaos, fractals, and applications in physics, biology, chemistry, and engineering. The book is celebrated for its accessible style and abundance of real-world examples.

## Unique contribution
Integrates geometric intuition (phase portraits, bifurcation diagrams) with rigorous analysis, and systematically connects mathematical theory to applications in neuroscience (FitzHugh-Nagumo), chemical oscillations (Belousov-Zhabotinsky), and electronics (van der Pol). The Lorenz equations chapter is particularly detailed.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Phase plane analysis: classify fixed points by linearisation (Jacobian eigenvalues).
- Index theory: the index of a closed curve constrains what fixed points can be enclosed.
- Hopf bifurcation creates or destroys limit cycles; can be supercritical (stable cycle) or subcritical (unstable).
- Lorenz attractor: deterministic yet chaotic; sensitive dependence on initial conditions means long-term prediction is impossible.
- Lyapunov exponent measures average rate of divergence of nearby trajectories; positive exponent = chaos.
