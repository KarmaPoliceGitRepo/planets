## Overview
A textbook from Drexel University introducing tensor analysis together with the Calculus of Moving Surfaces (CMS), a framework for computing derivatives of integrals over evolving surfaces. Covers curvilinear coordinates, covariant differentiation, Riemann curvature, and extends classical tensor calculus to time-dependent domains.

## Unique contribution
The Calculus of Moving Surfaces — the ability to differentiate surface integrals when the surface itself is moving — is Grinfeld's specialty and not covered in standard tensor textbooks. This is critical for fluid dynamics problems with free surfaces, phase boundaries, and shell theory.

## Key concepts
- Tensors in curvilinear coordinates
- Covariant and contravariant components
- Metric tensor
- Covariant derivative (Christoffel symbols)
- Riemann curvature tensor
- Surface tensors and surface coordinates
- Calculus of Moving Surfaces (CMS)
- Material derivative on evolving surfaces
- Transport theorem (Reynolds)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The metric tensor g_{ij} encodes lengths and angles in curvilinear coordinates; raising/lowering indices uses g_{ij} and g^{ij}.
- Covariant derivative generalises partial derivative to curved spaces; ∇_k T^i = ∂_k T^i + Γ^i_{kj} T^j.
- The CMS provides formulas for the time derivative of surface integrals when the surface velocity and curvature are known.
- Applications include Stefan problems (melting/freezing), droplet dynamics, and thin shells.
