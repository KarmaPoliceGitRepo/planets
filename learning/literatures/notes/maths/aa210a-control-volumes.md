## Overview
Chapter 3 of Stanford's AA210A Fundamentals of Compressible Flow course. The chapter develops the mathematical toolkit for control-volume analysis: gradient, divergence, curl, and Laplacian of scalar and vector fields, together with the Kronecker unit tensor and Levi-Civita alternating tensor. Gauss's theorem, Stokes' theorem, and their application to the continuity equation are derived.

## Unique contribution
Presents vector calculus in index notation side by side with standard notation, explicitly introducing the Kronecker and Levi-Civita tensors as tools for deriving vector identities. Connects directly to compressible flow by applying Gauss's theorem to derive the control-volume form of the continuity equation.

## Key concepts
- Control volume (closed, simply connected region)
- Gradient, divergence, curl, Laplacian
- Kronecker unit tensor (delta_ij)
- Levi-Civita (alternating) tensor (epsilon_ijk)
- Index notation for vector identities
- Gauss's (divergence) theorem
- Stokes' theorem
- Continuity equation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The dot product of a vector and a tensor, and the gradient of a vector, are presented using index notation.
- Gauss's theorem converts a volume integral of a gradient into a surface integral: applicable when F is scalar, vector, or tensor.
- A volume integral involving the curl is similarly converted to a surface integral.
- Stokes' theorem is introduced but not fully developed in this chapter.
- The development is concise (17 slides) and problem-focused; full proofs are left to references.
