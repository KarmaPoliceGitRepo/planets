## Overview
The definitive reference for engineering mathematics, covering: ODEs, linear algebra, vector calculus, Fourier analysis, PDEs, complex analysis, numerical methods, optimization, graphs, and probability/statistics. Used globally as a graduate engineering mathematics reference.

## Unique contribution
The most comprehensive single-volume engineering mathematics reference in common use. The 10th edition modernises notation, adds more numerical content, and includes new sections on mathematical software. Serves as both a course text and a reference throughout an engineer's career.

## Key concepts
- Ordinary differential equations (ODEs)
- Laplace transforms
- Fourier series and integral transforms
- PDEs (wave, heat, Laplace)
- Linear algebra (matrix methods)
- Vector calculus (Gauss, Stokes, Green)
- Complex analysis
- Numerical methods (ODEs, PDEs, linear algebra)
- Probability and statistics

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Organised as a sequence of mostly self-contained parts; each can be studied independently.
- Strong on applications: ODE solutions directly linked to circuit analysis, mechanics, heat transfer.
- Numerical methods part includes finite differences and FEM for PDEs.
- The unit conversion table on the front endpapers (cgs, mks/SI, engineering systems) is a practical reference.
