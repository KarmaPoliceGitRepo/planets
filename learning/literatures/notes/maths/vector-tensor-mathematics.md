## Overview
A concise reference section (4 pages, section 3.2) on vector and tensor mathematics intended for polymer processing engineers. Covers the basic definitions and operations: vector components, magnitude, dot/cross products; 2nd rank tensor components, transpose, symmetric and antisymmetric tensors, dyadic product, dot/double-dot products.

## Unique contribution
Very compact, notation-consistent reference for the tensor operations needed in continuum mechanics and polymer processing. Useful as a quick reference card for the specific operations used in that field (stress tensor, velocity gradient tensor).

## Key concepts
- Vector components in rectangular/cylindrical/spherical coordinates
- Magnitude of a vector
- Second-rank tensor (3×3 array)
- Transpose, symmetric tensor, unit tensor
- Dyadic product of two vectors
- Unit dyads
- Double-dot (scalar) product of two tensors
- Dot product of tensor with vector

## Methods / results / takeaways
- A vector v has components (v1, v2, v3); its magnitude |v| = sqrt(v1^2 + v2^2 + v3^2).
- The dyadic product vw has components (vw)_{ij} = v_i w_j.
- Double-dot product T:U = sum_{i,j} T_{ij} U_{ji} (scalar result).
- In cylindrical and spherical coordinates, unit vectors are not constant in direction (except δz in cylindrical), so differential operators must account for this.
- Tensor magnitude |T| = sqrt(T:T^T / 2).
