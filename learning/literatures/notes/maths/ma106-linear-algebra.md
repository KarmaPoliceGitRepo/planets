## Overview
Official lecture notes for the Warwick MA106 Linear Algebra module. Covers abstract linear algebra: fields and their axioms, vector spaces over a field, linear independence, spanning sets, bases, subspaces, linear transformations, kernels and images, rank-nullity theorem, and matrix representations. Developed at Warwick for first-year mathematics undergraduates.

## Unique contribution
Introduces linear algebra at the abstract level from the start (vector spaces over an arbitrary field, not just R^n), which is more general than most first-year treatments. This prepares students for Galois theory and abstract algebra in later years.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Rank-nullity theorem: dim(ker T) + dim(im T) = dim(domain).
- A linear map T: V → W is injective iff ker T = {0}; surjective iff im T = W.
- Any two bases of a vector space have the same cardinality (dimension is well-defined).
- Matrix of a linear map depends on choice of basis in domain and codomain; change of basis uses the transition matrix.
- The abstract vector space framework covers function spaces, polynomial spaces, etc., not only R^n.
