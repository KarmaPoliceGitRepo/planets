## Overview
An undergraduate text from Cambridge providing a unified treatment of algebra and geometry. Topics include complex numbers, scalar and vector products, determinants, linear algebra, group theory, permutation and symmetry groups, isometries, rotations, and spherical geometry. The emphasis is on the interplay between algebraic structures and geometric objects.

## Unique contribution
Introduces the concept of a group in Chapter 1 through the arithmetic of real and complex numbers, so algebraic structure is always contextualised by concrete geometry. Concepts are developed gradually, with each topic used to illuminate the others throughout the book.

## Key concepts
- Complex numbers and the Argand plane
- Vectors and dot/cross products
- Determinants
- Linear algebra (matrices, eigenvalues)
- Group theory
- Permutation groups
- Symmetry groups
- Isometries of the plane and sphere
- Rotations in 2D and 3D
- Spherical geometry

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The group axioms appear early so students learn to recognise algebraic structure in number systems and geometry.
- Symmetry groups of geometric figures (polygons, polyhedra) are developed alongside the abstract theory.
- Spherical geometry is treated as an application of rotation groups and SO(3).
- The text is self-contained and accessible to first/second year undergraduates with A-level mathematics.
