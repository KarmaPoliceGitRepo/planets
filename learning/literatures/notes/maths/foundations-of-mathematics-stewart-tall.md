## Overview
A rigorous introduction to mathematical foundations for undergraduate mathematics students. Starting from set theory, logic, and proof, the book constructs the natural numbers from axioms, builds the real numbers as a complete ordered field, and introduces infinite cardinal numbers. The second edition adds material on ordinal numbers and a self-explanation appendix drawn from educational research.

## Unique contribution
Unusually combines formal foundational mathematics (axiomatic set theory, construction of number systems) with explicit pedagogical guidance on how students learn to think formally. The appendix on self-explanation strategies is backed by empirical educational research and aims to improve proof comprehension.

## Key concepts
- Set theory and logic
- Mathematical proof and rigour
- Natural number axioms (Peano)
- Real numbers as complete ordered field
- Infinite sets and cardinality (Cantor)
- Ordinal numbers
- Formal definition of function
- Equivalence relations and partitions

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The book advocates learning foundations before (or alongside) applying mathematics, to develop true mathematical reasoning.
- Construction proceeds: logic → sets → natural numbers → integers → rationals → reals.
- The completeness axiom (every bounded set has a supremum) is the key property that distinguishes reals from rationals.
- Infinite cardinals: |N| = aleph_0 < |R| (Cantor's diagonal argument).
- The second edition's appendix on self-explanation has been demonstrated to improve long-term proof comprehension in controlled studies.
