## Overview
Official lecture notes for the Warwick MA250 module. Covers the five canonical PDEs of applied mathematics: the transport equation, wave equation, heat (diffusion) equation, and Laplace's equation, with Fourier series providing the spectral toolkit for the latter three. Appendices treat polar coordinates, the mean value theorem for harmonic functions, the Gibbs phenomenon, and finite differences.

## Unique contribution
A self-contained, rigorous yet accessible module-level treatment of all standard second-order linear PDEs, combined with a full Fourier series chapter that feeds directly into the heat and Laplace solutions. The appendix on the Gibbs phenomenon and finite differences is an unusual addition for an introductory module.

## Key concepts
- Partial differential equation (PDE)
- Transport equation (first-order linear PDE)
- Wave equation (hyperbolic)
- Heat / diffusion equation (parabolic)
- Laplace's / Poisson's equation (elliptic)
- Fourier series
- Separation of variables
- Harmonic functions, mean value property
- Gibbs phenomenon
- Finite difference schemes

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Each PDE type is treated with well-posedness (existence, uniqueness) in mind.
- Fourier series are developed before the heat and Laplace equations, which rely on them.
- Mean value theorem for harmonic functions is proved in an appendix.
- Gibbs overshoot (~9%) appears at jump discontinuities regardless of how many Fourier terms are taken.
- The module prepares students for follow-up courses in numerical methods and advanced PDE theory.
