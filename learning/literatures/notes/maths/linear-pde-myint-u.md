## Overview
A graduate-level textbook on linear PDEs aimed at scientists and engineers. Covers classical analytical methods (separation of variables, Green's functions, Fourier/Laplace transforms, method of characteristics) applied to the canonical PDEs: wave, heat, and Laplace equations. Also covers variational methods, eigenvalue problems, and perturbation methods.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive coverage of transform methods (Fourier, Laplace, Mellin) applied to PDEs, plus the method of characteristics for first-order equations. The fourth edition includes sections on nonlinear PDEs and solitons, broadening the scope beyond strictly linear systems.

## Key concepts
- First-order PDEs and method of characteristics
- Wave equation (hyperbolic)
- Heat/diffusion equation (parabolic)

- Separation of variables and eigenfunction expansions
- Fourier transform method

- Green's functions
- Variational methods
- Solitons (nonlinear extension)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method of characteristics: transforms first-order PDE to a system of ODEs along characteristic curves.
- Separation of variables: assumes u(x,t) = X(x)T(t), converts PDE to two ODEs linked by a separation constant.
- Green's functions encode the response to a delta-function source; the full solution is a convolution.
- Transform methods reduce PDEs to algebraic or ODE problems in transform space.
- Suitable as a graduate textbook; prerequisite is multivariable calculus and elementary ODEs.
