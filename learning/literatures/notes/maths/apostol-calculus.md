## Overview
A rigorous, theorem-proof-oriented first-year calculus textbook. Unlike most calculus texts, Apostol develops integration before differentiation, following historical and logical order. The book covers the real number system, limits, continuity, integration, differentiation, the fundamental theorem, sequences, series, and an introduction to linear algebra.

## Unique contribution
Integration-first approach: Apostol defines the integral (via step functions and the axioms for area) before defining derivatives. This is historically and logically sound and forces the student to engage with careful epsilon-delta arguments from the outset rather than manipulating derivatives mechanically.

## Key concepts
- Real number system and completeness
- Limit and continuity
- Riemann/step-function integral
- Fundamental theorem of calculus
- Differentiation
- Mean value theorem
- Taylor's theorem and series
- Linear algebra introduction (matrices, determinants, vector spaces)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The integral is defined axiomatically for step functions then extended; this separates the concept of area from the computational formula.
- The fundamental theorem connects integration and differentiation as inverse operations.
- The text strikes a balance between theoretical rigour and worked examples, aiming at students who will do further mathematics.
- The linear algebra chapters provide matrix operations and abstract vector space theory in the context of calculus (e.g. for ODEs).
