## Overview
Official lecture notes for the Warwick MA132 Foundations module, the standard first-year course introducing university-level mathematical reasoning and rigorous proof. Begins with an extended introduction to the purpose and nature of proof, then covers logic, sets, functions, number theory (primes), and the basic theory needed for analysis and algebra.

## Unique contribution
The notes motivate the need for rigour by contrasting the Jordan Curve Theorem (intuitively obvious, very hard to prove) with the infinity of primes (less obvious but provable in a few lines). This framing is pedagogically unusual and effective for first-year students encountering formal proofs for the first time.

## Key concepts

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The first theorem proved in the course is the infinitude of primes (Euclid's proof): assume finitely many, take the product plus one, obtain a contradiction.
- Rigour is emphasised not as pedantry but as the only reliable access to knowledge in abstract mathematics.
- The notes distinguish between "obvious" geometric facts (which often require careful proof) and algebraic results (where proof is the primary means of access).
