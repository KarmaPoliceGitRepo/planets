## Overview
A free introductory textbook on tensor calculus, differential geometry, and continuum mechanics, distributed as separate chapter PDF files. Part 1 covers index notation, tensor algebra, covariant differentiation, special tensors (Kronecker, Levi-Civita, metric), the Riemann-Christoffel tensor, and differential geometry of curves and surfaces. Part 2 applies tensors to dynamics, elasticity (solids), fluid mechanics, and electromagnetic field theory.

## Unique contribution
Uniquely combines a full tensor calculus introduction (Part 1) with a comprehensive continuum mechanics section (Part 2), all in one freely distributed textbook. The chapter on differential geometry and relativity (§1.5) extends the treatment to GR-relevant material. The electromagnetic fields chapter (§2.6) shows how Maxwell's equations take a natural covariant form.

## Key concepts
- Index (Einstein summation) notation
- Covariant and contravariant tensors
- Metric tensor g_{ij}
- Kronecker delta, Levi-Civita epsilon
- Christoffel symbols (2nd kind)
- Covariant derivative
- Riemann-Christoffel curvature tensor
- Differential geometry: space curves, surface curves, curvature
- Fundamental quadratic forms (1st and 2nd)
- Continuum mechanics: strain and stress tensors
- Navier-Stokes equations (fluids)
- Maxwell's equations in tensor form

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Index notation systematically tracks tensor type (upper = contravariant, lower = covariant); the summation convention eliminates explicit sigma symbols.
- Christoffel symbols encode how basis vectors change with position; they are not tensors themselves but appear in the covariant derivative.
- The Riemann tensor R^i_{jkl} is zero iff the space is flat; its contraction gives the Ricci tensor and Ricci scalar used in GR.
- Part 2 derives the fundamental equations of continuum mechanics (conservation of mass, momentum, energy) in tensor form.
- The fluid chapter derives the Navier-Stokes equations using the stress tensor for a Newtonian fluid.
- Available freely; designed for two-semester applied mathematics course at senior undergraduate or beginning graduate level.
