## Overview
The advanced companion to Stroud's Engineering Mathematics. Covers topics beyond the first-year course: further calculus, differential equations, Laplace transforms, Fourier analysis, numerical methods, matrix theory, and complex variable theory. Uses the same programmed-learning format as the main Engineering Mathematics book.

## Unique contribution
Extends Stroud's programmed-learning approach to advanced engineering mathematics topics, bridging from undergraduate calculus to graduate-level engineering applications.

## Key concepts
- Advanced differential equations
- Laplace transforms
- Fourier series and transforms
- Numerical analysis
- Matrix eigenvalues
- Complex variable theory and contour integration
- Vector analysis
