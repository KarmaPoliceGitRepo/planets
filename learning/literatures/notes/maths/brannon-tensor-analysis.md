## Overview
A draft textbook by Rebecca Brannon providing an intuition-based introduction to tensor analysis for engineering students. Emphasises the structural (component-free) and functional (index-notation) approaches side by side. Reviews popular notations used in contemporary materials modelling (including both direct and index notation, and the Voigt convention).

## Unique contribution
Serves as a translation guide between different tensor notations used across engineering disciplines (continuum mechanics, fluid dynamics, geomechanics). The "casual" style prioritises physical intuition over mathematical rigour, filling the gap between standard math texts and engineering application codes.

## Key concepts
- Scalar, vector, tensor hierarchy
- Direct notation vs. index (Einstein) notation
- Kronecker delta and Levi-Civita symbol
- Symmetric and antisymmetric tensors
- Trace and double-dot product
- Dyadic (outer/direct) product
- Voigt notation for symmetric tensors
- Coordinate transformations
- Material modelling applications

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The book is an open draft; the index was still under construction at the time of the draft, but the content is substantive.
- Direct notation (boldface) and index notation are used in parallel for each operation, aiding cross-reference with other texts.
- Includes worked examples in rectangular, cylindrical, and spherical coordinates.
- The target reader is a graduate student in mechanics who needs to read papers using different notational conventions.
