## Overview
The original (1st edition) of K. A. Stroud's famous Engineering Mathematics, the most widely used engineering mathematics textbook in the UK. It is designed as a complete first-year course using programmed learning: each topic is presented as a sequence of short frames with questions and immediate feedback, validated to achieve an 80/80 criterion (80% of students score at least 80% on tests).

## Unique contribution
The programmed-learning format is the key innovation: students work at their own pace through carefully validated sequences, answering questions at every step. Evaluated against control groups, students using the programmed version consistently outperformed lecture-taught students. The approach works well for self-study and distance learning.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Each programme is preceded by learning objectives and followed by criterion tests.
- The programmed structure gives immediate correction, reducing the spread of marks compared to lectures.
- The 1st edition (1970) covers a full year of engineering mathematics; later editions (by Stroud and Booth) extended coverage.
- Particularly useful for self-study or as a remedial reference.
