## Overview
Introductory notes on complex numbers covering arithmetic, the Argand plane, modulus and argument, the complex exponential, and Euler's formula. Develops complex numbers as an extension of the reals needed to solve x^2 + 1 = 0, then builds up arithmetic operations including division via conjugation.

## Unique contribution
Introduces complex numbers through the Argand-plane geometric picture before the algebraic formalism, grounding modulus, argument, and polar form visually. The treatment is pedagogically clear and aimed at engineering/physics students who need complex exponentials for Fourier analysis.

## Key concepts
- Imaginary unit i^2 = -1
- Argand / complex plane
- Modulus and argument (polar form)
- Complex conjugate
- Euler's formula: e^{i theta} = cos(theta) + i sin(theta)
- Complex exponential signal

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Division uses the conjugate trick: multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator to obtain a real denominator.
- |z|^2 = z * z-bar = a^2 + b^2.
- Polar form: z = |z|(cos theta + i sin theta) = |z| e^{i theta}.
- Argument is defined only up to a multiple of 2 pi.
