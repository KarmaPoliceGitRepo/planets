## Overview
A MATLAB-based companion book for digital signal processing courses, part of the BookWare Companion Series designed to integrate MATLAB computation with traditional DSP curricula. The text provides worked DSP problems and MATLAB scripts covering discrete-time signals, z-transforms, FIR and IIR filter design, DFT, FFT, and spectral analysis.

## Unique contribution
Bridges theory and computation: every concept is paired with executable MATLAB code, allowing students to explore DSP algorithms numerically. Compared to Proakis's main DSP textbook, this companion stresses the computational/problem-solving side.

## Key concepts
- Discrete-time signals and systems
- Z-transform
- DFT and FFT
- FIR and IIR filter design
- Spectral analysis
- MATLAB implementation
- Sampling and reconstruction

## Methods / results / takeaways
- The BookWare format uses worked problems as the primary teaching vehicle.
- Students work at their own pace using MATLAB scripts; problem solutions provide immediate feedback.
- MATLAB scripts for the DFT, FFT, and filter design are central to the learning experience.
- The book is explicitly designed to complement (not replace) a core DSP textbook.
