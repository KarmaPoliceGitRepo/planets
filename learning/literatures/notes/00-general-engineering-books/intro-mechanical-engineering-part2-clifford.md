## Overview
This is the second part of a two-volume undergraduate mechanical engineering textbook series developed at the University of Nottingham. It builds directly on Part 1 (which covers foundational solid mechanics, thermodynamics, and fluid mechanics) and extends these into more advanced territory. The book is organized into six units: fluid dynamics (boundary layers, drag, pipe flow, dimensional analysis), thermodynamics (air conditioning, gas mixtures, combustion, compressors, heat transfer, heat exchangers, vapour power cycle, IC engines), solid mechanics (combined loading, yield criteria, beam deflection, elastic-plastic deformation, elastic instability, shear stresses, thick cylinders, asymmetrical bending, strain energy, fatigue, fracture mechanics, thermal stresses), electromechanical drive systems, feedback and control theory, and structural vibration. The text is accompanied by a companion website with worked solutions and self-assessment materials.

## Unique contribution
Provides a coherent, integrated second-year undergraduate treatment spanning six distinct engineering subdisciplines in a single volume, tied to the University of Nottingham curriculum. The structural vibration unit (Unit 6) is particularly detailed, covering both analytical (natural frequencies, mode shapes, multi-DOF systems, modal analysis) and experimental modal analysis methods, plus vibration control. The electromechanical drives unit (Unit 4) covers DC motors, inverter-fed induction motors, and hydraulic/pneumatic power sources together with load-matching analysis, which is less common in introductory texts.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Fluid dynamics:** Derives laminar (5.0·x/√Re) and turbulent (0.37·x/Re^0.2) boundary-layer thickness formulae. Demonstrates Kármán integral method for drag calculation on flat plates. Covers flow separation mechanism: adverse pressure gradient causes velocity profiles to develop reverse flow (shape factor H rises sharply before separation).
- **Thermodynamics:** Covers psychrometric chart usage, air-gas mixture analysis by mole fractions, combustion stoichiometry and excess air calculations, reciprocating compressor work with intercooling, and multipass heat exchanger design using correction factor F on LMTD.
- **Solid mechanics:** Treats combined bending, torsion, and axial loads with Mohr's circle; yield under combined stresses using Tresca and von Mises; beam deflection by superposition and energy methods; Euler column buckling with various end conditions; thick-cylinder hoop/radial stress (Lamé equations); fatigue under variable amplitude loading (Miner's rule); fracture mechanics (K_I, fracture toughness).
- **Vibration (Unit 6):** Derives equations of motion for SDOF and MDOF systems; obtains natural frequencies and mode shapes; covers damped forced response (frequency response functions); introduces experimental modal analysis (FRF measurement, modal parameter extraction from Nyquist plots).
- **Drives (Unit 4):** Matches motor torque-speed characteristics to load characteristics graphically; analyses DC motor (armature current, back-EMF, torque equation) and inverter-fed induction motor (slip, torque-speed curve modification); covers pneumatic and hydraulic power sources.
- **Control (Unit 5):** Block diagram algebra, Laplace/s-domain, PID tuning, stability analysis, root-locus plotting.
