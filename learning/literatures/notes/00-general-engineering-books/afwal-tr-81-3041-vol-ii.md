## Overview
This is Volume II (of three) of a US Air Force (AFWAL) funded technical report on the static strength and fatigue life of mechanically fastened composite joints. The full program ran from 1978 to 1981 under AFWAL contract F33615-78-C-3140. Volume II specifically documents the test data, equipment, and procedures for Tasks 2, 3, and 4: evaluating twelve joint design variables, seven manufacturing/service anomalies, and critical design variables on fatigue life, respectively. The material system throughout is Hercules AS/3501-6 graphite-epoxy composite. Volume I covers methodology development; Volume III provides the BJSFM computer program user's manual.

## Unique contribution
Provides a comprehensive experimental dataset (hundreds of static and fatigue tests) for bolted composite joints under three environmental conditions (room temperature dry, room temperature wet, elevated temperature wet at 250°F/~0.86% moisture). The study is distinctive in quantifying the effect of realistic manufacturing anomalies—out-of-round holes, broken fibers on exit side, porosity, improper fastener seating, tilted countersinks, interference fit tolerances, and fastener removal/reinstallation cycles—on joint static strength. The Bolted Joint Stress Field Model (BJSFM) procedure is developed as a predictive tool.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Test matrix (Task 2):** 428 tests evaluating 12 design variables on a 50/40/10 (0°/±45°/90°) baseline laminate, 20-ply, using two-bolt-in-tandem specimens in double-shear configuration. Variables included w/d ratio, e/d ratio, laminate layup, number of plies, off-axis loading angle, and load interaction.
- **Failure modes** observed include bearing, bearing-shearout, shearout-tension-cleavage, net-section, and tension-cleavage. Off-axis loading shifts the failure mode from bearing (0°) toward shearout/tension-cleavage (45°–80°) to net section (90°).
- **Multi-test specimen reuse:** Failed specimens were machined and re-drilled; ultrasonic C-scan confirmed that gross laminate damage is confined to the immediate hole vicinity, validating the reuse procedure.
- **Task 3 (anomalies):** 116 tests; seven anomaly types evaluated against the Task 2 baseline. Interference fit, porosity, and hole drilling quality all measurably affect joint strength.
- **Task 4 (fatigue):** Spectrum and constant-amplitude fatigue tests on seven critical design variables. Spectrum fatigue loads were measured (truncated spectrum), and hole elongation and failure mode were tracked with life.
- **BJSFM:** Stress-field model correlates analytic predictions with static strength data; model limitations identified for stress concentration at loaded holes.
- **Practical takeaway:** Environmental conditioning (ETW) consistently reduces joint strength relative to RTD; the magnitude depends on layup and load type. Interference fit improves bearing strength but introduces delamination risk visible in photomicrographs.
