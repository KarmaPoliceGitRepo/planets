## Overview
An introductory graduate-level textbook on the mechanical design and development of reciprocating piston internal-combustion engines, covering both diesel and spark-ignition types with emphasis on automobile engines. The book fills a gap in the engineering curriculum: while thermodynamics-focused engine texts are common, this volume focuses on structural design, mechanical development, durability validation, and the engineering decision-making process that dominates actual industry practice. It spans engine configuration through every major sub-system — block, head, bearings, lubrication, cooling, gaskets, pistons, crankshaft, connecting rods, and valve train.

## Unique contribution
The book's primary contribution is its engineering-process orientation: it teaches not just what components look like, but how design decisions are made and validated under real constraints (fatigue life, NVH, cost, manufacturability). It integrates the engine development process (Chapter 4) and durability validation methodology (Chapter 3) before diving into component design, providing a framework that pure thermodynamics texts lack. Figures were created specifically for the book to emphasize isolated design features, avoiding the complexity of production drawings.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Engine maps (Chapter 2) illustrate how torque and power curves differ across automobile, motorcycle, light-truck, heavy-truck, and off-highway applications; understanding market requirements is prerequisite to design decisions.
- Durability validation (Chapter 3) is built around fatigue analysis: S-N curves, mean stress corrections (Goodman diagram), and the distinction between high-cycle (belt/chain loads on block) and low-cycle (thermal fatigue on head) failure modes.
- Friction, lubrication, and wear in engines are addressed as an integrated topic — hydrodynamic bearing operation, asperity contact, and wear mechanisms all appear in Chapter 3 as inputs to bearing sizing and ring design.
- Bearing sizing uses both classical (Sommerfeld number approach) and dynamic (orbit simulation) methods; material selection balances fatigue strength, conformability, embedability, and corrosion resistance.
- Bore-to-stroke ratio is optimized as a trade-off among displacement, surface-to-volume ratio (affecting combustion efficiency and heat loss), engine height/package, and piston speed limits.
- Piston ring dynamic analysis addresses ring flutter and collapse, oil consumption, and blow-by — cylinder wall machining finish (honing) is shown to be critical to ring seating.
- Crankshaft torsional vibration is managed by tuned dampeners; the chapter provides analytical approaches to natural frequency estimation and dampener sizing.
- The book explicitly targets engineers entering the engine industry and graduate students, providing references at each chapter end for deeper study.
