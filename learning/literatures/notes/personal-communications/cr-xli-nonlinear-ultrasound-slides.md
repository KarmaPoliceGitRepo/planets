## Overview
Confirmation review presentation slides for Xiangwei Li's PhD, covering the theory of nonlinear ultrasound at frictional interfaces, analytical and experimental investigation of Contact Acoustic Nonlinearity (CAN), and planned future work. This is an intermediate milestone document before the completed 2018 thesis.

## Unique contribution
Documents the state of the research at the confirmation stage (2015), including early experimental evidence of third harmonic generation in aluminium-interface specimens. Shows the research trajectory: from theory through preliminary experiment to the full friction coefficient extraction methodology completed later.

## Key concepts
- Contact Acoustic Nonlinearity (CAN)
- Clapping mechanism (longitudinal wave)
- Stick-slip motion under shear ultrasound
- Harmonic generation (3rd, 5th, odd harmonics)
- Shear ultrasonic technique for friction measurement
- Nonlinear ultrasound vs. traditional NDE
- Piezoelectric element frequency response / harmonic artefacts
- FFT signal processing, Hanning window, zero padding
- High frequency nonlinear ultrasonic technique (transmitter PZT + wideband receiver)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Analytical finding:** Shear ultrasound at a frictional interface (Coulomb model) generates only odd harmonics (3ω, 5ω) in both reflected and transmitted waves, due to the symmetric clipping from stick-slip. Harmonic amplitude increases at low contact pressure and decreases at high contact pressure.
- **Friction coefficient effect:** Higher friction coefficients lead to greater harmonic generation (from analytical model).
- **Experimental setup (early stage):** Aluminium cylinders compressed at varying load; arbitrary function generator + amplifier drives PZT transmitter; wideband receiver captures fundamental at 3.6 MHz and 3rd harmonic at 10.8 MHz.
- **Early results:** Third harmonic was detected experimentally. Different trends were observed between Al-Al interface specimens and continuous Al reference specimens, consistent with CAN theory.
- **Challenge identified:** PZT elements (especially 1 MHz) generate their own odd harmonics; 5 MHz element showed cleaner single-frequency response. Excitation frequency must be chosen to avoid overlap with element self-harmonics.
- **Planned work:** Advanced model with interfacial stiffness, numerical simulation, improved specimen materials and surface preparation, longitudinal and surface wave investigation.
