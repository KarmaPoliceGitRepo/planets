## Overview
A structured reference document covering piezoceramic terminology, material classifications, and thermal stability data from three commercial suppliers: Physik Instrumente (PI), Meggitt (Ferroperm), and Del Piezo. The focus is selecting appropriate piezoceramic materials for ultrasonic transducers operating at elevated temperatures, particularly for engine and industrial measurement applications.

## Unique contribution
Aggregates comparative specifications (Curie temperature, working temperature, dielectric constant, piezoelectric charge coefficient, coupling factor, quality factor) from three manufacturers into one reference, enabling side-by-side selection for the STAMINA/journal-bearing sensing application. Highlights lead-free BNT-based alternatives to conventional PZT.

## Key concepts
- Piezoelectric effect, poling, polarization axis (direction 3)
- Piezoelectric charge/strain coefficient d_ij (e.g., d33)
- Relative permittivity ε (free vs. clamped)
- Elastic compliance s_ij; Young's modulus
- Frequency coefficient N_i (describes geometry-resonance relationship)
- Mechanical quality factor Q_m
- Soft PZT vs. hard PZT
- Lead Zirconate Titanate (PZT); BNT-based lead-free ceramics
- Curie temperature; recommended operating temperature
- Dielectric constant vs. temperature curves
- Ultrasonic transducer applications: actuators, NDT, sonar, medical

## Methods / results / takeaways
- **Soft PZT** (e.g., PI PIC151, PIC155): high d33 and coupling factor, but limited high-temperature performance. Suitable for low-power sensors and actuators.
- **Hard PZT** (e.g., PI PIC181, PIC300; Del Piezo DL-40 to DL-48): lower d33 but high Q_m and stable at high mechanical/electrical stress. Preferred for high-power resonant ultrasonic transmitters (cleaning, welding, bearing sensing). PIC300 rated up to 250 °C (briefly 300 °C).
- **Lead Metaniobate** (Del Piezo DL-31 to DL-33): low Q_m, low dielectric constant, high Curie point — useful for high-temperature, high-frequency NDT.
- **BNT-based lead-free (PI):** Curie ~200 °C, properties below standard PZT, but compliant with EU RoHS exemptions being reviewed.
- **Thermal stability key finding:** Dielectric constant and piezoelectric coefficient both degrade as temperature approaches Curie temperature. Recommended working temperature is typically 50% of Curie temperature.
- **Selection guidance for engine bearings:** PIC255 (working up to 175 °C) and PIC300 (up to 200 °C) are recommended for elevated-temperature in-situ measurements. Del Piezo BiT DL-10 (Curie 650 °C) suitable for very high-temperature sensor applications.
