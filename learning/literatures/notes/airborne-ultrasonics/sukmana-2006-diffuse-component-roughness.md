# Surface Roughness Characterization through the Use of Diffuse Component of Scattered Air-Coupled Ultrasound

- **Source:** Sukmana 2006 diffuse component roughness
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/114lWPertNxMMquhtc-6OoLTqhnFFtB8O/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Sukmana & Ihara, 2006 (Japanese Journal of Applied Physics, 45(5B), 4534–4540)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Extends Sukmana & Ihara 2005 (coherent component for Rq) to characterise the surface correlation length λ₀ using the diffuse (incoherent) component of scattered air-coupled ultrasound. Nine sandpaper specimens (Rq = 0.04–244.1 μm, λ₀ = 29–445 μm) are measured at 0.5 MHz with BAT capacitance transducers at θᵢ = 60°. The Kirchhoff model diffuse amplitude is sensitive to both Rq and λ₀; with Rq known from coherent component, λ₀ is extracted from diffuse amplitude at non-specular angles. Validated against stylus profilometer measurements.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that the diffuse (incoherent) scattering component from air-coupled ultrasound provides access to the lateral surface scale (λ₀ = correlation length), complementing the coherent component which gives the vertical scale (Rq). Together these two components enable two-parameter surface topography characterisation — the key building block for the full Sukmana 2007 (JJAP 46) two-parameter method.

## Key concepts
- Diffuse (incoherent) component of scattered ultrasound
- Surface correlation length λ₀
- Air-coupled ultrasound 0.5 MHz BAT capacitance transducers
- Kirchhoff-based scattering model diffuse term
- Two-parameter surface description (Rq + λ₀)
- Pitch-catch scattering geometry (θᵢ = 60°, θₛ varied)
- Non-specular scattering angles
- Transition angle coherent-to-diffuse ≈ 45°

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducers: 10 mm diameter BAT capacitance pair, 0.5 MHz; distance ~35 mm; pulser Ritec SP-801A; receiver BR-640A.
- Geometry: θᵢ = 60°; θ₂ scanned 0°–90° (x-z plane); θ₃ scanned ±70° (x-y plane).
- Specimens: 9 sandpapers + polished reference; Rq 0.04–244.1 μm; λ₀ 29–445 μm; height PDFs Gaussian for small Rq, slight deviation for large Rq.
- Diffuse model (Kirchhoff): Idiffuse = k²F²σ²e^(−g) / (4πr²) × AM × Σₙ gⁿ/(n!n) × exp[−k²(A²+B²)λ₀²/(4n)].
- Key result: at non-specular angles (θ₂ < 40°), diffuse amplitude increases with Rq — opposite to coherent component.
- Transition angle ≈ 45°: amplitude nearly constant with Rq (coherent and diffuse cancel).
- λ₀ estimation: once Rq known (from coherent), λ₀ extracted from diffuse amplitude via eq. (2); agrees with stylus for most specimens; discrepancy for Rq < 52 μm (signal too weak for reliable diffuse measurement).
- Directional isotropy: x-z and x-y diffuse amplitudes are similar — consistent with isotropic sandpaper texture.
- Limitation: diffuse signal very small for Rq < 52 μm; λ₀ estimation unreliable without known Rq.
