## Overview
Reviews state-of-the-art air-coupled ultrasonic NDE applications and transducer technologies (25 kHz–20 MHz). Demonstrates that acoustic-impedance mismatch (not atmospheric absorption) is the dominant obstacle. Covers a commercial aerospace inspection system (400 kHz, C-scan), and a pressurised gas-coupled system for natural-gas pipeline inspection (pulse-echo at elevated pressure).

## Unique contribution
Systematic quantitative argument showing that air-to-solid transmission losses (56–90 dB) dominate over atmospheric absorption (<10 dB at 1 MHz, 50 mm path) in the 100 kHz–1 MHz range. Demonstrates aerospace composite inspection achieving >40 dB SNR at 400 kHz. Presents first gas-coupled acoustic microscopy at elevated argon pressure (30 atm). Compares theoretical sensitivity limits of all non-contact transducer types.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- SNR formula: S/N = Pa·D / (NF·CL·AL·AB·DL·EL·4kTΔf).
- Transmission loss D for air-to-solid: 56–90 dB depending on material (foam → steel).
- Atmospheric absorption AL at 1 MHz, 50 mm path: <10 dB — not the dominant loss.
- Transducer conversion loss CL (PZT-5A round trip): up to 87 dB.
- With impedance-matched thin porous layer on transducer face: commercial system achieves >40 dB SNR at 400 kHz.
- Aerospace C-scan system: 400 kHz, 25 mm diameter focused, 50 mm focal length, 500 V pk-pk pulser, real-time operation (no averaging needed).
- Gas pipeline: pulse-echo in pressurised methane/nitrogen (6.9 MPa); shear-wave reflections from EDM notches (20%/40% wall depth) clearly detected.
- Micromachined capacitive transducer: 0.3 μm membrane displacement at 2 MHz — comparable to PZT; expected to approach laser interferometer sensitivity.
- Key message: practical air-coupled NDE requires simultaneous optimisation of transducer matching, pulser power, and receiver noise matching.
