# Cylindrical and Quasi-Cylindrical Focalization of Air-Coupled Single Element and Linear Array Transducers

- **Source:** Gomez Alvarez-Arenas 2017 cylindrical focused ACU
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1UmOqTPFgIQjCaHFmed_6S2Ehh9UwqRva/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Gómez Alvarez-Arenas, Camacho & Díez, 2017 (IEEE Ultrasonics Symposium)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Design, fabrication and test of 250 kHz cylindrically focused air-coupled 1-3 piezocomposite transducers (monolithic, quasi-cylindrical, and 4-element linear array). Cylindrical lens integrated into impedance matching stack. Demonstrates similar peak sensitivity (−24 to −27 dB) and bandwidth (75% at −20 dB) as flat equivalents, with improved lateral resolution in the focused direction (Δy = 3.9 mm at −6 dB).

## Unique contribution
Shows that a cylindrical lens can be incorporated into the multi-layer impedance matching stack of a 1-3 composite ACU transducer with <4 dB sensitivity penalty and negligible bandwidth change. The 4-element linear array prototype narrows the non-focused axis beam from Δx = 26.2 mm to Δx = 12.8 mm. Also demonstrates robustness to ±5° angle variation for Lamb-wave generation (flat transducers detune completely over the same range).

## Key concepts
- Cylindrically focused air-coupled transducer
- 1-3 piezocomposite 250 kHz
- Impedance matching stack with lens
- Linear array (kerfless, 4 elements)
- Lamb wave generation (A0 mode)
- Curved surface through-transmission
- Focal spot size
- Beam forming

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer: PZT5A fibers 250 μm in epoxy, 65% ceramic fraction; 25×25 mm slab; 250 kHz.
- Cylindrical lens radius: 75 mm; focus at ~49 mm.
- Monolithic cylindrical: Δy = 3.9 mm (-6 dB), Δx = 26.2 mm; peak sensitivity −24 dB.
- 4-element linear array element #3: Δy = 3.9 mm, Δx = 12.8 mm at focal plane (z = 47 mm).
- Cylindrical pipe test (200 mm OD, 3 mm GFRP wall): larger transmitted amplitude with focused transducer due to better wavefront matching.
- Lamb wave (A0, 2 mm Al): smaller amplitude but angle-robust within ±5° vs. complete detuning for flat transducers.
