# A Summation Formula for Use in Determining the Reflection from Irregular Bodies

- **Source:** Neubauer 1963 summation formula reflection irregular bodies
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1FhhelVeyq3IJ_d37pPYHg76CKK4lcj53/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Neubauer, 1963 (JASA 35(3), 279–285)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Derives a summation formula (based on the Kirchhoff approximation) for computing the monostatic far-field reflection from rigid bodies of arbitrary shape, using half-wavelength zones on the normal plane projection. Applies to planar reflectors, wedges, and general convex shapes. Tests against exact sphere solution — error <16% (~1.5 dB) for ka > 6.

## Unique contribution
Extends Fresnel/half-wavelength zone construction to numerically tractable summation for arbitrary shapes, providing a graphical/computational method for reflection prediction from shapes not analytically describable. First publication of this general Kirchhoff-based summation approach for irregular reflectors.

## Key concepts
- Kirchhoff approximation
- Monostatic reflection
- Far-field reflection
- Half-wavelength zones
- Fresnel zones
- Rigid finite plane
- Wedge reflector
- General convex body
- Complex plane representation (phasor diagram)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Method: project reflector onto normal plane; divide projection into half-wavelength subzones; sum phasors in complex plane.
- Finite plane solution: integral form + finite sum; plotted in complex plane as spiral.
- Wedge: sum of two plane contributions; graphical phasor addition.
- General shape: requires physical measurement of subzone areas Δap' and distances from normal plane.
- Validation against Stenzel's exact sphere solution: error <16% for ka > 6.
- Limitation: only one reflection mechanism (smooth convex surface specular reflection); surface waves excluded; requires Kirchhoff approximation (principal radii > λ).
