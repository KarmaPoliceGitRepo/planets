# Atmospheric Absorption of Sound: Theoretical Predictions

- **Source:** Evans 1972 atmospheric absorption theoretical
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1cRS_USDIbwHbq6C8SQhO1J2W_WgxLjcU/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Evans, Bass & Sutherland, 1972 (Journal of the Acoustical Society of America, 51(5B), 1565–1575)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Derives a first-principles theoretical prediction of atmospheric sound absorption using a four-gas mixture model (N₂, O₂, H₂O, CO₂) with 24 binary vibrational energy transfer mechanisms. Classical absorption (Stokes-Kirchhoff viscothermal losses) plus molecular relaxation absorption for all relevant modes. Good agreement with Harris 1966 experimental data over 0–100% RH at 20°C. Provides the "Fig. 8" absorption curves (dB/1000 ft vs f/P) that became widely cited — later corrected and superseded by Bass 1990 and Bass 1995.

## Unique contribution
First rigorous multi-mechanism theoretical prediction of total atmospheric absorption that correctly accounts for the coupled relaxation of N₂, O₂, H₂O, and CO₂. Resolves earlier single-relaxation discrepancies and explains the role of trace CO₂ at very low humidity (without CO₂: f_r,0 → 6 Hz; with 300 ppm CO₂: f_r,0 → 30 Hz). The 24-mechanism framework became the foundation for all subsequent ANSI/ISO atmospheric absorption standards.

## Key concepts
- Four-gas mixture: N₂, O₂, CO₂, H₂O
- 24 binary vibrational energy transfer mechanisms (V-T, V-V, V-R processes)
- Classical absorption: α_cl ∝ f²; viscosity + thermal conductivity + diffusion (Stokes-Kirchhoff)
- Rotational relaxation: small additional classical-type contribution
- Vibrational relaxation: dominant at audio/low ultrasonic frequencies; humidity-dependent f_r
- Role of CO₂ at low humidity: trace CO₂ (300 ppm) controls f_r,0 ≈ 30 Hz (vs 6 Hz CO₂-free)
- Absorption curves normalised: α/α_max vs f/f_max (humidity-independent shape)
- Total absorption: classical + rotational + vibrational relaxation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Model: air as ideal four-gas mixture; energy transfer rates from Shields, Taylor & Bitterman; vibrational specific heat computed from 24 rate equations.
- Classical term: α_cl = (2π²f²/γP₀V₀)[4η/3 + (γ−1)K/C_p]; rotational relaxation treated as effective C_p.
- Normalized curves α/α_max vs f/f_max collapse to single shape for each humidity range.
- Results vs Harris 1966 data: excellent agreement for 0.3%–2.5% H₂O molecules (0–100% RH at 20°C).
- Fig. 8: total absorption curves in dB/1000 ft vs f/P at 20°C — widely reproduced; contains error in saturation vapour pressure corrected in Bass 1995.
- CO₂ sensitivity: Fig. 1 shows f_r,0 varies by factor ~5 between 0 and 300 ppm CO₂ at zero humidity.
- Temperature extrapolation: method valid but rate constants uncertain; authors caution ±5°C around 20°C.
- Limitation: rates for H₂O vibrational modes poorly known; temperature dependence not fully validated.
