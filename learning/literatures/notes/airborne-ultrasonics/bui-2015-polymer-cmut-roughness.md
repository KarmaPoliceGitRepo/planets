# Polymer-Based Capacitive Micromachined Ultrasonic Transducer for Surface Roughness Measurement

- **Source:** Bui 2015 polymer CMUT roughness
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1KMKMtXYlza8N-3YqnBApYt5Qw9o16whN/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Bui, Chen & Pang, 2015 (Key Engineering Materials, 661, 22–28)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Develops a polymer-based capacitive micromachined ultrasonic transducer (CMUT) at 880 kHz for air-coupled surface roughness measurement. The transducer membrane cells are designed in a 2:1 ratio (two groups), enabling five operating modes: full TX/full RX, majority TX/majority RX, majority TX/minority RX, minority TX/majority RX, and minority TX/minority RX. Tested on 11 sandpaper grades (Rrms = 11.4–179.8 μm; 3M, P100–P2000). Demonstrates that amplitude decreases with roughness and CMUT works accurately up to Rrms ≈ 50 μm.

## Unique contribution
Applies polymer-based CMUT (rather than piezoelectric or conventional capacitance transducers) to airborne roughness measurement, exploiting flexible membrane design and multi-mode operation. The minority TX/majority RX mode achieves better energy efficiency than conventional arrangements while maintaining good signal output — relevant for embedded/low-power sensing.

## Key concepts
- Polymer-based CMUT (capacitive micromachined ultrasonic transducer)
- Air-coupled ultrasound 880 kHz
- Five measurement modes (TX/RX cell ratio)
- Surface roughness Rrms = 11.4–179.8 μm
- Reflection coefficient vs. roughness (Kirchhoff normal-incidence model)
- 10 mm standoff, normal incidence
- Energy efficiency of minority/majority mode

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer: polymer-based CMUT; resonance 880 kHz; driven at DC 100V + AC 300V bias; single T/R element; 10 mm working distance; normal incidence (θ = 0°).
- Specimens: 11 sandpaper grades (3M, P100–P2000); Rrms = 11.4–179.8 μm; stainless reference.
- Five modes evaluated: full TX/full RX has maximum output; minority TX/majority RX has best energy efficiency.
- Result: echo amplitude decreases monotonically with increasing Rrms in time-domain and frequency-domain plots; good match with Kirchhoff model for Rrms ≤ 50 μm.
- Deviation > 50 μm: scattering dominates and reduces correlation with simple model.
- Full TX/full RX: most accurate; minority TX/majority RX: better efficiency (fewer TX cells, more RX cells captures scattered energy).
- Limitation: accurate only to Rrms = 50 μm; no lateral parameter (λ₀) extraction; basic Kirchhoff model only.
