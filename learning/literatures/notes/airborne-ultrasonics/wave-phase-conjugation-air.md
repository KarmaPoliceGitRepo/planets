# Wave Phase Conjugation in Air at MHz Frequencies

- **Source:** Pylnov et al. 2009 wave phase conjugator in air

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Describes implementation of wave phase conjugation (time reversal) in air at 6.3–7.5 MHz using oil-impregnated porous membranes as acoustic impedance matching layers. Applied to air jet velocimetry demonstrating practical utility.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that MHz-range phase conjugation (normally implemented in liquids or solids) can work in air using porous membrane matching layers, enabling air-based acoustic time-reversal and velocimetry — a technique not previously demonstrated at these frequencies in air.

## Key concepts
- Wave phase conjugation / time reversal
- MHz air-coupled ultrasonics (6.3–7.5 MHz)
- Impedance matching with porous membranes
- Oil-impregnated porous membrane transducer matching
- Air jet velocimetry
- Acoustic focusing

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Frequency range: 6.3–7.5 MHz — exceptionally high for air-coupled ultrasonics.
- Matching layer: oil-impregnated porous membranes bridge impedance gap between PZT and air.
- Phase conjugation achieved by recording and time-reversing the received waveform.
- Applied to measure velocity profiles of an air jet: the conjugated wave retraces its path through the jet, allowing cross-correlation velocity extraction.
- Demonstrates coherent wave control in air at MHz despite 74 dB/m attenuation at ~7 MHz.
- Practical limitation: very short range (cm scale) due to high attenuation; applicable only to near-field lab setups.
