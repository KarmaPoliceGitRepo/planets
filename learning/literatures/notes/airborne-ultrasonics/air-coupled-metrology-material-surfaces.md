# Air-Coupled Ultrasonic Metrology of Material Surfaces Using 1-3 Composites

- **Source:** Gomez-Ullate & Montero 2004 surface metrology with 1-3 composites

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Investigates air-coupled 1-3 piezocomposite transducers for surface metrology over 800 kHz to 4 MHz. Achieves −6 dB beamwidth of 220 μm at 2 MHz, enabling high lateral resolution profilometry.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that 1-3 piezocomposite transducers can operate effectively in air at frequencies up to 4 MHz with adequate sensitivity, reaching sub-millimetre lateral resolution (220 μm at 2 MHz). This extends surface metrology capability well beyond the 40–200 kHz typical of standard air transducers.

## Key concepts
- 1-3 piezocomposite transducers
- Air-coupled ultrasonics 800 kHz – 4 MHz
- Surface metrology / profilometry
- Lateral resolution
- Beam profile
- Focused transducer

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducers: 1-3 piezocomposite, single-element focused, 800 kHz to 4 MHz variants.
- −6 dB beamwidth: 220 μm at 2 MHz (focused).
- Lateral resolution scales with frequency; higher frequency → better lateral definition but more attenuation.
- Surface profiling by pulse-echo TOF; step resolution ~1–2 μm at 4 MHz.
- 1-3 composites lower acoustic impedance vs bulk PZT, improving air-coupling efficiency.
- Tradeoff: at 4 MHz, air attenuation (~1.6 dB/cm) limits practical range to a few centimetres.
- Comparison with optical profilometry shows good agreement on machined metal and ceramic surfaces.
