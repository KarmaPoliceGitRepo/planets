# Application of Air-Coupled Ultrasound to Noncontact Evaluation of Paper Surface Roughness

- **Source:** Saniman 2014 paper roughness ACU
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ehGtM2LnN1WAiSfdsYjCmo56Jk4BiIuF/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Saniman, Sukmana & Ihara, 2014 (Journal of Physics: Conference Series, 520, 012016)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Applies air-coupled ultrasound (ACU) to measure surface roughness of paper specimens without contact. Uses pulse-echo normal-incidence configuration at multiple frequencies (0.3–4.2 MHz) and compares ultrasonic Rq estimates against stylus profilometer. Demonstrates that the frequency-roughness product Rq·f = 5–60 μm·MHz defines the valid measurement window. Higher frequencies (4 MHz) extend resolution to paper-relevant Rq = 2–6 μm. Skewness Rsk is detectable from deviations between the measured reflection coefficient and the Kirchhoff Gaussian prediction.

## Unique contribution
First systematic ACU study targeting paper roughness specifically. Identifies the dimensionless Rq·f product as the key operating parameter for ACU roughness measurement, providing a practical design rule for selecting measurement frequency given target Rq range. Shows that asymmetry (skewness) of paper fibre networks produces measurable deviations from the symmetric Kirchhoff model, paving the way for non-Gaussian extensions.

## Key concepts
- Air-coupled ultrasound (ACU) pulse-echo normal incidence
- Paper surface roughness measurement
- Kirchhoff scattering model
- Measurement window: Rq·f = 5–60 μm·MHz
- Multiple frequencies: 0.3, 0.5, 0.75, 1.5, 4.2 MHz
- Surface roughness Rq = 2–93 μm (paper range: 2–20 μm)
- Skewness Rsk detectable from Kirchhoff deviation
- Non-Gaussian surface characteristics of paper

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Specimens: paper samples with Rq measured by stylus profilometer; Rq range 2–93 μm.
- Transducers: capacitance-type ACU transducers (BAT system); frequencies 0.3–4.2 MHz; pulse-echo normal incidence.
- Model: Kirchhoff coherent reflection coefficient I = exp(−2k²Rq²) for normal incidence.
- Valid range: Rq·f = 5–60 μm·MHz; below 5 surface too smooth for measurable attenuation; above 60 signal too weak.
- At 4.2 MHz: Rq = 2–6 μm measurable — covers typical printing paper roughness.
- At 0.3 MHz: Rq = 20–93 μm measurable — covers rough industrial papers.
- Stylus vs. ultrasonic agreement: good within valid Rq·f window; deviations at extremes.
- Skewness indication: paper specimens with negative Rsk (valleys from fibre mat) show systematically lower I than Kirchhoff Gaussian prediction; positive Rsk specimens show higher I — consistent with Saniman 2016 modified Kirchhoff model.
- Practical note: ACU avoids contact deformation of soft paper surfaces, unlike stylus; non-destructive.
