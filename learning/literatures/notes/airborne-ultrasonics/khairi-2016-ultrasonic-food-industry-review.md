## Overview
Comprehensive topical review of contact and non-contact ultrasonic measurement techniques applied to food industry safety and quality control. Covers low-frequency (18–100 kHz, high-power) ultrasound for food modification (emulsification, crystallisation, fermentation, drying) and high-frequency (2–10 MHz) ultrasound for non-destructive evaluation of food properties (thickness, velocity, density, defects, microstructure). Reviews both transmission and pulse-echo sensing modes.

## Unique contribution
Consolidates a broad cross-section of food ultrasonic applications: contact immersion sensing for liquid/solid food characterisation, and non-contact air-coupled sensing for in-line monitoring without contamination risk. Provides a structured comparison of contact vs. non-contact modes across application categories with references to specific food types (dairy, meat, baked goods, liquids).

## Key concepts
- Contact vs. non-contact ultrasonic measurement
- Food quality control (safety, thickness, velocity, density)
- Low-frequency ultrasound (18–100 kHz) for food modification
- High-frequency ultrasound (2–10 MHz) for NDE
- Transmission mode, pitch-catch mode, pulse-echo mode
- Longitudinal, shear and surface (Rayleigh) waves in food
- Air-coupled ultrasound for non-contact food inspection
- Emulsification, crystallisation, drying, fermentation
- Acoustic velocity and attenuation in food materials

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Review scope: food preparation, packaging, preservation, safety, and storage monitoring.
- Low-frequency (high-power) ultrasound: >1 W/cm²; used for emulsification, freezing, thermosonication, cavitation; modifies food structure.
- High-frequency (diagnostic) ultrasound: 2–10 MHz; non-destructive, non-invasive, in-line capable; assesses thickness, velocity, attenuation, microstructure.
- Wave types: longitudinal waves applicable to solids, liquids and gases; shear waves only for solid food; surface/Rayleigh waves for near-surface features.
- Non-contact (air-coupled) mode: avoids direct contact/contamination; suitable for production lines; lower sensitivity than contact due to impedance mismatch.
