# Surface Metrology Using Reflected Ultrasonic Signals

- **Source:** Robertson 2002 air-coupled surface metrology
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1FUeG6BCu-IJNXfaJRI9kJbf6vyQx3_an/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Robertson, 2002
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Presents a non-contact, air-coupled ultrasonic surface profilometry system using a capacitance transducer focused via an off-axis parabolic mirror. Achieves lateral resolution of 0.5 mm and vertical (height) resolution of 2–5 μm over a bandwidth of 280–820 kHz.

## Unique contribution
Uses an off-axis parabolic mirror to focus broadband air-coupled ultrasound to a spot, overcoming the diffraction limit at low frequencies. Demonstrates sub-5 μm vertical resolution without contact or immersion, making it uniquely suited to delicate or contamination-sensitive surfaces.

## Key concepts
- Air-coupled surface profilometry
- Off-axis parabolic mirror focusing
- Capacitance (electrostatic) transducer
- Broadband ultrasound 280–820 kHz
- Lateral and vertical resolution
- Non-contact surface measurement

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer: wideband capacitive, bandwidth 280–820 kHz.
- Focusing: off-axis parabolic mirror; focal spot ~0.5 mm diameter at mid-band.
- Vertical (TOF) resolution: 2–5 μm (depends on SNR and bandwidth).
- Lateral resolution: 0.5 mm.
- Scan performed mechanically; a step-and-measure protocol records pulse-echo waveforms.
- Peak detection or cross-correlation used to extract surface height from TOF.
- Compared well with stylus profilometer on machined metal samples.
- Limitation: mirror geometry limits angular access; off-specular surfaces scatter signal away.
