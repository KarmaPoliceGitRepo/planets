# Amplitude-Modulated Phase-Inverted (AMPI) Signal for TOF Measurement

## Overview
Proposes the amplitude-modulated phase-inverted (AMPI) driving signal for 40 kHz narrow-band air-coupled transducers to achieve sub-0.02 mm TOF accuracy over 0.1–0.5 m range, suppressing ringing artefacts that limit accuracy with conventional burst excitation.

## Unique contribution
AMPI signal design specifically suppresses ring-down and multi-path interference in narrow-band (40 kHz resonant) transducers by using a phase-inverted amplitude envelope, enabling sub-0.02 mm distance accuracy — better than typically achievable with standard burst excitation on the same transducers.

## Key concepts
- AMPI (amplitude-modulated phase-inverted) signal
- 40 kHz narrow-band air-coupled transducer
- TOF accuracy
- Ring-down suppression
- Transducer excitation waveform design
- Short-range distance measurement 0.1–0.5 m

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer: standard 40 kHz narrow-band piezoelectric (high-Q resonant type).
- AMPI waveform: amplitude modulation with a phase inversion at the centre; creates destructive interference with ring-down tail.
- Accuracy achieved: < 0.02 mm over 0.1–0.5 m.
- Compared against conventional burst: AMPI reduces time-sidelobe level by >20 dB.
- Minimum measurable distance limited by ring-down; AMPI shortens effective ring-down.
- Temperature correction applied using a reference thermistor.
- Key insight: waveform shaping can substitute for expensive broadband transducer hardware when sub-mm precision is required with low-cost resonant transducers.
