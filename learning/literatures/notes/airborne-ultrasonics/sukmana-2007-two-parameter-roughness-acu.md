# Quantitative Evaluation of Two Kinds of Surface Roughness Parameters Using Air-Coupled Ultrasound

- **Source:** Sukmana 2007 two-parameter roughness ACU
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1b7aa0euUM2AOG5dgsuliB7XiTNOSKzR7/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Sukmana & Ihara, 2007 (Japanese Journal of Applied Physics, 46(7B), 4508–4513)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Applies broadband air-coupled capacitance transducers (0.5 MHz centre frequency) to simultaneously determine two surface roughness parameters: rms height Rq (from coherent component) and surface correlation length λ₀ (from diffuse component), using Kirchhoff-based scattering model optimisation. Tested on nine sandpaper specimens with Rq = 9.8–244 μm and λ₀ = 29–446 μm. Ultrasonic results agree with stylus measurements for Rq < 115 μm.

## Unique contribution
Proposes a two-parameter optimisation framework using coherent and diffuse components of scattered air-coupled ultrasound to separately extract rms roughness Rq and correlation length λ₀. Multi-frequency (0.25, 0.5, 0.75 MHz) combination extends measurable roughness range beyond single-frequency capability, from optical-limit submicron up to ~244 μm Rq — a range not accessible to optical scattering.

## Key concepts
- Air-coupled ultrasound surface roughness measurement
- Kirchhoff scattering model (coherent + diffuse components)
- rms roughness Rq from coherent component
- Surface correlation length λ₀ from diffuse component
- Two-parameter optimisation (generalised reduced gradient)
- Broadband capacitive transducers (Micro Acoustic BAT, 0.5 MHz, 10 mm)
- Pitch-catch configuration, 35 mm working distance
- Multi-frequency analysis

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducers: Micro Acoustic BAT capacitance transducers, 0.5 MHz centre, 10 mm diameter; pitch-catch, working distance 35 mm (near-field focus).
- Specimens: 9 sandpapers bonded to steel plate; Rq range 9.8–244 μm; smooth stainless steel as reference (Rq = 0.04 μm).
- Rq determination: from normalised coherent spectrum amplitude; optimise with multi-frequency N=3 (0.25, 0.5, 0.75 MHz); good agreement with stylus for Rq < 115 μm.
- λ₀ determination: from diffuse component amplitude at non-specular angle (θr = 30°); agreement poor for Rq < 52 μm (low diffuse amplitude) and Rq > 115 μm (multiple scattering / self-shadowing).
- Kirchhoff model breaks down at large roughness and large incident angles (60°); Rq > 115 μm shows large discrepancy.
- Optimal measurement angle: incident 40° preferred for wide Rq range.
- Lateral information (λ₀) useful for distinguishing surfaces with same Rq.
