# Paper Roughness Measurement Using Airborne Ultrasound

- **Source:** Stor-Pellinen 1995 paper roughness airborne
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1MTVGrVl0yUFDKVGwrlk7CLIiPkOYaheX/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Stor-Pellinen & Luukkala, 1995 (Sensors and Actuators A, 49, 37–40; University of Helsinki)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Early study applying high-frequency airborne ultrasound (0.5–4 MHz) to measuring paper roughness non-contactly in pulse-echo mode. The measurement principle is coherent reflection attenuation: A(λ, Rq) = A₀·exp(−8π²Rq²/λ²). Results are compared with Bendtsen and Parker-Printsurf air-leak reference methods on 21 different paper types. Focused transducers (1.8 MHz, 7.5 mm dia, 14.6 mm focal length, beam spot 1 mm) perform best. Detectable roughness range: 1–3 μm minimum; moving web test at 4–5 m/s gives consistent results.

## Unique contribution
First paper to systematically test airborne ultrasonic roughness measurement specifically on paper, with comparison to established paper-industry air-leak standards (Bendtsen, Parker-Printsurf). Identifies focusing as the key design feature — reduces beam diameter and eliminates sensitivity to large-scale sample bending or tilt up to ~3°. Shows that the ultrasonic method weights roughness spectrum components differently from contact air-leak methods, reflecting the different physical measurement principle (no clamping pressure).

## Key concepts
- Airborne ultrasound paper roughness measurement
- Pulse-echo normal incidence reflection coefficient
- Coherent reflection attenuation: A = A₀·exp(−8π²Rq²/λ²)
- Focused transducers (1.8 MHz, 0.5–4 MHz range)
- Bendtsen and Parker-Printsurf air-leak reference methods
- Beam diameter and cut-off wavelength
- Moving paper web measurement at 4–5 m/s
- Rayleigh smoothness criterion: surface smooth if Rq ≪ λ

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer range tested: 500 kHz–4 MHz; focused and plane types; focused preferred.
- Optimised config: 1.8 MHz, 7.5 mm dia, 14.6 mm focal length; beam spot on paper = 1 ± 0.2 mm.
- Specimens: 21 paper types + machined metal/plastic reference samples.
- Measurement: 8 spots per sample; normalised to smooth steel plate reference.
- Results: air-ultrasonic amplitude (inverse of relative echo) correlates well with Bendtsen G₃ and Printsurf roughness for most samples; some deviation because paper has varying height distribution (non-Gaussian) and the methods weight spectrum differently.
- Key physical note: upper measurable frequency limited to ~5 MHz by air absorption; λ = 70 μm at 5 MHz — most paper is in "smooth" Rayleigh regime so only diffraction losses (not profiling) are observable.
- Minimum detectable roughness: 1–3 μm depending on transducer.
- Moving web: results at 4–5 m/s same as stationary — practical for on-line use.
- Limitation: low-frequency profile components (bending, waviness) cause misalignment loss unless focused transducer used; air-leak methods have opposite weighting (clamping emphasises macro-scale).
