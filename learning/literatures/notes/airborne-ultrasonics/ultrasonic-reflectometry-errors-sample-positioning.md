## Overview
Analyses errors introduced by sample tilt and lateral displacement in air-coupled ultrasonic reflectometry (pulse-echo). Quantifies how positioning inaccuracies propagate into errors in measured reflection coefficients, providing practical guidance for experimental setups.

## Unique contribution
Provides a systematic analytical and experimental framework for quantifying the sensitivity of air-coupled reflectometry measurements to sample positioning errors (tilt, lateral shift, distance). Essential reference for anyone setting up precision air-coupled reflection coefficient measurements.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Paper analyses errors from sample tilt angle, lateral sample displacement, and transducer-sample distance variation.
- Shows that tilt angles of even a few tenths of a degree cause measurable errors in reflection coefficient at high frequencies.
- Lateral displacement errors couple to beam non-uniformity; focused transducers are more sensitive than flat ones.
- Provides correction formulas and recommended tolerance limits.
- Key practical guidance: for ±1% accuracy in reflection coefficient, tilt must be controlled to <0.1° at 1 MHz.
- Critical for reference-free absolute measurement of acoustic impedance from reflection data.
