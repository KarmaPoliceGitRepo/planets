# Pulse-Echo Nonlinearity of a Focused Transducer: KZK / MGB Model

- **Source:** Zhang 2017 pulse-echo nonlinearity focused transducer
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1nQxkZeMXSs8_8BAhZd70V8GuWvVCq2Lq/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Zhang et al., 2017 (Applied Acoustics 117)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Studies second-harmonic generation in pulse-echo mode with a focused transducer immersed in water, using the KZK equation with the MGB (multiscale Gauss-Bernoulli) model. Derives that the phase difference between the newly generated second harmonic and the reflected second harmonic is π/2 regardless of the boundary reflector type (rigid, soft, or intermediate impedance). Validated with water–steel, water–aluminum, and water–air interfaces.

## Unique contribution
Analytical proof that the pulse-echo second harmonic phase offset is universally π/2 at the focal region, independent of boundary acoustic impedance — simplifying nonlinear ultrasonic NDE interpretation. Provides validated KZK + MGB model for focused-transducer nonlinearity prediction.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Medium: water immersion; focused transducer.
- Model: KZK equation solved with MGB decomposition for focused beams.
- Interfaces tested: water–steel (high impedance), water–aluminum (intermediate), water–air (low impedance / nearly free surface).
- Key finding: phase of reflected second harmonic relative to incident second harmonic = π/2 at focal point, independent of boundary type.
- Implication: nonlinear NDE with pulse-echo focused transducers can use this universal phase relationship to isolate material nonlinearity from boundary effects.
- Note: study is water-immersion; not directly air-coupled, but provides nonlinear acoustic framework relevant to high-power air-coupled systems.
