## Overview
Short 2-page conference proceedings presenting the integrated two-parameter (Rq + λ₀) characterisation of surface roughness using air-coupled ultrasound. Builds directly on the two previous JJAP papers (Sukmana 2005 coherent component for Rq; Sukmana 2006 diffuse component for λ₀). Proposes a combined inversion: Rq from coherent component using multi-frequency optimisation; λ₀ from diffuse component once Rq is known. Validated on 9 sandpaper specimens; Rq agreement with stylus up to ~120 μm, λ₀ with some discrepancy from noise at low Rq.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration of the complete two-parameter extraction from a single air-coupled ultrasound measurement session. Identifies the validated Rq range limit (~120 μm beyond which coherent component disappears) and the λ₀ extraction procedure using eq. (3) from Kirchhoff diffuse model. This is the conference precursor to the full Sukmana 2007 JJAP journal paper.

## Key concepts
- Two-parameter surface roughness characterisation (Rq + λ₀)
- Air-coupled ultrasound 0.5 MHz BAT transducers
- Coherent component → Rq estimation
- Diffuse component → λ₀ estimation
- Multi-frequency optimisation (0.25, 0.5, 0.75 MHz) for Rq
- Kirchhoff scattering model
- θᵢ = 60° pitch-catch geometry
- Valid Rq range: up to ~120 μm

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducers: 0.5 MHz BAT capacitance pair; 35 mm standoff; θᵢ = 60°; diffuse measured at θ = 30°.
- Rq extraction: multi-frequency amplitude at specular angle (α = θ = 60°); optimise Rq·f normalised parameter with N = 3 components (0.25, 0.5, 0.75 MHz); valid for Rq < 120 μm.
- λ₀ extraction: measure diffuse amplitude at θ = 30°; solve eq. (3) with known Rq: Idiffuse ∝ AM × exp[−g] × Σ gⁿ/(n!n) × exp[−k²(A²+B²)λ₀²/(4n)].
- Result Rq: good agreement with stylus for Rq < 120 μm (Fig. 3); deviates for Rq > 120 μm (coherent nearly vanishes).
- Result λ₀: reasonable agreement (Fig. 5) but some scatter — attributed to noise in diffuse amplitude for small Rq specimens.
