# Calculation of Volumetric Sound Field of Pulsed Air-Coupled Ultrasound Transducers Based on Single-Plane Measurements

- **Source:** Sanabria 2018 volumetric sound field ACU

## Overview
Proposes a Rayleigh–Sommerfeld + time-reversal reradiation method to reconstruct the full 3D volumetric pressure field of an air-coupled ultrasound transducer from a single measured plane scan. Validated for circular, square, and rectangular (2D) planar transducers in 50–230 kHz range. RMSE <2.5% in far field, <5.5% in near field.

## Unique contribution
Closed-form single-plane reradiation enabling volumetric field reconstruction without scanning the entire 3D volume — critical for transducer QC and calibration of ACU probes. Also shows the method outperforms baffled piston models and demonstrates accurate waveform reproduction even at the transducer radiation surface.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Frequency range: 50–230 kHz; transducer geometries: circular, square (3D), rectangular (2D).
- Measurement: condenser microphone on grid; window ≥ 4× transducer area; step size ≤ λ/3.
- Reradiation: combined RS integral + time-reversal to propagate measured plane to all other planes.
- RMSE: <2.5% far field, <5.5% near field; speed-of-sound drift contribution <0.3%.
- Outperforms baffled piston models, especially in near field and at transducer surface.
- Applications: defect detection in transducer radiation surface, side-lobe characterisation, source functions for quantitative NDE inverse problems.
