# Application of Air-Coupled Ultrasound to Noncontact Surface Roughness Evaluation

- **Source:** Sukmana 2005 ACU roughness first
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1c4iUs1MUY0q0-30vl45pxAI-8vYkeK8p/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Sukmana & Ihara, 2005 (Japanese Journal of Applied Physics, 44(6B), 4417–4420)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Foundational paper demonstrating for the first time that air-coupled ultrasound (ACU) can measure surface roughness quantitatively without any liquid couplant. Uses BAT capacitance transducers (0.5 MHz) in pitch-catch specular reflection mode on nine sandpaper specimens (Rq = 0.04–244 μm). The coherent component of the reflected wave is measured; its amplitude decreases with Rq following the Kirchhoff model (I = I₀·exp(−k²Rq²(cosθᵢ + cosθᵣ)²/2)). Valid for Rq·f < 100 μm·MHz. Multi-frequency analysis collapses measurements onto a single Rq·f master curve.

## Unique contribution
First experimental proof that ACU can measure surface roughness non-contactly across Rq = 0.04–244 μm using coherent component attenuation. Identifies the dimensionless product Rq·f as the master parameter governing sensitivity: when Rq·f < 100 μm·MHz, amplitude changes are significant and measurable; beyond 100 μm·MHz the signal is too attenuated. Also shows that increasing roughness not only reduces amplitude but shifts center frequency from 0.5 MHz toward 0.2 MHz — i.e., high-frequency components are preferentially scattered.

## Key concepts
- Air-coupled ultrasound (ACU) coherent component
- Kirchhoff scattering model: I = I₀·exp(−k²Rq²(cosθᵢ + cosθᵣ)²/2)
- Pitch-catch specular reflection configuration
- BAT capacitance transducers, 0.5 MHz, 10 mm diameter
- 35 mm standoff from surface
- Multiple incident angles: 0°, 30°, 40°, 50°, 60°
- Normalised roughness parameter Rq·f < 100 μm·MHz
- Frequency spectrum shift with roughness (center frequency drop)
- Sandpaper specimens Rq = 0.04–244 μm

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducers: BAT capacitance pair, 0.5 MHz; 35 mm standoff; Ritec SP-801A pulser, BR-640A receiver; 100 MHz sampling; 20-signal average.
- Geometry: pitch-catch at θᵢ = θᵣ for five angles: 0°, 30°, 40°, 50°, 60° (0° uses single-transducer pulse-echo).
- Kirchhoff coherent model: I/I₀ = exp(−k²Rq²(cosθᵢ + cosθᵣ)²/2).
- Result 1: amplitude decreases markedly with Rq for all angles; coherent almost vanishes for Rq = 244 μm.
- Result 2: no strong incident-angle dependence — angle is not a critical factor for this amplitude-based measurement.
- Result 3: multi-frequency collapse — amplitude vs Rq·f plots at 0.25, 0.5, 0.75 MHz fall on a single theoretical curve; valid for Rq·f < 100 μm·MHz.
- Result 4: frequency spectrum shifts — center frequency drops from 0.5 MHz to 0.2 MHz as Rq increases; higher frequency components preferentially scattered.
- Critical Rq: theoretically 2k²Rq² ≈ 1 gives Rq ≈ 80 μm for incoherent dominance at 0.5 MHz; experimentally ≈100 μm.
- Limitation: only Rq from coherent component; no lateral information (λ₀); this paper establishes foundation for diffuse component work (Sukmana 2006).
