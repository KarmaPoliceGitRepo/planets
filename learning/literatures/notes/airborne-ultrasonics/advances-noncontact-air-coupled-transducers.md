# Advances in Non-Contact Air-Coupled Transducers

- **Source:** Hutchins & Schindel 1994 advances in non-contact transducers

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Review paper covering advances across multiple non-contact transducer technologies: laser ultrasonics, electromagnetic acoustic transducers (EMATs), capacitive (electrostatic) transducers, and piezocomposites. Includes NDE imaging results on composite materials using air-coupled transducers.

## Unique contribution
Broad comparative review that benchmarks laser, EMAT, capacitive, and piezocomposite non-contact transducers side-by-side for NDE applications. Presents C-scan imaging data demonstrating practical defect detection in composite panels, providing the reference comparison for air-coupled NDE capability circa 1994.

## Key concepts
- Non-contact ultrasonic transducers
- Laser ultrasonics
- EMAT (electromagnetic acoustic transducer)
- Capacitive / electrostatic transducer
- 1-3 piezocomposite
- Air-coupled NDE
- C-scan imaging
- Composite material inspection

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Laser generation: Nd:YAG pulse; broadband, no contact; limited by ablation threshold for generation.
- EMATs: work without couplant on conductive surfaces; lower sensitivity than piezoelectric.
- Capacitive transducers: broadband, flat frequency response, lower sensitivity than piezoelectric; require high voltage bias.
- 1-3 composites: best sensitivity/bandwidth trade-off for air-coupled NDE.
- C-scan results on PEEK/graphite composite: defects (delaminations ~5 mm) visible with capacitive and composite transducers.
- Key message: no single technology dominates; choice depends on material, access constraints, and required bandwidth.
- Sets agenda for 1990s NDE research emphasising air-coupled composites.
