# Quantitative Roughness Characterization of Non-Gaussian Random Rough Surfaces by Ultrasonic Method Using Pitch-Catch and Pulse-Echo Configurations

- **Source:** Saniman 2020 non-Gaussian roughness ultrasonic
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1-NfJRedy7Flgg_H0UjPBLC8xDlf9B_qw/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Saniman, Wahid, Foudzi, Ladin & Ihara, 2020 (International Journal of Mechanical & Mechatronics Engineering IJMME-IJENS, 20(1), 80–87)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Extends Kirchhoff-based ultrasonic roughness measurement to non-Gaussian surfaces by incorporating Johnson distribution statistics. Derives a "Johnson characteristic function" giving the theoretical relationship among reflection coefficient I, Rq, and skewness Rsk. Proposes a two-configuration method (pitch-catch at PC: f=0.3 MHz, θ=60° to determine Rq; pulse-echo at PE: f=0.6 MHz, θ=0° to determine Rsk). Numerically validated on 12 non-Gaussian surface profiles with Rq = 50–85 μm, Rsk = −1 to 1; Rq error ±3%, Rsk error ±20%.

## Unique contribution
First derivation of the Johnson characteristic function for ultrasonic scattering from non-Gaussian rough surfaces, enabling simultaneous non-contact extraction of both Rq and Rsk from air-coupled ultrasound. Identifies that at small Rq·cosθ/λ (PC configuration), Rsk influence on I is negligible, enabling Rq extraction; at large Rq·cosθ/λ (PE configuration), I is sensitive to Rsk, enabling skewness extraction when Rq is known.

## Key concepts
- Non-Gaussian surface roughness characterisation
- Johnson distribution for height PDF
- Kirchhoff characteristic function (CF)
- Pitch-catch (PC) + pulse-echo (PE) dual-configuration method
- Rq from PC coherent component
- Rsk (skewness) from PE reflection coefficient
- Air-coupled ultrasound 0.3–0.6 MHz
- Kurtosis-skewness coupling: Rku = 1.89Rsk² + 1.65|Rsk| + 3

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Theoretical result: I depends on Rq·cosθ/λ; at Rq·cosθ/λ ≤ 0.037 (region A), Rsk influence negligible; at Rq·cosθ/λ ≥ 0.081 (region B), Rsk influence significant.
- PC config (f=0.3 MHz, θ=60°): use Gaussian CF to extract Rq; valid for Rq ≤ 87 μm in air.
- PE config (f=0.6 MHz, θ=0°): use Johnson CF with known Rq to extract Rsk; valid for Rq ≥ 47 μm.
- Numerical validation: RqPC error ±3%; RskPE error ±20%.
- Limitation: dynamic range of I vs Rsk is small → sensitive to measurement noise; precise I measurement required.
- Method applicable to contact and non-contact configurations (air-coupled, water-immersion).
