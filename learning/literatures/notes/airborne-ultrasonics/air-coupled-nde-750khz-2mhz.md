# Air-Coupled Ultrasonic NDE: Experiments in the Frequency Range 750 kHz–2 MHz

- **Source:** Blomme et al. 2002 NDE 750 kHz-2 MHz
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1EhlTNoskRvnTw260Y9-K7Z6xZVCnod_g/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Blomme, Bulcaen & Declercq, 2002 (NDT&E International 35, 417)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Demonstrates air-coupled ultrasonic NDE (both through-transmission and reflection) at 0.75–2 MHz on diverse materials: coated textile, zamak thin casting, aluminium plate, spot welds on steel, and epoxy with copper layer. Uses ULTRAN NCT-series piezo transducers with matching layers and chirp pulse compression in NCA1000 system.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive survey of MHz-range air-coupled NDE on a deliberately diverse material set, including high-impedance metals (steel, aluminium). Demonstrates spot weld quality assessment — detecting a poorly formed weld — and distinguishes correct vs over-penetrated textile coating. Uses both CW continuous-mode and pulse-compression (chirp) methods.

## Key concepts
- Air-coupled NDE / NDT
- Chirp / pulse compression
- Through-transmission
- Reflection C-scan
- Impedance mismatch (Table: air→Al = 80 dB loss)
- Extinction distance at 1 MHz: ~5 cm
- ULTRAN NCT-series transducers
- Spot weld inspection
- Textile coating inspection
- Zamak thin casting inspection
- B-scan, C-scan imaging

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Impedance mismatch: air→aluminum = 80 dB; air→steel = 88 dB (Table 1 in paper).
- Extinction distance: ~5 cm at 1 MHz; ~3.1 mm at 4 MHz — limits air gap to few cm.
- Textile coating: C-scan at 1.1 MHz; CW mode; distinguished correct vs over-penetrated coating zones.
- Thin zamak casting: air inclusions from X-ray confirmed in ultrasonic C-scan at 1 MHz; high-Z material still inspectable.
- Aluminium plate (8 mm): chirp NCA1000, 0.76 MHz; 2 mm cylindrical hole clearly visible in C-scan.
- Spot welds on steel (2+4 mm sheets): 1.2 MHz focused chirp; correctly-formed welds visible, poorly-formed weld absent — confirmed destructively.
- Reflection mode (1.77 MHz): etched copper PCB logo imaged; transitions epoxy↔copper clearly resolved.
- Key limitation: pulse-echo reflection mode problematic due to strong surface reflection masking internal echoes.
