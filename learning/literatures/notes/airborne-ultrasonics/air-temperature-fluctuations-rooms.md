# Air Temperature Fluctuations in Occupied Rooms

## Overview
Field measurements of air temperature fluctuations in 19 rooms under various HVAC configurations. Examines temporal statistics of temperature variation relevant to occupant comfort and, by extension, to any acoustic sensor that relies on a stable speed of sound in indoor air.

## Unique contribution
Provides a real-world statistical dataset of indoor temperature dynamics: amplitude, frequency content (0.2–1.2 Hz dominant), spatial gradients near window slots and diffusers. Quantifies up to 3 °C peak-to-peak fluctuations in poorly mixed zones — directly bounding worst-case TOF errors for indoor air-coupled ranging.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- 19 rooms measured; thermistor probes at multiple heights and positions.
- Near window slots and supply diffusers: fluctuations up to ±1.5 °C (3 °C pk-pk).
- Dominant frequency range: 0.2–1.2 Hz; near-field gusts can be higher.
- Well-mixed room cores: fluctuations typically < 0.3 °C.
- Practical implication for ultrasonics: a 1 °C error in assumed air temperature introduces ~0.18% speed-of-sound error (~0.6 mm over 330 mm), so real-time temperature compensation is essential for sub-mm ranging in occupied rooms.
