# 1-3 Piezocomposite Transducers for Airborne Ultrasound

- **Source:** Haller & Khuri-Yakub 1992 1-3 Kapton composites for air

## Overview
Presents 1-3 piezocomposite transducers using Kapton (polyimide) polymer phase for airborne ultrasound. Uses λ/8 design methodology with total one-way insertion loss of approximately 10 dB — a dramatic improvement over bare PZT (>60 dB).

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates 1-3 composites with Kapton polymer as the matching medium, achieving ~10 dB one-way insertion loss in air. Provides the λ/8 design framework and characterises bandwidth and sensitivity, establishing 1-3 composites as the key enabling technology for practical air-coupled ultrasonics.

## Key concepts
- 1-3 piezocomposite transducer

- Broadband response

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Composite: PZT pillars in Kapton polymer matrix; pillar aspect ratio chosen for λ/8 lateral resonance.
- Effective acoustic impedance of composite: ~3–5 MRa (vs PZT 30 MRa, air 430 Ra).
- One-way insertion loss: ~10 dB (vs >60 dB for bare PZT).
- Bandwidth: −6 dB bandwidth >50% (much broader than single-crystal resonant transducer).
- Temperature dependence mild; Kapton dimensionally stable.
- Key design rule: composite impedance Zc ≈ √(Z_air × Z_PZT) for optimal quarter-wave matching; 1-3 geometry achieves this with polymer filler.
- Subsequent work confirmed these composites enable MHz-range air-coupled sensing for metrology.
