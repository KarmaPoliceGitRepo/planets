# Ultrasonic Sensors in Air

- **Source:** Magori 1994 ultrasonic sensors in air
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1eCfeWy0UFJbQM3sJns2ghwgr2pSQoZ92/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Magori, 1994 (IEEE Ultrasonics Symposium)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Broad review of air-coupled ultrasonic sensor systems by Siemens' lead researcher, covering propagation-path sensors (flow, temperature, gas concentration), distance/presence sensors, and intelligent signal processing. Describes commercial products (household gas meter, automotive intake air sensor, Sonar Bero presence sensors). Covers PVDF array transducers, L2QZ composites, RU-type piezoceramic, and aerogel matching.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive 1994 overview of the full ultrasonic sensor ecosystem for air from the commercial-application perspective, with unique detail on Siemens industrial products. First publication of sing-around flow meter with <0.5% error over 1:1000 dynamic range, and description of fuzzy/neural network object recognition systems. Mentions aerogels as future matching layer material.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Flow measurement: sing-around method; reciprocal time difference proportional to flow velocity; profile-independent with broad-beam filling rectangular tube cross-section.
- Household gas meter: W-shaped path in rectangular tube; <0.5% error over 1:1000 flow range; battery life 10 years.
- Automotive intake air: faster response than hot-wire at pulsating/reversible flows; correct reversed-flow measurement.
- Distance sensors: time-gated presence detection with Boolean window logic; 60–400 kHz variants.
- Object recognition: inverse-filtered excitation for matched-filter recognition in 3 s learning, millisecond detection.
- PVDF arrays: phased array with electronic beam steering; lateral resolution <1 mm with pulse holography.
- Aerogels (density 300–2000 kg/m³): promising low-impedance matching material; water-stability being addressed.
- L2QZ (low-Q, low-Z) composite: stack of thin PZT sheets + plastic; broad bandwidth, good local resolution.
