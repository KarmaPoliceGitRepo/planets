## Overview
Short conference proceedings (2 pages) from Tohoku University presenting the early version of the angular-echo beam-steering method for detecting micron-order surface roughness. A 10 MHz phased-array probe (Aloka SSD-6500) steers 30 beams through a common point of interest; four aluminium specimens with step heights of 0, 35, 57, 90 μm are measured. 2D power spectrum of the RF echo angle-time array shows that spatial-frequency components increase as step height increases.

## Unique contribution
First presentation (conference abstract) of the 2D Fourier transform approach to angular echo analysis for sub-100 μm surface features. Provides the foundational experimental evidence later expanded in Kudo 2007 (JJAP 46, 4873). Explicitly motivates application to early-stage atherosclerosis detection (endothelial roughness in arterial lumen).

## Key concepts
- Angular dependence of ultrasonic echo (beam steering)
- 10 MHz phased-array probe
- Step-height aluminium specimens (0, 35, 57, 90 μm)
- 2D power spectrum (spatial frequency vs. temporal frequency)
- Spatial frequency increases with step height
- Atherosclerosis / endothelial roughness motivation
- Aloka SSD-6500 diagnostic equipment

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Probe: 10 MHz linear array (Aloka SSD-6500); L = 0.4 mm aperture spacing; 30 beams per frame; sampled at 40 MHz, 16-bit.
- Beam steering: all beams focus through point O on surface; beam angle range ±23.9°; standoff l = 13.7 mm.
- Specimens: aluminium flat plates with single steps d = 0, 35, 57, 90 μm.
- 2D Fourier transform of echo spatial distribution (angle-axis) vs. temporal axis.
- Result: smooth surface (d = 0) — dominant components at very low spatial frequency (u = 0.00–0.20 deg⁻¹); stepped surfaces show increased components at u ≈ 0.40–0.62 deg⁻¹; spatial frequency at maximum increases with step height.
- Physical interpretation: deeper step introduces finer angular interference pattern → higher spatial frequencies in 2D FFT.
- Note: This is the 2-page conference precursor to Kudo 2007 (JJAP); limited quantitative analysis; full method in journal paper.
