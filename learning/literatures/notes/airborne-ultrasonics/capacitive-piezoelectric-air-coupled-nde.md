# Capacitive vs Piezoelectric Air-Coupled Transducers for Resonant NDE

- **Source:** Schindel et al. 1996 capacitive vs piezoelectric for resonant NDE inspection

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Compares micromachined silicon-backplate capacitive (electrostatic) transducers with piezoelectric transducers for air-coupled resonant NDE inspection of PEEK composite plates containing graphoil defects. Capacitive transducers show superior broadband response and sensitivity for this application.

## Unique contribution
First systematic side-by-side comparison of micromachined capacitive vs piezoelectric transducers for air-coupled NDE of polymer composites. Demonstrates that micromachined Si-backplate capacitive transducers provide broader bandwidth and lower resonant sensitivity mismatch, enabling detection of defects not visible with conventional piezoelectric transducers.

## Key concepts
- Micromachined silicon backplate capacitive transducer
- CMUT precursor / electrostatic air transducer

- Air-coupled NDE / non-destructive evaluation

- Resonant inspection
- Bandwidth comparison

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Test samples: PEEK composite plates with inserted graphoil delamination defects.
- Capacitive transducer: micromachined Si backplate, broadband (flat to >1 MHz), low Q.
- Piezoelectric transducer: narrow-band resonant, high Q, higher peak sensitivity.
- C-scan imaging: capacitive transducers clearly resolved graphoil defects; piezoelectric missed smaller defects due to narrower bandwidth.
- Capacitive transducers operate with DC bias voltage (~100–300 V) plus AC excitation.
- Key finding: for broadband resonant inspection (multiple plate resonances), capacitive transducers outperform piezoelectric despite lower peak sensitivity.
- Noise floor of capacitive type is higher; suitable SNR achieved with averaging.
