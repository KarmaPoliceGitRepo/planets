# Passive Focusing Techniques for Piezoelectric Air-Coupled Ultrasonic Transducers

- **Source:** Gomez 2016 passive focusing ACU

- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Gómez Álvarez-Arenas, Camacho & Fritsch, 2016 (Ultrasonics 67, 85–93)
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Compares three passive focusing approaches for 400 kHz air-coupled piezoelectric transducers: Fresnel Zone Plate (FZP), off-axis parabolic/spherical mirror, and a novel Newtonian-Cassegrain (NC) coaxial design. NC design uses a spherical primary mirror with output hole; transducer surface itself acts as secondary mirror. All share 35 mm focal distance.

## Unique contribution
Introduces the Newtonian–Cassegrain acoustic focusing concept for ACU transducers: coaxial, compact, uses a spherical (not parabolic) primary mirror with negligible aberrations (geometric error <20 μm vs λ = 850 μm), producing a 1.5 mm focus FWHM and 15 mm collimated depth. Comprehensive experimental comparison of FZP vs off-axis mirror vs NC.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Transducer: 400 kHz, 25 mm diameter, 1-3 composite; peak sensitivity −29 dB at 478 kHz.
- FZP: 30 mm diameter, f = 400 kHz, F = 35 mm. Measured FWHM 2.5 mm (theoretical 1.3 mm); secondary foci and noise floor visible.
- Off-axis spherical mirror: large aberrations, non-isotropic FWHM 3×10 mm → unsuitable.
- NC design: primary mirror RP = 70 mm, L = 16 mm, output hole a = 8 mm. Measured FWHM 1.5 mm (theoretical 1.3 mm), collimated beam to 15 mm depth, 1.5 dB loss from hole area ratio.
- Time delay between direct pulse (t1 = L/c) and focused pulse (t2 = 3L/c): well separated, not problematic.
- NC preferred for symmetric focus and low background noise; FZP preferred for compactness.
