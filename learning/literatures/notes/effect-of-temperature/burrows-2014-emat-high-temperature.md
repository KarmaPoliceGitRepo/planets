# EMAT and Laser-EMAT Ultrasonics at High Temperature

## Overview
Investigates the performance of electromagnetic acoustic transducers (EMATs) and laser-EMAT systems for ultrasonic measurements in stainless steel pipes up to 900°C and ferromagnetic carbon steel up to the Curie temperature limit (~770°C). Maps out signal amplitude, frequency content, and SNR as functions of temperature.

## Unique contribution
Provides a systematic characterisation of EMAT performance vs temperature for both ferromagnetic and non-ferromagnetic steels, establishing the practical upper temperature limits and signal degradation behaviour of EMAT systems.

## Key concepts
- EMAT (electromagnetic acoustic transducer)
- Laser-EMAT
- High-temperature ultrasonics
- Stainless steel pipe
- Ferromagnetic steel
- Curie temperature limit
- Non-contact measurement

## Methods / results / takeaways
- EMATs on ferritic (ferromagnetic) steel lose efficiency rapidly above Curie temperature (~770°C) as permeability drops to 1.
- On non-ferromagnetic austenitic stainless steel, EMATs operate via Lorentz force mechanism; signal amplitude lower but no Curie limit.
- Laser-EMAT (laser generation + EMAT detection) shows better SNR than EMAT-EMAT at high temperatures on stainless steel.
- SS pipe measurements to 900°C successful; signal-to-noise ratio decreases with temperature but remains usable.
- Results guide selection of non-contact measurement system for different steel grades and temperature ranges.
