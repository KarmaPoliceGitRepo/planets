## Overview
Comprehensive laser-ultrasonic study of mild steel from room temperature to 1200°C, reporting both velocity and attenuation data through all major phase transformations (Curie at ~750°C, ferrite→austenite at ~910°C). Measures attenuation as a function of frequency and temperature.

## Unique contribution
Provides the most complete published V(T) and α(T) dataset for mild steel longitudinal waves from room temperature to 1200°C, including quantification of the attenuation peak in the 600–750°C regime and values above 1 dB/cm at 1000°C.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- V_L decreases from ~5923 m/s at 17°C to ~4913 m/s at 1200°C (see companion spreadsheet).
- Attenuation peak in range 600–750°C associated with domain wall motion near Curie transition.
- Attenuation at 1000°C: ~1–1.5 dB/cm, dominated by grain scattering in coarse austenite grains.
- Phase transformation features clearly visible as discontinuities/changes in slope in both V(T) and α(T).
- Uses Nd:YAG laser for generation; interferometric detection; no sample contact required.
- Primary source for the V(T) data in the companion summary document and spreadsheet.
