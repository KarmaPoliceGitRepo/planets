# High-Temperature Ultrasonic Transducer Based on LiNbO3 with Porous ZrO2 Backing

## Overview
Develops a high-temperature ultrasonic transducer using a 36°Y-cut lithium niobate (LiNbO₃) element with a porous zirconia (ZrO₂) backing layer, designed to operate to 700°C. Characterises performance (bandwidth, sensitivity, pulse-echo SNR) as a function of temperature.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates LiNbO₃ 36°Y-cut with porous ZrO₂ backing as a viable transducer architecture to 700°C, identifying the couplant failure above ~200°C as the primary practical limitation (not the crystal itself).

## Key concepts
- Lithium niobate (LiNbO₃)
- 36°Y-cut
- Porous zirconia (ZrO₂) backing
- High-temperature transducer
- Couplant
- 700°C operation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- LiNbO₃ Curie temperature ~1150°C; suitable for high-temperature use.
- 36°Y-cut chosen for longitudinal wave generation with good electromechanical coupling.
- Porous ZrO₂ backing provides thermal insulation and acoustic damping while surviving high temperatures.
- Couplant (conventional ultrasonic couplant) fails above ~200°C; above this, dry coupling or gold bonding required.
- With appropriate couplant, pulse-echo measurements successful to 700°C with acceptable SNR.
- Bandwidth and centre frequency shift with temperature as acoustic properties of LiNbO₃ change.
