# High-Temperature Immersion Ultrasonic Probe Using PZT/PZT Sol-Gel Composite

- **Source:** 2013 - Inoue - HT immersion probe.pdf

## Overview
Develops an immersion ultrasonic probe using a composite PZT/PZT sol-gel structure that eliminates the need for a delay line, operating directly immersed in silicone oil at 200°C. Demonstrates pulse-echo imaging in the heated oil bath.

## Unique contribution
Eliminates the delay-line requirement for elevated-temperature immersion probes by using the PZT sol-gel composite's own acoustic isolation properties, enabling a more compact probe design operating at 200°C.

## Key concepts
- PZT sol-gel composite
- Immersion probe
- High-temperature ultrasonics
- Silicone oil couplant
- No delay line
- 200°C operation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Composite structure: conventional PZT element bonded to sol-gel PZT backing/isolation layer.
- No external delay line needed; sol-gel layer provides thermal gradient and acoustic isolation.
- Silicone oil (up to ~200°C) used as immersion couplant.
- Pulse-echo imaging demonstrated at 200°C with acceptable spatial resolution.
- Probe can be periodically cooled and reused; no performance degradation after thermal cycling.
