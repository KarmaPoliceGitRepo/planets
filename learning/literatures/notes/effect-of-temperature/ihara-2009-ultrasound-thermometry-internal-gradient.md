# Ultrasound Thermometry for Internal Temperature Gradient Measurement

## Overview
Extends the pulse-echo bisection-algorithm approach to reconstruct internal temperature gradients in steel using a quadratic V(T) calibration, improving accuracy over the linear approximation especially above 150°C.

## Unique contribution
Derives and validates a quadratic V(T) calibration for steel that significantly reduces reconstruction error compared to the linear model, particularly for steep internal temperature gradients.

## Key concepts
- Internal temperature gradient
- Pulse-echo ultrasound thermometry
- Quadratic V(T) calibration
- Bisection algorithm
- Steel
- Non-linear velocity–temperature relation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Quadratic calibration for steel (longitudinal waves): V(T) = −0.036×10⁻²·T² − 0.542·T + 5931.7 m/s.
- Linear approximation adequate below ~150°C; quadratic model necessary for higher temperatures.
- Reconstructed profiles agree with thermocouple reference measurements within ±2°C for gradients up to ~300°C across a 50 mm rod.
- Bisection algorithm unchanged from 2007 version; improvement is entirely due to better V(T) model.
