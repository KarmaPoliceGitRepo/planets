# 2D Surface Temperature Profiling by Laser Ultrasonics (SAW)

- **Source:** 2011 - Ihara - 2D surface temperature laser.pdf
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1example-ihara-2011-saw/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Ihara / 2011
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Uses laser-generated surface acoustic waves (SAW) with galvanometer-scanned detection to reconstruct 2D surface temperature maps of aluminium plates. Relies on the linear V_SAW(T) calibration for aluminium and inverts spatially resolved transit times into a temperature image.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates real-time 2D surface temperature field reconstruction from laser-SAW measurements using galvanometer scanning, achieving spatial maps without mechanical scanning of a transducer.

## Key concepts
- Surface acoustic wave (SAW)
- 2D surface temperature mapping
- Laser ultrasonics
- Galvanometer scanning
- Aluminium
- V_SAW(T) calibration

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Calibration for aluminium SAW: V_SAW(T) = −0.7557·T + 2981.7 m/s.
- Galvanometer scanner deflects the detection laser beam to sample multiple path directions rapidly.
- Reconstructed 2D temperature maps agree with infrared camera measurements within ~5°C for uniform heating.
- SAW is sensitive to surface and near-surface temperature; bulk temperature gradients not captured.
- Method requires optically accessible surfaces; not suitable for inside closed furnaces without windows.
