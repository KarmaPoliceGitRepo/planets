# MACOR Ceramic Waveguide for Ultrasonic Measurements Above 1200°C

- **Source:** 2013 - Wang - waveguide ceramic honeycomb.pdf

## Overview
Develops a MACOR (machinable glass-ceramic) waveguide probe for ultrasonic measurements at temperatures exceeding 1200°C, targeting applications such as ceramic honeycomb catalyst substrates and high-temperature furnace monitoring.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates that MACOR is a viable ultrasonic waveguide material above 1200°C, extending the range of contact ultrasonic measurement well beyond the limits of metal waveguides and standard piezoelectric transducers.

## Key concepts
- MACOR (machinable glass-ceramic)
- Ceramic waveguide
- High-temperature ultrasonics
- Buffer rod
- >1200°C measurement
- Longitudinal wave velocity

## Methods / results / takeaways
- MACOR longitudinal wave velocity: ~5900 m/s at room temperature; decreases with temperature.
- MACOR softening point well above 1200°C; thermally stable in air.
- Transducer (PZT or BIT) mounted at cool end of MACOR rod; sample at hot end.
- Successfully demonstrated in laboratory furnace at 1200°C with reflections from sample back face.
- MACOR has moderate attenuation but acceptable for rod lengths of a few centimetres.
