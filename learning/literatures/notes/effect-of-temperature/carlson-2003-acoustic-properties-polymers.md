## Overview
Experimental study of longitudinal ultrasonic velocity and attenuation in polymer materials (PMMA, PEEK) at temperatures from 5 to 37°C and frequencies of 5 and 10 MHz. Provides baseline acoustic characterization for use of polymers as transducer delay lines or buffer rods.

## Unique contribution
Provides systematic tabulated V and α data for PMMA and PEEK over a biologically/industrially relevant temperature range, useful for calibrating delay-line systems and modelling polymer waveguides.

## Key concepts
- PMMA (polymethylmethacrylate)
- PEEK (polyetheretherketone)
- Longitudinal velocity vs temperature
- Attenuation vs temperature
- Delay line
- Polymer buffer rod

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Longitudinal velocity in PMMA and PEEK decreases with temperature; PEEK more thermally stable than PMMA.
- Measurements at 5 and 10 MHz; attenuation increases with frequency.
- Data useful for correcting time-of-flight measurements when polymer delay lines are used in variable-temperature environments.
- Highlights that velocity-temperature sensitivity of polymers is much larger than for metals, requiring careful calibration.
