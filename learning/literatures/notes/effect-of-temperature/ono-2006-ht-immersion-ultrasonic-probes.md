# High-Temperature Immersion Ultrasonic Probes Using Sol-Gel BIT Films

## Overview
Develops high-temperature immersion ultrasonic probes based on bismuth titanate (BIT, Bi₄Ti₃O₁₂) sol-gel films (~90 µm thick) deposited directly on steel substrates. Demonstrates imaging capability in liquid zinc at 450°C, and operation to 500°C.

## Unique contribution
First demonstration of sol-gel BIT film transducers for immersion imaging in liquid metal (zinc at 450°C), confirming that BIT sol-gel films can survive and function in aggressive high-temperature liquid environments.

## Key concepts
- Bismuth titanate (BIT, Bi₄Ti₃O₁₂)
- Sol-gel deposition
- High-temperature transducer
- Immersion ultrasound
- Liquid zinc
- 500°C operation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- BIT sol-gel films: ~90 µm thick, deposited by spin-coating and pyrolysis at ~700°C.
- Curie temperature of BIT: ~675°C, allowing use to ~500°C before significant depoling.
- Imaging performed in liquid zinc (Zn) bath at 450°C: resolves reflectors within the melt.
- d₃₃ of sol-gel BIT films: ~10–15 pC/N after poling; lower than bulk BIT but sufficient for pulse-echo.
- No delay line needed; film deposited directly on the probe face immersed in liquid.
