## Overview
AIP conference proceedings paper presenting sol-gel bismuth titanate (BIT, Bi₄Ti₃O₁₂) film transducers for high-temperature ultrasonic thickness measurement. Reports d₃₃ of ~17 pC/N and demonstrates operation to 250°C.

## Unique contribution
Conference proceedings counterpart to the RSC Advances journal paper, providing more application-focused data on BIT transducer performance for industrial thickness monitoring at elevated temperatures.

## Key concepts
- Bismuth titanate (BIT)
- Sol-gel deposition
- High-temperature transducer
- d₃₃ piezoelectric coefficient
- Thickness measurement
- 250°C operation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Sol-gel BIT films: d₃₃ ≈ 17 pC/N after electric-field poling.
- Curie temperature of BIT: ~675°C; significant depoling begins above ~300–350°C for extended operation.
- Thickness measurements on steel substrate at up to 250°C validated against micrometer.
- Film deposition: multiple spin-coat + pyrolysis cycles to achieve ~90 µm total thickness.
- Companion paper (Burrows 2012 RSC) provides detailed materials characterization.
