## Overview
Conference abstract discussing theoretical and experimental aspects of ultrasonic attenuation in metals at low temperatures, focusing on electron-ion scattering (thermoelastic and electron-drag) contributions that become significant when thermal phonon scattering diminishes.

## Unique contribution
Highlights the electron-phonon / electron-ion scattering contribution to ultrasonic attenuation that is distinct from the grain-scattering (Rayleigh) mechanism dominant at room temperature, relevant to understanding residual attenuation at cryogenic temperatures.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic attenuation in metals
- Low temperature
- Electron-ion scattering
- Thermoelastic attenuation
- Phonon scattering
- Grain boundary scattering

## Methods / results / takeaways
- At low temperatures, grain-scattering (Rayleigh) attenuation decreases (fewer thermal phonons), but electron-drag attenuation (Pippard mechanism) can become observable.
- Provides theoretical framework for separating magnetic, thermal, and scattering contributions to total attenuation.
- Context for Darbari (1968) and Tripathi (1973) work, which build on this foundation.
