# Temperature Compensation in Guided Wave SHM: OBS and BSS Comparison

## Overview
Compares Optimal Baseline Selection (OBS) and Baseline Signal Stretch (BSS) methods for temperature compensation in guided-wave SHM, and proposes a combined OBS+BSS approach that outperforms either alone across wide temperature ranges.

## Unique contribution
Provides the first rigorous comparison of OBS vs BSS and demonstrates that their combination further reduces temperature-induced residuals, enabling robust SHM over large temperature excursions.

## Key concepts
- Guided wave SHM
- Optimal Baseline Selection (OBS)
- Baseline Signal Stretch (BSS)
- Temperature compensation
- Lamb waves
- Damage detection

## Methods / results / takeaways
- BSS stretches the time axis of the baseline signal to account for velocity change with temperature, then subtracts from current signal.
- OBS alone is limited by baseline library density; BSS alone fails when signal shape changes beyond a simple stretch.
- Combined OBS+BSS achieves best residuals across both small and large ΔT.
- Applied to steel and aluminium panels; damage indices show improved sensitivity after combined compensation.
- Establishes the standard approach used in subsequent guided-wave SHM literature.
