## Overview
A systematic review of methods for measuring the heat transfer coefficient (HTC). The paper classifies HTC measurement techniques into five groups: direct method, transient method, Wilson plot method, heat/mass/momentum transfer analogy, and boundary layer thickness method. Applicable to boiling, condensation, and single-phase convection flows.

## Unique contribution
Comprehensive taxonomy of HTC measurement approaches with discussion of applicability, accuracy, and practical limitations for each group. Provides a unified framework for selecting the right method for a given experiment.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Five measurement categories: (1) direct (measure q and ΔT simultaneously), (2) transient (record temperature history after a step change), (3) Wilson (varies flow rate to separate thermal resistances), (4) analogy (use Stanton number or mass transfer to infer HTC), (5) boundary layer (use velocity/temperature profiles).
- Each method has different requirements for instrumentation and boundary conditions.
- Transient methods are well suited to rapid heating/cooling situations (relevant to steel processing).
- Wilson method separates fouling resistance from convective HTC and is useful for two-fluid systems.
