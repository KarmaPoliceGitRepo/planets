# Ultrasonic Internal Temperature Profiling with Single-Sided Heating (Including Molten Aluminium)

- **Source:** 2008 - Takahashi - temperature single-sided heating.pdf

- **Type:** paper

- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Demonstrates the bisection-algorithm pulse-echo method for internal temperature profiling of a steel rod with single-sided heating, and extends validation to molten aluminium at 700°C to show applicability near and above the melting point.

## Unique contribution
First reported application of pulse-echo ultrasonic thermometry to molten aluminium at 700°C, using a quartz buffer rod to isolate the transducer from the extreme temperature, and demonstrating that the method works in liquid metals.

## Key concepts
- Internal temperature profile
- Pulse-echo method
- Single-sided heating
- Molten aluminium
- Quartz buffer rod
- Bisection algorithm

## Methods / results / takeaways
- V(T) calibration for steel (longitudinal waves): V(T) = −0.691·T + 5920.0 m/s.
- Molten aluminium measurements at 700°C using quartz buffer rod (transducer at cool end).
- Reconstructed temperature profiles agree with thermocouple within ±5°C.
- In liquid aluminium, reflection from solid/liquid interface provides additional information.
- Quartz rod has very low attenuation and well-known V(T), making it a reliable waveguide to ~800°C.
