# Ultrasonic Internal Temperature Profile Measurement Using Pulse-Echo Method

## Overview
Presents a pulse-echo ultrasonic method to reconstruct the internal temperature profile of a steel sample by measuring time-of-flight (TOF) differences between successive echoes and applying a bisection algorithm that iteratively matches measured TOF to a calibrated V(T) relation.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates a practical bisection-algorithm inversion for internal temperature profiling using only a single-sided transducer and a calibrated V(T) curve. Achieves good agreement between reconstructed temperature profile and thermocouple measurements for a steel rod with applied heat gradient.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Calibration equation for steel (longitudinal waves, up to 150°C): V(T) = −0.647·T + 5918.1 m/s.
- Bisection algorithm: divides sample into thin layers; uses difference in consecutive echo travel times to infer local velocity and hence local temperature.
- Applied to a steel rod with one end heated; reconstructed profile matches thermocouple measurements within acceptable tolerance.
- Single transducer on one face sufficient; no coupling required on hot face.
