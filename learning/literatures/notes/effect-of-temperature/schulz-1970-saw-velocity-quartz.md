# Surface Acoustic Wave Velocity on Quartz

- **Source:** 1970 - Schulz - SAW velocity quartz.pdf
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1example-schulz-1970/view

- **Author/Year:** Schulz / 1970
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Experimental measurement of surface acoustic wave (SAW / Rayleigh wave) velocity on quartz as a function of crystal orientation and temperature, identifying the ST-cut (42.5° rotated Y-cut) as the orientation with minimum temperature coefficient of delay.

## Unique contribution
Establishes the ST-cut as the optimal quartz orientation for temperature-stable SAW devices, foundational for quartz SAW resonators and filters used in telecommunications and sensing.

## Key concepts
- Surface acoustic wave (SAW)
- Rayleigh wave
- Quartz crystal
- ST-cut (42.5° rotated Y-cut)
- Temperature coefficient of delay
- Crystal orientation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- ST-cut has near-zero first-order temperature coefficient of SAW velocity near room temperature.
- SAW velocity on ST-cut quartz: ~3158 m/s at room temperature.
- Higher-order coefficients cause a parabolic V(T) curve with turning point near 25°C.
- Results directly applicable to SAW temperature sensors (where a different cut with high temperature sensitivity is preferred for sensing applications).
