# High-Temperature Immersion Ultrasonic Probes Using Lead Niobate and Clad Buffer Rods

## Overview
Develops immersion ultrasonic probes for operation up to 200°C using lead niobate (PbNb₂O₆) piezoelectric elements combined with clad buffer rods. Demonstrates focused imaging in a heated liquid bath at 200°C.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates focused immersion imaging at 200°C using PbNb₂O₆ (lead niobate, Curie temperature ~570°C) combined with a clad buffer rod for thermal isolation, providing a practical alternative to PZT for moderately elevated-temperature immersion inspection.

## Key concepts
- Lead niobate (PbNb₂O₆)
- Clad buffer rod
- Immersion probe
- Focused ultrasound
- High-temperature imaging
- 200°C operation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- PbNb₂O₆ Curie temperature ~570°C; can operate at 200°C with margin.
- Clad buffer rod (steel core, stainless steel cladding) provides thermal isolation and mode filtering.
- Focused lens at buffer rod tip enables focused immersion imaging.
- Demonstrated imaging in liquid bath at 200°C; spatial resolution comparable to room temperature operation.
- PbNb₂O₆ has lower d₃₃ than PZT but higher Curie temperature, enabling use in applications where PZT depolation is a concern.
