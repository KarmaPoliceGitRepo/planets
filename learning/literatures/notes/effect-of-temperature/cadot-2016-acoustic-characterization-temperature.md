## Overview
Characterizes ultrasonic velocity and attenuation of Rexolite 1422 and PEEK 1000 over the temperature range 40–170°C, providing data for delay-line design in elevated-temperature ultrasonic systems.

## Unique contribution
Extends acoustic characterization of Rexolite and PEEK to temperatures up to 170°C, covering conditions relevant to industrial process monitoring, where these polymers are used as buffer/delay-line materials.

## Key concepts
- Rexolite 1422
- PEEK 1000
- Longitudinal velocity vs temperature
- Attenuation vs temperature
- Delay line
- Elevated temperature characterization

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Both Rexolite and PEEK show significant velocity decrease with temperature across 40–170°C.
- Rexolite has lower attenuation than PEEK at elevated temperature, making it preferable for delay-line applications.
- Data presented as tabular V(T) and α(T) values at multiple frequencies.
- Results underscore need for real-time temperature compensation when using polymer delay lines above ambient temperature.
