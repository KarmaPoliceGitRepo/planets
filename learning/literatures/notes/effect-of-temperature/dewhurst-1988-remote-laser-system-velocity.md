# Remote Laser Ultrasonic System for Velocity Measurement in Iron to 1000°C

## Overview
Describes a remote (non-contact) laser ultrasonic system based on Nd:YAG generation and Michelson interferometer detection, used to measure longitudinal wave velocity in iron from room temperature to 1000°C. Identifies the Curie transition at 768°C and the ferrite→austenite transformation at 910°C as distinct features in V(T).

## Unique contribution
Early demonstration of a fully remote laser-ultrasonic velocity measurement system in iron, clearly resolving both the Curie transition and the alpha→gamma iron transformation from velocity data alone.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Nd:YAG pulsed laser (1064 nm) generates thermoelastic ultrasound; Michelson interferometer detects surface displacement.
- Curie transition at 768°C: visible as change in slope of V(T) as magnetostrictive elastic contribution vanishes.
- Alpha→gamma transformation at ~910°C: abrupt discontinuity in V(T) as crystal structure changes from BCC to FCC.
- Measurements to 1000°C without any sample contact or special coupling.
- System design includes vibration isolation and long-working-distance optics for furnace environments.
