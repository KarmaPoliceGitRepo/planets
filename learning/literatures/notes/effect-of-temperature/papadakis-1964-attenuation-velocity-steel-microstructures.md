## Overview
Experimental study of ultrasonic attenuation and longitudinal/shear wave velocities in four microstructures of SAE 4150 steel: pearlite+ferrite, bainite, martensite, and tempered martensite. Measured at 2–100 MHz by pulse-echo technique. Interprets results in terms of Rayleigh scattering theory.

## Unique contribution
First detailed comparison of attenuation versus microstructure across the full frequency range 2–100 MHz for multiple steel microstructures in a single systematic study. Provides grain-size corrections using the Papadakis empirical formula for average scatterer volume.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Attenuation order (highest to lowest): pearlite > bainite > martensite > tempered martensite.
- Velocity order: highest in pearlite (coarser effective grain boundary spacing), lowest in as-quenched martensite (heavily distorted lattice).
- Densities: all microstructures ~7.79–7.81 g/cm³.
- Longitudinal velocities: V_L ~0.587–0.594 cm/µs (5870–5940 m/s).
- Shear velocity Cs ≈ 0.548 × CL (empirical, used in attenuation calculations).
- Rayleigh scattering formula: α ∝ f⁴·D³ where D = effective grain diameter. Confirms scattering dominates at high frequency.
- Provides grain volume formula: T = (4π/3)·⟨R⁶⟩_av/⟨R³⟩_av (weighted mean sphere volume).
