## Overview
PhD thesis investigating multi-doped bismuth titanate (BIT) ceramics and films for high-temperature acoustic applications, specifically targeting gas flow measurement. Reports enhanced d₃₃ up to 35 pC/N through multi-element doping, Curie temperature of 667°C, and use of gallium-based bonding for high-temperature attachment.

## Unique contribution
Systematic study of the effect of multi-element doping on BIT piezoelectric properties and Curie temperature, demonstrating improved d₃₃ and thermal stability, with specific application to high-temperature gas flow ultrasonic measurement.

- Bismuth titanate (BIT, Bi₄Ti₃O₁₂)

- High-temperature ceramics

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Multi-doped BIT: d₃₃ up to 35 pC/N (significantly higher than undoped BIT ~15 pC/N).
- Curie temperature: ~667°C for optimised doping; trade-off between d₃₃ and Tc.
- Gallium (liquid metal) bonding at elevated temperatures provides reliable acoustic contact above solder-usable temperatures.
- BIT applied as transducer for clamp-on ultrasonic gas flow measurement at high temperatures.
- Thesis provides comprehensive materials characterisation: XRD, SEM, impedance spectroscopy, d₃₃ vs temperature.
