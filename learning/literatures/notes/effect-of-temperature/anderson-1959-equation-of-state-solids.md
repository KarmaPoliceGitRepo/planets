# Equation of State of Vitreous Silica by Ultrasonics (75 K – 300 K)

- **Source:** 1959 - Anderson - equation of state solids.pdf

## Overview
Derives the equation of state of vitreous silica (fused quartz) from ultrasonic velocity measurements at cryogenic to room temperature (75 K – 300 K). Uses the Grüneisen parameter and elastic constants to relate acoustic data to thermodynamic properties.

## Unique contribution
Establishes the use of ultrasonic velocity measurements at low temperatures to determine the Grüneisen parameter and equation of state of amorphous solids, providing foundational data for vitreous silica that is widely referenced in high-temperature waveguide and delay-line design.

## Key concepts
- Vitreous silica (fused quartz)
- Equation of state
- Grüneisen parameter
- Elastic constants
- Low-temperature ultrasonics
- Longitudinal and shear wave velocity

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Longitudinal and shear wave velocities measured in vitreous silica from 75 K to 300 K.
- Grüneisen parameter derived from pressure and temperature dependence of velocities.
- Vitreous silica shows anomalous positive temperature coefficient of velocity at low temperatures (unlike crystalline solids); velocity increases from 75–300 K before decreasing at higher temperatures.
- Results provide reference acoustic properties for quartz buffer rods used in high-temperature ultrasonic instrumentation.
- Elastic constants (C₁₁, C₄₄) tabulated as functions of temperature.
