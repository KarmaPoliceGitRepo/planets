## Overview
Describes ultrasonic tomographic methods to reconstruct the internal 2D temperature distribution in hot steel billets and cylinders (up to 750°C). Uses multiple ultrasonic path measurements combined with a priori knowledge of the heat-flow pattern to drastically reduce the number of required paths.

## Unique contribution
Introduces the key idea of incorporating heat-conduction a priori information to reduce the problem from thousands of required paths to as few as 3–5, making real-time industrial tomography feasible. Tests on austenitic steel cylinders and square billets.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic tomography
- Internal temperature reconstruction
- A priori heat-flow information
- Path integrals of slowness
- Ferritic/austenitic steel
- Ultrasonic velocity–temperature relation

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Path integral: total travel time = integral of 1/V(T(x)) dx along the ray path.
- With homogeneous boundary conditions (known surface T), only a few paths suffice to reconstruct the interior.
- Demonstrates 2D reconstruction in laboratory tests on steel specimens heated up to 750°C.
- Method requires calibrated V(T) curve for the steel grade under test.
- Companion paper to Norton, Testardi, Wadley (J. Res. NBS 1984) which gives the mathematical inversion framework.
