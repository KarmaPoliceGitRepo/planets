# Non-Contact Ultrasonic Measurement in Aluminium at Elevated Temperature

- **Source:** 1989 - Hutchins - noncontact ultrasonic aluminium.pdf
- **Drive link:** https://drive.google.com/file/d/1example-hutchins-1989/view
- **Type:** paper
- **Author/Year:** Hutchins et al. / 1989
- **Coverage:** full

## Overview
Demonstrates combined laser generation and EMAT detection (non-contact) for ultrasonic measurement in a 25.4 mm aluminium plate heated to ~400°C. Measures longitudinal wave velocity and attenuation as functions of temperature without any physical contact on the hot surface.

## Unique contribution
One of the first demonstrations of fully non-contact laser + EMAT ultrasonic measurements on aluminium at industrially relevant elevated temperatures, showing the feasibility of contactless process monitoring.

## Key concepts
- Non-contact ultrasonics
- Laser generation
- EMAT (electromagnetic acoustic transducer)
- Aluminium
- Elevated temperature
- Velocity vs temperature

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Nd:YAG laser generates thermoelastic ultrasound on one face; EMAT on same or opposite face detects.
- Plate: 25.4 mm aluminium, temperatures up to ~400°C.
- Longitudinal wave velocity in aluminium decreases approximately linearly with temperature in this range.
- Attenuation increases with temperature; signal-to-noise adequate for process monitoring.
- EMAT performance on non-ferromagnetic aluminium is lower than on ferromagnetic steel; laser generation compensates by providing stronger initial pulse.
