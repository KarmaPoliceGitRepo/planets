## Overview
An internal summary document discussing Scruby and Moss (1993) velocity–temperature data for mild steel. Focuses on the error introduced in thickness and thickness-change measurements when the velocity is assumed constant despite varying temperature.

## Unique contribution
Quantifies the systematic error in ultrasonic thickness measurement due to incorrect velocity assumption across different temperature ranges. Provides a concise error table not found in the original paper abstract.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic velocity–temperature dependence
- Thickness measurement error
- Mild steel
- Longitudinal wave velocity
- Shear wave velocity

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Key error table (relative error in thickness-change measurement per °C):
  - 17–105°C: longitudinal 4.7%/°C, shear 7.1%/°C
  - 168–233°C: longitudinal 45%/°C, shear 73.5%/°C
- These large errors at higher temperature arise because the velocity gradient dV/dT increases (especially near phase transitions), so a constant-velocity assumption breaks down rapidly.
- The document emphasises that real-time velocity calibration against temperature is essential for accurate ultrasonic thickness measurement at elevated temperatures.
