# Ultrasonic Measurements in Nickel Single Crystal from 77 K to 650 K

## Overview
Measures ultrasonic velocity and attenuation in nickel single crystals from 77 K (liquid nitrogen) to 650 K (~377°C), quantifying the effects of magnetocrystalline anisotropy, magnetostriction, and domain wall motion on acoustic wave propagation.

## Unique contribution
Provides comprehensive low-temperature to above-room-temperature data on both velocity and attenuation for nickel single crystals, enabling separation of magnetic and elastic contributions to the acoustic properties.

## Key concepts
- Nickel single crystal
- Magnetocrystalline anisotropy
- Magnetostriction
- Domain wall attenuation
- Low temperature ultrasonics
- Curie temperature (Ni: ~358°C / 631 K)

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Attenuation peak near Ni Curie temperature (~358°C / 631 K) due to maximum domain wall mobility.
- Below Curie temperature, attenuation from domain wall motion decreases as spontaneous magnetization saturates.
- Velocity shows anomaly near Curie temperature: magnetostrictive contribution to elastic modulus disappears above T_c.
- Data on [100], [110], [111] crystal directions shows strong anisotropy below T_c.
- Relevant to understanding magnetic contributions to attenuation in carbon steels (via cementite Fe₃C and ferrite).
