# Ultrasonic Velocity vs Temperature in Sintered Ceramic Materials

## Overview
Measures longitudinal ultrasonic velocity as a function of temperature in sintered ceramic materials, particularly alumina (Al₂O₃), demonstrating the feasibility of ultrasonic thermometry in non-metallic engineering materials.

## Unique contribution
Provides V(T) calibration data for alumina extending to elevated temperatures, enabling ultrasonic thermometry in ceramic components such as kiln furniture, refractories, and ceramic heat exchangers.

## Key concepts
- Sintered ceramics
- Alumina (Al₂O₃)
- Longitudinal velocity vs temperature
- Ultrasonic thermometry
- Ceramic waveguide

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Calibration for alumina (Al₂O₃): V(T) = −0.9576·T + 5953.3 m/s.
- Linear fit valid over the measured temperature range; ceramic shows smaller relative velocity change than metals per °C.
- Absolute velocity (~5950 m/s at room temperature) is higher than steel due to higher elastic moduli relative to density.
- Demonstrates viability of ultrasonic thermometry in ceramic industrial components.
- Attenuation in sintered ceramics is higher than in metals due to grain scattering from porosity and grain boundary phases.
