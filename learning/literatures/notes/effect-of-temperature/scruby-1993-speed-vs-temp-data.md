## Overview
Excel spreadsheet containing tabulated longitudinal wave velocity vs temperature data for mild steel, extracted from Scruby and Moss (1993). Covers the full range 17–1200°C including all solid-state phase transformations.

## Unique contribution
Provides machine-readable V(T) data covering the widest published temperature range for mild steel longitudinal waves, including features at the Curie transition (~750°C) and ferrite→austenite transformation (~910°C).

## Key concepts
- Longitudinal wave velocity vs temperature
- Mild steel
- Phase transformations
- Curie transition
- Ferrite–austenite transformation
- Data table

## Methods / results / takeaways
- V_L (compressional wave): ranges from ~5923 m/s at 17°C to ~4913 m/s at 1200°C.
- Notable features: velocity decrease accelerates near Curie temperature (~750°C); discontinuous change at ferrite→austenite transformation (~910°C); velocity continues to decrease in austenite phase.
- Spreadsheet format: two columns (temperature °C, velocity m/s), suitable for direct interpolation in measurement software.
- Companion to the summary document (scruby-1993-summary-velocity-temp.md) which derives thickness-measurement error from these data.
