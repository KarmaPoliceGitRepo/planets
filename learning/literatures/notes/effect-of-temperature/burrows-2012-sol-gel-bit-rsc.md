## Overview
RSC Advances journal paper providing detailed materials characterisation of sol-gel deposited bismuth titanate (BIT) films for high-temperature ultrasonic transducer applications. Reports structural, dielectric, and piezoelectric properties across a range of temperatures.

## Unique contribution
Provides the most complete characterisation of sol-gel BIT films as a transducer material including XRD phase analysis, dielectric spectroscopy, and d₃₃ measurements as a function of temperature up to the Curie point.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Sol-gel BIT films show Aurivillius-phase perovskite structure confirmed by XRD; well-crystallised after 700°C anneal.
- Dielectric permittivity peaks at Curie temperature (~675°C).
- d₃₃ ≈ 17 pC/N at room temperature; decreases above ~300°C due to increasing conductivity and partial depoling.
- Films are thermally stable in ambient air to ~600°C for short durations.
