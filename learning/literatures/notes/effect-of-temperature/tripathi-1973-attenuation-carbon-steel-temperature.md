# Ultrasonic Attenuation in Carbon Steel up to 985°C

## Overview
Measures ultrasonic attenuation in a 1% carbon steel from room temperature to 985°C, using the shoulder technique. Identifies the Curie temperature peak of cementite at ~215°C and the main Curie transition of ferrite at ~750°C.

## Unique contribution
Extends the Darbari (1968) shoulder-technique measurements to higher carbon content (1% C) steel and higher temperatures (up to 985°C), confirming and quantifying two distinct magnetic attenuation peaks.

## Key concepts
- Ultrasonic attenuation vs temperature
- Carbon steel (1% C)
- Curie temperature of cementite (~215°C)
- Curie temperature of ferrite (~750°C)
- Shoulder technique
- Grain scattering
- Phase transformations

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Two attenuation peaks: first at ~215°C (cementite Fe₃C Curie temperature), second at ~750°C (ferrite Curie temperature / ferrite→austenite transformation).
- Attenuation between the two peaks falls to a local minimum around 400–600°C.
- Above 750°C, attenuation rises again as grain growth accelerates in austenite phase.
- Results consistent with Bhatia–Moore grain scattering theory; magnetic contributions identified by comparison with non-magnetic steel reference.
- Temperature range of 20–985°C is among the widest published for carbon steel attenuation measurements.
