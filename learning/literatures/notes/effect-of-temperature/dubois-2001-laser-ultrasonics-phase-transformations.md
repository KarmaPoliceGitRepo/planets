# Laser Ultrasonics for Steel Phase Transformations at 500–1000°C

## Overview
Uses laser ultrasonics to monitor phase transformations in steel during heating and cooling cycles from 500 to 1000°C. Identifies the Curie transition at ~750°C as a distinctive feature in V(T), hysteresis in hot-rolled steels, and texture changes in cold-rolled steels detectable via shear-wave birefringence.

## Unique contribution
Demonstrates laser ultrasonics as a non-contact probe for in-situ monitoring of steel phase transformations, including the ferromagnetic Curie transition and ferrite↔austenite transformation, with the ability to detect texture evolution in cold-rolled steel.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- V(T) shows clear feature at Curie point (~750°C): velocity drop accelerates as magnetostrictive contribution to elastic modulus disappears.
- Hysteresis observed in hot-rolled steels between heating and cooling V(T) curves due to different transformation kinetics (austenite nucleation vs. martensite formation).
- Cold-rolled steels show shear-wave birefringence (V_SH ≠ V_SV) related to texture; birefringence changes as texture is randomized above recrystallization temperature.
- Measurements performed at 500–1000°C with Nd:YAG generation and Fabry-Pérot detection.
- Method enables real-time monitoring of heat-treatment processes without sample contact.
