## Overview
Manufacturer datasheet comparing Curie temperatures and maximum recommended use temperatures for a range of piezoelectric ceramics and single crystals. States the general rule that maximum operating temperature is approximately half the Curie temperature (in °C), and tabulates d₃₃ and Tc for key materials.

## Unique contribution
Provides a concise manufacturer reference for the d₃₃ vs Tc trade-off across standard and high-temperature piezoelectric materials, useful for transducer material selection.

## Key concepts
- Curie temperature (Tc)
- Maximum use temperature (≈ ½ Tc)
- d₃₃ piezoelectric coefficient
- PZT
- Lead-free piezoelectrics
- Material selection

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Maximum safe operating temperature rule of thumb: T_max ≈ ½ Tc (in °C).
- High-d₃₃ materials (e.g., soft PZT: d₃₃ ~600 pC/N, Tc ~190°C) have low T_max ~95°C.
- High-Tc materials (e.g., BIT: Tc ~675°C) have lower d₃₃ (~15–35 pC/N), T_max ~330°C.
- LiNbO₃ (Tc ~1150°C) usable to ~500–600°C but has low d₃₃ (~6 pC/N).
- Trade-off between sensitivity (d₃₃) and maximum operating temperature is a fundamental constraint in high-temperature transducer design.
