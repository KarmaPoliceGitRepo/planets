## Overview
Measures elastic constants and ultrasonic velocities (longitudinal and shear) in multiple steel grades from room temperature to 1000°C using both resonance and pulse-echo techniques. Provides comprehensive elasticity data through the ferrite→austenite phase transformation.

## Unique contribution
One of the most comprehensive single studies of elastic constants of steel up to 1000°C, covering multiple grades with both longitudinal and shear wave data, enabling derivation of Young's modulus, shear modulus, and Poisson's ratio as functions of temperature.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Longitudinal velocity decreases from ~5900 m/s at room temperature to ~4900 m/s at 1000°C.
- Shear velocity decreases from ~3200 m/s at room temperature to ~2800 m/s at 1000°C.
- Discontinuous velocity changes at Curie transition (~750°C) and ferrite→austenite transformation (~910°C).
- Elastic moduli (Young's, shear) derived from velocity data for multiple steel grades; carbon content has minor effect compared to temperature.
- Both resonance (vibrating bar) and pulse-echo results agree within 1%, validating both methods.
