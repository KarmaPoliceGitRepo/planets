## Overview
Reviews experimental techniques for measuring piezoelectric properties (d₃₃, permittivity, coupling coefficient) of piezoelectric materials at high temperatures. Covers PZT, potassium bismuth titanate (KBT-BFPT) and other high-temperature piezo materials. Discusses Jamin interferometer method and lossy-material corrections.

## Unique contribution
Provides a critical evaluation of measurement artifacts and corrections needed when characterizing piezoelectric materials at high temperatures where conductivity, dielectric loss, and clamping constraints complicate standard impedance methods.

## Methods / results / takeaways
- Conventional quasi-static d₃₃ meters unreliable above ~200°C due to charge leakage in lossy materials.
- Jamin interferometer measures optical path length change from piezoelectric strain; applicable to high-temperature d₃₃.
- Lossy model correction essential for impedance spectroscopy analysis above ~300°C.
- KBT-BFPT materials show d₃₃ ~60–80 pC/N with Curie temperatures >400°C, balancing sensitivity and thermal stability.
- Review compiles best-practice measurement protocols for high-temperature piezo characterisation.
