# Surface Temperature Measurement by Surface Acoustic Waves in Aluminium and Steel

- **Source:** 2009 - Takahashi - SAW surface temperature.pdf

## Overview
Presents SAW-based surface temperature measurements for both aluminium and steel, providing linear V_SAW(T) calibration equations for both materials and demonstrating 1D surface temperature profile reconstruction using laser generation and detection.

## Unique contribution
Provides directly comparable SAW V(T) calibration equations for two industrially important metals (aluminium and steel), enabling material-specific surface thermometry.

## Key concepts
- Surface acoustic wave (SAW)
- Surface temperature measurement
- Aluminium
- Steel
- V_SAW(T) calibration
- Laser ultrasonics

## Methods / results / takeaways
- V_SAW calibration for aluminium: V_SAW(T) = −0.7557·T + 2981.7 m/s.
- V_SAW calibration for steel: V_SAW(T) = −0.377·T + 3057.0 m/s.
- Steel has higher absolute SAW velocity but smaller temperature sensitivity than aluminium.
- SAW generated by pulsed laser; detected by second laser (interferometric) or EMAT.
- Reconstructed surface temperature profiles along line agree with thermocouple reference within ±3°C.
