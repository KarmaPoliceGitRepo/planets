# Pulse-Echo Method
**Aliases:** pulse-echo; PE method; pulse-echo mode; pulse-echo configuration; single-transducer mode; pulse-echo ultrasound; pulse-echo-overlap method; B-scan; A-scan; C-scan

**Definition:** In the pulse-echo (PE) method, a single transducer both transmits a short ultrasonic pulse and receives echoes reflected from internal interfaces, back walls, or defects. The round-trip time-of-flight (TOF = 2L/v) gives thickness or velocity; echo amplitudes encode impedance mismatch and absorption. A-scans display echo amplitude vs time at a fixed position; B-scans (transducer scanned linearly) show cross-sectional images; C-scans (2D raster) show planar maps at a fixed depth gate. The method is sensitive to coupling condition and geometric effects near the near-field; a stable reference reflection (e.g., air-backed back wall) calibrates the system response.
