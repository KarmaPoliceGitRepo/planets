# Elasticity Constants
**Aliases:** Young's modulus; E; Poisson's ratio; ν; shear modulus; G; bulk modulus; K; Lamé constants; λ; μ; elastic stiffness tensor; C_ijkl; elastic compliance tensor; s_ijhk; isotropic elasticity; anisotropic elasticity; second-order elastic constants; SOE; Voigt notation; Hooke's law

**Definition:** Second-order elastic (SOE) constants relate stress to strain in the linear regime. For isotropic materials, two independent constants suffice; common choices are (E, ν), (λ, μ) (Lamé), or (K, G). E (Young's modulus) governs axial stiffness; ν (Poisson's ratio) relates lateral to axial strain; G = E/[2(1+ν)]. For anisotropic crystals, the elastic stiffness tensor C_ijkl (fourth-rank, up to 21 independent components for triclinic; 3 for cubic) is needed. Ultrasonic velocity relates directly to elastic constants: V_L = √(C_11/ρ) for longitudinal waves along a principal axis. The elastic anisotropy factor W (combination of C₁₁, C₁₂, C₄₄ for cubic metals) scales grain scattering attenuation.
