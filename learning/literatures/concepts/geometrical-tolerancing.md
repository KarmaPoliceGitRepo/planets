# Geometrical Tolerancing
**Aliases:** GD&T; geometric dimensioning and tolerancing; GPS; geometrical product specifications; feature control frame; datum; datum reference frame; MMC; LMC; flatness; cylindricity; roundness; circularity; straightness; perpendicularity; runout; positional tolerance; true position; limits and fits; ISO 2768; ISO 1101; BS 8888

**Definition:** Geometrical tolerancing is the ISO/ASME system for specifying and controlling the allowable variation in form (flatness, roundness, cylindricity, straightness), orientation (parallelism, perpendicularity, angularity), location (true position, concentricity), and runout of part features. A feature control frame on the drawing encodes tolerance type, value, and datum references. GPS (ISO Geometrical Product Specifications) is the comprehensive ISO standard system. The limits-and-fits system (ISO 286) specifies shaft/hole tolerance grades (IT grades) and fundamental deviations. Maximum material condition (MMC) allows bonus tolerance when features are not at maximum material.
