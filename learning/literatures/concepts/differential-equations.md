# Ordinary Differential Equations
**Aliases:** ODE; ordinary differential equation; first-order ODE; second-order ODE; initial value problem; boundary value problem; Sturm-Liouville; eigenfunction expansion; variation of parameters; Euler method; Runge-Kutta; linear ODE; homogeneous; particular solution; integrating factor

**Definition:** An ordinary differential equation (ODE) involves derivatives of a function of a single variable. First-order linear ODEs dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x) are solved by an integrating factor μ = e^{∫P dx}. Second-order linear ODEs with constant coefficients have solutions that are superpositions of exponentials (or sinusoids when roots are complex); for forced equations, variation of parameters or undetermined coefficients gives a particular solution. Sturm-Liouville problems produce orthogonal eigenfunctions that form the basis for series solutions (including Fourier series). Numerical methods (Euler, Runge-Kutta RK4) integrate ODEs when closed-form solutions do not exist.
