# Complex Numbers
**Aliases:** complex number; Argand plane; complex plane; complex conjugate; Euler's formula; phasor; imaginary unit; modulus; argument; polar form

**Definition:** A complex number z = a + jb has real part a and imaginary part b; it is represented as a point in the Argand (complex) plane. The modulus |z| = √(a²+b²) and argument arg(z) = atan2(b,a) give the polar form z = |z|e^{j arg(z)}. Euler's formula e^{jθ} = cos θ + j sin θ links the exponential and trigonometric representations and is the foundation of Fourier analysis. The complex conjugate z* = a − jb satisfies |z|² = z·z*. In signal processing, the analytic signal uses complex representation to encode both amplitude and phase; phasors represent sinusoidal steady-state quantities as fixed complex numbers.
