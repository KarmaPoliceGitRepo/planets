# Dislocation Effects on Ultrasonics
**Aliases:** dislocation attenuation; dislocation damping; internal friction; Granato-Lücke model; Granato-Lucke string model; Akhiezer mechanism; dislocation density; dislocation-acoustic interaction; magnetoelastic attenuation; plasticity ultrasound

**Definition:** Dislocations interact with ultrasonic waves in two main ways. (i) Dislocation damping (internal friction): segments pinned between nodes vibrate like plucked strings (Granato–Lücke 1956), contributing a frequency-dependent attenuation and modulus defect proportional to dislocation density Λ and loop length L. At high amplitudes (Koehler mechanism), dislocations break away from pinning points causing irreversible hysteresis. (ii) Akhiezer mechanism: the acoustic wave modulates the thermal phonon distribution and relaxation via phonon–phonon scattering dissipates energy. Dislocation density and arrangement thus affect both attenuation and velocity, making ultrasound a diagnostic for plastic damage accumulation and fatigue.
