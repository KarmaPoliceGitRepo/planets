# Wear Mechanisms
**Aliases:** abrasive wear; adhesive wear; fatigue wear; corrosive wear; oxidative wear; scuffing; galling; two-body abrasion; three-body abrasion; wear map

**Definition:** Wear is the progressive loss of material from a solid surface due to mechanical or chemical action at a sliding, rolling, or impacting contact. The main mechanisms are: (1) abrasive wear — hard asperities or particles micro-plough material; (2) adhesive wear — cold-welded junctions shear and transfer material between surfaces; (3) fatigue wear — cyclic subsurface stress nucleates cracks that release fragments; (4) corrosive/oxidative wear — chemical attack forms surface films that are subsequently removed. Scuffing (seizure) is catastrophic adhesive failure when oil-film breakdown allows direct metal-metal contact at high flash temperatures. Wear maps correlate wear mechanism to load and velocity.
