# Longitudinal Wave
**Aliases:** P-wave; compressional wave; compression wave; pressure wave; longitudinal acoustic mode; longitudinal wave velocity; V_L; compressional wave velocity

**Definition:** A longitudinal (compressional) wave is a bulk elastic wave in which particle displacement is parallel to the propagation direction, producing alternating compressions and rarefactions. In isotropic solids the phase velocity is V_L = √((K + 4G/3)/ρ) = √(C₁₁/ρ), where K is bulk modulus and G is shear modulus. Longitudinal waves travel faster than shear waves in the same material (V_L ≈ 1.7–2.0 × V_S for typical metals) and are the mode most commonly used in standard pulse-echo ultrasonic testing.
