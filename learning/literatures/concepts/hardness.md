# Hardness
**Aliases:** Vickers hardness; HV; Brinell hardness; HB; Rockwell hardness; HR; micro-hardness; nano-hardness; indentation hardness; scratch hardness; hardness testing; surface hardening; case hardening; through-hardening

**Definition:** Hardness is a material's resistance to localised plastic deformation, measured as the load divided by the projected (Vickers, Berkovich) or curved (Brinell) area of a residual indentation. Vickers hardness HV = 1.854 F/d² (F in kgf, d in mm). Empirically, for steels HV ≈ UTS/3 (MPa). Hardness correlates with yield strength and wear resistance. Surface hardening (case carburising, nitriding, induction hardening) increases bore/bearing surface hardness for wear resistance while preserving a tough core. The abrasive wear rate is inversely proportional to hardness (Archard's law modified: k ∝ 1/H).
