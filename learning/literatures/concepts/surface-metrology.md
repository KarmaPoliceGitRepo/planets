# Surface Metrology
**Aliases:** surface measurement; profilometry; stylus profilometer; skidded profilometer; skidless profilometer; AFM; white-light interferometry; phase-shifting interferometry; confocal microscopy; tactile measurement; optical measurement; surface texture measurement; ISO GPS

**Definition:** Surface metrology is the science of measuring and characterising surface topography. Tactile (stylus) profilometry uses a diamond-tipped stylus dragged across the surface; skidded instruments use a datum skid (attenuating waviness) while skidless instruments record absolute profile and are preferred for plateau-honed bores. Optical methods include phase-shifting interferometry (PSI, ~5 nm vertical resolution) and white-light scanning interferometry (capable of measuring Ra down to nm range). AFM provides nm-scale resolution on any material. Results are filtered with ISO-standardised Gaussian or robust filters to separate roughness, waviness, and form error.
