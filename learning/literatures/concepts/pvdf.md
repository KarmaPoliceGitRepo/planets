# PVDF Piezoelectric Polymer

**Aliases:** polyvinylidene fluoride; piezo film; PVDF transducer; PVDF film; PVDF sensor; polyvinylidene difluoride; piezoelectric polymer film

**Definition:** PVDF (polyvinylidene fluoride, -(CH₂-CF₂)ₙ-) is a semi-crystalline fluoropolymer that exhibits piezoelectric and pyroelectric properties when the β-crystalline phase is preferentially formed and uniaxially poled by application of a strong DC electric field above the Curie temperature. Its piezoelectric d31 and d33 coefficients are much lower than PZT ceramics, but its low acoustic impedance (~4 MRayl) makes it a natural match to tissue and water, and its flexible, thin-film form enables large-area conformable sensors. It is widely used as broadband hydrophones, ultrasonic receivers, and pressure sensors.
