# Beam Focusing
**Aliases:** acoustic focusing; focused ultrasonic transducer; focal zone; F-number; depth of focus; FWHM beamwidth; spherically focused transducer; annular array transducer; Fresnel zone plate; bowtie Bessel beam; apodization; Rayleigh–Sommerfeld model; diffraction-limited focusing; KZK equation focusing

**Definition:** Acoustic focusing concentrates ultrasonic beam energy at a target focal point, increasing intensity and lateral resolution. A curved (spherically focused) transducer achieves a focal spot of diameter ≈ 1.02·F_N·λ (diffraction-limited), where F_N = focal length/aperture is the F-number; depth of focus is proportional to F_N². Focusing modes include flat-plate focus (FPF), point-target focus (PTF), and optical-limit focus (OLF, best achievable with F_N ≥ 0.8). Electronic focusing via phased or annular arrays allows dynamic focusing depth. Apodization (non-uniform aperture weighting) reduces side-lobes at the cost of broader main lobe. At high amplitudes, nonlinear effects (modelled by the KZK equation) distort the focal waveform and redistribute energy harmonically.
