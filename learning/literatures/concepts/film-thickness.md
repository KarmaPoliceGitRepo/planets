# Film Thickness
**Aliases:** oil film thickness; lubricant film thickness; EHL film thickness; central film thickness; minimum film thickness; Dowson-Higginson equation; h; lambda ratio

**Definition:** Film thickness is the separation distance between two lubricated surfaces in the direction normal to the interface. In EHL contacts it ranges from ~10 nm to a few µm, requiring specialised measurement techniques. The Dowson-Higginson equation gives central film thickness as a function of load, speed, lubricant viscosity, pressure-viscosity coefficient, and contact geometry. The lambda ratio (Λ = h_min / σ_combined) indicates the lubrication regime: Λ > 3 full-film, 1–3 mixed, < 1 boundary. Ultrasonically, film thickness is inferred from the spring-model stiffness (sub-resonance regime) or from the layer resonance frequency (f·h = c/2).
