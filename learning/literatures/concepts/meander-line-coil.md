# Meander-Line Coil

**Aliases:** meander coil; periodic coil; EMAT meander coil; meandering coil; racetrack coil

**Definition:** A meander-line coil is a planar coil whose conductors run in alternating parallel lines spaced by half a wavelength (d = λ/2), creating a periodic spatial pattern of eddy currents. When combined with an in-plane or normal magnetic field, the alternating Lorentz forces radiate coherently at the wavelength λ = 2d, enabling efficient generation of shear-horizontal (SH) Rayleigh or Lamb waves with a well-defined wave vector direction. The coil spatial period directly selects the operating frequency-to-wavelength ratio, making meander coils the standard choice in EMAT design for guided-wave inspection.
