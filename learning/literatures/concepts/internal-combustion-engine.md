# Internal Combustion Engine
**Aliases:** ICE; spark-ignition engine; SI engine; compression-ignition engine; CI engine; diesel engine; bore-to-stroke ratio; mean effective pressure; BMEP; IMEP; volumetric efficiency; brake specific fuel consumption; BSFC; camshaft; crankshaft; piston; connecting rod; engine knock; NOx emissions; Otto cycle; Diesel cycle

**Definition:** Internal combustion engines convert chemical energy in fuel to mechanical work by combustion inside the cylinder. SI (spark-ignition/gasoline) engines use the Otto cycle; CI (diesel) engines use the Diesel cycle. Key performance metrics are BSFC (mass fuel per unit work output), BMEP (mean effective pressure representing specific work output), and volumetric efficiency. Bore-to-stroke ratio determines engine geometry; undersquare designs (stroke > bore) suit low-speed torque applications. Engine knock in SI engines is autoignition of end-gas ahead of the flame front; NOx emissions are minimised by reducing peak combustion temperature.
