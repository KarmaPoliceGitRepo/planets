# Lamb Wave
**Aliases:** plate wave; guided plate wave; A0 mode; S0 mode; antisymmetric mode; symmetric mode; Lamb waves; Rayleigh–Lamb waves

**Definition:** Lamb waves are guided elastic waves propagating in a free plate (traction-free top and bottom surfaces), existing in two infinite families: symmetric (S₀, S₁, …) and antisymmetric (A₀, A₁, …) modes. All modes are dispersive; their phase velocities depend on the dimensionless frequency–thickness product f·d. At low f·d the A₀ mode is quasi-flexural (v ∝ √(f·d)) and S₀ is quasi-extensional; multiple higher modes cut on at f·d thresholds given by the Rayleigh–Lamb dispersion equation. Lamb waves are the primary tool in long-range guided-wave inspection and structural health monitoring of plates, pipes, and shells.
