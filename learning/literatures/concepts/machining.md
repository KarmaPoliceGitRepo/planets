# Machining
**Aliases:** cutting; turning; milling; drilling; material removal; chip formation; tool wear; Taylor's tool life equation; machinability; uncut chip thickness; cutting speed; feed rate; depth of cut; non-traditional machining; EDM; ECM

**Definition:** Machining is the family of manufacturing processes that remove material from a workpiece by chip formation using a cutting tool. Key variables are cutting speed, feed, depth of cut, and tool geometry. Taylor's tool life equation VTⁿ = C relates cutting speed V to tool life T; machinability index rates a material's ease of machining relative to a standard (AISI 1112 = 100%). Wear land height VB (flank wear) is the standard tool condition metric (ISO limit VB = 0.3 mm). Non-traditional processes (EDM, ECM, laser machining) remove material without mechanical shear and are used for hard-to-machine materials.
