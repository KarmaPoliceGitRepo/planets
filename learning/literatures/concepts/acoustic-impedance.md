# Acoustic Impedance
**Aliases:** characteristic acoustic impedance; Z = ρc; Z₀; z; ultrasonic impedance; mechanical impedance; Za; impedance mismatch

**Definition:** Acoustic impedance is the product of a medium's mass density (ρ) and the speed of sound (c), giving Z = ρc (units: MRayl = 10⁶ kg m⁻² s⁻¹). It is the fundamental material property governing the partition of wave energy at any interface: the reflection and transmission coefficients depend solely on the impedance mismatch between adjacent media. Thin layers behave as an impedance-converting element whose effective stiffness and the resulting reflection coefficient are frequency- and thickness-dependent. For a solid-air interface the mismatch is extreme (~10⁵:1), severely limiting air-coupled ultrasonics without impedance-matching treatment.
