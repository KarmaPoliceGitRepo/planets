# Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos
**Aliases:** nonlinear dynamics; chaos; strange attractor; bifurcation; limit cycle; Lyapunov exponent; fixed point; Lyapunov stability; period doubling; phase portrait; wave chaos; Whispering Gallery modes

**Definition:** Nonlinear dynamical systems can exhibit qualitatively different behaviours inaccessible to linear analysis: fixed points (equilibria), limit cycles (periodic orbits), bifurcations (qualitative changes in dynamics as a parameter varies), and chaos (sensitive dependence on initial conditions). The Lyapunov exponent λ quantifies the average rate of exponential divergence of nearby trajectories; positive λ indicates chaos. A strange attractor is a fractal set to which chaotic trajectories converge. Wave chaos occurs in ray-optics cavities where classical ray trajectories are chaotic; Whispering Gallery modes are resonant eigenstates confined near the boundary of a circular or spherical cavity.
