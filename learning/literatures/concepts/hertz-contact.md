# Hertz Contact
**Aliases:** Hertzian contact; Hertz contact law; Hertz contact theory; contact radius; contact pressure distribution; Greenwood-Williamson model; Bush-Gibson-Thomas model

**Definition:** Hertz contact theory gives the elastic stress and deformation field when two curved bodies are pressed together. For a sphere on a flat: contact radius a = (3FR/4E*)^(1/3), maximum pressure p₀ = 3F/(2πa²), and approach δ ∝ F^(2/3). For cylinders, F ∝ δ ln(δ) approximately. The theory assumes smooth surfaces, linear elasticity, and no friction. Statistical extensions (Greenwood-Williamson model for Gaussian asperities; Bush-Gibson-Thomas model for elliptical asperities) aggregate single-asperity Hertz contacts to predict real contact area, load, and interfacial stiffness for rough surfaces.
