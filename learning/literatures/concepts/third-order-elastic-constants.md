# Third-Order Elastic Constants
**Aliases:** TOE constants; TOE; Murnaghan constants l,m,n; Brugger constants C_ijk; Landau-Lifshitz A,B,C; third-order elastic coefficients; nonlinear elastic constants; SOE constants (second-order elastic constants)

**Definition:** Third-order elastic (TOE) constants are the coefficients of the cubic terms in the strain-energy density expansion W = ½C_{ijkl}ε_{ij}ε_{kl} + ⅙C_{ijklmn}ε_{ij}ε_{kl}ε_{mn} + …, describing the anharmonicity of interatomic potentials. For isotropic solids three independent TOE constants suffice: Murnaghan's l, m, n (or equivalently Landau–Lifshitz's A, B, C); for cubic crystals six independent Brugger constants C_{ijk}. TOE constants govern: (i) acoustoelastic velocity changes under stress, (ii) harmonic generation during finite-amplitude wave propagation, (iii) thermal expansion and Grüneisen parameters. They are measured by velocity-under-stress or second-harmonic-generation experiments.
