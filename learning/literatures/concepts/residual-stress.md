# Residual Stress
**Aliases:** residual stress; self-equilibrating stress; compressive residual stress; tensile residual stress; autofrettage; shot peening; Almen intensity; stress relaxation; hoop stress; thick-walled cylinder stress; Lamé equations; acoustoelastic residual stress measurement

**Definition:** Residual stresses are self-equilibrating internal stresses present in a body in the absence of external loads; they arise from non-uniform plastic deformation, thermal gradients, phase transformations, or surface treatment. Compressive residual stresses (e.g., from shot peening, autofrettage, or surface grinding) are beneficial — they retard fatigue crack initiation and propagation. Tensile residual stresses (e.g., from welding or thermal grinding damage) are detrimental. In thick-walled cylinders, autofrettage introduces compressive hoop stress at the bore (Lamé equations). Residual stresses can be measured by X-ray diffraction, neutron diffraction, hole-drilling, or ultrasonically via the acoustoelastic effect.
