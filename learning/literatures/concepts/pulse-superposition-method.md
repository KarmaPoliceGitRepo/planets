# Pulse Superposition Method
**Aliases:** pulse superposition; McSkimin method; PEO method; pulse-echo-overlap method; pulse-superposition method; overlap method; McSkimin pulse-superposition

**Definition:** In the pulse-superposition (McSkimin) method, the pulse repetition rate is servo-controlled so that successive echoes overlap coherently (constructively interfere on the oscilloscope display), providing a phase-locked condition from which absolute phase velocity is extracted with resolution ~1 part in 10⁷. It is the highest-precision contact method for elastic constant measurement. The related pulse-echo-overlap (PEO) method drives the oscilloscope X-axis at a frequency that makes successive echoes overlap visually, enabling manual tuning to exact overlap. Both methods require a stable reference reflector (back wall or buffer rod face) and correction for diffraction phase shifts.
