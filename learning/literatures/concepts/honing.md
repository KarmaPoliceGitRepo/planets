# Honing
**Aliases:** bore honing; cylinder honing; abrasive honing; superfinishing; microhoning; microfinishing; plateau honing; rough honing; finish honing; hot honing; torque plate honing; electrochemical honing; ECH; helical slide honing; hone forming; triboconditioning

**Definition:** Honing is a controlled low-speed abrasive sizing and surface-finishing process in which expandable bonded-abrasive stones simultaneously rotate and reciprocate inside a bore. The combined motion creates the characteristic crosshatch pattern essential for piston-ring/bore tribology (oil retention in valleys, load bearing on plateaus). Plateau honing is a two-step variant: rough honing removes stock, then finish honing removes peaks to create Rpk → 0 while preserving Rvk valleys. Hot honing pre-heats the cylinder to simulate thermal distortion under operation; torque plate honing bolts a head fixture to pre-distort the bore. ECH hybridises electrochemical dissolution with abrasive action for faster stock removal.
