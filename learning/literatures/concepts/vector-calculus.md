# Vector Calculus
**Aliases:** divergence theorem; Gauss's theorem; Stokes' theorem; Gauss-Green theorem; surface integral; flux; control volume; calculus of moving surfaces

**Definition:** The divergence theorem (Gauss's theorem) relates the outward flux of a vector field through a closed surface to the volume integral of its divergence: ∮_S F·dA = ∫_V (∇·F) dV. Stokes' theorem relates the circulation of a field around a closed curve to the surface integral of its curl: ∮_C F·dl = ∬_S (∇×F)·dA. These theorems underlie control-volume formulations of conservation laws in fluid mechanics and heat transfer. The calculus of moving surfaces extends divergence/Reynolds-transport theorems to surfaces that deform in time.
