# Contact Acoustic Nonlinearity
**Aliases:** CAN; contact acoustic nonlinearity; clapping nonlinearity; kissing bond; interface nonlinearity; harmonic generation at interface; odd harmonic generation; non-synchronous nonlinear effect; subharmonic

**Definition:** Contact acoustic nonlinearity (CAN) arises at partially-contacting interfaces (rough surfaces, kissing bonds, microcracks) where the interface opens (tension) and closes (compression) asymmetrically with each cycle, acting as a nonlinear spring with a clapping mechanism. The result is rich harmonic content (including odd harmonics 3f, 5f and subharmonics f/2) in the transmitted or reflected signal, even when the material bulk nonlinearity is negligible. CAN is orders of magnitude stronger than material intrinsic nonlinearity, making it a highly sensitive indicator of early-stage fatigue cracks, delaminations, and imperfect bonds.
