# Friction
**Aliases:** coefficient of friction; Coulomb friction; viscous friction; sliding friction; rolling friction; junction growth

**Definition:** Friction is the tangential resistance force at the interface between two bodies in relative motion (kinetic friction) or resisting the onset of motion (static friction). At the microscopic level, friction arises from adhesion at real contact junctions and plastic ploughing of asperities; junction growth under combined normal and tangential stress is the mechanistic explanation for Amontons' law (F = μN). In lubricated contacts, viscous shear of the film contributes a velocity-dependent term. The global tribological significance of friction is highlighted by estimates that 23% of world energy consumption is lost to friction.
