# Dispersion
**Aliases:** dispersive wave; velocity dispersion; phase velocity dispersion; frequency-dependent velocity; dispersive propagation; dispersion curves; dispersion relation

**Definition:** Dispersion is the dependence of wave phase velocity on frequency (or wavenumber), causing different frequency components to travel at different speeds and a broadband pulse to spread in time as it propagates. It arises physically from geometric confinement (guided waves), scattering from heterogeneities (grain boundaries, inclusions), and viscoelastic absorption. Dispersion curves — plots of phase or group velocity vs f·d for each mode — are the central design tool for guided-wave inspection. In polycrystalline scattering media, Kramers–Kronig relations couple frequency-dependent attenuation to phase velocity dispersion.
