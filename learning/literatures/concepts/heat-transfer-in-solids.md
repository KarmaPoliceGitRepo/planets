# Heat Transfer in Solids
**Aliases:** thermal conduction; Fourier's law; thermal conductivity; Biot number; lumped-capacitance model; Nusselt number; Prandtl number; thermal resistance; fin efficiency; heat exchanger; NTU method; conduction; convection; radiation; Fourier number

**Definition:** Heat transfer in solids is governed primarily by conduction: q = −k·∇T (Fourier's Law), where k is thermal conductivity (W/(m·K)). The Biot number Bi = hL/k distinguishes between surface-convection-limited (Bi ≪ 1, lumped model valid) and conduction-limited (Bi ≫ 1) regimes. Transient conduction solutions depend on the Fourier number Fo = αt/L². Thermal resistance networks analogise heat flow to electrical circuits. For convection, Nusselt and Prandtl numbers are key dimensionless groups. In metallic alloys, thermal conductivity correlates with electrical conductivity (Wiedemann-Franz law) and varies with microstructure, temperature, and alloying.
