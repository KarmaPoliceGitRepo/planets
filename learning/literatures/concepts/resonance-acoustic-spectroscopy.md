# Resonance Acoustic Spectroscopy

**Aliases:** RAS; resonance acoustic spectroscopy; ultrasonic NDE cylindrical resonance; resonance ultrasound spectroscopy; RUS

**Definition:** Resonance acoustic spectroscopy (RAS) is a technique that characterises a component (particularly cylindrical objects) by measuring its acoustic resonance spectrum and comparing it to theoretical predictions. The resonance frequencies depend on the material elastic constants, geometry, and defects; slight departures from expected frequencies indicate material nonuniformity, residual stress, or flaws. For cylinders, both longitudinal standing-wave modes (f = nc/(2L)) and transverse/flexural modes are excited. RAS is used for process quality control and non-destructive evaluation of manufactured parts.
