# Noise Types
**Aliases:** thermal noise; Johnson noise; shot noise; flicker noise; pink noise; 1/f noise; Brownian noise; white noise; Gaussian noise; phase noise

**Definition:** Thermal (Johnson) noise arises from random thermal motion of charge carriers and has a flat power spectral density S = 4k_B T R per unit bandwidth (white). Shot noise results from the discrete nature of charge flow with PSD = 2eI; it is also white. Flicker (1/f, pink) noise has PSD proportional to 1/f^α (α ≈ 1) and dominates at low frequencies; its origin is generally defect-state trapping in semiconductors. Brownian (1/f²) noise arises from random-walk diffusion processes. White noise has uniform PSD across all frequencies; Gaussian noise refers to the amplitude distribution being normal, which is independent of the spectral shape. Phase noise describes short-term frequency instability of an oscillator, quantified as the single-sideband noise power relative to the carrier in a 1 Hz bandwidth.
