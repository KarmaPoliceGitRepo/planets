# Crosshatch Pattern
**Aliases:** crosshatch lay; hatch angle; honing angle; bore lay; cross-hatch groove; plateau-valley texture

**Definition:** The crosshatch pattern is the characteristic angular groove texture produced on a honed bore surface by the simultaneous rotation and linear reciprocation of honing stones. The resulting lay angle (typically 40–60° inclusive, i.e. ±20–30° from horizontal) is determined by the ratio of rotational surface speed to reciprocating velocity. The crosshatch serves a dual tribological function: the grooves (valleys) retain lubricating oil during the stroke while the flat plateau areas between grooves carry the compressive load from piston rings. The honing angle also controls oil transport and ring sealing performance; angles outside the specified range are a common quality defect.
