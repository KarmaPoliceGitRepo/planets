# Shear Wave Birefringence
**Aliases:** acoustic birefringence; shear wave splitting; acoustoelastic birefringence; birefringence method; shear wave polarization splitting; double birefringence

**Definition:** When a shear wave propagates in a direction normal to a uniaxial or biaxial stress field, it splits into two orthogonally polarized components (fast and slow) with different velocities: Δv/v = K_B (σ₁ − σ₂), where K_B is the birefringence acoustoelastic constant and (σ₁ − σ₂) is the stress difference in the plane perpendicular to propagation. The birefringence method directly measures the in-plane principal stress difference without knowledge of material constants if the calibration constant K_B is known from material characterisation. It is unaffected by temperature because both polarisations travel the same path, cancelling common-mode velocity changes.
