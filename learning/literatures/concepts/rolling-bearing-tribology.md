# Rolling Bearing Tribology
**Aliases:** rolling bearing; ball bearing; roller bearing; bearing internal clearance; radial clearance; C2; CN; C3; bearing tolerance class; ISO 492; ABEC; fit; interference fit; bearing load; dynamic load rating; L10 life; Stribeck number; bearing friction; grease lubrication; journal bearing

**Definition:** Rolling bearings transmit load between rotating and stationary machine elements via rolling-element contacts (balls or rollers). In the EHL regime, a thin oil film (~0.1–1 µm) separates the rolling elements from the raceways; the lambda ratio determines the lubrication effectiveness. Bearing internal clearance (radial play before mounting) is specified in ISO 492 classes (C2 < CN < C3 < C4 < C5); it decreases under interference fitting and thermal expansion and must be managed to achieve the desired preload. Bearing tolerance classes (ABEC/ISO P-class) control raceway roundness and surface finish. Fatigue spalling (L₁₀ life) is the primary failure mode for adequate lubrication.
