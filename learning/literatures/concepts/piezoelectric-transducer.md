# Piezoelectric Transducer
**Aliases:** PZT transducer; piezoelectric crystal; ultrasonic transducer; PZT; lead metaniobate; PVDF transducer; piezo element; piezoelectric element; air-coupled piezoelectric transducer; focused ultrasonic transducer; delay-line transducer; wedge transducer; immersion transducer

**Definition:** A piezoelectric transducer converts electrical signals to mechanical (ultrasonic) vibration and vice versa, exploiting the piezoelectric effect in poled ceramics (PZT, lead metaniobate), piezoelectric crystals (quartz, lithium niobate, bismuth titanate), or polymers (PVDF). The KLM and Mason equivalent-circuit models describe the electromechanical conversion including matching layer and backing effects. Key parameters are the centre frequency (set by thickness resonance f ≈ V/2t), bandwidth (determined by Q and backing impedance), and coupling efficiency. Transducer variants include flat contact, delay-line, angle-beam (wedge), immersion, and air-coupled designs; focused transducers use curved elements or acoustic lenses.
