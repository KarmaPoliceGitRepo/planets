# Resistance Temperature Detector (RTD)

**Aliases:** RTD; Resistance Temperature Detector; resistance thermometer; platinum resistance thermometer; PRT

**Definition:** An RTD is a temperature sensor that exploits the predictable, repeatable increase in electrical resistance of a pure metal (typically platinum) with rising temperature. Platinum RTDs (Pt100, Pt1000) are the industrial standard because platinum has high chemical stability, a well-characterised R-T relationship described by the Callendar–Van Dusen equation, and wide operating range (−200 °C to +850 °C). Unlike thermocouples, RTDs require an external current source and are susceptible to self-heating; unlike thermistors (PTC/NTC), they are nearly linear and highly reproducible. RTDs are governed by the EN 60751/IEC 60751 standard.
