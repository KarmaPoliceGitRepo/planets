# Second Harmonic Generation
**Aliases:** SHG; acoustic second harmonic generation; harmonic generation; higher harmonic generation; THG; acoustic harmonic generation; Bragg diffraction optical method; stabilization distance; nonlinear parameter measurement

**Definition:** Second harmonic generation (SHG) in acoustics refers to the production of a wave at twice the fundamental frequency (2f) as a finite-amplitude wave propagates in a nonlinear elastic medium. The second-harmonic amplitude A₂ grows linearly with propagation distance L and quadratically with fundamental amplitude A₁: A₂ = (β/2)(k²A₁²L) until saturation at the stabilisation distance. SHG is the standard experimental method for measuring the nonlinearity parameter β; it can be detected by capacitive transducer, Bragg optical diffraction, or calibrated piezoelectric receiver. In damaged or plastically deformed materials, dislocation density and microcrack density raise β significantly above pristine values.
