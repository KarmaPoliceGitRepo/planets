# Viscosity
**Aliases:** dynamic viscosity; kinematic viscosity; viscosity index; SAE grade; pressure-viscosity coefficient; non-Newtonian fluid; viscoelastic fluid; frequency-dependent viscosity; shear thinning

**Definition:** Dynamic viscosity (η, Pa·s) is the resistance of a fluid to shear flow; kinematic viscosity ν = η/ρ. For lubricants, viscosity increases strongly with pressure (Barus equation: η = η₀ exp(αP)) — this pressure-viscosity coefficient α is central to EHL film formation. The viscosity index (VI) measures the sensitivity of viscosity to temperature; high-VI oils are preferred for wide-temperature-range engine service. Non-Newtonian lubricants have viscosity dependent on shear rate or frequency; dynamic (oscillatory) viscosity measured by ultrasonic (MHz) methods can differ from static values. SAE grades (e.g., SAE 5W-30) classify lubricant viscosity at standardised temperatures.
