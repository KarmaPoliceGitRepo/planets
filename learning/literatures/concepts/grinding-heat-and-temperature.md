# Grinding Heat and Temperature
**Aliases:** flash temperature; maximum temperature rise; MTR; heat partition ratio; moving heat source theory; Jaeger solution; Rosenthal solution; thermal contact coefficient; grindable thermocouple; foil thermocouple; workpiece burn; grinding burn; arc contact length; Peclet number

**Definition:** Grinding generates intense, localised heat in the wheel-workpiece contact zone (arc contact length le). The maximum temperature rise ΔT at the workpiece surface is governed by: the heat flux q (proportional to specific grinding energy × material removal rate), the heat partition ratio (fraction going into the workpiece), and the thermal properties of both bodies through the thermal contact coefficient b = √(kρc). Flash temperature theory (Blok) gives the critical surface temperature for scuffing/burn onset. Moving heat source theory (Jaeger) solves the transient temperature field. The grindable thermocouple (thin foil ground flush) is the primary in-process sensor but introduces a transparency error.
