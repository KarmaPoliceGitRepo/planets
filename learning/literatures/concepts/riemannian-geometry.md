# Riemannian and Differential Geometry
**Aliases:** Riemannian geometry; pseudo-Riemannian manifold; Riemann curvature tensor; geodesic; Christoffel symbols; covariant derivative; metric tensor; calculus of moving surfaces; differential manifold; Ricci tensor; scalar curvature

**Definition:** A Riemannian manifold is a smooth manifold equipped with a metric tensor gᵢⱼ that defines lengths and angles. The covariant derivative ∇ generalises differentiation to curved spaces; the Christoffel symbols Γᵢⱼᵏ encode the rate of change of basis vectors. The Riemann curvature tensor R^l_{ijk} measures intrinsic curvature: it vanishes iff the space is locally flat. Geodesics are curves satisfying ∇_{u}u = 0 (zero acceleration along themselves). A pseudo-Riemannian manifold has an indefinite metric (e.g., Minkowski spacetime). The calculus of moving surfaces extends these tools to time-dependent surfaces embedded in Euclidean space.
