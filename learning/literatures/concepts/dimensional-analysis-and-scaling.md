# Dimensional Analysis and Scaling
**Aliases:** dimensional analysis; Buckingham Pi theorem; scaling; similitude; non-dimensionalisation; characteristic scales; Reynolds number; Strouhal number

**Definition:** Dimensional analysis determines the functional form of physical relationships by requiring dimensional consistency. The Buckingham Pi theorem states that a physically meaningful equation with n variables and k independent base dimensions can be expressed in terms of n−k dimensionless groups (Pi parameters). Non-dimensionalisation scales governing equations by characteristic quantities (length L₀, time T₀, velocity V₀), revealing dominant terms and enabling similitude between models. The Reynolds number Re = ρVL/μ and Strouhal number St = fL/V are classic examples arising from fluid-mechanics scaling. In measurement science, dimensional consistency is a first-order check on derived formulas.
