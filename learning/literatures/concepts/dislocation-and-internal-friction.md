# Dislocation and Internal Friction
**Aliases:** dislocation attenuation; dislocation damping; Granato-Lücke model; dislocation string model; internal friction; amplitude-dependent attenuation; dislocation density; pinning point; dislocation network; plastic deformation; slip; cold work; annealing

**Definition:** Dislocations are line defects in crystal lattices whose motion controls plastic deformation. Under an oscillating acoustic stress, dislocation segments between pinning points vibrate like a taut string (Granato-Lücke model): at low amplitudes they produce amplitude-independent attenuation (breakaway not needed); at high amplitudes segments break away from weak pins causing irreversible (amplitude-dependent) attenuation and acoustic nonlinearity. Dislocation density and loop length — both altered by cold work, fatigue, and heat treatment — thus produce measurable changes in ultrasonic attenuation and velocity. The Granato-Lücke string model predicts α ∝ Λ³L² (Λ = density, L = loop length between pins).
