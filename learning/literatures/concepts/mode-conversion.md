# Mode Conversion
**Aliases:** wave mode conversion; mode-converted ultrasound; L→T conversion; T→L conversion; longitudinal-to-shear conversion; shear-to-longitudinal conversion; bulk-to-surface wave conversion; mode-converted signal

**Definition:** Mode conversion occurs when an ultrasonic wave impinges on an interface at oblique incidence, partly transmitting/reflecting in the incident mode and partly converting energy into other wave types (e.g., longitudinal → shear, shear → Rayleigh). At a free surface or fluid–solid interface the mode-conversion amplitudes are governed by the boundary conditions (continuity of displacement and traction); critical angles separate regions where converted modes are propagating or evanescent. In pulse-echo measurements mode-converted signals (e.g., L→T→L) have different TOF and path, exploited in bolt-load measurement where the mode-converted signal is independent of bolt elongation.
