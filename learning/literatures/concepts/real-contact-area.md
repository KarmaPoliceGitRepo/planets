# Real Contact Area
**Aliases:** RCA; fractional contact area; A/A₀; true contact area; apparent contact area vs real contact area; bearing area; bearing ratio

**Definition:** Real contact area (RCA) is the sum of all micro-contact spot areas where asperities of two rough surfaces are in solid-solid contact; it is much smaller than the nominal (apparent) area at typical engineering loads. For elastic contacts, RCA ∝ normal load F and is independent of nominal area. RCA governs friction (shear of adhesive junctions), wear rate, and thermal/electrical contact resistance. Ultrasonically, the reflection coefficient R at an interface approaches unity for complete separation (R=1, no contact) and falls as RCA increases; the spring interface model relates R to interfacial stiffness K, which in turn is proportional to RCA for a given material pair.
