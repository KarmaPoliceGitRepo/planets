# Coordinate Systems and Geometry
**Aliases:** polar coordinates; cylindrical coordinates; spherical coordinates; Argand plane; Cardioid; limaçon; rose curve; geodesic; Riemannian geometry; pseudo-Riemannian manifold; metric tensor; curvilinear coordinates

**Definition:** Polar coordinates (r, θ) represent planar points by distance r from the origin and angle θ; common polar curves include the cardioid (r = a(1 + cos θ)), limaçon (r = b + a cos θ), and rose curves (r = a cos(nθ)). In 3D, cylindrical (r, θ, z) and spherical (r, θ, φ) coordinates simplify problems with axial or spherical symmetry. A geodesic is the shortest path (length-minimising curve) on a surface; on a flat plane it is a straight line, on a sphere it is a great circle. The metric tensor gᵢⱼ encodes distances in a curved space; the pseudo-Riemannian metric with signature (−,+,+,+) underlies general relativity.
