# Laser Ultrasonics
**Aliases:** laser-generated ultrasound; laser-based ultrasonics; photoacoustic ultrasonics; LUS; optical ultrasonics; opto-acoustic; non-contact laser ultrasound; Nd:YAG laser source

**Definition:** Laser ultrasonics (LUS) is a non-contact technique in which a pulsed laser generates broadband ultrasonic waves through rapid surface heating (thermoelastic regime, sub-ablation) or surface ablation (ablation regime), and a second laser (interferometer: Fabry–Pérot, Michelson, or photorefractive) detects the resulting surface displacement. In the thermoelastic regime the source is a dipole (normal stress tangential to surface) producing a characteristic directivity pattern with lobes at ~65° from the surface normal for bulk waves and a strong Rayleigh wave. Applications include non-contact velocity and attenuation measurement, SAW dispersion profiling, phase transformation detection, and high-temperature process monitoring.
