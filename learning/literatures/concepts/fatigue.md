# Fatigue
**Aliases:** fatigue life; S-N curve; Wöhler curve; endurance limit; Goodman diagram; Miner's rule; fatigue crack initiation; fatigue crack growth; stress ratio R; alternating stress; mean stress; fatigue limit; contact fatigue; rolling contact fatigue; pitting; spalling

**Definition:** Fatigue is failure of a material under repeated cyclic loading at stresses well below the monotonic yield strength. The S-N (Wöhler) curve plots stress amplitude vs. cycles to failure. Ferrous metals exhibit a true endurance limit (fatigue limit) below which infinite life is predicted; non-ferrous metals do not. The Goodman diagram combines alternating stress and mean stress failure criteria. Miner's linear damage rule accumulates fatigue damage: Σ(ni/Ni) = 1 at failure. Rolling contact fatigue (RCF) — pitting and spalling initiated by subsurface shear stress maxima — is the primary failure mode for rolling bearings and cams. Compressive residual stress and surface finish strongly influence fatigue life.
