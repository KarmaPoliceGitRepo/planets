# Experimental Design and Research Methods
**Aliases:** experimental design; randomisation; blinding; control tests; repeat tests; replication; pre-registration; replication crisis; publication bias; effect size; literature review; research gap; doctoral standards; focal theory; synthesis; hedging; reporting verbs; paraphrasing; boosters; background theory

**Definition:** Good experimental design controls sources of systematic error through randomisation (random assignment of treatments to units), blinding (concealing group membership from observers), matched controls, and adequate replication. The number of repeats needed is determined by the target statistical power and expected effect size. Pre-registration commits to the analysis plan before data collection, reducing researcher degrees of freedom and publication bias. A literature review identifies research gaps, synthesises prior work using appropriate academic language (hedging, reporting verbs), and justifies the focal theory. Doctoral standards require original contribution, rigorous methodology, and critical evaluation of sources.
