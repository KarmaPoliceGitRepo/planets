# Lubrication Regimes
**Aliases:** Stribeck curve; hydrodynamic lubrication; elastohydrodynamic lubrication; EHL; EHD; boundary lubrication; mixed lubrication; iso-viscous EHL; thermohydrodynamic lubrication; hydrostatic lubrication; full-film lubrication

**Definition:** Lubrication regimes classify the degree of surface separation and load-sharing between asperities and the fluid film. In hydrodynamic lubrication (HL) a self-generating wedge film fully separates surfaces; in elastohydrodynamic lubrication (EHL) elastic deformation of the surfaces and pressure-dependent viscosity are both significant — the regime relevant to gears and rolling bearings. Mixed lubrication occupies the Stribeck-curve minimum where both asperities and film carry load. Boundary lubrication occurs when the film is molecular-thin and protection comes from chemically or physically adsorbed boundary films. ISO-viscous EHL (soft EHL) applies when viscosity is pressure-insensitive but elastic deformation is large (rubber contacts).
