# Attenuation
**Aliases:** attenuation coefficient; α; acoustic attenuation; ultrasonic attenuation; attenuation per wavelength; frequency-dependent attenuation; dislocation damping; magnetoelastic attenuation; broadband ultrasonic attenuation (BUA)

**Definition:** Acoustic attenuation describes the exponential decay of wave amplitude with propagation distance, A(x) = A₀ exp(−αx), where α (Np/m or dB/m) is the frequency-dependent attenuation coefficient. Attenuation originates from scattering at microstructural inhomogeneities (grain boundaries, inclusions), viscothermal absorption, dislocation damping (Granato–Lücke), thermoelastic coupling, and magnetic domain interactions (magnetoelastic attenuation in ferromagnets). In polycrystalline metals, grain scattering attenuation obeys distinct frequency power-laws in the Rayleigh (α ∝ f⁴D³), stochastic (α ∝ f²D), and geometric diffuse-field regimes; measured attenuation therefore serves as a microstructural diagnostic.
