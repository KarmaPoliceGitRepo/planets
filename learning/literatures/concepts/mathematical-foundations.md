# Mathematical Foundations
**Aliases:** mathematical proof; complete ordered field; cardinality; mathematical induction; axioms; set theory; real numbers; foundations of mathematics; rank-nullity; separability; Riemann integral; fundamental theorem of calculus; polynomial algebra; infinite series

**Definition:** A rigorous mathematical foundation starts from axioms (Peano for arithmetic, ZFC for set theory) and builds the real numbers as the unique complete ordered field. Cardinality distinguishes countable from uncountable infinity. Mathematical proof methods include direct proof, proof by contradiction, and induction. The Riemann integral defines ∫f(x)dx as the limit of Riemann sums; the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus links differentiation and integration. Polynomial algebra studies polynomial rings; the Cayley-Hamilton theorem and minimal polynomial arise from the structure of such rings.
