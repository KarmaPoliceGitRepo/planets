# Complex Analysis
**Aliases:** complex analysis; analytic function; holomorphic; Cauchy-Riemann equations; residue theorem; contour integration; poles; essential singularity; Taylor series; Laurent series; conformal mapping

**Definition:** A complex function f(z) = u + iv is analytic (holomorphic) in a region if it satisfies the Cauchy-Riemann equations: ∂u/∂x = ∂v/∂y and ∂u/∂y = −∂v/∂x. The residue theorem relates a contour integral to the sum of residues at enclosed poles: ∮_C f(z) dz = 2πj Σ Res(f, zₖ). Residues are used to evaluate real definite integrals by closing contours in the complex plane. Conformal mappings (angle-preserving analytic functions) transform boundary-value problems to simpler geometries. Poles of the Laplace transform in the left half-plane correspond to stable modes; poles on the imaginary axis are marginally stable.
