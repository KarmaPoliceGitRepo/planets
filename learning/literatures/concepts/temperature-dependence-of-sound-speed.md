# Temperature Dependence of Sound Speed
**Aliases:** velocity–temperature dependence; sound velocity vs temperature; V(T); ultrasonic velocity–temperature dependence; sigmoidal velocity curve; speed of sound vs temperature; dv/dT; temperature coefficient of velocity; acoustic thermometry calibration

**Definition:** The speed of sound in a solid decreases with increasing temperature, primarily because thermal expansion softens the elastic moduli; the relationship V(T) is typically nonlinear (sigmoidal in metals: steep near phase transitions, gradual otherwise). For steels, dV/dT ≈ −0.6 to −1.5 m/s/°C for longitudinal waves; Curie and phase-transformation temperatures appear as inflection points or discontinuities in V(T). Calibrated V(T) curves are the basis for non-invasive ultrasonic thermometry: measuring TOF and inverting through V(T) gives internal temperature. Acoustic velocity hysteresis (non-recoverable shifts after plastic cycling) must be distinguished from reversible thermal effects.
