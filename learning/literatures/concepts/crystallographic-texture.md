# Crystallographic Texture
**Aliases:** texture in metals; rolling texture; preferred orientation; orientation distribution function; ODF; Roe coefficients; anisotropy of polycrystal; microtexture regions; MTRs; banded microstructure; elastic anisotropy; single-crystal elastic constants

**Definition:** Crystallographic texture is the non-random distribution of crystallographic orientations in a polycrystalline material, quantified by the orientation distribution function (ODF). Texture arises from deformation (rolling, forging) or recrystallisation. It causes elastic anisotropy at the macroscopic level: wave velocities and attenuation become direction-dependent. In titanium alloys, microtexture regions (MTRs) — clusters of similarly oriented grains — act as large effective scatterers that control inspection sensitivity. The ODF enters scattering models through Roe coefficients (texture expansion coefficients), modifying the single-crystal elastic anisotropy factor W.
