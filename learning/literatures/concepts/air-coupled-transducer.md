# Air-Coupled Piezoelectric Transducer

**Aliases:** non-contact ultrasonic transducer; air-coupled transducer; air transducer; air-coupled piezoelectric transducer; non-contact UT

**Definition:** An air-coupled piezoelectric transducer is designed to radiate and receive ultrasound through air rather than a liquid or solid couplant, enabling fully non-contact inspection. The principal challenge is the very large acoustic impedance mismatch between PZT (~30 MRayl) and air (~0.0004 MRayl), which causes >99.99% energy reflection at each interface without matching. Solutions include multi-layer quarter-wavelength matching stacks using low-density materials (aerogel, porous polymers, membranes) and resonant structures operating near a mechanical resonance of the transducer in air.
