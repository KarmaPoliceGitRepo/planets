# Guided Waves
**Aliases:** guided wave testing; guided wave ultrasound; plate waves; Lamb waves; guided wave SHM; long-range ultrasound; Pochhammer–Chree modes; Love wave; cylindrical guided waves

**Definition:** Guided waves are elastic waves confined between two or more boundaries (plate surfaces, pipe walls, rod surface) so that energy is channelled along the structure rather than radiating into the surrounding medium. All guided-wave modes are dispersive: phase velocity depends on frequency–thickness product f·d. In plates the modes are classified as Lamb waves (in-plane sagittal plane motion) and Love waves (SH in-plane); in cylinders as longitudinal, torsional, and flexural Pochhammer–Chree families. Long-range guided-wave inspection exploits low attenuation at low f·d to screen large structures from a single sensor position.
