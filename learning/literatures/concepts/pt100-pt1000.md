# Pt100 / Pt1000

**Aliases:** Pt100; Pt-100; Pt1000; platinum RTD; 100-ohm RTD; 1000-ohm RTD

**Definition:** Pt100 and Pt1000 are the most widely used platinum RTD standards, with nominal resistance 100 Ω and 1000 Ω at 0 °C respectively, both conforming to EN 60751. The temperature coefficient of resistance (TCR) for both is nominally 0.00385 Ω/(Ω·°C) (the "385" class). Pt1000 produces a larger voltage signal for a given excitation current, making it preferable for 2-wire installations or long cable runs where lead resistance is a significant fraction of the sensor resistance. Pt100 is the dominant choice in process industry, often used in 3-wire or 4-wire configurations. The R-T relationship is described by the Callendar–Van Dusen equation.
