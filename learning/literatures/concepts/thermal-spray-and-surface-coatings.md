# Thermal Spray and Surface Coatings
**Aliases:** thermal spray; TWAS; twin-wire arc spray; LDS; Lichtbogendrahtspritzen; APS; atmospheric plasma spray; PTWA; plasma transferred wire arc; NANOSLIDE®; DLC; diamond-like carbon; bore coating; cylinder liner coating; thermal spray bore coating; PVD; CVD; surface engineering

**Definition:** Thermal spray and other surface coating processes deposit thin functional layers onto substrate surfaces to improve wear resistance, friction, or corrosion protection. In engine bore applications, iron-based thermal spray coatings (TWAS, LDS, PTWA) replace cast-iron liners in aluminium blocks, reducing weight and enabling tighter bore tolerances. These coatings have an as-sprayed microstructure with porosity and splat boundaries that require specialised multistep honing processes to achieve target Rk parameters. DLC coatings reduce friction in piston ring-bore contacts. Coating adhesion, residual stress, and thermal mismatch with the substrate are key engineering concerns.
