# Acoustic Impedance Matching Layer

**Aliases:** impedance matching layer; acoustic matching layer; acoustic impedance matching; transducer matching layer; matching material

**Definition:** An acoustic impedance matching layer is an intermediate material inserted between two media of different acoustic impedances (Z = ρc) to maximise acoustic energy transfer across the interface. Without matching, the large impedance mismatch between a PZT ceramic (~30–35 MRayl) and water (~1.5 MRayl) or tissue would reflect most energy. A single λ/4 matching layer with impedance Z_m = √(Z₁Z₂) yields theoretically zero reflection at the design frequency; multiple layers extend the bandwidth. The technique is fundamental to medical, industrial, and air-coupled transducer construction.
