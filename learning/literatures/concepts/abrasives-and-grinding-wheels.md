# Abrasives and Grinding Wheels
**Aliases:** abrasive; honing stone; grinding wheel; superabrasive; cBN; cubic boron nitride; diamond abrasive; alumina; silicon carbide; SiC; Al2O3; vitrified bond; resinoid bond; metal bond; abrasive grain; grit size; G-ratio; wheel wear; dressing; cutting tool materials; HSS; carbide

**Definition:** Abrasive processes use hard particles (grains) to remove material by micro-cutting, micro-ploughing, and micro-fracture. Conventional abrasives are aluminium oxide (Al₂O₃, alumina) for ferrous metals and silicon carbide (SiC) for non-ferrous and brittle materials. Superabrasives — cBN (cubic boron nitride) for hardened steels and diamond for ceramics, carbides, and cast iron — give far lower wheel wear. The G-ratio (volume of workpiece removed / volume of wheel worn) is the efficiency metric. Bond types (vitrified, resinoid, metal, electroplated) control grain retention and chip clearance. Cutting tool materials (HSS, cemented carbide, ceramic, CBN, diamond) follow the same hardness hierarchy.
