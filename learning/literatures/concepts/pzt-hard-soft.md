# PZT Hard and Soft Grades

**Aliases:** hard PZT; soft PZT; hard ceramic; soft ceramic; high-Q PZT; low-Q PZT; PIC material grades; hard piezoceramic; soft piezoceramic

**Definition:** Lead zirconate titanate (PZT) ceramics are commercially divided into "hard" and "soft" grades by donor/acceptor dopants. Hard PZT (acceptor-doped) has lower piezoelectric activity (lower d33, lower ε), higher mechanical quality factor Qm (>500), lower dielectric loss, and greater resistance to depoling under high drive levels; it is preferred for high-power and resonator applications. Soft PZT (donor-doped) has higher d33 and permittivity, lower Qm (<100), greater sensitivity, and broader bandwidth; it is preferred for receiver and low-power transmitter applications.
