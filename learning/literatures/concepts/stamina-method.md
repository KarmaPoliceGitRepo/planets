# STAMINA Method

**Aliases:** STAMINA; standing wave measurement of interfaces and layers; continuous-wave ultrasonics; STAMINA method; CW resonance method; frequency-swept ultrasonic resonance

**Definition:** STAMINA (Standing-wave Acoustic Transducer Method for Interface and layer NAlysis) is a continuous-wave ultrasonic measurement technique in which a frequency-swept sinusoidal signal drives a piezoelectric transducer bonded to a specimen. At specific drive frequencies constructive interference of multiple internal reflections creates a standing wave, producing a resonance peak in the received amplitude spectrum. The standing-wave reflection coefficient S (ratio of resonant amplitude to reference amplitude) is related to the interface or layer reflection coefficient R. By analysing the resonance frequency and amplitude, the technique measures thin-film thickness, oil film thickness, interfacial stiffness, bolt load, or material acoustic properties.
