# Critical Angle and Refraction
**Aliases:** critical angle; first critical angle; second critical angle; Snell's law; total internal reflection; refraction; head wave; creep wave; angle-beam testing; LCR wave

**Definition:** When an obliquely incident wave crosses an interface between media of different wave speeds, mode conversion and refraction occur according to Snell's law: sin θ₁/v₁ = sin θ₂/v₂. The first critical angle θ_c1 = arcsin(v₁/v_L2) is the incidence angle at which the refracted longitudinal wave in medium 2 reaches 90°; beyond θ_c1, only shear waves (and/or surface-skimming longitudinal, LCR) penetrate medium 2. The second critical angle θ_c2 = arcsin(v₁/v_T2) similarly cuts off refracted shear waves, beyond which only evanescent (surface) waves penetrate. Angle-beam testing uses refraction to generate shear or Rayleigh waves in a solid from a liquid or wedge coupler.
