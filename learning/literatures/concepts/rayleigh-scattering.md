# Rayleigh Scattering (Ultrasonic)
**Aliases:** Rayleigh scattering regime; Rayleigh law; f⁴ law; f⁴D³ law; sub-wavelength scattering; Rayleigh scatterer; Rayleigh grain noise

**Definition:** In the Rayleigh scattering regime (grain diameter D ≪ wavelength λ, typically D < λ/10), ultrasonic attenuation scales as α ∝ f⁴D³ for longitudinal waves in polycrystalline metals, and backscattering cross-section scales as σ ∝ f⁴D⁶. The Rayleigh law arises from treating each grain as a small elastic inhomogeneity whose scattering cross-section increases steeply with frequency. This regime dominates at low inspection frequencies (< ~10 MHz for fine-grained steels) and enables grain-size measurement from the frequency dependence of attenuation. Note: this is the elastic-wave analogue of Rayleigh optical scattering (λ ≫ scatterer size), physically distinct from Rayleigh surface waves.
