# Flash Temperature
**Aliases:** flash temperature concept; Blok flash temperature; scuffing criterion; bulk temperature; contact temperature; maximum temperature; surface temperature rise

**Definition:** Flash temperature is the transient peak surface temperature rise at an asperity micro-contact or at a moving sliding/grinding contact, superimposed on the bulk (nominal) temperature of the surface. Blok (1937/1963) defined the flash temperature Tf as a function of friction coefficient, load, velocity, and thermal properties of both contacting bodies; scuffing (seizure) occurs when Tf exceeds a critical threshold. Flash temperature is higher for metals with low thermal conductivity, high friction, and high sliding speed. In grinding, the flash temperature at the abrasive-grain contact is distinct from the workpiece surface temperature at the arc contact zone.
