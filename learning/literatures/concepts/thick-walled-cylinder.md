# Thick-Walled Cylinder
**Aliases:** Lamé equations; pressure vessel; hoop stress; radial stress; bore stress; autofrettage; hydraulic pressure; thick cylinder; gun barrel; high-pressure vessel; Lamé solution; Barlow formula; bore distortion; cylinder bore pressure

**Definition:** A thick-walled cylinder (wall thickness > ~5–10% of bore radius) cannot be analysed with the thin-wall approximation because hoop and radial stresses vary significantly through the wall. The Lamé equations give the exact elastic solution: σ_θ = A + B/r²; σ_r = A − B/r² (constants A and B from boundary conditions). The maximum hoop stress occurs at the bore (r = r_i). Autofrettage applies internal pressure beyond local yield at the bore to introduce beneficial compressive residual hoop stress, significantly increasing fatigue life. Bore distortion in engine cylinders (from head-bolt clamping loads or thermal gradients) is a non-cylindrical Lamé variant.
