# Lubricant Degradation
**Aliases:** oil degradation; oxidation stability; acid number; TAN; total acid number; ASTM D4636; viscosity breakdown; oil ageing; sludge; varnish; antioxidant; EP additive; extreme pressure additive; AW additive; anti-wear additive; tribofilm; boundary film

**Definition:** Lubricant degradation reduces the oil's ability to maintain adequate film thickness and chemical protection. Oxidation (catalysed by heat and metal surfaces) increases viscosity and generates acidic by-products quantified by the total acid number (TAN; mg KOH/g oil; ASTM D4636). TAN rise signals the end of oil useful life. Thermal and mechanical shear can reduce base-oil viscosity irreversibly. EP/AW additives react with the metal surface under high pressure or temperature to form protective tribofilms (ZDDP, MoS₂) that reduce wear in boundary and mixed lubrication. Monitoring TAN, viscosity, and spectroscopic metal content is the basis of oil condition monitoring.
