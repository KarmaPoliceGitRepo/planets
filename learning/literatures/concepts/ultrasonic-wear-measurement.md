# Ultrasonic Wear Measurement
**Aliases:** ultrasonic wear; pulse-echo wear measurement; time-of-flight wear; resonant frequency dip; phase-comparison wear; waveform integral; mass loss wear; gravimetric wear; real-time wear; in-situ wear; wear land VB; calandria tube wear; barrel/screw wear

**Definition:** Ultrasonic techniques measure progressive material loss non-destructively and, in some configurations, in real time during operation. Three principal methods: (1) ToF pulse-echo — thickness from d = v·t/2; resolution ~1 µm limited by SNR and velocity knowledge; (2) phase-comparison — tracks CW echo phase for sub-µm wear (theoretical 0.015 µm resolution); (3) resonant frequency dip — sub-surface holes create interference dips in the FFT whose frequency shifts directly yield remaining material thickness, independent of bulk temperature. Gravimetric (mass-loss) measurement is the classical reference method but cannot distinguish metal-loss from metal-transfer. Temperature compensation is essential for in-situ measurements.
