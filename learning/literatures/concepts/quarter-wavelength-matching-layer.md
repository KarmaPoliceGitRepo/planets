# Quarter-Wavelength Matching Layer

**Aliases:** λ/4 layer; lambda/4 layer; quarter-wave matching layer; λ/4 matching layer; quarter-wave acoustic transformer

**Definition:** A quarter-wavelength matching layer is a material slab whose thickness equals one quarter of the acoustic wavelength at the operating frequency (t = λ/4 = c_layer/(4f₀)). Its characteristic acoustic impedance Z_m = √(Z₁Z₂) achieves theoretically perfect impedance transformation between media with impedances Z₁ and Z₂ at the design frequency, analogous to the electrical quarter-wave transformer. At frequencies away from f₀ the reflection rises, giving a bandwidth that widens with increasing Z₁/Z₂ contrast; multiple cascaded λ/4 layers with graded impedances can achieve broadband matching.
