# Piston-Bore Tribology
**Aliases:** piston ring/bore contact; ring pack; piston skirt; cylinder liner; bore distortion; oil control ring; compression ring; ring conformability; oil consumption; ring-liner friction; piston secondary motion

**Definition:** Piston-bore tribology encompasses the friction, lubrication, and wear at the three main piston interfaces: (1) ring pack-bore — rings reciprocate against the honed bore surface; EHL or mixed lubrication during mid-stroke, boundary lubrication at dead centres; (2) piston skirt-bore — governs lateral noise and oil-film thickness; (3) cam-follower and bearing contacts. The bore surface texture (crosshatch, plateau honing) is optimised to provide load-bearing plateaus and oil-retaining valleys. Bore distortion under assembly and thermal loads causes non-circular bore profiles that compromise ring sealing and oil control. Global estimates attribute ~50% of engine mechanical friction to the piston assembly.
