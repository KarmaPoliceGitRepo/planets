# Grain Scattering Attenuation
**Aliases:** polycrystal scattering; ultrasonic grain noise; grain boundary scattering; grain scattering; stochastic scattering; diffuse field scattering; backscattering coefficient; BSC; backscatter amplitude

**Definition:** In polycrystalline metals, elastic waves scatter at grain boundaries due to impedance mismatch arising from crystallographic misorientation between adjacent grains. Three frequency regimes are distinguished by the ratio of wavelength λ to mean grain diameter D: Rayleigh regime (D ≪ λ, α ∝ f⁴D³), stochastic regime (D ~ λ/10, α ∝ f²D), and geometric/diffuse-field regime (D ~ λ, strong multiple scattering). Attenuation measurements thus serve as a grain-size diagnostic; the backscattering coefficient BSC provides microstructural information independent of wave path and is related to attenuation via energy conservation. Crystallographic texture and grain shape anisotropy introduce directional dependence.
