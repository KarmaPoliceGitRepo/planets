# Thermal Diffusivity
**Aliases:** thermal diffusivity; alpha; thermal conductivity; volumetric heat capacity; laser flash method; LFA; flash diffusivity; Cape-Lehman model; thermal contact coefficient; Fourier number; specific heat

**Definition:** Thermal diffusivity α = k/(ρcp) (m²/s) governs how quickly a temperature disturbance diffuses through a solid; it is the ratio of thermal conductivity k to volumetric heat capacity ρcp. High α means fast thermal equilibration (metals ~1–100 mm²/s; ceramics ~0.5–10 mm²/s; polymers ~0.1 mm²/s). The laser flash method (LFA, ASTM E1461) is the standard technique: a pulse heats one face of a disc, and α is extracted from the rear-face temperature rise curve using the Parker formula or the Cape-Lehman analytical model (which accounts for heat losses). Thermal conductivity k is often derived from k = α·ρ·cp.
