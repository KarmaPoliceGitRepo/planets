# Asperity Contact
**Aliases:** asperity; peak; summit; asperity shakedown; bedding-in; spectral moments; asperity density; asperity radius; Beilby layer

**Definition:** An asperity is a local surface protrusion (peak in 2D, summit in 3D) that makes first contact when two rough surfaces are brought together. Asperity geometry is characterised statistically through spectral moments of the height profile: m₀ (mean-square height), m₂ (mean-square slope), and m₄ (mean-square curvature), from which asperity density, mean radius, and height distribution are derived for input to Greenwood-Williamson and BGT contact models. On repeated loading, plastic asperities deform progressively until the contact is nominally elastic — a process called asperity shakedown (bedding-in), detectable as hysteresis reduction in cyclic ultrasonic reflection measurements.
