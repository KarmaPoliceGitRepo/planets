# Curie Temperature

**Aliases:** Tc; Curie point; depolarisation temperature; piezoelectric Curie temperature

**Definition:** The Curie temperature Tc is the temperature above which a ferroelectric ceramic loses its spontaneous polarisation and piezoelectric properties irreversibly due to a phase transition from the ferroelectric to the paraelectric state. For PZT ceramics Tc typically ranges from 150 °C to 350 °C depending on composition and dopants; hard PZT grades generally have higher Tc than soft grades. Operating temperatures should be kept well below Tc (typically below Tc/2) to prevent partial depolarisation. For PVDF, the effective Curie-like limit is ~100 °C.
