# Partial Differential Equations
**Aliases:** PDE; partial differential equation; wave equation; heat equation; Laplace equation; separation of variables; method of characteristics; Green's function; Fourier series; boundary conditions

**Definition:** A partial differential equation (PDE) involves partial derivatives with respect to two or more independent variables. The wave equation u_{tt} = c²∇²u governs acoustic and elastic wave propagation; the heat (diffusion) equation u_t = κ∇²u describes thermal conduction; Laplace's equation ∇²u = 0 describes steady-state fields. Separation of variables reduces a PDE to coupled ODEs by assuming a product solution; it works for equations with separable boundary conditions. The method of characteristics converts first-order PDEs to a family of ODEs along characteristic curves. Green's functions provide the impulse-response solution for linear PDEs, expressing the response to any forcing by superposition.
