# Frequency–Thickness Product
**Aliases:** f·d; f·t; f×d; ft scaling; frequency-thickness; frequency-thickness product scaling; dimensionless frequency; f×h

**Definition:** The frequency–thickness product (f·d, in MHz·mm) is the natural dimensionless parameter governing all dispersive phenomena in layered or plate-like geometries. Lamb wave mode shapes and phase velocities depend on f·d, not on f and d separately; reflection coefficients from thin layers are functions of f·d (when layer thickness is below resonance); and near-field/far-field transition distances scale as f·a² (where a is transducer radius). The f·d parameter also enables scaling: reflection coefficient data measured at one frequency can be used to predict behaviour at another frequency provided the product f·d is matched. In practice f·d < ~1 MHz·mm is the quasi-static (spring model) regime; f·d ≈ 1 MHz·mm is the resonance transition; f·d ≫ 1 MHz·mm requires full wave treatment.
