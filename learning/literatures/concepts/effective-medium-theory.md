# Effective Medium Theory
**Aliases:** effective medium approximation; homogenisation; self-consistent theory; Hashin-Shtrikman bounds; Voigt average; Reuss average; Hill average; Wood's law; rule of mixtures; Biot-Gassmann

**Definition:** Effective medium theory (EMT) replaces a heterogeneous composite (polycrystal, porous solid, particulate suspension) with an equivalent homogeneous medium having effective elastic constants and density that reproduce the long-wavelength wave behaviour. The Voigt (strain-uniform) and Reuss (stress-uniform) averages bracket the true effective constants; the Hashin–Shtrikman variational bounds are tighter. For fluid-saturated porous media the Biot–Gassmann theory provides the effective bulk and shear moduli as functions of porosity and pore fluid properties. EMT is valid only when λ ≫ heterogeneity size; it breaks down in the stochastic and geometric scattering regimes where explicit scattering models are needed.
