# Contact Mechanics
**Aliases:** contact zone analysis; contact pressure; contact area; real contact area; nominal contact area; mean contact pressure; asperity contact; elastic contact; plastic contact

**Definition:** Contact mechanics studies the stresses, deformations, and energy exchange when two solid bodies are pressed together. It underpins friction, wear, and lubrication models. For smooth curved bodies, Hertz theory applies. For real rough surfaces, a statistical distribution of asperity contacts must be considered; the real contact area A_r ≪ nominal area A_n at engineering loads, and A_r ∝ F for elastic contacts (proportional loading). Contact stiffness — the incremental ratio dF/dδ — links contact mechanics to ultrasonic measurements that infer it from reflection coefficients.
