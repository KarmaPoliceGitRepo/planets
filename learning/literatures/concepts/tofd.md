# Time-of-Flight Diffraction (TOFD)
**Aliases:** TOFD; time-of-flight diffraction; tip diffraction; TOFD defect sizing

**Definition:** Time-of-flight diffraction (TOFD) is an ultrasonic NDT technique that sizes and locates planar defects by measuring the arrival times of waves diffracted from crack or notch tips, rather than relying on specular reflection amplitude. Two transducers (pitch-catch) straddle the weld or inspection zone; diffracted tip signals arrive between the lateral wave (direct path near surface) and the back-wall reflection. Defect depth z is calculated from the time difference between upper and lower tip signals. TOFD exploits the broadband, omni-directional character of tip diffraction, making it largely independent of defect orientation; laser generation's broad bandwidth particularly suits TOFD applications. Detection probability is high but sizing accuracy degrades near the surface.
