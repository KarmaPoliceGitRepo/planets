# Acoustoelastic Effect
**Aliases:** acoustoelasticity; acousto-elastic effect; acoustoelastic principle; speed of sound vs stress; stress-dependent velocity

**Definition:** The acoustoelastic effect is the dependence of elastic-wave velocity on the applied or residual stress state of a solid. It arises because a prestress alters the effective elastic constants seen by the propagating wave through the third-order elastic (TOE) terms in the strain-energy expansion. For a uniaxially stressed isotropic solid the fractional velocity change is proportional to stress: Δv/v₀ = K·σ, where K (the acoustoelastic coefficient) depends on the wave mode, propagation direction, and the material's second- and third-order elastic constants. The effect is the physical basis for non-destructive stress and load measurement by ultrasonics.
