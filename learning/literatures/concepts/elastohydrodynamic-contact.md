# Elastohydrodynamic Contact
**Aliases:** EHL; EHD lubrication; elastohydrodynamic lubrication; Dowson-Higginson equation; iso-viscous EHL; piezoviscous EHL; Hertz contact pressure; pressure spike; starvation; cavitation

**Definition:** Elastohydrodynamic lubrication (EHL) is the regime where both elastic deformation of the contacting bodies and the pressure-dependent viscosity of the lubricant are essential to film formation. It applies to non-conformal contacts (gears, rolling bearings, cams) where Hertz pressures reach 0.5–3 GPa. Key features are: nearly flat central film determined by the Dowson-Higginson equation, a constriction and pressure spike at the exit, and starvation/cavitation in the outlet region. ISO-viscous EHL (soft EHL, common in polymer and rubber contacts) has large elastic deformation but negligible viscosity-pressure rise.
