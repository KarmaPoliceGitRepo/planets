# Electromagnetic Acoustic Transducer (EMAT)

**Aliases:** EMAT; electromagnetic acoustic transducer; non-contact ultrasonic transducer (EMAT)

**Definition:** An EMAT is a non-contact ultrasonic transducer that generates and detects elastic waves in electrically conducting or ferromagnetic materials without liquid couplant. It comprises a coil (carrying a radio-frequency current) positioned close to the specimen surface within a static magnetic field. Generation: eddy currents induced by the coil interact with the magnetic field via the Lorentz force, directly stressing the material. In ferromagnetic materials the magnetostrictive mechanism also contributes. Reception is by the inverse process. EMATs can generate a wide variety of wave modes (longitudinal, shear horizontal, Rayleigh, Lamb) by choosing coil geometry and field orientation, and operate at elevated temperatures and through coatings.
