# Shear Wave
**Aliases:** transverse wave; S-wave; SV wave; SH wave; shear horizontal wave; shear vertical wave; shear wave velocity; V_S; transverse wave velocity; transverse ultrasonic waves

**Definition:** A shear (transverse) wave is a bulk elastic wave in which particle displacement is perpendicular to the propagation direction. In isotropic solids the phase velocity is V_S = √(G/ρ) = √(C₄₄/ρ). Shear waves do not propagate in fluids (G = 0), a property exploited in shear-wave viscometry. They are more sensitive to shear stress and plasticity-related microstructural changes than longitudinal waves, and acoustic birefringence (splitting of two orthogonally polarized shear waves) enables direct stress-difference measurement.
