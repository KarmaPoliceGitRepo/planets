# Uniaxial Poling

**Aliases:** PVDF poling; electric field poling; polarisation process; piezoelectric poling; piezoceramic poling

**Definition:** Uniaxial poling is the process of aligning the spontaneous polarisation of ferroelectric domains in a piezoelectric ceramic or polymer along a single preferred axis by applying a strong DC electric field (typically several MV/m) at elevated temperature (near but below Tc), then cooling while maintaining the field. In PZT ceramics this produces net macroscopic piezoelectric activity from a sintered polycrystalline body that was initially isotropic. For PVDF, poling aligns the CF₂ dipoles in the β-crystalline phase. The remnant polarisation determines the magnitude of the piezoelectric coefficients, and partial depolarisation occurs on re-heating near Tc.
