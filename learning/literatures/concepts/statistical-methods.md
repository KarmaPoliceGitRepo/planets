# Statistical Methods
**Aliases:** p-value; statistical power; effect size; confidence interval; normal distribution; Poisson distribution; variance; standard deviation; coefficient of variation; Jaccard index; normalised mean difference; false positive; publication bias; pre-registration; replication crisis; randomisation; blinding; control tests; repeat tests; effect size; statistical significance

**Definition:** Null hypothesis significance testing (NHST) uses the p-value — the probability of observing a test statistic at least as extreme as the data under the null hypothesis — to decide whether to reject H₀. Statistical power (1−β) is the probability of correctly rejecting H₀ when it is false; it increases with effect size, sample size, and significance threshold. The coefficient of variation (CV = σ/μ) expresses relative variability. Publication bias (preferential publication of significant results) inflates effect sizes in the literature; pre-registration and replication are countermeasures. A Jaccard index measures set similarity.
