# Mechanical Quality Factor

**Aliases:** Qm; Q_pm; Q_tm; mechanical Q; Q factor (piezoelectric); piezoelectric Qm

**Definition:** The mechanical quality factor Qm of a piezoelectric resonator is the ratio of energy stored per cycle to energy dissipated per cycle; it equals resonant frequency divided by the 3-dB bandwidth of the admittance/impedance resonance peak. High Qm (>500, hard PZT) implies low internal mechanical losses, narrow resonance bandwidth, and suitability for continuous-wave high-power applications such as sonar and ultrasonic cleaning. Low Qm (<100, soft PZT or PVDF) implies broader bandwidth and higher sensitivity, preferred for pulse-echo imaging and broadband receivers. Qm is distinct from the electrical quality factor of the dielectric (1/tan δ).
