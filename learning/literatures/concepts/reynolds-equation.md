# Reynolds Equation
**Aliases:** hydrodynamic Reynolds equation; Vogelpohl parameter; Sommerfeld number; thin-film lubrication equation

**Definition:** The Reynolds equation is the governing PDE for pressure distribution in a thin lubricant film between two approaching or sliding surfaces, derived by simplifying the Navier-Stokes equations under the thin-film assumption. It balances pressure-driven and wedge-film (squeeze and stretch) terms: ∂/∂x(h³/η · ∂p/∂x) + ∂/∂z(h³/η · ∂p/∂z) = 6U·∂h/∂x + 12·∂h/∂t. The Vogelpohl substitution linearises the Reynolds equation for numerical solution. The Sommerfeld number (S = (r/c)²·ηN/P) is the key dimensionless parameter for journal bearing design.
