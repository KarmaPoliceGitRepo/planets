# Nonlinear Acoustics
**Aliases:** nonlinear wave propagation; finite-amplitude acoustics; acoustic nonlinearity; nonlinear elasticity; nonlinear ultrasound; nonlinear wave modulation spectroscopy; NWMS; KZK equation; Westervelt equation; Burgers equation; quasilinear approximation

**Definition:** Nonlinear acoustics describes wave propagation when finite-amplitude effects (governed by TOE constants and the B/A parameter) cause harmonic distortion, wave steepening, and waveform asymmetry as the wave propagates. In solids, the nonlinear parameter β characterises the rate of second-harmonic growth; in fluids, the B/A parameter does the same. Key physical phenomena include harmonic generation, self-demodulation, and wave-wave interaction. The KZK (Khokhlov–Zabolotskaya–Kuznetsov) equation governs nonlinear focused beams including diffraction and attenuation. Defects and contacts introduce strongly nonlinear signatures (contact acoustic nonlinearity) even at very small strain amplitudes — far exceeding the material intrinsic nonlinearity.
