# Resonant Frequency

**Aliases:** resonance frequency; fs; characteristic frequency; thickness resonance; longitudinal resonance; fundamental frequency; fr; fa

**Definition:** The resonant frequency of a piezoelectric transducer or specimen is the frequency at which constructive interference of reflected waves within the body maximises the standing-wave amplitude, minimising acoustic impedance. For a plate of thickness t and longitudinal wave velocity c, the fundamental thickness resonance is f₀ = c/(2t). In piezoelectric transducers, the series resonance frequency f_s (minimum impedance) and parallel resonance f_p (maximum impedance) bracket the useful bandwidth; the difference f_p − f_s is related to the coupling coefficient k. In the STAMINA method, the resonant frequency is measured to infer layer thickness or material velocity.
