# Rayleigh Wave
**Aliases:** surface acoustic wave; SAW; Rayleigh surface wave; surface wave; Rayleigh wave speed; cR; Rayleigh wave penetration depth; Rayleigh determinant

**Definition:** A Rayleigh wave is an elastic surface wave propagating along a stress-free solid half-space, with particle motion tracing a retrograde ellipse in the sagittal plane and amplitude decaying exponentially into the bulk (penetration depth ≈ one wavelength). Phase velocity cR satisfies the Rayleigh secular equation (the Rayleigh determinant), giving cR ≈ 0.92 × V_S for most metals with Poisson ratio ~0.3. Because the wave samples only near-surface material, it is sensitive to surface stress state, surface-breaking cracks, and thin coatings; depth sensitivity is tunable by changing frequency.
