# RTD Element Types

**Aliases:** thin-film measuring resistor; wire-wound measuring resistor; F-type; W-type; ceramic RTD; glass RTD; platinum film RTD; platinum wire RTD

**Definition:** RTD sensing elements are manufactured in two principal forms. Thin-film (F-type): a platinum film is deposited by sputtering or printing onto a ceramic substrate, trimmed by laser to the target resistance, and encapsulated; this produces a small, low-cost, robust element with fast response, and is the dominant form for industrial Pt100/Pt1000. Wire-wound (W-type): fine platinum wire (~10–30 µm) is wound on a ceramic or glass mandrel; less sensitive to mechanical strain, wider temperature range, and more stable for calibration applications, but larger and more expensive. Mineral-insulated (MI) cable versions package the element in a robust metal sheath with inorganic insulation for harsh environments.
