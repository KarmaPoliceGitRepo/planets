# Backscattering Coefficient
**Aliases:** BSC; backscatter amplitude; backscattering cross-section; grain noise amplitude; coherent backscattering; diffuse backscattering

**Definition:** The backscattering coefficient (BSC) quantifies the amplitude of ultrasonic energy scattered back toward the source from microstructural features (grains, inclusions, pores) per unit volume. It is experimentally accessible from pulse-echo amplitude statistics without requiring knowledge of the propagation path. In the Rayleigh regime BSC ∝ f⁴D⁶ (D = grain diameter); in the stochastic regime BSC ∝ f²D³. BSC is related to the attenuation coefficient via energy conservation in the independent-scattering approximation. Unlike attenuation measurements, BSC can be spatially resolved through gating, enabling mapping of local microstructural heterogeneity.
