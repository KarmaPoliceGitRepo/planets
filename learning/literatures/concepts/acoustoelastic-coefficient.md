# Acoustoelastic Coefficient
**Aliases:** acoustoelastic constant; K; B; Lzz; M; pseudo-elastic coefficient

**Definition:** The acoustoelastic coefficient K (or B, M, Lzz depending on notation) is the material- and mode-specific constant of proportionality in the linearised acoustoelastic relation Δv/v₀ = K·σ. Its value is a combination of second-order elastic (SOE) constants (Lamé constants) and third-order elastic (TOE) constants, and it differs for longitudinal, transverse, and surface wave modes and for different propagation/stress orientations. For steel, K typically lies in the range −10⁻⁵ to −10⁻⁴ MPa⁻¹ for longitudinal waves propagated parallel to the stress axis.
