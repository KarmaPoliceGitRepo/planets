# Buffer Rod Technique
**Aliases:** buffer rod; delay line; waveguide rod; buffer rod sensor; delay-line transducer; clad buffer rod; acoustic waveguide; buffer rod thermometry

**Definition:** The buffer rod (delay-line) technique interposes an acoustic waveguide rod between a room-temperature piezoelectric transducer and a hot or inaccessible measurement surface. The rod provides (i) thermal isolation protecting the transducer from temperatures above its Curie point, (ii) a known acoustic delay separating the transducer ringdown from the measurement signal, and (iii) impedance transformation if the rod material is chosen appropriately. Clad buffer rods (core + dissimilar sleeve, e.g., steel core + stainless steel cladding) suppress mode-converted flexural signals that would otherwise contaminate the longitudinal echo. The technique enables contact ultrasonics at process temperatures up to ~1000°C.
