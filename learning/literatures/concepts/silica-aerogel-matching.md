# Silica Aerogel (Acoustic Matching)

**Aliases:** aerogel; silica aerogel matching layer; aerogel transducer matching

**Definition:** Silica aerogel is an ultra-low-density porous silica solid with acoustic impedance ~0.06–0.1 MRayl and very low acoustic attenuation, making it nearly ideal as a quarter-wavelength matching layer between PZT (~30 MRayl) and air (~0.0004 MRayl). Its required impedance for a two-layer matching stack is approximately (30 × 0.0004)^(1/4) ≈ 0.07 MRayl, which aerogel can achieve. The primary practical challenges are fragility, difficulty bonding to the piezoelectric element, and batch-to-batch porosity variation affecting impedance and velocity.
