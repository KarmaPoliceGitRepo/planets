# Grinding
**Aliases:** abrasive machining; cylindrical grinding; surface grinding; creep-feed grinding; specific grinding energy; grinding burn; workpiece burn; thermal damage; arc contact length; Peclet number; grinding ratio; G-ratio

**Definition:** Grinding is a high-speed abrasive machining process where material is removed by hard abrasive grains embedded in a wheel. It achieves tight dimensional tolerances and fine surface finishes inaccessible to cutting tools. The specific grinding energy (energy per unit volume removed) is ~10–100× higher than turning because of small chip sizes and ploughing losses. Heat generation is the primary limiting factor: the arc contact length le and workpiece speed govern the Peclet number Pe = Vw·le/α, which determines the heat partition into the workpiece. Thermal damage (grinding burn) causes microstructural changes, residual stress reversal, and possible re-hardening or softening that compromise component life.
