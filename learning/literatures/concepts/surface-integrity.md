# Surface Integrity
**Aliases:** surface integrity; subsurface damage; work-hardened layer; white layer; heat-affected zone; machined surface; ground surface; hardness profile; microhardness; residual stress profile; surface layer; Beilby layer; mechanically affected zone; thermally affected zone

**Definition:** Surface integrity is the totality of surface and subsurface properties induced by a manufacturing process, encompassing: (1) geometric features (roughness, waviness, lay); (2) mechanical properties — residual stress profile, micro-hardness depth profile, and work-hardened layer; (3) metallurgical state — microstructural alterations such as white layers (remelted or nanocrystalline surface), heat-affected zones (softening in hardened steel), and phase changes. High surface integrity requires controlled cutting/grinding parameters; thermal damage (grinding burn) and tensile residual stresses are major quality failures. The Beilby layer is an amorphous nano-layer formed during highly plasticised cutting or polishing.
