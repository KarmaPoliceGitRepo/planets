# Multiple Reflection Method

**Aliases:** multiple wave method; continuous-wave standing wave technique; multiple reflection technique; standing wave resonance method

**Definition:** The multiple reflection method is a variant of the standing-wave resonance technique in which the constructive interference of successive reflections within a layer (transducer–specimen sandwich) amplifies the wave field, increasing the sensitivity to small reflection coefficient changes at the measurement interface. When the drive frequency equals a resonance of the combined system, a small R at the interface produces a large change in the standing-wave amplitude, enabling measurement of very thin films or slight contact. The mathematical model sums the infinite series of multiple reflections to give the total transmitted or received amplitude as a function of frequency and interface R.
