# Ultrasonic Thermometry
**Aliases:** acoustic thermometry; ultrasonic temperature measurement; ultrasonic internal temperature profiling; 2D temperature field reconstruction; temperature tomography; bisection algorithm; temperature inversion; internal temperature gradient measurement

**Definition:** Ultrasonic thermometry infers temperature — including internal profiles not accessible to contact probes — by inverting the measured time-of-flight through a calibrated velocity–temperature curve V(T). For surface temperature: a SAW or Rayleigh wave samples the near-surface region. For internal (through-thickness) profiling: a pulse-echo or pitch-catch arrangement with multiple reflections and a layer-peeling bisection algorithm reconstructs the temperature gradient profile layer by layer. For 2D mapping: TOF measurements along multiple crossing paths are tomographically inverted. High-temperature applications require buffer rods or non-contact (laser/EMAT) methods.
