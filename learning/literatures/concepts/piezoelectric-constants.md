# Piezoelectric Constants

**Aliases:** d constant; g constant; e constant; h constant; piezoelectric charge constant; piezoelectric voltage constant; d33; d31; d15; Kp; K33; K31; K15; coupling coefficient; piezoelectric charge constant (d); coupling coefficient

**Definition:** Piezoelectric materials are characterised by a family of tensor coupling coefficients relating mechanical and electrical quantities. The charge constants d_ij (C/N or m/V) relate applied stress to induced charge density or applied field to induced strain; d33 acts along the poling axis, d31 transversely, d15 in shear. The voltage constants g_ij (Vm/N) relate stress to open-circuit field. The coupling coefficient k (dimensionless, 0–1) is the square root of the fraction of input energy converted to the other domain; common forms are k_p (planar), k_33 (thickness), k_31 (lateral), k_15 (shear). A full description requires the stiffness tensor c, piezoelectric tensor e (or d), and permittivity tensor ε at constant stress or strain.
