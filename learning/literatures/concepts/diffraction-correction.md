# Diffraction Correction
**Aliases:** beam spreading correction; near-field correction; diffraction phase correction; acoustic diffraction bias; diffraction loss; diffraction phase shift

**Definition:** Diffraction correction removes the systematic effect of beam spreading (diffraction) from measured echo amplitudes or phase shifts, isolating true material attenuation or velocity. Amplitude correction accounts for geometric divergence of the beam beyond the near field. Phase correction (critical for precise velocity measurement) accounts for the phase accumulated as the beam evolves from a plane wave at the transducer face into the far-field pattern; this phase varies significantly in the near field and must be subtracted from the measured phase difference. For laser-generated waves (dipole source), the diffraction correction differs from that for a piston (uniform velocity) source.
