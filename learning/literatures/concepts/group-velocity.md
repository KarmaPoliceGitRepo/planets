# Group Velocity
**Aliases:** vg; U = dω/dk; energy velocity; packet velocity

**Definition:** Group velocity is the speed at which the envelope of a wave packet (and, for non-absorbing media, the acoustic energy) propagates: v_g = dω/dk. In non-dispersive media v_g = v_p; in dispersive media (guided waves, scattering materials) they differ. For Lamb and SAW modes the group velocity curve (v_g vs f·d) exhibits minima at zero-group-velocity (ZGV) points associated with strong resonances. Accurate group velocity measurement requires broadband excitation and time–frequency analysis; group velocity in practice is the measurable arrival time of a recognizable feature in the pulse envelope.
