# Reflection Coefficient
**Aliases:** R; acoustic reflection coefficient; reflectance; ultrasonic reflectance; global reflection coefficient; reflection factor; standing wave reflection coefficient; S (standing-wave amplitude ratio)

**Definition:** The acoustic reflection coefficient R is the ratio of reflected to incident wave amplitude at an interface or layered structure. For a plane wave at normal incidence on a single interface, R = (Z₂ − Z₁)/(Z₂ + Z₁); more generally, for multilayer systems, R is frequency-dependent and given by the global transfer-matrix formulation. At a partially-contacting or thin-layer interface, R is also frequency-dependent and encodes the interfacial stiffness K or layer thickness h. R = +1 for a rigid boundary, R = −1 for a stress-free boundary; experimentally R is obtained by comparing received amplitudes to an air-backed reference (R_ref ≈ 1).
