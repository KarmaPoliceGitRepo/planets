# Beam Directivity (EMAT)

**Aliases:** steering angle; beam pattern; EMAT beam directivity; angular radiation pattern

**Definition:** EMAT beam directivity is the angular distribution of radiated ultrasonic energy at a fixed radial distance from the transducer. For a meander-line coil array of N elements with spatial period d, the far-field radiation pattern is a phased-array sinc-like function; the main lobe direction is determined by the ratio of wavelength to coil pitch and can be steered electronically by applying inter-element time delays. The half-power beam width narrows as N increases, and grating lobes appear when d > λ/2.
