## SCRIPT

### [COLD OPEN — no music]
> **HOST:** Ask almost anyone who left Nepal if they hate it. They'll look at you
> like you're mad. *"Hate it? I dream about it."* So here's the strange part —
> nobody who leaves seems to think Nepal is… *shit.* And yet the planes keep
> filling up. [beat] If it's not hate that makes people leave… what is it?

### [THEME MUSIC up, then under]
> **HOST:** This is **The Missing Link** — a show about the gap between loving a
> place and being able to live in it. I'm [your name]. Welcome to episode one.

### [WELCOME — the big question]
> **HOST:** Here's the thing this whole series is built on. When people leave
> Nepal, they don't leave out of anger. They leave for **economic reasons.** The
> job, the wage, the hospital that's too far, the road that washes out every
> monsoon. The love is real. The maths just doesn't add up at home.
>
> So the question I keep circling is not *"why don't people love their country?"*
> They do. The real question is: **what's missing — the missing link — that makes
> love not enough to stay?**

### [STORY — one person who left]
> **HOST:** Let me tell you about someone. [Introduce a real returnee/migrant —
> with consent — or narrate a composite clearly labelled as such.]
> [Their story: where they're from, what they loved, the exact economic moment
> that tipped them into leaving, what they send home, what they miss.]
>
> [If you have a guest clip, drop it here. End on their answer to: *"What would
> have made you stay?"* — that answer is the heartbeat of the show.]

### ["ONE NUMBER"]
> **HOST:** One number for today. [Insert a single, sourced statistic — e.g. the
> share of Nepal's GDP that comes from remittances, or the number of Nepalis who
> leave for foreign work each day. **Cite the source in the show notes.**] Sit
> with that number. That's not a country people are abandoning. That's a country
> that's been *quietly exporting the people who love it most.*

### [MAHENDRA & GYANENDRA — sourced framing]
> **HOST:** This isn't a new worry. Decades ago there was a push — associated
> with **King Mahendra** — that said, roughly: *let's return to our villages and
> improve our local infrastructure.* Build the road, the clinic, the school, the
> mill — so people don't have to leave to live a decent life.
> [Attribute carefully — quote a historian or document in the notes.]
>
> And here's what's curious: a version of that same idea is being raised
> again today, in the circles around **Gyanendra.** Same instinct, new decade.
> Which tells me the problem never got solved — it just went quiet and came back.
> [Again: source the claim. We attribute; we don't assert.]
>
> So *why* is it still the case? That's what the rest of this season chases.

### [THE FOUR THREADS]
> **HOST:** I think the missing link isn't one thing — it's four, and they fit
> together. Over this season we'll pull each one.
>
> **One — energy.** Nepal is sitting on heat. *Tato pani* — hot springs — and
> thermal vents. Geothermal energy a village could actually own. [Tease ep. 5.]
>
> **Two — high-value tourism.** A trekker will pay **a thousand pounds** for one
> short trip into **Mustang** or **Manang.** That money is real. The question is
> whether it reaches the village or leaks past it. [Tease ep. 6.]
>
> **Three — licensing.** If locals are going to guide that tourism, they need a
> proper **licensing system** — not red tape, but **security**: recognised,
> safe, paid fairly, trusted by visitors. [Tease ep. 7.]
>
> **Four — the link that connects them.** Imagine an app — think **Uber, but for
> trekking guides** — that connects a visitor straight to a licensed local, and
> takes only a small fee. The income goes to the guide; the village keeps the
> value. [Tease ep. 8.]

### [THE "MISSING LINK" OF THE WEEK]
> **HOST:** So if you make me name it for episode one, here it is: the missing
> link is **opportunity that stays local.** Not charity. Not a lecture about
> patriotism. A way for the love people already feel to finally *pay rent.*

### [CALL TO ACTION — outro music up]
> **HOST:** Here's what I want from you. Tell me about **your** village's missing
> link. What's the one thing that would let your family stay — or come back? Send
> it, comment it, share this with the one person who needs to hear it.
>
> Next week: the village we left, and a movement that tried to bring us back.
> This has been **The Missing Link.** I'm [your name]. *Pheri bhetaula* — see you
> again.

### [OUTRO MUSIC up and out]
