# The Missing Link — Series Arc (Season 1)

> The content plan. Each episode is a self-contained story that also advances one
> big question: **what is the "missing link" between loving Nepal and being able
> to stay in it?** The arc moves from *problem* → *resources* → *solution* →
> *call to action*.

## The thesis in one line
> *Nobody who leaves Nepal hates it. They leave for economic reasons. So the
> question isn't "why don't people love their country?" — it's "what's missing
> that makes love not enough to stay?"*

## Format
- **Length:** 20–35 min. **Cadence:** weekly. **Languages:** Nepali + English
  (key segments translated; transcripts both ways over time).
- **Spine of every episode:** a human story → the data behind it → a concrete,
  hopeful idea → a call to the listener.

## Recurring segments
- **"One number"** — a single statistic that frames the episode.
- **"From the trail"** — a field clip (a guide, a hot spring, a village).
- **"The missing link"** — the host names, each week, the one thing that would
  let that story's family stay.

## How the venture (guide app) threads through
The Tourism Guide Platform is **introduced as content** (ep. 6–8), not built by
this repo. A concept sketch lives in [`research-pack.md`](research-pack.md). The
podcast's job is to investigate it honestly (including risks) and build a
community around it via the per-episode CTA (F10 → F1).

## Editorial rules (from CR-3, CR-4)
- Attribute claims about people and movements (Mahendra, Gyanendra) to sources.
- Get **consent** from every guest/subject (use the release note in
  [`show-notes-template.md`](show-notes-template.md)).
- Represent migrants and villagers **with dignity** — they are the heroes, not a
  problem to be solved.
