# Research Pack — themes, questions & the venture concept

> Raw material to make episodes *true* and *deep*. **Verify every figure before
> you say it on air** (CR-3) — treat the prompts below as leads, not facts. Log
> confirmed sources in each episode's show notes.

## Theme 1 — Migration: love vs. economics
**Claim to explore:** Nepalis don't leave from disliking Nepal; they leave for
economic reasons (jobs, wages, services).
- Questions: What share of GDP is remittances? How many leave for foreign work
  per day/year? Top destinations? What would make them stay/return?
- Where to verify: Nepal Rastra Bank remittance reports, Dept. of Foreign
  Employment data, World Bank, ILO. *(Confirm current numbers.)*

## Theme 2 — "Return to the villages" (Mahendra → Gyanendra)
**Claim to explore:** A historic push (associated with King Mahendra) to develop
villages/local infrastructure so people needn't leave; a version re-emerging in
circles around Gyanendra today.
- Questions: What exactly did the historic programme propose? Why did it stall —
  funding, politics, geography? What's being said now, by whom, and how similar
  is it?
- Editorial care: **attribute** — "associated with", "reportedly", quote
  historians/primary documents. Avoid putting words in anyone's mouth.

## Theme 3 — Natural disasters
**Claim:** Quakes, floods, landslides repeatedly reset village economies and push
out-migration.
- Questions: Impact of the 2015 earthquake on rural livelihoods? Annual
  monsoon/landslide road losses? How does disaster risk factor into the
  "stay vs. leave" maths? What does *resilient* local infrastructure look like?

## Theme 4 — Geothermal energy ("tato pani" / thermal vents)
**Claim:** Nepal's hot springs and thermal vents are an under-used local
energy/heat resource.
- Questions: Where are the main hot springs (e.g. along the Trans-Himalayan
  belt)? Realistic uses — direct heat, drying, bathing tourism, small power? What
  are the limits (geology, cost, grid)? Any existing pilots?
- Honesty: geothermal at village scale is mostly *direct-use heat*, not big
  electricity — present it realistically.

## Theme 5 — High-value tourism (Mustang, Manang)
**Claim:** A tourist may spend ~£1,000 on a short trip; restricted areas like
Upper Mustang carry special permits.
- Questions: What does that £1,000 cover, and how much reaches the village vs.
  agencies/airlines abroad? What are the permit systems? How do you raise the
  *local* share of each tourist pound?
- Insight: the goal is **high-value, low-volume** tourism that protects culture
  and environment while maximising local income.

## Theme 6 — Licensing for local guides
**Claim:** A licensing system gives guides *security* — recognition, safety,
fair pay, tourist trust.
- Questions: What licensing exists now (e.g. trekking-guide rules, mandatory-guide
  policies)? Gaps? How could licensing protect both guide and tourist without
  becoming gatekeeping that locals can't afford?

## Theme 7 — The "Uber for guides" venture (context/future system)
**Concept (subject of the show, NOT built in this repo):**

```
Tourist app  ──search/book──▶  [ Platform ]  ──connects──▶  Licensed local guide
                                   │
                                   ├─ verifies guide licence (Theme 6)
                                   ├─ handles booking + reviews + safety
                                   └─ takes a SMALL fee; most money → guide
```
- **Value proposition:** tourists get a trusted, licensed local; guides get
  steady, fair income with dignity; villages keep more of the tourist pound.
- **Why "small fee" matters:** it's the opposite of extractive middlemen — the
  point is to *route* money to locals, not skim it.
- **Hard questions to put on air (be honest):** connectivity in remote areas;
  smartphone/payment access; trust & safety; who verifies licences; avoiding a
  race-to-the-bottom on price; what happens to guides without phones; regulation.
- **Where it would live if built:** a separate project (mobile app + backend +
  payments + licence registry). This podcast's job is to *investigate and rally a
  community around it* — see the per-episode CTA (F10 → F1).

## Interview question bank (reusable)
1. Where are you from, and what do you love about it?
2. Tell me about the moment leaving started to feel necessary.
3. What would have made you stay — be specific?
4. What does home need that it doesn't have?
5. If money flowed *to* the village instead of past it, what changes?
6. What's the one "missing link" you'd fix first?
